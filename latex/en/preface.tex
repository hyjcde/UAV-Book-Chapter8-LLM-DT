\preface

Urban building stocks are aging while cities continue to densify. Structural
health monitoring therefore needs safer, faster, and more consistent inspection
workflows than manual rope-access surveys can provide. Unmanned aerial vehicles
(UAVs) have changed how fa\c{c}ade imagery is collected, yet the literature still
often separates flight planning, 3D reconstruction, defect recognition, and
facility decision support into disconnected silos.

This monograph presents an end-to-end view of AI-driven UAV building inspection.
It connects task and motion planning, digital representation and GIS, deep
learning for defect analysis, and---in the final technical chapter---large
language models grounded in digital twins for inspection reporting and
maintenance decision support.

The book is written for graduate students, researchers, and practitioners in
civil engineering, robotics, and artificial intelligence who wish to design or
deploy autonomous inspection pipelines.
