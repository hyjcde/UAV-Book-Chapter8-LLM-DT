\chapter{Digital Twin with LLMs in Inspection Analysis}
\label{ch:llm-dt}

\abstract*{After UAV planning, reconstruction, defect perception, and GIS anchoring, building inspection still needs a decision layer that can answer natural-language questions, cite twin evidence, and support maintenance actions. This chapter introduces LLM technology families for civil engineering, maps each family to fa\c{c}ade inspection, and shows how retrieval, structure, tools, memory, and bounded agents attach to a language-ready digital twin---not as a generic AI tutorial and not as a single-paper reprint.}

\abstract{Chapters~2--7 of this monograph explain how to fly, reconstruct, detect, and map. Chapter~8 asks what an inspector or facility manager can do next with that twin: query it in ordinary language, fuse modality-tagged evidence already produced by perception, obtain answers that name real assets, and move toward reports and schedules. Matching Book Proposal Phase~4 and the frozen TOC~8.1--8.6, the chapter first clarifies what a language-ready twin must expose; then introduces LLM technology families (pretraining, instruction, reasoning prompts, RAG, structured generation, tools, memory, bounded agents) and specialises each to inspection; then shows how twin records, multimodal evidence, reports, and decision metrics close the loop. Perception remains the source of typed defect findings; the LLM reads structured twin memory under citation rules. Vision-language end-to-end detection is left to Chapter~6 and related perception literature---this chapter consumes modality-tagged observations rather than replacing detectors.}

\section{Digital Twins for Building Inspection}
\label{sec:ch8-dt}

The monograph is one pipeline. Task and motion planning (Chapters~2--3) produce safe coverage. Reconstruction (Chapter~4) turns imagery into geometry. Datasets and perception models (Chapters~5--6) name defects. GIS and GeoBIM (Chapter~7) give assets addresses in the city. None of those products, alone, answers the questions that dominate fa\c{c}ade operations and maintenance (O\&M): which panels participate in a multi-storey moisture path, which assets should be staged for gondola access this quarter, or how to draft a repair brief that an engineer can audit. Those are decision-layer questions. They require connecting twin entities to language, retrieval, memory, and---when actions are proposed---tool-using agents.

Digital twins in construction and civil infrastructure are best understood as evolving information systems rather than photorealistic viewers~\cite{boje2020,opoku2021,lu2020}. Survey work on construction twins emphasises lifecycle information flows, stakeholder coordination, and the gap between visualisation and actionable semantics. Building and heritage documentation literature likewise shows that geometry without provenance quickly becomes ornamental~\cite{bruno2018,tsilimantou2020}. For UAV fa\c{c}ade programmes the same lesson appears in sharper form: a mesh that cannot answer ``which maintainable host owns this detection?'' cannot support language-level O\&M.

A geometric twin answers location. A language-ready twin for inspection must also expose three further commitments. First, stable asset identifiers that facility teams already use in work orders---not ad-hoc filenames invented at capture time. Second, provenance for every observation: sensor modality (for example RGB or infrared), capture time, detector or annotator signature, and a pointer back to imagery that an engineer can open. Third, relations that support propagation reasoning: which maintainable hosts sit above, below, or beside one another on a fa\c{c}ade stack. Without those commitments, an LLM can only chat over loose files: fluent, but not auditable for facility management.

UAV--BIM inspection reviews sketched early automation agendas that already pointed toward this decision layer~\cite{zhang2022}. Group work on GeoBIM-coupled twin modelling~\cite{zhang2025} and detection--reconstruction--registration pipelines~\cite{yang2025detreconreg} supplies the geometric and identifier premise used below; related twin deployments extend to site monitoring beyond fa\c{c}ades~\cite{han2025}. Facility-oriented digital-twin discussions further stress that O\&M value appears only when twins connect to work processes and document stores~\cite{chen2023facility,wu2022}. The teaching point for readers of this book is not to re-derive reconstruction or GIS methods. It is to treat Chapters~4--7 as an \emph{interface contract}: if the twin cannot tell a language system which Asset-ID hosts a detection, when the image was captured, and which neighbours sit next to that host, then language interfaces will invent structure.

Consider a concrete O\&M scene. After a warehouse fa\c{c}ade campaign, a manager asks whether mid-storey delamination on the west elevation is linked to moisture staining above. A mesh viewer can show both regions. A Chapter~6 detector can label both patches. Only a language-ready twin can assemble neighbour passports, cite observation IDs with openable images, and either draft a repair brief or abstain when evidence is thin. That scene is the pedagogical target of the rest of the chapter: the twin is the long-term store; the LLM is a reader and proposer under rules, not a second source of geometry.

The same scene also clarifies what this chapter does \emph{not} do. It does not replace Chapters~5--6 perception models with a second detector stack inside the language system. Typed defect labels, confidences, and host attachments remain perception products. The decision layer consumes those products as structured records, retrieves related twin context, and generates language that can be audited. Readers who need detector design should return to Chapter~6; readers who need flight geometry should return to Chapters~2--4. What follows is how language systems should behave once those upstream products exist.

Beyond the warehouse vignette, language-ready twins must survive organisational reality. Inspection programmes rarely have a single owner: UAV contractors capture imagery, consultants interpret defects, facility teams issue work orders, and asset managers reconcile budgets. A twin that is only a visualisation shared by email cannot mediate those roles. Identifiers must be stable across organisations; observation provenance must survive file hand-offs; neighbour relations must be computable without calling the person who built the mesh. Those are information-system requirements, not rendering requirements~\cite{boje2020,opoku2021,lu2020,chen2023facility}.

It is also useful to distinguish three twin maturity levels that readers will meet in practice. A \emph{geometric twin} stores meshes, point clouds, or BIM shells. A \emph{semantic twin} attaches typed entities and Asset-IDs. A \emph{decision-ready twin} additionally exposes observation cards, topology for propagation, and interfaces that retrieval and tools can call. This chapter assumes the second level as a minimum and teaches the third. Many disappointing ``AI for inspection'' demos fail because they bolt an LLM onto the first level and then blame the model for inventing panels.

Stakeholder questions further clarify the interface contract. An inspector asks what changed since the last campaign. A facility manager asks which assets block a gondola day. A compliance officer asks which clause and which photo support a repair recommendation. A planner asks where to fly next. Only the last question returns partly to Chapters~2--3; the first three are decision-layer questions that need language over structured twin memory. If the twin cannot answer them with citations, no amount of prompt engineering will create auditability after the fact.



Historic and contemporary inspection practice already encodes many of the disciplines this chapter formalises for machines. Paper checklists force inspectors to name locations; photo logs force provenance; repair tickets force action fields. What digital twins and LLMs add is scale and queryability across campaigns---not a licence to abandon those disciplines. On the contrary, automation without checklist discipline amplifies error. Readers from facilities backgrounds should therefore recognise familiar quality controls in the Asset-ID, provenance, and cite-or-abstain rules below; only the carrier changes from clipboard to twin memory~\cite{zhang2022,chen2023facility,wu2022}.

Geographic scale introduces further constraints. A single warehouse elevation is already multimodal and multi-storey; a campus or housing estate multiplies identifier collisions and manual-edition conflicts. Chapter~7's GIS integration is the right place to keep city-scale identity coherent. Chapter~8 assumes that coherence and refuses to ``fix'' identity drift with clever language. If two contractors use different panel codes for the same host, the language system should surface the collision, not pick a winner in prose~\cite{zhang2025,han2025}.



\textbf{From checklist culture to twin contracts.}
Seasoned inspectors already practise a form of grounding: they photograph before writing, they name bays before diagnosing, and they refuse to sign a report that cannot be walked on site. The decision-ready twin simply makes those habits machine-checkable across campaigns. Table~\ref{tab:ch8-twin-contract} is therefore not an IT wishlist; it is the digital continuation of field craft~\cite{zhang2022,chen2023facility}. When organisations skip the contract and buy a chat interface first, they usually rediscover the same craft six months later---after the first invented Asset-ID reaches a work order.

\textbf{What ``language-ready'' changes in procurement.}
Procurement language often asks for ``AI dashboards.'' A more precise ask is: Can the system return openable evidence for every named asset? Can it abstain? Can it expand neighbours from BIM rather than from text similarity? Can writes be gated? Vendors that answer only with model-size slides are selling parametric memory. Inspection buyers need twin interfaces~\cite{boje2020,opoku2021,wu2022}. Chapter~8 gives readers vocabulary to insist on those interfaces before licence fees are paid.


\section{Foundations of LLMs for Civil Engineering Applications}
\label{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}

Contemporary LLMs rest on the Transformer architecture~\cite{vaswani2017attention}. Self-attention models long-range dependencies in text, which matters when an inspection question mixes symptoms, locations, and code clauses in one utterance. Large-scale pretraining produced few-shot learners that follow natural-language instructions with little task-specific fine-tuning~\cite{brown2020}. Instruction tuning and preference optimisation further align models to user intent~\cite{ouyang2022instructgpt}. Chain-of-thought prompting can elicit intermediate steps that help multi-hop questions~\cite{wei2022cot}, but it does not guarantee that a named fa\c{c}ade panel actually exists in the twin. Reviews of GPT-style tools in construction make the same caution for AEC practice: capability is real, governance and factual grounding are not automatic~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction}.

For inspection teaching it helps to separate three model behaviours that are often conflated. \emph{Linguistic competence} is the ability to parse and produce fluent text about buildings. \emph{Parametric recall} is the ability to remember facts absorbed during training---useful for generic vocabulary, unreliable for site-specific Asset-IDs. \emph{Grounded reasoning} is the ability to answer using evidence that the system retrieved or was given in the current session. Fa\c{c}ade O\&M lives or dies on the third behaviour. An LLM that can write elegant moisture narratives without citing twin observations is not an inspection assistant; it is a drafting toy.


\textbf{LLM technology families and how each applies to fa\c{c}ade inspection.}
Book Proposal Phase~4 asks this chapter to introduce generative language technology for civil engineering and then attach it to inspection analysis. For teaching, we group LLM technologies into families. Each family is first defined, then immediately specialised to UAV fa\c{c}ade O\&M. Perception remains Chapter~6's job; here language technologies consume typed, modality-tagged observations.

\emph{Family T1 --- Pretraining and few-shot language competence.}
Transformers pretrained at scale learn general language and shallow building vocabulary~\cite{vaswani2017attention,brown2020}. \emph{Inspection use:} parse mixed questions (``mid-storey hollow with upstairs staining''), draft readable briefs, translate between stakeholder registers. \emph{Not for:} remembering last campaign's Asset-IDs or inventing crack lengths.

\emph{Family T2 --- Instruction tuning and preference alignment.}
Instruction-tuned models follow task prompts and user preferences more reliably~\cite{ouyang2022instructgpt}. \emph{Inspection use:} enforce answer templates (list IDs, separate observation vs hypothesis, emit abstention codes). \emph{Not for:} overriding twin facts when a hurried manager asks to ``just mark repaired.''

\emph{Family T3 --- Reasoning prompts (chain-of-thought and variants).}
Stepwise prompting can help multi-hop verbal reasoning~\cite{wei2022cot}. \emph{Inspection use:} explain a propagation hypothesis \emph{after} neighbours are in the package. \emph{Not for:} inventing a structural story when topology was never retrieved~\cite{ji2023}.

\emph{Family T4 --- Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG).}
Non-parametric memory loads editioned text or cards at query time~\cite{lewis2020,shuster2021}. Variants include hypothetical queries, self-reflective retry, and adaptive retrieval~\cite{gao2023hyde,asai2024selfrag,jeong2024adaptive}. Civil deployments cover codes, safety manuals, FM documents, and defect dialogue~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,krutli2025fmrags,jeon2025hybridrag,he2025regulationhybrid}. \emph{Inspection use:} retrieve asset passports, observation cards, and pinned manuals. \emph{Design choice:} prefer entity/passport units over arbitrary PDF chunks for asset-centric questions~\cite{peng2024}.

\emph{Family T5 --- Structured and constrained generation.}
Schemas, ontologies, and typed decoding keep outputs machine-checkable~\cite{pan2024b,chen2024bimllmontology,iversen2026,buga2025llmfmsql}. \emph{Inspection use:} report JSON with Asset-IDs $\subseteq$ evidence set; CMMS field maps; cite-or-abstain validators. \emph{Not for:} unconstrained paragraphs scraped afterwards for IDs.

\emph{Family T6 --- Tools and function calling.}
Models that call typed tools (SQL, IFC APIs, calculators) separate reasoning from world actions~\cite{schick2023toolformer,yao2023react,guo2025bimllmquery,liu2025bimcoder,li2025b,li2025c}. \emph{Inspection use:} \texttt{get\_passport}, \texttt{expand\_neighbours}, \texttt{list\_observations}, \texttt{draft\_report}, \texttt{propose\_revisit}. \emph{Gate:} server-side approval for twin writes and dispatch~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent}.

\emph{Family T7 --- Memory architectures beyond the prompt.}
Explicit memory streams and hybrid graph--vector stores support multi-step behaviour~\cite{park2023generativeagents,rawat2025llmmas,pan2024b}. \emph{Inspection use:} the four-rung ladder below---parametric / contextual / RAG / twin---with the twin as authoritative long-term site memory.

\emph{Family T8 --- Bounded agents and multi-agent orchestration.}
Agent surveys and AEC multi-agent systems combine planning, memory, and tools~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,rawat2025llmmas,pan2026graphdtgpt,zhang2025llmagentschema}. \emph{Inspection use:} procedures (tickets, revisits) under geofence and human gates. \emph{Not for:} unsupervised aircraft command or silent twin mutation.

\textbf{One inspection question, eight families in sequence.}
Consider again: ``Is mid-storey hollow on the west elevation related to upstairs staining, and should we stage a gondola this quarter?'' A grounded stack does not pick one family; it composes them. T1 parses the mixed spatial--symptom utterance. T2 forces a template: named Asset-IDs, observation versus hypothesis, optional abstention. T6 tools fetch seed passports and expand BIM neighbours; T4 packs those cards plus any pinned manual clauses into context. T7 ensures the long-term twin---not yesterday's chat---is the store being read. T3 may then verbalise a propagation hypothesis \emph{only over neighbours already present}. T5 emits a schema-constrained draft (IDs $\subseteq$ evidence; repair priority fields optional). T8, if enabled, opens a ticket or revisit proposal behind a human gate. Skip T4/T6 and the answer becomes parametric storytelling; skip T5/gates and fluent text leaks into CMMS. That composition is the proposal's ``generative AI for decision support'' made operational for fa\c{c}ade O\&M~\cite{lewis2020,yao2023react,ji2023}.

\textbf{Memory ladder for inspection.}
Before tools and agents, readers need a memory ladder. \emph{Parametric memory} is whatever the weights absorbed at training time---useful for language and general building knowledge, almost useless for last week's crack growth on a specific warehouse elevation~\cite{brown2020}. \emph{Working or contextual memory} is the prompt window and short dialogue state: powerful, length-limited, and session-fragile. If a manager opens a new chat tomorrow, yesterday's clarification about ``the west loading-dock bay'' may be gone unless the twin or an external store kept it. \emph{Non-parametric retrieval memory} is retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) and related indexes that load editioned manuals or observation cards on demand~\cite{lewis2020,shuster2021}; facility-management deployments already use this pattern on building document corpora~\cite{krutli2025fmrags}. \emph{Structured long-term twin memory} is the Asset-ID store itself---geometry, topology, and time-stamped observations in BIM/GIS, optionally linked to knowledge graphs~\cite{pan2024b,zhang2025}. Generative-agent studies show why explicit memory streams matter for multi-step behaviour~\cite{park2023generativeagents}; construction multi-agent frameworks likewise combine graph memory with vector retrieval~\cite{rawat2025llmmas}. For fa\c{c}ade O\&M, the twin is the authoritative long-term store. The model should read it through retrieval and tools, not pretend the weights remember the site.

Hallucination surveys explain what goes wrong when that discipline is missing: fluent systems invent entities, numbers, and citations~\cite{ji2023}. In inspection the costly failures are concrete---invented Asset-IDs, repair advice without attributable evidence, and summaries that erase disagreement between RGB and infrared observations produced by perception. Grounding therefore means more than a clever prompt. Four strategies recur in NLP and AEC: constrained schemas; retrieval-augmented generation and its adaptive variants~\cite{lewis2020,gao2023hyde,asai2024selfrag,jeong2024adaptive,peng2024}; tool-using reason--act loops~\cite{yao2023react,schick2023toolformer,wang2024surveyagents}; and typed structured knowledge~\cite{pan2024b}. Graph-oriented and hierarchical retrieval further remind readers that document similarity is not the same as building adjacency~\cite{peng2024,sarthi2024raptor,sun2024tog}. Dense retrieval methods such as ColBERT-style late interaction improve passage ranking on open corpora~\cite{khattab2020}; inspection systems can borrow the ranking machinery while still choosing asset passports---not arbitrary chunks---as the unit of retrieval.

\textbf{Civil applications, mapped to inspection.}
Civil and building applications already exercise these strategies, and each maps onto inspection with a twist. BIM virtual assistants, query--DSL systems, and NL-to-BIM retrieval stacks help users navigate models~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,guo2025bimllmquery,liu2025bimcoder}, but navigation is not yet topology-aware defect diagnosis across fa\c{c}ade assets. Building semantic query systems combine RAG and structured stores for building data access~\cite{li2025c,li2025b}. RAG over codes and safety manuals improves factual answers relative to parametric recall alone~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,yang2024promptcode}; hybrid retrieval has been studied for defect-management dialogue~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag}; LLM--ontology and knowledge-graph checkers push automated compliance beyond pure chat~\cite{chen2024bimllmontology,iversen2026,li2024kgllmcheck,nuyts2024comparativeacc,kruiper2024platformnlp}. Energy-analysis agents, IFC tool agents, and graph-twin multi-agent systems show how domain tools can be versioned and how LLMs can broker among users, twins, and robots under human approval~\cite{zhang2025llmagentschema,luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,pan2026graphdtgpt}; structured FM querying further rewards exposing schemas rather than free-form SQL~\cite{buga2025llmfmsql,arslan2024ragenergy}. Knowledge-enhanced industrial LLMs for causal diagnosis illustrate the value of structured domain graphs alongside generation~\cite{zhou2024causalkgpt}. Cognitive and interoperable twin proposals push toward machine-readable industrial knowledge that language systems can query rather than invent~\cite{shi2025cognitivedt}. High-resolution fa\c{c}ade datasets and physical quantification pipelines matter because language inherits the granularity of perception~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2025icra,zhao2026physical}: if perception writes coarse labels, the LLM cannot honestly emit millimetre claims.

\textbf{What to assess when selecting an LLM stack.}
Model choice matters less than interface choice for inspection. Teams should assess: native tool-calling reliability; long-context behaviour on passport sets; willingness to abstain under constrained decoding; deployment constraints (on-prem versus cloud) for building data governance; and cost per grounded answer, not cost per token of chat~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,wang2024surveyagents}. A smaller model with strict cite-or-abstain and solid retrieval often beats a larger model that improvises Asset-IDs. Benchmarks that only score fluent prose are insufficient for O\&M procurement.

The teaching takeaway for inspection is therefore not ``which commercial model,'' but three design choices: retrieve over maintainable assets and neighbours; force citation of IDs that appear in the retrieved set; and place irreversible actions behind approval gates. Prompt-only workflows fail the first two choices as soon as the site leaves the training distribution. Fine-tuning on generic building text can improve style, but it does not install last month's campaign into the weights. Retrieval and twin tools are the practical path for living buildings.

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

Readers new to LLMs often ask whether fine-tuning on inspection reports would ``solve grounding.'' Fine-tuning can adapt tone and domain vocabulary, and it can help the model prefer certain JSON schemas. It cannot, by itself, keep a living building up to date. Every new campaign would demand continual re-training or continual risk of stale parametric memory. Retrieval over editioned manuals and twin stores scales more honestly with campaign frequency~\cite{lewis2020,krutli2025fmrags,joffe2025buildingcodes}. In teaching terms: fine-tune for style and schema obedience if needed; retrieve for facts that change.

Prompting patterns still matter inside that discipline. Instruction-style prompts should force the model to list retrieved Asset-IDs before diagnosis, to separate observations from hypotheses, and to emit an explicit abstention token when evidence is thin~\cite{ouyang2022instructgpt,wei2022cot,ji2023}. Chain-of-thought is helpful for propagation questions only when the intermediate steps name neighbours that actually entered the context. Otherwise the model may invent a causal story that merely sounds structural. Self-reflective and adaptive RAG variants attempt to detect weak retrieval and retry~\cite{asai2024selfrag,jeong2024adaptive,gao2023hyde}; inspection systems can adopt the retry idea while keeping asset passports as the retrieval unit~\cite{peng2024,jeon2025hybridrag}.

Civil engineering readers should also watch for \emph{corpus mismatch}. A model that answers building-code questions well on a public benchmark may still fail on a private fa\c{c}ade handbook written in local administrative language. Safety-manual RAG studies show gains precisely because the corpus is editioned and domain-specific~\cite{lee2024ragsafety}. Defect-management dialogue studies show that hybrid retrieval helps when maintenance text is noisy~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag}. Neither result transfers automatically to UAV twins unless Asset-IDs and topology are in the loop. The mapping table in this section is therefore not a literature trophy case; it is a checklist for specialisation.

Finally, memory governance belongs in foundations, not only in IT policy. Who may write to twin memory after an LLM draft? How long do dialogue clarifications persist? Which manual edition is authoritative when two PDFs disagree? Generative-agent work makes memory streams explicit because multi-step behaviour otherwise drifts~\cite{park2023generativeagents}. Construction multi-agent systems that mix graph and vector memory make the same point in AEC settings~\cite{rawat2025llmmas}. For fa\c{c}ade O\&M we recommend a simple rule: the twin store is write-gated; the dialogue window is ephemeral; RAG indexes are editioned; parametric memory is never treated as a site database.



\textbf{Prompt patterns that respect the memory ladder.}
A practical system prompt for fa\c{c}ade O\&M can be short. Instruct the model to (i)~classify intent, (ii)~speak only about Asset-IDs present in the retrieved package, (iii)~label hypotheses separately from observations, (iv)~preserve modality tags, and (v)~emit \texttt{ABSTAIN:<reason>} when checks fail. Few-shot examples should demonstrate abstention, not only success. Chain-of-thought examples should name real neighbours from the package; otherwise students copy fluent but ungrounded reasoning~\cite{ouyang2022instructgpt,wei2022cot,ji2023}. Retrieval remains outside the prompt: the orchestrator, not the model, chooses which passports enter context~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024}.

\textbf{Where civil LLM papers help---and where they stop.}
BIM assistants teach query discipline on model objects~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,guo2025bimllmquery}. Code and safety RAG teach edition fidelity~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,he2025regulationhybrid}. Defect dialogue RAG teaches noisy maintenance language~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag}. FM document RAG teaches building corpora at scale~\cite{krutli2025fmrags}. Energy and IFC agents teach tool versioning and approval~\cite{zhang2025llmagentschema,gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot}. None of them, alone, is a fa\c{c}ade inspection twin. The specialisation step---Asset-ID passports plus topology---is the contribution readers must make when transferring those papers into UAV O\&M. Construction GPT reviews remain useful as cautionary governance reading~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction}.

\textbf{Token budgets and passport packing.}
Long context is not a licence to dump an entire elevation. Packing should prefer structured digests: Asset-ID, top symptoms, last $n$ observation IDs, neighbour IDs, and short location phrases. Raw image bytes do not belong in the LLM context for this chapter's architecture; image paths do, so humans can open evidence. If a passport digest still overflows, drop low-confidence observations first, not topology links needed for a propagation question. That packing rule is an inspection-specific reading of general RAG context management~\cite{lewis2020,asai2024selfrag,jeong2024adaptive}.



\textbf{Worked memory failures (and repairs).}
Failure~M1: a manager clarifies ``west'' means the loading-dock face; next morning a new chat forgets it and answers about the street face. Repair: persist session aliases into contextual memory keyed by user+site, or require GeoBIM seed selection before generation. Failure~M2: RAG returns an outdated maintenance PDF because the index was not edition-pinned. Repair: edition pins and corpus smoke tests~\cite{krutli2025fmrags,joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety}. Failure~M3: the model ``remembers'' a crack length from pretraining blog text. Repair: ban numeric defect claims unless an observation field supplies them~\cite{ji2023,zhao2026physical}. Failure~M4: twin memory has the correct Asset-ID but the orchestrator never fetched it, so the model improvises. Repair: treat missing passport fetch as infrastructure failure, not as a prompting problem~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024}. These four failures map one-to-one onto the four ladder rungs.

\textbf{How much foundation is enough before agents?}
Readers eager for agents should still finish the memory ladder. ReAct-style loops without retrieval memory re-enact parametric storytelling with extra steps~\cite{yao2023react,schick2023toolformer}. Toolformer-style tool use without twin memory can call the wrong host. Generative-agent memory streams without write gates can accumulate speculative notes as if they were facts~\cite{park2023generativeagents}. In inspection pedagogy, agents are week~7; memory and retrieval are weeks~1--6 in the roadmap above.

\textbf{Embedding spaces versus building spaces.}
Semantic embeddings cluster phrases like ``damp'', ``stain'', and ``seepage''. Building spaces cluster panels that share joints, storeys, and drainage paths. Both are useful; they are not substitutes. A hybrid retriever may use embeddings to rank symptom digests inside an already topology-constrained candidate set. It must not use embeddings to invent the candidate set for propagation questions~\cite{peng2024,jeon2025hybridrag,khattab2020}. Teaching this distinction early prevents later ``graph RAG'' cargo cults that are only document graphs.

\textbf{Instruction hierarchy for inspection systems.}
When system rules, retrieved manuals, twin facts, and user requests conflict, inspection systems need an explicit precedence: (1)~safety and write gates, (2)~twin facts and observation fields, (3)~pinned manual editions, (4)~user style preferences. A user saying ``just mark it repaired'' must not override gate~(1). A witty user tone must not override twin facts~(2). This hierarchy is an operational reading of alignment and instruction-following work~\cite{ouyang2022instructgpt,saka2024gptconstruction}; without it, the politest model still ships unsafe writes.

\textbf{Minimal evaluation while learning foundations.}
Even before full laboratory scoring, students can hand-check ten answers for: listed Asset-IDs $\subseteq$ retrieved set; hypotheses tagged; modality tags preserved; abstention when neighbours missing. These four checks already separate grounded homework from chat demos~\cite{ji2023,shuster2021,lewis2020}.



\textbf{Practice map of published LLM applications (specialise to inspection).}
A growing set of peer-published systems shows how LLMs are already used in AEC practice---each transferable to fa\c{c}ade O\&M only after Asset-ID and topology specialisation. BIM virtual assistants and NL-to-query stacks lower the barrier to model navigation~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,guo2025bimllmquery,liu2025bimcoder}. BuildingGPT-style and related building-data query pipelines combine chain-of-thought, RAG, and structured retrieval over semantic building stores~\cite{li2025c,li2025b}. Facility-management RAG retrieves editioned manuals and O\&M documents under corporate constraints~\cite{krutli2025fmrags}; structured FM experiments further show that schema-aware function calling or text-to-SQL improves execution validity when database schemas are exposed~\cite{buga2025llmfmsql}. Multi-source RAG for energy-transition guidance illustrates corpus pinning outside pure construction text~\cite{arslan2024ragenergy}.

Code and compliance practice is equally active. Prompt-based transformation of building-code information supports automated compliance checking workflows~\cite{yang2024promptcode}; LLM-plus-ontology pipelines check BIM models against regulatory texts~\cite{chen2024bimllmontology,iversen2026}; knowledge-graph fusion guides LLMs when checking construction schemes~\cite{li2024kgllmcheck}; comparative and platform studies situate NLP-driven compliance digitalisation in the wider ACC landscape~\cite{nuyts2024comparativeacc,kruiper2024platformnlp}. Safety-manual and hybrid defect-management RAG remain the closest published cousins to inspection dialogue~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,jeon2025hybridrag,joffe2025buildingcodes}.

On the agent and twin side, IFC tool agents, energy-analysis agent schemas, FM robot brokers, and construction multi-agent frameworks demonstrate tool versioning, shared memory, and human approval~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,zhang2025llmagentschema,luo2025llmdtrobot,rawat2025llmmas}. Graph-grounded multi-agent digital twins show that natural-language interaction can stay tied to structured twin graphs at building and city scales~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt}. Construction-oriented GPT reviews remain the governance caution~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction}. For UAV fa\c{c}ade inspection, the monograph reading is consistent: borrow retrieval discipline, schema obedience, and gated tools from these systems; replace their default retrieval units with asset passports and BIM neighbours; keep Chapter~6 detectors as the writers of modality-tagged observations.

\section{From Digital Twins to Language Reasoning}
\label{sec:ch8-dt-to-language}

Language reasoning over an inspection twin is not free-form storytelling. It is assembly of evidence that earlier chapters already created---the operational substrate for technology families T4--T8 in Section~\ref{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}. We keep three layers conceptually separate even when implementations differ. An \emph{observation record} is one defect sighting with sensor provenance (imagery, evidence, semantics, and host attachment---the teaching content often packaged as an integrated defect profile). An \emph{asset passport} aggregates observations and geometry or topology for a maintainable host (an integrated asset profile for retrieval). \emph{Query assembly} decides which passports and neighbours enter the context before generation; neighbour expansion along fa\c{c}ade adjacency supports vertical propagation reading. Perception writes observations; facility managers think in assets; language models must not invent either~\cite{pan2024b,zheng2023bimassistant}.

In UAV inspection practice, useful observation records store host Asset-ID, time, modality (RGB, infrared, and related channels), detector signature, pose or surface coordinates, and paths to imagery. Registration should attach detections to BIM hosts before any LLM is involved~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg}. Natural-language location phrases help models (``west elevation, third bay above the loading dock''), while metric coordinates remain for field navigation. Asset passports then add symptom summaries, geometry for severity normalisation, and neighbour links (above, below, lateral). Those links enable propagation-style reading---for example, mid-storey delamination considered with upstairs moisture---without confusing a document-similarity graph with a structural or BIM adjacency graph~\cite{peng2024}.

\textbf{Observation fields that language can trust.}
A minimal observation schema for language use includes: observation ID; host Asset-ID; campaign or capture time; modality tag; defect type and confidence from Chapter~6; optional severity features derived from geometry; image or crop URI; and a short human-readable location phrase. Optional fields include weather flags, inspector notes, and links to prior observations on the same host. What must not be left as free text alone is the host ID: if the only link from image to asset is a filename convention, retrieval will eventually invent neighbours.

\textbf{Asset passports as retrieval units.}
Enterprise RAG often chunks PDFs. Inspection questions are usually about maintainable hosts and their neighbourhoods. Passports therefore package, for each Asset-ID, a compact symptom digest, recent observation IDs, geometry summaries used for severity normalisation, and explicit neighbour lists. Severity normalisation matters because a 20\,cm crack on a small panel is not the same operational object as a 20\,cm crack on a large wall bay; language systems should inherit that normalisation from twin geometry rather than inventing it~\cite{zhao2026physical}. Neighbour lists should come from BIM or GeoBIM adjacency, not from embedding similarity among report sentences.

\textbf{Query assembly by intent.}
Query assembly is where inspection differs from generic enterprise RAG. A local lookup (``what defects were logged on asset~$A$ last month?'') needs the passport of~$A$ and little else. A propagation question needs neighbours along fa\c{c}ade topology, not merely the top-$k$ text chunks that happen to share vocabulary with the query. A floor- or elevation-level audit needs a controlled set of passports under a spatial filter from Chapter~7. Intent should therefore change \emph{which} twin records enter the context, not only the prompt wording~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024}. Editioned manuals and safety clauses may be retrieved in parallel when the question mixes site evidence with code language~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,krutli2025fmrags}.

One recurring implementation pattern---used in ongoing group experiments and therefore not cited here as a peer-reviewed source---organises observations into asset passports and expands neighbours along BIM adjacency for multi-hop diagnosis. Published siblings share the grounding intuition on other corpora: hybrid defect RAG~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag}, code and safety RAG~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety}, facility-document RAG~\cite{krutli2025fmrags}, and BIM assistants~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant}. The monograph lesson is the pattern, not a brand name: inspection questions are asset-centric, so retrieval units should be asset-centric. When the assembly cannot support a claim, the correct behaviour is abstention or escalation to a human inspector---not a confident paragraph.

Worked micro-example. Suppose the manager asks: ``Is water ingress on the west stack likely propagating from above?'' Assembly should load the target passport, upstairs and lateral neighbours, observation cards tagged RGB and infrared, and any editioned moisture-related maintenance note linked to those Asset-IDs. Generation may then hypothesise a path \emph{only} if neighbour observations exist; otherwise it must say that propagation cannot be supported from the twin. That discipline is language reasoning over a twin, not storytelling beside a twin.

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

Implementation teams often ask how large a passport should be. Too small, and propagation reading starves. Too large, and the context window fills with irrelevant neighbours from an entire elevation. A practical teaching heuristic is intent-conditioned expansion: local lookup uses zero hops; propagation uses one hop (or a small $k$) along BIM adjacency; elevation audits use spatial filters from Chapter~7 rather than unbounded hops~\cite{peng2024,zhang2025}. Manual clauses are retrieved only when the question mentions compliance, safety, or repair standards~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety}.

Indexing choices should follow the same heuristic. Vector indexes over passport digests support fuzzy symptom search (``hollow sounding panels with staining above''). Graph or relational indexes over Asset-IDs support exact neighbour expansion. Keyword indexes over observation IDs support audit lookups. Hybrid retrieval is attractive precisely because inspection questions mix fuzzy language with exact identifiers~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag,krutli2025fmrags}. What should not be hybridised away is the authority of topology: embedding similarity must not silently replace BIM adjacency when the question is about propagation.

Abstention deserves equal teaching time. Engineers already know how to say ``insufficient evidence'' on site; language systems must learn the same habit. Cite-or-abstain rules can be checked automatically: every Asset-ID in the answer string must appear in the retrieved set; every severity claim must point to an observation ID; every propagation path must be a sequence of neighbour links that were expanded. If checks fail, the system returns a structured refusal plus the missing evidence type (no upstairs neighbour, conflicting modalities, stale campaign, and so on). That behaviour is more valuable than a fluent paragraph that cannot be opened in the twin UI~\cite{ji2023,shuster2021}.

A second worked micro-example clarifies elevation audits. The manager asks: ``Summarise west-elevation issues above level three that need gondola access.'' Assembly should apply a Chapter~7 spatial filter, load the resulting passport set, keep modality tags, and rank by severity features inherited from geometry. It should not narrate the whole building, and it should not drop Asset-IDs in favour of storey prose alone. The output is a decision artefact for scheduling, not a travelogue.



\textbf{Seasonal and cross-campaign reading.}
Managers often ask whether a stain is ``new.'' Cross-campaign comparison is a first-class intent: it requires the same Asset-ID across time, comparable modality coverage, and explicit handling of missing prior campaigns. If the previous campaign lacks infrared, the system must not invent a thermal delta. If geometry was remeshed and Asset-IDs were remapped carelessly, language will compare the wrong hosts. Longitudinal discipline is therefore twin hygiene before it is model cleverness~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg,zhao2026physical}.

\textbf{Severity language without false precision.}
Perception may emit confidences; geometry may support length or area features; O\&M may need ordinal severity for scheduling. The language layer should not convert ordinal triage into false millimetre certainty. Prefer phrases tied to fields (``severity=medium from geometry-normalised crack length; confidence=0.71 from detector'') over cinematic adjectives. Physical quantification pipelines in earlier group work show why numbers need provenance~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}; the LLM's job is to carry that provenance into sentences, not to decorate it.



\textbf{Assembly pseudocode (teaching).}
\begin{verbatim}
function answer(q):
  intent <- classify(q)
  seeds  <- resolve_assets(q)          # else ABSTAIN:asset_unresolved
  packs  <- [passport(s) for s in seeds]
  if intent in {propagation}:
      packs <- packs + expand(seeds,k=1)
  if intent in {elevation_audit}:
      packs <- spatial_filter(packs,q)
  obs <- list_obs(packs)
  manuals <- retrieve_manuals(q) if needs_code(q) else []
  package <- {intent,packs,obs,manuals,edition_pin}
  draft <- generate(package)           # cite-or-abstain
  validate_ids(draft, package)         # else ABSTAIN:id_not_in_package
  return draft
\end{verbatim}
The pseudocode is deliberately dull. Every branch is a place students try to be clever---and where inspection systems usually break~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024,jeon2025hybridrag}.

\textbf{Resolve, then retrieve.}
Natural-language locations are ambiguous: ``third bay'' may mean different hosts after remeshing. Resolution against GeoBIM aliases must precede passport fetch. Fuzzy string match can propose candidates; it must not silently pick one when two candidates score closely. Disambiguation questions are successful system behaviour, not UX failure~\cite{zhang2025,han2025,guo2025bimllmquery}.


\section{Multi-Modal Fusion}
\label{sec:ch8-multimodal}

Fa\c{c}ade programmes are multimodal by construction: campaigns routinely produce RGB imagery, infrared channels, and textual metadata. In this chapter, \emph{multi-modal fusion} means disciplined assembly of those modality-tagged observation records---already written by Chapter~6 perception and attached by Chapters~4 and~7---inside grounded LLM retrieval and generation. Defect finding remains a Chapter~6 perception responsibility; language remains responsible for retrieval, explanation, abstention, and action proposals under citation rules.

\textbf{Fusing modalities without erasing conflict.}
Textbook query types for fa\c{c}ade O\&M include local lookup, asset-health summary, propagation or root-cause reading, and floor- or elevation-level audit. A minimal assembly is the passport of asset~$A$, its neighbours, and optional editioned manuals. Every Asset-ID or defect ID named in the answer must appear in that retrieved set; otherwise the system abstains or escalates. When RGB and infrared observations disagree, disclosure beats silent averaging: RGB-only evidence should not become a subsurface claim; infrared-only evidence should keep uncertainty flags. High-resolution fa\c{c}ade sensing and physical quantification work show why those flags matter: language that over-claims geometry inherits perception error~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}. A practical fusion rule for teaching is therefore record-level, not pixel-level: keep modality tags on observations; let the LLM narrate agreement or conflict; never collapse conflict into a single confident defect type without a human check flag.

\textbf{Agents with memory, specialised to inspection.}
Agents enter when inspection work requires procedures rather than paragraphs: open a ticket, propose a UAV revisit polygon for Chapters~2--3, request a robot photo, or stage a twin update after approval~\cite{yao2023react,schick2023toolformer,wang2024surveyagents,park2023generativeagents}. Memory is part of agency, not an afterthought. Short-term dialogue state tracks the current question; retrieval memory refreshes manuals and observation cards; twin memory supplies Asset-IDs and topology; procedural memory records which tools and approvals already ran~\cite{rawat2025llmmas,gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot}. Surveys of LLM agents and multi-agent systems emphasise the same ingredients---planning, memory, and tools---while warning that autonomy without constraints is unsafe~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent}. Published AEC agent systems---IFC tool agents, FM robot brokers, construction multi-agent coordinators, and schema-guided energy agents---illustrate the trajectory from answering to acting~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot,rawat2025llmmas,zhang2025llmagentschema}. Tool-augmented reasoning should therefore be taught as an expected ingredient of inspection language systems, not as an optional plugin~\cite{yao2023react,schick2023toolformer}.

For fa\c{c}ade inspection we recommend \emph{bounded agency}. Multi-step retrieval and tool calls are welcome. Final claims stay citation-locked to the retrieved evidence set. Dispatch and twin writes stay behind geofence and human gates. A small typed tool surface---get passport, expand neighbours, list observations, draft report, propose revisit---beats free-form SQL or unrestricted code execution. That stance specialises general agent literature to the inspection setting: the twin already encodes the world state that an agent must not rewrite casually; Chapters~2--3 already own flight planning; the language agent proposes, it does not commandeer the aircraft.

Worked micro-example. The manager asks for a revisit where infrared suggests moisture but RGB is inconclusive. A bounded agent may call \texttt{list\_observations}, notice the modality conflict, draft a report with \texttt{human\_check=true}, and emit a revisit polygon hint. It must not invent a flight trajectory in prose, and it must not write a new defect type into the twin without approval. That is multimodal fusion in the sense of this book: modality-aware records, grounded language, and tools that respect upstream chapter ownership.

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

Multi-modal fusion also interacts with campaign design. If infrared and RGB are captured on different days, time stamps must remain visible in observation cards; otherwise the LLM may treat them as simultaneous evidence. If one modality has systematically lower detector confidence, trust flags should inherit that asymmetry rather than average it away~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}. Fusion at language time is therefore inseparable from perception metadata quality. Garbage in still yields fluent garbage out---only now with citations that look official.

Agent design should stay boring on purpose. Spectacular autonomous stacks that rewrite BIM, dispatch aircraft, and email contractors in one loop are poor teaching targets for inspection safety. Bounded tools with explicit gates match how responsible O\&M already works: analysts propose; seniors approve; field teams execute~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,zhang2025llmagentschema}. Multi-agent role splits (retriever, topology expander, report drafter, action proposer) can be useful pedagogically, provided they share one twin memory and one approval policy~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,rawat2025llmmas}. Competing agents that invent different Asset-IDs for the same panel are not ``diversity''; they are a failure mode.

Procedural memory deserves a concrete inspection reading. When an agent has already expanded neighbours and listed observations, it should not re-expand endlessly in a loop. When a human rejected a twin write, that rejection should be remembered for the session so the agent does not resubmit the same mutation. When a revisit was proposed, the planning hint should carry the observation IDs that justified it, so Chapters~2--3 receive evidence rather than vibes~\cite{yao2023react,park2023generativeagents}. Memory, in this sense, is operational discipline.



\textbf{Tool traces as teaching objects.}
When demonstrating agents, show the tool trace beside the answer: which passports were fetched, which neighbours expanded, which manual edition pinned, which gate blocked a write. Traces make bounded agency inspectable. Surveys of LLM agents emphasise planning and tools; inspection teaching should emphasise the audit trail those tools leave~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,yao2023react,schick2023toolformer}. A beautiful answer without a trace is a demo; a plain answer with a trace is an O\&M artefact.

\textbf{Multi-agent roles without multi-truth.}
Splitting retriever, topology, drafter, and action roles can clarify software modules. It must not create competing Asset-ID realities. All roles read the same twin memory; only the gated writer may stage updates; only humans approve. Construction multi-agent frameworks that combine graph and vector memory are useful precedents for shared memory, not for unsupervised committee decisions about defects~\cite{rawat2025llmmas,gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot}.



\textbf{Agent trace walkthrough (propagation to ticket).}
Step~1: parse intent=propagation for question about W-3-17 moisture path. Step~2: \texttt{get\_passport(W-3-17)}. Step~3: \texttt{expand\_neighbours(W-3-17,k=1)}. Step~4: \texttt{list\_observations} on seed and neighbours; keep modality tags. Step~5: validate package non-empty; if upstairs missing, \texttt{ABSTAIN:no\_upstairs\_neighbour}. Step~6: draft hypothesis text under cite-or-abstain. Step~7: if manager requests action, \texttt{draft\_report} with \texttt{human\_check} when RGB/IR disagree. Step~8: \texttt{propose\_revisit} only for conflicted assets, binding obs IDs. Step~9: wait for approval events in procedural memory. Step~10: on approve, \texttt{stage\_twin\_write}; on reject, store rejection and stop. Logging every step is the product; hiding steps to look ``smart'' is the anti-pattern~\cite{yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents,luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent}.

\begin{verbatim}
TRACE example (illustrative)
intent: propagation
tools: get_passport -> expand_neighbours -> list_observations
package_assets: [W-3-17, W-4-17, W-3-16]
modality_conflict: W-3-17
gates: draft_report(human_check=true); propose_revisit(pending)
writes: none
\end{verbatim}

\textbf{Geofence and chapter ownership in tool design.}
\texttt{propose\_revisit} must emit planning hints, not executable UAV commands. Coordinates should be coarse polygons or asset sets that Chapters~2--3 refine under their own safety stack. Likewise, robot photo requests (when available in a facility) should be tickets, not direct motor commands from the LLM~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot}. Bounded agency is partly an organisational boundary between chapters of this book.

\textbf{State machines beat free chat for actions.}
Once the user asks for tickets or revisits, the orchestrator can enter an explicit state machine: DRAFT $\rightarrow$ REVIEW $\rightarrow$ APPROVED/REJECTED $\rightarrow$ SCHEDULED. The LLM fills fields inside a state; it does not choose to skip REVIEW because the user is in a hurry. Construction and FM agent papers that emphasise approval are aligned with this state-machine reading~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,zhang2025llmagentschema,rawat2025llmmas}.

\textbf{Testing agents without flying.}
Agent tests can be offline: stub tools return fixtures; assert that forbidden writes never occur; assert revisit hints always carry obs IDs; assert rejected drafts never reappear without new evidence. These tests are cheaper than flight campaigns and catch the worst failures early~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent}.

\textbf{When not to call an agent.}
If the user only asks what is logged on an asset, tools \texttt{get\_passport} and \texttt{list\_observations} suffice; no ticket machinery. Agents earn their complexity only for procedures. Premature agency increases gate surface area without increasing grounding~\cite{yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents}.


\section{Inspection Report Generation and Management}
\label{sec:ch8-reports}

Inspection answers become useful when they land in artefacts facility-management systems already understand. Free-form paragraphs are hard to audit. A practical report schema groups identity fields (asset and defect IDs), diagnosis, propagation notes, recommended actions, and trust flags. Identity fields must be subsets of the retrieved evidence set---the report-level analogue of constrained decoding used in BIM and code assistants~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes}. Diagnosis fields should point back to observation IDs whose images open in the twin UI. Action fields should be readable by schedulers without requiring another LLM to re-parse prose.

Language quality collapses when site tags and twin IDs diverge, so field identity hygiene belongs in this chapter, not only in IT operations~\cite{zhang2025}. Consider again the manager asking about water ingress on a vertical stack. The system returns neighbour passports, cites observation IDs whose images open in the twin UI, and either drafts a repair brief under the schema above or abstains if evidence is thin. If a revisit is needed, an action proposer emits a planning hint for Chapters~2--3 rather than inventing a flight path in prose.

\textbf{Report lifecycle.}
Report management means more than one-shot generation. Drafts should be versioned; human sign-off should be recorded; approved facts may be written back into twin memory---the same long-term store introduced in Section~\ref{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}. Rejected drafts should not silently pollute passports. Where organisations already use CMMS or BIM issue trackers, the schema above should map to those fields rather than inventing a parallel universe of prose PDFs. Natural-language query interfaces can help non-specialists ask questions, but the authoritative artefact remains the constrained report plus openable evidence.

Report schemas should be negotiated with facility stakeholders early. Fields that nobody will open in a CMMS will not be maintained. Fields that lawyers require for liability may need stricter cite-or-abstain checks than fields used for internal triage. A teaching compromise is a core schema (identity, diagnosis, evidence links, trust) plus optional organisation-specific extensions (cost codes, contractor IDs, access windows). The LLM may draft extensions only from retrieved context or human-provided forms; it must not invent cost codes~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes}.

Human-in-the-loop is not a confession of failure; it is the product. Sign-off records should store who approved what, which evidence IDs were visible at approval time, and whether modality conflict flags were overridden. Those records become part of twin provenance for the next campaign. Without them, next season's LLM will treat last season's speculative draft as fact. Identity hygiene figures in this section are therefore not IT folklore: they are the precondition for longitudinal language reasoning~\cite{zhang2025}.

A short anti-pattern catalogue helps teams review vendor demos. Anti-pattern~1: PDF-only RAG with no Asset-IDs. Anti-pattern~2: chat UI that cannot open the cited image. Anti-pattern~3: automatic twin writes from unapproved drafts. Anti-pattern~4: severity language without geometry normalisation. Anti-pattern~5: propagation stories built from embedding neighbours rather than BIM adjacency. Any demo that exhibits two or more should be treated as a prototype, not as an O\&M system.



\textbf{Mapping reports into CMMS fields.}
A schema-constrained draft is only half the delivery. The other half is a field map: \texttt{asset\_ids} to equipment codes, \texttt{priority} to work-order priority, \texttt{human\_check} to inspection hold flags, evidence URIs to attachment slots. If the map does not exist, fluent reports accumulate as PDFs that nobody schedules. Facility digital-twin discussions already stress process coupling; language systems inherit that requirement~\cite{chen2023facility,wu2022,krutli2025fmrags}. Teaching projects should submit both a sample JSON draft and a one-page CMMS map.

\textbf{Versioning, redaction, and handover.}
Campaigns change contractors. Handover packages should include: Asset-ID dictionary, observation schema, manual edition pins, approval logs, and a frozen twin snapshot used for language evaluation. Redact personal data in inspector notes before RAG indexing. Without handover artefacts, the next team will fine-tune on prose and lose the twin contract---returning to parametric storytelling~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,zhang2025}.



\textbf{Report narrative generated from schema (pattern).}
Bad pattern: write a paragraph, then scrape IDs with a regex. Good pattern: fill schema fields first; render a paragraph from fields; if rendering would require an ID not in the schema, stop. BIM and code assistants already lean on constrained structures; inspection reports should too~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes}. A one-line renderer template is enough for teaching: ``Assets \{ids\} show \{type\} (severity \{sev\}) based on observations \{obs\}. Propagation path \{path\}. Action \{action\}. Trust: \{trust\}.''

\textbf{Attachment discipline.}
Every evidence URI in a report must open to the correct crop or frame. Broken links are grounding failures even when IDs are correct. Nightly link checkers belong in O\&M pipelines alongside detector retraining~\cite{zhang2025,chen2023facility}.


\section{Decision Support and Predictive Maintenance}
\label{sec:ch8-dss}

Monograph readers need evaluation criteria that travel across implementations, not a bake-off among brand names. For inspection language systems, ask whether cited IDs appear in the evidence set~\cite{ji2023,shuster2021}; whether propagation questions improve when neighbours are included; whether code and manual RAG beats parametric recall on editioned corpora~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,joffe2025buildingcodes,krutli2025fmrags}; whether outputs can drive operational schedules; and whether agent tool traces respect approval and geofence constraints~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,rawat2025llmmas}. Published community evaluations on defect, safety, and facility corpora already show that structured retrieval helps. Ongoing group experiments on warehouse fa\c{c}ades may illustrate the same structural lesson, but unpublished numbers are not treated as bibliography claims in this chapter.

Open issues remain: topology completeness, Asset-ID hygiene, missing agent benchmarks for safe inspection tool traces, tighter coupling between detector confidence and language abstention thresholds, and governance questions for building LLMs~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction}. Those frontiers belong with Chapter~9 and with the planning chapters that already address multi-UAV operations.


Decision support should be scored at the level of actions, not only at the level of sentences. A grounded paragraph that never changes a maintenance schedule has limited O\&M value. Conversely, a terse ranked list of Asset-IDs with openable evidence may be enough to plan a gondola week. Metrics should therefore include operability: fraction of outputs that map to work-order fields; fraction of agent traces that respect gates; fraction of propagation answers that use true neighbours~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,luo2025llmdtrobot,rawat2025llmmas}. Exact-match bake-offs among commercial chat models are secondary.

Predictive maintenance claims need temporal humility. Priority scores from one campaign are triage, not prognosis. Prognosis requires repeated observations on the same Asset-IDs, comparable modalities, and models that live outside the LLM prompt---survival models, deterioration curves, or physics-informed predictors, depending on the defect family. The language system can present those model outputs and keep them cited; it should not improvise a forecast to satisfy a manager's wording. Transferability figures in this section mean reusable schemas and tools, not reusable prophecies~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,zhang2025llmagentschema}.

Looking toward Chapter~9, the open problems are mostly socio-technical. Topology will remain incomplete on older buildings. Asset-ID regimes will collide across contractors. Agent benchmarks for safe tool use in inspection barely exist. Governance questions---who is responsible when a grounded-looking answer is wrong?---will not be solved by larger models alone~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction}. The constructive response taught here is modest and actionable: make the twin decision-ready, retrieve over assets, fuse modalities without erasing conflict, bound the agent, and evaluate what facility teams can actually use.



\textbf{Campaign walkthrough (teaching narrative).}
Consider a two-day warehouse fa\c{c}ade campaign that Chapters~2--7 have already turned into a decision-ready twin. Day~1 yields RGB coverage of the west and south elevations; Day~2 adds infrared passes over suspected moisture zones. Perception writes modality-tagged observations; registration attaches them to BIM hosts; GeoBIM keeps city-scale Asset-IDs aligned with the facility register. On Day~3 a facility manager opens the language interface with four questions that mirror real O\&M meetings.

Question~1 is local: ``What is logged on asset W-3-17?'' Assembly loads only that passport and returns typed observations with openable image links. Question~2 is comparative: ``What changed on W-3-17 since the previous campaign?'' Assembly needs historical observation IDs on the same Asset-ID; if history is missing, the system abstains on change and asks for the prior campaign import rather than inventing a delta. Question~3 is propagative: ``Could staining above W-3-17 explain hollow readings on W-3-17?'' Assembly expands upstairs and lateral neighbours, keeps RGB/IR tags, and either drafts a path hypothesis with citations or refuses. Question~4 is operational: ``Which west-elevation assets above level three should we stage for gondola access next week?'' Assembly applies a spatial filter, ranks by geometry-normalised severity and access constraints, and emits a schema-constrained list that a CMMS can ingest.

The walkthrough continues into agency only when the manager asks for action. ``Open a ticket for the top three assets and propose a revisit where IR and RGB disagree.'' The bounded agent drafts tickets from the ranked list, attaches evidence IDs, sets \texttt{human\_check=true} on modality conflicts, and emits revisit hints for Chapters~2--3. It does not email contractors, does not rewrite BIM geometry, and does not clear the conflict flags. A senior inspector approves two tickets, rejects one for insufficient evidence, and the rejection is stored in procedural memory so the agent does not resubmit it in the same session. Approved facts are written back to twin memory; rejected drafts are not. That end-to-end story is the chapter's pedagogical spine: twin contract, memory ladder, asset-centric assembly, multimodal disclosure, bounded tools, report lifecycle, and operability metrics.

\textbf{Dialogue discipline.}
In teaching demos it is tempting to show long chat transcripts. Useful transcripts are short and checkable. Each assistant turn should (i)~restate the intent class, (ii)~list Asset-IDs entered into context, (iii)~separate observations from hypotheses, and (iv)~end with either citations or an abstention code. Turns that jump from a vague question to a repair cost estimate without evidence violate the decision-layer contract even if they sound helpful. Facility users can be trained to ask better questions, but the system must remain safe when they do not~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,ji2023}.

\textbf{Relation to earlier monograph chapters.}
Chapter~8 should be readable after Chapters~4--7 without requiring readers to re-learn detectors or planners. Conversely, authors of those chapters can treat this chapter as a consumer specification: if your reconstruction export lacks host IDs, language fails; if your detector omits modality tags, fusion fails; if your GIS identifiers drift from field tags, reports fail. The cleanest cross-chapter collaboration is a shared Asset-ID and observation schema, not a shared buzzword list.



\textbf{A concrete retrieval recipe for inspection teaching.}
The following recipe is intentionally boring; that is its virtue. (1)~Parse the user question into an intent class among local lookup, asset health, propagation, elevation audit, and code-plus-site. (2)~Resolve mentioned locations to Asset-IDs using GeoBIM aliases and field tags; if resolution fails, ask a disambiguation question rather than guessing. (3)~Load passports for the resolved seeds. (4)~Expand neighbours only if the intent is propagation or if the user explicitly asks about adjacency. (5)~Attach observation cards with modality tags and confidences. (6)~Optionally retrieve editioned manual clauses when compliance language appears. (7)~Build a context package whose every ID is typed. (8)~Generate under cite-or-abstain constraints. (9)~Validate the output string against the package. (10)~If validation fails, return a structured refusal naming the missing evidence. Steps~(2)--(6) are twin and corpus engineering; steps~(7)--(10) are language-system engineering. Confusing the two is how projects become endless prompt tuning~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024,jeon2025hybridrag,joffe2025buildingcodes}.

\textbf{Sample constrained report (illustrative).}
A minimal machine-readable draft might look as follows (field names are illustrative, not a standard):
\begin{verbatim}
{
  "asset_ids": ["W-3-17", "W-4-17"],
  "defect_ids": ["obs-2403-118", "obs-2403-204"],
  "diagnosis": {
    "type": "possible_moisture_path",
    "severity": "medium",
    "hypothesis": "upstairs staining with mid-storey hollow"
  },
  "propagation": ["W-4-17", "W-3-17"],
  "action": {"repair": "local_probe", "priority": 2},
  "trust": {"confidence": 0.62, "human_check": true,
            "notes": "RGB/IR disagreement on W-3-17"}
}
\end{verbatim}
Every identity in such a draft must be subset-checked against retrieval. The narrative paragraph shown to humans should be generated from this structure, not the other way around~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes}.

\textbf{Evaluation protocol one can run without a bake-off.}
A teaching laboratory can evaluate an inspection language system with a few dozen gold questions and a frozen twin snapshot. Score grounding by ID overlap; score topology by whether neighbour-required questions improve when expansion is enabled; score corpus fidelity by swapping manual editions on purpose and checking whether answers follow the active edition; score operability by asking facility staff whether outputs can become work orders without rewriting; score agent safety by attempting forbidden writes and verifying gates~\cite{ji2023,lee2024ragsafety,luo2025llmdtrobot,rawat2025llmmas}. Publish the protocol and the twin schema; do not publish unpublished site numbers as if they were general laws.

\textbf{Why fifty-page depth matters for a monograph chapter.}
Springer monograph readers are not conference-paper skimmers. They need enough worked structure to implement or to supervise implementers. Depth here means more interface contracts, memory rules, intent tables, refusal behaviours, and evaluation checklists---not denser equations for their own sake. The chapter therefore prefers repeated specialisation of the same spine (twin $\rightarrow$ memory $\rightarrow$ assembly $\rightarrow$ agents $\rightarrow$ reports $\rightarrow$ metrics) over a forest of weakly connected subsections.



\textbf{Extended worked questions (manager $\leftrightarrow$ system).}
The following dialogues are compressed teaching scripts. They are not logs from a deployed product; they show the behaviours readers should demand.

\textit{Q:} ``Is the west elevation safe?''\\
\textit{Intent:} too vague for a twin answer.\\
\textit{System:} ask for Asset-ID scope or storey band; refuse binary ``safe/unsafe'' without evidence package.\\
\textit{Why:} safety is a professional determination; the language layer can only summarise cited observations~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction}.

\textit{Q:} ``List hollow-sounding panels on the west elevation that also have IR anomalies within one storey above.''\\
\textit{Intent:} propagation-style filter.\\
\textit{System:} resolve west elevation hosts; load passports; keep observations with hollow and IR tags; expand one storey up; return Asset-IDs with both evidence types; abstain where only one modality exists.\\
\textit{Why:} this is multimodal fusion without collapsing conflict~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}.

\textit{Q:} ``Draft a repair brief for W-3-17 citing HKHA maintenance language if applicable.''\\
\textit{Intent:} code-plus-site.\\
\textit{System:} load passport(W-3-17); retrieve editioned manual clauses; draft schema fields; if manual edition is unset, stop and ask which edition is active.\\
\textit{Why:} corpus fidelity before prose~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,krutli2025fmrags}.

\textit{Q:} ``Update the twin: mark W-3-17 as repaired.''\\
\textit{Intent:} write.\\
\textit{System:} refuse direct write; stage a pending update; require human approval and evidence of repair inspection.\\
\textit{Why:} bounded agency~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent}.

\textit{Q:} ``Plan tomorrow's flight to re-check conflicting assets.''\\
\textit{Intent:} action toward Chapters~2--3.\\
\textit{System:} emit revisit polygons/hints tied to observation IDs; do not invent waypoints in chat.\\
\textit{Why:} chapter ownership~\cite{yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents}.

These scripts also show how memory layers interact. Contextual memory holds the disambiguation that ``west elevation'' means the warehouse west face in this session. Retrieval memory loads manuals. Twin memory supplies Asset-IDs. Procedural memory records that a write was staged and not yet approved. If any layer is missing, the dialogue collapses into either endless clarification or unsafe improvisation~\cite{park2023generativeagents,rawat2025llmmas}.

\textbf{Design notes for course projects.}
Student or internship projects can implement a thin vertical slice: ten assets, thirty observations, twenty questions, one manual edition, and a five-tool agent. Grading should privilege refusal quality and citation correctness over conversational charm. Extending to full elevations is then an engineering scaling problem, not a new scientific story. That is how monograph chapters should steer practice: by making the thin slice honest enough that scaling does not invent a different ethics.

\textbf{Common failure post-mortems.}
Failure~A: the system cites an Asset-ID that never entered retrieval because a prompt example mentioned it---fix by validating IDs. Failure~B: neighbour expansion used embedding similarity and linked two panels that share the word ``stain'' but sit on opposite elevations---fix by authority of BIM adjacency. Failure~C: infrared-only evidence was verbalised as confirmed seepage---fix by modality tags and human\_check. Failure~D: an agent cleared a ticket after drafting a report without approval---fix by separating draft\_report from stage\_twin\_write. Failure~E: two manual editions disagreed and the model averaged them---fix by edition pins~\cite{ji2023,peng2024,lee2024ragsafety}.



\textbf{Illustrative priority scoring (not a field law).}
Suppose a scheduler scores assets by $s = 0.4\,\tilde{\ell} + 0.3\,c + 0.2\,a + 0.1\,m$, where $\tilde{\ell}$ is geometry-normalised defect size in $[0,1]$, $c$ is detector confidence, $a$ is access urgency (gondola day soon), and $m$ is a modality-conflict penalty (lower when RGB/IR disagree). The formula is a teaching toy: coefficients belong to the facility, not to the LLM. The language system should expose the terms and citations behind each score, then let humans reorder. Predictive maintenance begins only when repeated campaigns feed a temporal model; until then, $s$ is triage~\cite{zhao2026physical,gao2026ifcagent}.

\textbf{Governance checklist for building owners.}
Before production, owners should answer: Who owns Asset-IDs? Which manual editions are pinned? Who may approve twin writes? What is logged when the system abstains? How are contractor collisions surfaced? What is the retention policy for dialogue logs? Construction-oriented LLM reviews make clear that governance is part of technical success~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction}. Chapter~8's technical rules---cite-or-abstain, bounded tools, edition pins---are incomplete without those organisational answers.

\textbf{Reading guide for the rest of the book.}
Readers heading to Chapter~9 should carry three questions forward: which twin commitments are still missing at city scale; which agent gates must harden for multi-UAV operations; and which evaluation protocols can be shared across chapters without turning into brand bake-offs. Readers returning to Chapters~2--3 with revisit hints should carry observation IDs, not vibes. Readers returning to Chapters~5--6 should carry modality-tag and confidence requirements as consumer specs. That bidirectional reading is how a monograph stays one pipeline.



\textbf{Defect-family reading notes for language systems.}
Different fa\c{c}ade symptoms stress the decision layer differently. \emph{Surface cracking} is often RGB-rich; language should keep crack-length features tied to geometry normalisation and avoid structural-cause stories without neighbour or manual support. \emph{Spalling or delamination} may be visible in RGB yet linked to moisture hypotheses that need infrared or upstairs evidence; disclosure of missing modalities is mandatory. \emph{Staining and moisture cues} are multimodal by nature; infrared-only claims must stay uncertain until a human or a second modality confirms. \emph{Sealant and joint failures} are local but operationally urgent for water paths; elevation audits should not bury them under wall-bay averages. \emph{Previous repair patches} create temporal traps: a ``new'' stain beside an old patch is not automatically progression without cross-campaign IDs. These notes do not replace Chapter~6 taxonomies; they tell the language layer how to talk once taxonomy labels exist~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2025icra,zhao2026physical,jeon2025hybridrag}.

\textbf{Laboratory protocol (thin vertical slice).}
A course-scale laboratory can freeze a toy twin with 12 Asset-IDs on one elevation, 40 observations (mixed RGB/IR), a 1-hop neighbour graph, and one pinned manual edition of five clauses. Prepare 25 gold questions: 8 local, 5 health, 5 propagation, 4 audit, 3 code-plus-site. For each question store the gold Asset-ID set and whether abstention is required. Students implement the ten-step recipe, then score: exact ID precision/recall; abstention accuracy; neighbour necessity (propagation questions with expansion off versus on); edition fidelity (swap the manual pin and expect answer change); forbidden-write tests (agent must not call \texttt{stage\_twin\_write} without approval). Publish student scores as protocol compliance, not as claims about commercial models~\cite{ji2023,lee2024ragsafety,peng2024}.

\textbf{Operator playbook (one page in prose).}
Before a live query session, operators pin the campaign snapshot and manual edition; verify Asset-ID collision reports are empty; confirm image URIs open. During the session, they reject any answer that names an ID absent from the side panel evidence list; they treat \texttt{human\_check=true} as a hold, not as decoration. After the session, they export approved schema drafts to CMMS, store abstention logs, and open revisit hints for planning only after senior sign-off. This playbook is deliberately non-heroic. Inspection language systems fail when operators are asked to be heroes and succeed when operators enforce boring gates~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,luo2025llmdtrobot}.

\textbf{Data-contract excerpt (teaching).}
An observation record accepted by the language layer should validate against a JSON Schema conceptually equivalent to: required \texttt{obs\_id}, \texttt{asset\_id}, \texttt{time}, \texttt{modality}, \texttt{defect\_type}, \texttt{confidence}, \texttt{image\_uri}; optional \texttt{geometry\_features}, \texttt{location\_phrase}, \texttt{detector\_id}. Passports require \texttt{asset\_id}, \texttt{neighbours[]}, \texttt{obs\_ids[]}, and a digest string. Retrieval packages require \texttt{intent}, \texttt{seed\_assets[]}, \texttt{passports[]}, \texttt{manual\_hits[]}, \texttt{edition\_pin}. Generation may not invent keys outside the package. Such contracts sound software-heavy; without them, monograph advice collapses into prompt folklore~\cite{pan2024b,zheng2023bimassistant,gao2026ifcagent}.

\textbf{Comparing retrieval units on the same question.}
Take ``Is moisture likely propagating to W-3-17?'' PDF-chunk RAG may retrieve generic moisture paragraphs and invent a path. Passport-only RAG without neighbours may correctly list local stains yet miss upstairs evidence. Topology-aware passport RAG loads W-3-17 plus upstairs hosts and can either support or abstain honestly. Manual-only RAG may cite a clause without site evidence. The teaching comparison is qualitative: students should see all four behaviours on one frozen twin. Published hybrid and code RAG studies motivate pieces of this comparison; the topology axis is the inspection-specific addition~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag,joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,peng2024,krutli2025fmrags}.

\textbf{Human factors: trust calibration.}
If the interface always answers fluently, inspectors stop opening evidence. If it abstains too often without naming missing evidence types, inspectors abandon it. Calibrated systems abstain with reasons (``no IR on upstairs neighbour'', ``Asset-ID collision'', ``manual edition unset'') and succeed with openable citations. Trust literature in hallucinating systems warns that fluency is a trap~\cite{ji2023}; facility settings amplify the trap because work orders move money and scaffolding. Design the UI to show evidence first and prose second.

\textbf{Closing bridge toward metrics.}
Once contracts, symptom notes, laboratory scores, and operator playbooks exist, decision-support metrics stop being abstract. Grounding scores map to ID checks; topology scores map to expansion ablation in the lab; corpus scores map to edition swaps; operability scores map to CMMS field maps; safety scores map to forbidden-write tests. Section~\ref{sec:ch8-dss} then reads as the grading rubric for the same spine, not as a separate competition track.



\textbf{Week-by-week implementation roadmap (teaching).}
Week~1 freezes Asset-IDs and neighbour edges for one elevation; no LLM yet. Week~2 lands observation ingest with modality tags and image URI checks. Week~3 builds passport digests and a read-only retrieval API. Week~4 adds intent parsing and cite-or-abstain generation on local/health questions only. Week~5 enables one-hop expansion for propagation questions and records topology ablation scores. Week~6 pins a manual edition and adds code-plus-site questions. Week~7 introduces the five-tool agent with all writes gated. Week~8 connects schema drafts to a stub CMMS map and runs the operator playbook on a live dry-run with three facility users. Teams that skip Week~1 almost always spend Week~8 debugging invented panels. The roadmap is intentionally retrieval-first; model swaps are allowed only after ID metrics plateau~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024,zhang2025,saka2024gptconstruction}.

Manager: ``Give me next week's gondola list for the west elevation.''\\
System: Intent=elevation audit. Seeds=west elevation filter (Ch.~7). Loaded 14 passports. Ranking by geometry-normalised severity and access flags. Returning 6 Asset-IDs with evidence URIs. Two assets flagged \texttt{human\_check} due to RGB/IR conflict.\\
Manager: ``Open tickets for the four without conflict; propose revisit for the two with conflict.''\\
System: Drafting four \texttt{draft\_report} objects; staging no twin writes. Emitting two revisit hints bound to obs IDs. Awaiting approval.\\
Senior: Approves three tickets; rejects one (duplicate of last month); approves both revisit hints.\\

This transcript is short on purpose. Every turn names intent, IDs, and gates. Longer chat that buries those elements is worse teaching material even if it feels natural~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,luo2025llmdtrobot,ji2023}.

\textbf{On bilingual operations.}
Many fa\c{c}ade programmes mix English manuals, local administrative Chinese, and site slang. The twin IDs must stay language-agnostic. Location phrases may be bilingual, but Asset-IDs should not. RAG indexes may be language-specific per corpus, yet cite-or-abstain still keys on IDs. When translating drafts for stakeholders, translate prose after schema validation, never before. Otherwise translation can smuggle in uncited entities. Construction LLM reviews that discuss workplace language change are relevant here as governance context, not as a translation API endorsement~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,krutli2025fmrags,joffe2025buildingcodes}.

\textbf{Security and misuse briefly.}
A language interface that can draft work orders is a process surface, not a toy chatbot. Access control should follow facility roles; tool gates should be server-side, not prompt-only; audit logs should be tamper-evident enough for internal review. Prompt injection via malicious PDF manuals is a real RAG risk: pin editions and prefer structured clauses. These points stay brief because the chapter's centre is grounding for inspection, but ignoring them turns a grounded demo into an operational hazard~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,rawat2025llmmas,saka2024gptconstruction}.

\textbf{What success looks like after one quarter.}
Success is not ``the model sounds like an engineer.'' Success is measurable: fewer invented IDs in drafts; higher fraction of answers with openable evidence; fewer silent modality collapses; revisit hints that planning can consume; CMMS tickets that do not need full rewrite; abstentions that name missing evidence. If those curves are flat, change retrieval and contracts before changing base models. That is the monograph's recurring specialisation of general LLM advice to building inspection~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024,zhao2026physical,gao2026ifcagent}.



\textbf{Deepening case: moisture-path assembly.}
Let upstairs asset W-4-17 carry RGB stain obs-A (confidence 0.78) and IR anomaly obs-B (0.66); mid-storey W-3-17 carry hollow obs-C (RGB, 0.81) with no IR. The manager asks about propagation. The orchestrator marks intent=propagation, seeds W-3-17, expands one hop to W-4-17 and lateral hosts, and builds a package with three passports and three observations (no manual). Generation may hypothesise coexistence of upstairs moisture cues and mid-storey hollow, but must cite the IDs, declare missing IR on W-3-17, set \texttt{human\_check=true}, and refuse prices or certain seepage paths. Student systems that emit costs or hide missing IR fail the lab. The case compresses Sections~\ref{sec:ch8-dt-to-language}--\ref{sec:ch8-reports} into one gradable trace~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical,peng2024,ji2023}.

\textbf{Deepening case: manual edition pin.}
The same asset-health question under a 2018 pin versus a 2024 pin should yield distinguishable citation sets. Soft-averaging two editions is a corpus-fidelity failure. Code-RAG papers motivate editioned corpora; inspection classes must implement the pin as a switch, not a footnote~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,krutli2025fmrags}.

\textbf{Deepening case: identifier collision.}
Field tag ``West-3-17'' may alias to Asset-ID ``W-3-17''; contractor export ``Panel-West-317'' may not. Resolution must return unresolved/collision, never ``nearest embedding ID.'' Wrong hosts are more expensive than abstention~\cite{zhang2025,han2025}.

\textbf{Turning the evaluation table into a quarterly board.}
Track monthly: invented-ID rate; openable-evidence rate; modality-conflict disclosure rate; revisit-hint adoption rate; CMMS no-rewrite rate. If invented IDs fall but openable evidence does not rise, the system learned silence without retrieval. If evidence rises but CMMS no-rewrite does not, the schema map is broken. Table~\ref{tab:ch8-eval} thereby becomes management language~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,luo2025llmdtrobot,rawat2025llmmas}.

\textbf{Pushing back on ``just add another agent.''}
Failure often triggers role inflation. Without shared memory and gates, more roles invent more Asset-ID stories. Stabilise a five-tool single agent before splitting personalities. Multi-agent surveys and AEC agent papers should be read as arguments for shared memory and tool contracts, not for more personas~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,rawat2025llmmas,gao2026ifcagent}.

\textbf{Re-stating the predictive-maintenance boundary.}
This chapter may explain triage terms and may cite external temporal-model outputs; it must not invent ``73\% spalling probability in eighteen months'' from a single campaign context. Product claims that equate Chapter~8 with full predictive maintenance over-promise; the monograph stays modest so Chapter~9 and real temporal methods still have work to do~\cite{zhao2026physical,saka2024gptconstruction,gao2026ifcagent}.

\textbf{Executable rules for RGB/IR conflict.}
R1: RGB-only defect observations must not become ``confirmed internal seepage'' sentences. R2: IR-only anomalies require uncertainty markers and \texttt{human\_check=true}. R3: if modality timestamps differ beyond a facility threshold (e.g., 48\,h), disclose non-simultaneity in trust fields. R4: conflicted assets default below same-severity consistent-modality assets on gondola lists unless overridden. R5: overrides must enter sign-off logs. These rules are executable multimodal fusion for this chapter, not a claim to replace pixel-level fusion networks~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical,ji2023}.

\textbf{Passport digest template (teaching).}
\texttt{[Asset-ID] | storey/bay | neighbours: above/below/lateral | symptoms: ... | obs: id@modality@conf@time | flags: ...}. Digests must be short enough to batch into context and long enough to support cite-or-abstain. Free sentences without host attachment are forbidden inside digests. Once stable, embed digests for ranking; expand topology via adjacency tables~\cite{peng2024,jeon2025hybridrag,zhang2025}.

\textbf{Ten files from demo to handover.}
(1) Asset-ID dictionary; (2) neighbour edge table; (3) observation schema; (4) passport build notes; (5) manual edition pins; (6) intent--assembly policy table; (7) tool surface and gates; (8) CMMS field map; (9) gold questions and abstention set; (10) one success TRACE and one abstention TRACE. Missing any item, demos can still dazzle and handovers still fail. The ten files compress Sections~\ref{sec:ch8-dt}--\ref{sec:ch8-dss} into an engineering checklist~\cite{zhang2025,krutli2025fmrags,saka2024gptconstruction}.



\textbf{Forty-five-minute memory-ladder mini-lab.}
Prepare four cards: parametric, contextual, RAG, twin. Pose ``Is W-3-17 worse than last month?'' With only the parametric card, students must abstain. Adding contextual aliases is still insufficient for cross-campaign comparison. Adding RAG manuals still lacks site history. Only the twin card with prior observation IDs permits comparison. Then hand a wrong twin card (ID collision) and require abstention again. No laptop required; Section~\ref{sec:ch8-llm-foundations} becomes muscle memory~\cite{lewis2020,ji2023,zhang2025}.

\textbf{Reading civil papers as transferable sentences.}
From BIM assistants: ``They solved object navigation; we still need topology-aware defect diagnosis.'' From code RAG: ``They pin clause editions; we must pin clauses and Asset-IDs.'' From defect dialogue RAG: ``They hybridise noisy text; we must change the unit to passports.'' From FM RAG: ``They retrieve document stores; we must link documents back to hosts.'' From IFC/FM agents: ``They have tools and approvals; we must add geofence and chapter ownership.'' One transferable sentence per paper; no brand worship~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes,jeon2025hybridrag,krutli2025fmrags,gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot}.

\textbf{TRACE grading rubric (instructor).}
Five items, 0--2 each: correct intent; sensible tool order; answer IDs covered by package; gates before writes; rejections enter procedural memory. Score $\ge 8$ to pass a demo. No style points---ornate prose must not hide missing gates. The rubric makes Section~\ref{sec:ch8-multimodal} gradable~\cite{yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents,rawat2025llmmas}.

\textbf{Modality-conflict notice template.}
``Asset \{Asset-ID\}: RGB observation \{obs\_rgb\} disagrees with IR observation \{obs\_ir\} (time gap \{delta\}). Diagnosis remains uncertain; \texttt{human\_check} set. Recommend human review or revisit. Do not write a repaired state to the twin before review.'' Templates beat improvised disclaimers~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical,ji2023}.

\textbf{Ten-minute script with schedulers.}
After a six-asset ranking, schedulers ask only: Can evidence open? Are conflicts flagged? Do revisit hints carry obs IDs? All yes $\rightarrow$ gondola week planning; any no $\rightarrow$ back to the decision layer. Short on purpose~\cite{chen2023facility,luo2025llmdtrobot}.

\textbf{Standard abstention codes (excerpt).}
\texttt{ABSTAIN:asset\_unresolved}; \texttt{ABSTAIN:no\_history}; \texttt{ABSTAIN:no\_neighbour}; \texttt{ABSTAIN:edition\_unset}; \texttt{ABSTAIN:empty\_package}; \texttt{ABSTAIN:id\_not\_in\_package}. Codes belong in logs and monthly boards, not only in apologetic chat~\cite{ji2023,peng2024,lee2024ragsafety}.



\textbf{Metric definitions students can implement.}
Invented-ID rate $=$ count of answer IDs absent from package $/$ count of answer IDs. Openable-evidence rate $=$ fraction of cited obs URIs that HTTP-head successfully. Conflict-disclosure rate $=$ fraction of RGB/IR conflict packages whose answers mention conflict or set human\_check. Revisit adoption rate $=$ fraction of approved revisit hints that appear in next planning batch. CMMS no-rewrite rate $=$ fraction of tickets accepted without field edits. These five need no commercial leaderboard~\cite{ji2023,lee2024ragsafety,luo2025llmdtrobot}.


In summary, Chapter~8 is the book's decision layer. It consumes Chapters~4--7, teaches LLM foundations with an inspection-centred memory ladder, grounds answers through asset-centric retrieval and bounded agents that fuse modality-tagged evidence, and delivers reports that facility teams can audit. The measure of success is not rhetorical fluency, but named assets, cited evidence, and actions that remain under professional control.
