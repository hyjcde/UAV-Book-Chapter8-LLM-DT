\section{Digital Twins for Building Inspection}
\label{sec:ch8-dt}

Cities maintain large stocks of ageing buildings under rising safety and cost pressure. Manual fa\c{c}ade inspection remains labour-intensive and hazardous, so unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs), sensing, and artificial intelligence have entered structural health monitoring (SHM) and building operations and maintenance (O\&M). Chapters~2--7 of this monograph already follow that pipeline in sequence: task and motion planning, three-dimensional reconstruction, datasets and defect models, and geographic information that hosts typed findings on maintainable assets. What those chapters deliver is a surveyed, registered, and spatially organised building. What they do not yet finish is ordinary-language decision support. Day-to-day O\&M is rarely complete when a detection box appears on a mesh. Managers still ask which panels share a moisture path, what changed since the last campaign, which assets should enter next week's gondola schedule, and how to draft a repair brief that an engineer can audit. This chapter is therefore continuous with the upstream stack, yet distinct in job: it consumes those products and develops the language and decision layer above them.

Digital twins are a useful name for the information systems that try to answer such questions, but the term is broader than a photorealistic viewer. In construction and civil infrastructure, twin research emphasises lifecycle information flows, stakeholder coordination, and the gap between visualisation and actionable semantics~\cite{boje2020,opoku2021,lu2020}. Heritage and building documentation likewise stress diagnosis-linked semantics and monitoring provenance beyond geometry alone~\cite{bruno2018,tsilimantou2020}. Facility-oriented discussions add a further point: O\&M value appears when twins connect to work processes, not when they only decorate a meeting slide~\cite{chen2023facility,lu2020}. A geometric model that cannot say which maintainable host owns a detection, when the image was captured, or which neighbours sit beside that host, cannot support language-level O\&M.

Large language models (LLMs) enter this picture as language interfaces, not as replacements for detectors or as site databases. Ready models can parse mixed spatial-symptom questions, draft stakeholder briefs, and follow answer templates. Alone, they remain unreliable for named assets and editioned manuals: fluent text can invent panel codes, average away RGB/infrared disagreement, or recall the wrong handbook edition~\cite{ji2023,lewis2020}. Retrieval-augmented generation, structured outputs, and tool calling are therefore general engineering responses to that failure mode: keep living facts outside the weights, fetch them at query time, and validate claims against retrieved evidence~\cite{lewis2020,shuster2021,yao2023react}. Vision--language models (VLMs), when used, play a similarly bounded role: they may help narrate an already attached image crop; they should not invent host codes or rewrite detector labels.

A feasible approach for inspection decision support follows from those general ingredients. Keep campaign facts in an editioned twin store. Let the language system retrieve host-centred records, cite openable evidence, abstain when the package is thin, and propose actions only behind human or geofence gates. In that arrangement the twin is the long-term memory; the LLM is a reader and drafter under rules. Perception remains responsible for typed defect findings; language remains responsible for grounded explanation and schedulable drafts. Historic inspection craft already points the same way: paper checklists force named locations, photo logs force provenance, and repair tickets force action fields. Digital twins and LLMs add scale and queryability; they do not licence teams to abandon those disciplines~\cite{zhang2022,chen2023facility,ji2023}.

Readers will meet several twin maturity levels in practice. A \emph{geometric twin} stores meshes, point clouds, or BIM shells. A \emph{semantic twin} attaches typed entities and stable host codes. A \emph{decision-ready twin} additionally exposes observation cards, topology for propagation, and interfaces that retrieval and tools can call. Bolting a chat model onto the first level usually produces fluent inventions. The workable target for this chapter is the third level, with the second as a minimum premise. Organisational reality reinforces that target: UAV contractors, consultants, facility teams, and asset managers rarely share one owner, so identifiers, provenance, and neighbour relations must survive file hand-offs without calling the person who built the mesh~\cite{boje2020,opoku2021,chen2023facility}.

Consider a concrete scene once those ideas are in place. After a warehouse fa\c{c}ade campaign, a manager asks whether mid-storey delamination on the west elevation is linked to moisture staining above. A mesh viewer can show both regions; a detector can label both patches. Only a language-ready twin can assemble neighbouring host records, cite observation IDs whose images open, and either draft a repair brief or abstain when evidence is thin. That scene is the running example for later sections: the twin stores facts; the LLM proposes language under citation rules, not a second source of geometry. Later worked examples use short placeholder host codes such as \texttt{W-3-17} and \texttt{W-4-17} only to keep traces readable; live programmes should substitute their own facility register codes.

Only after that conceptual path is clear does this book need a concrete picture of how a digital twin and a large language model sit inside one inspection process. Chapters~2--7 already turn flights into geometry, typed defects, and spatially hosted assets. Figure~\ref{fig:ch8-framework} shows how those products become language-usable twin memory, how an LLM reads that memory under retrieval and citation rules, and how facility teams receive auditable answers, reports, and gated maintenance actions. It is a process framework for inspection decision support, not a catalogue of software modules from a single system paper. Figure~\ref{fig:ch8-dt-pipeline} then shows the modelling path that language retrieval should treat as an interface contract: once defects are localised on BIM hosts with stable identifiers, the language system may retrieve them; if registration stops at meshes or unlinked detector boxes, later retrieval can only invent structure~\cite{zhang2022,zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg}. Table~\ref{tab:ch8-twin-contract} is not an IT wishlist; it is the digital continuation of field craft. Seasoned inspectors photograph before writing, name bays before diagnosing, and refuse to sign reports that cannot be walked on site. A decision-ready twin makes those habits machine-checkable across campaigns~\cite{zhang2022,chen2023facility}. Geographic scale sharpens the same point: a campus or housing estate multiplies identifier collisions and manual-edition conflicts, so city-scale identity must stay coherent in the GIS layer rather than being ``fixed'' later by clever prose~\cite{zhang2025,han2025}. Neighbouring language systems already show useful fragments and clear limits. BIM assistants improve navigation; document RAG improves clause lookup; multi-agent demos can look autonomous. Navigation is not topology-aware defect diagnosis, embedding neighbourhood is not BIM adjacency for moisture-path questions, and fluent tickets without citation checks or write gates are not auditable O\&M artefacts~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,lee2024ragsafety,peng2024,luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent}. The rest of the chapter therefore develops one workable specialisation: ready-to-use LLM layers plus a model harness that keeps Asset-ID contracts, edition pins, and approval gates outside replaceable model weights. Site-specific fine-tuning is outside the agenda; campaign facts belong in editioned twin memory and retrieval indexes~\cite{lewis2020,krutli2025fmrags,ma2026tenquestions}.

After reading this chapter, a student or implementer should be able to list the minimum twin commitments in Table~\ref{tab:ch8-twin-contract} and explain one failure mode for each missing commitment; map a fa\c{c}ade question to Layers~L1--L3 and to one harness part in Table~\ref{tab:ch8-harness}; assemble a cite-or-abstain package for a propagation or historical-record question using passports and BIM neighbours; state the VLM-versus-detector split in Section~\ref{sec:ch8-multimodal}; and show a tool TRACE in which a write or revisit request stops at a human gate~\cite{peng2024,yao2023react,liang2023helm,hellin2025bimmas}. Those outcomes define a decision-layer contract that earlier chapters can satisfy and later chapters can harden. They do not introduce a new detector.

\section{Foundations of LLMs for Civil Engineering Applications}
\label{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}

Contemporary LLMs rest on the Transformer architecture~\cite{vaswani2017attention}. Self-attention models long-range dependencies in text, which matters when an inspection question mixes symptoms, locations, and code clauses in one utterance. Ready-to-use models already follow natural-language instructions and can draft fluent building prose~\cite{brown2020,ouyang2022instructgpt}. Chain-of-thought prompting can elicit intermediate steps that help multi-hop questions~\cite{wei2022cot}, but it does not guarantee that a named fa\c{c}ade panel actually exists in the twin. Reviews of GPT-style tools in construction make the same caution for AEC practice: capability is real, governance and factual grounding are not automatic~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction}.

Beyond architecture and prompting, civil deployments should also separate three LLM behaviours that product descriptions often conflate. Instruction-following models can answer under templates supplied by an orchestrator, so short replies can separate observation from hypothesis~\cite{ouyang2022instructgpt,brown2020}. Tool-calling models can request typed functions without writing world state into weights, which keeps passport reads and neighbour expansion outside the model~\cite{schick2023toolformer,yao2023react}. Long-context models can hold packed evidence digests, but they still need validators: a longer window does not invent Asset-IDs that were never retrieved~\cite{lewis2020,ji2023,liang2023helm}. Construction GPT reviews and building-application agendas therefore treat governance, data readiness, and human oversight as design variables alongside model choice~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,ma2026tenquestions}. For fa\c{c}ade inspection those variables become edition pins, modality tags, cite-or-abstain checks, and write gates. Contemporary foundation-model interfaces also differ in shape: chat-tuned APIs emphasise instruction following; tool-calling APIs expose typed function schemas; retrieval-augmented deployments keep site facts outside the weights. Inspection systems should choose and compose those interfaces by contract~\cite{ouyang2022instructgpt,schick2023toolformer,lewis2020,saka2024gptconstruction}.

This chapter therefore takes a deliberate stance. We treat LLMs as \emph{deployable language components}: systems that are prompted, constrained, and wired to twin stores and tools. We do not develop a site-specific training or fine-tuning agenda. Campaign facts change every flight; the honest path for living buildings is to keep those facts outside the model weights, in editioned manuals and Asset-ID twin memory, and to let the model read them at query time~\cite{lewis2020,krutli2025fmrags}. It also helps to separate three behaviours that are often conflated. \emph{Linguistic competence} is the ability to parse and produce fluent text about buildings. \emph{Parametric recall} is whatever generic knowledge the weights already contain, useful for vocabulary, unreliable for site-specific Asset-IDs. \emph{Grounded reasoning} is the ability to answer using evidence that the system retrieved or was given in the current session. Fa\c{c}ade O\&M lives or dies on the third behaviour. An LLM that can write elegant moisture narratives without citing twin observations is not an inspection assistant; it is a drafting toy.

This section introduces generative language technology for civil engineering and attaches it to inspection analysis. It organises ready-to-use techniques into three layers that compose into one decision stack; see Table~\ref{tab:ch8-tech-families}. Perception remains Chapter~6's job; here language technologies consume typed, modality-tagged observations. Layer~L1 is the \emph{language interface}: how an off-the-shelf model is steered with prompts and answer templates. Layer~L2 is \emph{evidence grounding}: how retrieval, structured outputs, and tools keep answers attached to twin records. Layer~L3 is \emph{memory and bounded procedures}: how long-term twin memory and gated agents support multi-step O\&M work without unsupervised action. The rest of this section develops the three layers as continuous narrative and then shows how published AEC systems specialise, or fail to specialise, to fa\c{c}ade inspection.

Layer~L1 begins with what a ready model can already do when an orchestrator supplies a careful prompt. The model can parse mixed spatial-symptom questions such as ``mid-storey hollow with upstairs staining,'' draft readable briefs for different stakeholders, and follow a short answer template that forces named Asset-IDs, separates observations from hypotheses, and emits an abstention code when checks fail~\cite{ouyang2022instructgpt,ji2023}. Stepwise prompting can then verbalise a propagation hypothesis, but only after neighbours have already entered the evidence package; otherwise the model may invent a structural story that merely sounds plausible~\cite{wei2022cot,ji2023}. A practical system prompt for fa\c{c}ade O\&M can therefore be short: classify intent; speak only about Asset-IDs present in the retrieved package; preserve modality tags from Chapter~6; and abstain with an explicit reason when evidence is thin. Few-shot examples in the prompt should demonstrate abstention, not only success. What L1 must \emph{not} be asked to do is remember last campaign's Asset-IDs from parametric recall, invent crack lengths, or override twin facts when a hurried manager says ``just mark it repaired.'' Those duties belong to grounding and gates, not to fluent wording.

Layer~L2 is where inspection systems leave chat demos. Retrieval-augmented generation loads editioned manuals or observation cards at query time~\cite{lewis2020,shuster2021}; civil deployments already cover codes, safety manuals, facility-management documents, and defect dialogue~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,krutli2025fmrags,jeon2025hybridrag,he2025regulationhybrid}. Adaptive and self-reflective variants retry when retrieval looks weak~\cite{gao2023hyde,asai2024selfrag,jeong2024adaptive}, but the inspection design choice is sharper than ``add RAG'': for asset-centric questions, prefer entity or passport units over arbitrary PDF chunks~\cite{peng2024}. Structured and constrained generation then keeps outputs machine-checkable, report fields, CMMS maps, and validators that require cited Asset-IDs to be subsets of the evidence set~\cite{pan2024b,chen2024bimllmontology,iversen2026,buga2025llmfmsql}. Tools and function calling separate reasoning from world actions: the model may request a host passport, expand neighbours, list observations, draft a report, or propose a revisit, but it must obtain those facts from the twin rather than invent them~\cite{schick2023toolformer,yao2023react,guo2025bimllmquery,liu2025bimcoder,li2025b,li2025c}. Twin writes and dispatch remain behind server-side approval~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent}. Long context does not authorise teams to dump an entire elevation into the prompt. Packing should prefer structured digests, Asset-ID, top symptoms, recent observation IDs, neighbour IDs, short location phrases, with image paths so humans can open evidence. Raw pixel dumps are avoided. If a digest still overflows, drop low-confidence observations first, not the topology links needed for a propagation question~\cite{lewis2020,asai2024selfrag}.

Layer~L3 makes the stack durable across sessions and actionable under control. Before agents, readers need a memory ladder; see Table~\ref{tab:ch8-memory}. Cognitive accounts of agent memory often distinguish working, episodic, semantic, and procedural stores; the inspection ladder is a field specialisation of that idea rather than a second taxonomy. Parametric memory is whatever generic knowledge the weights already hold, useful for language, almost useless for last week's crack growth on a specific warehouse elevation~\cite{brown2020}. Contextual memory is the prompt window and short dialogue state, the working store: powerful, length-limited, and session-fragile; tomorrow's new chat may forget that ``west'' meant the loading-dock face unless an external store kept the alias. Retrieval memory loads editioned manuals and observation cards on demand, the episodic and semantic digests that must stay edition-pinned~\cite{lewis2020,shuster2021,krutli2025fmrags}. Structured long-term twin memory is the Asset-ID store itself, geometry, topology, and time-stamped observations in BIM/GIS, optionally linked to knowledge graphs~\cite{pan2024b,zhang2025}. For fa\c{c}ade O\&M the twin is authoritative; the model should read it through retrieval and tools, not pretend the weights remember the site. Bounded agents then compose planning, memory, and tools into procedures such as tickets or revisits under geofence and human gates: procedural memory that lives in the harness, not as silent weight updates~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,rawat2025llmmas,pan2026graphdtgpt,zhang2025llmagentschema}. They are not a licence for unsupervised aircraft command or silent twin mutation. ReAct-style loops without retrieval merely re-enact parametric storytelling with extra steps~\cite{yao2023react}; tool calls without twin memory can hit the wrong host; unchecked memory streams can accumulate speculative notes as if they were facts~\cite{park2023generativeagents}; write gates therefore belong in the harness~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent}.

Agent-memory research often writes a generate-update-evolve loop over experiential entries~\cite{park2023generativeagents,yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents}. For fa\c{c}ade inspection we keep the same symbols but force \emph{externalised} evolution: the twin and harness change, model weights do not. Let $S_t$ be the reasoning state of query~$t$, covering intent, tool TRACE, and validator outcomes, and $M_t$ the external memory of twin store, edition pins, and procedural traces. Then
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ch8-mem-loop}
M_{t+1}=\mathrm{Update}\!\bigl(\mathrm{Generate}(S_t,M_t),\,M_t\bigr),\qquad
\pi_{t+1}=\mathrm{Evolve}_{\mathrm{ext}}(\pi_t,M_{t+1}),
\end{equation}
where $\mathrm{Generate}$ may emit a passport digest, a rejected-draft note, or a revisit hint; $\mathrm{Update}$ merges only through gated writeback or procedural logs; and $\mathrm{Evolve}_{\mathrm{ext}}$ changes tool policy, edition pins, or retrieved packages inside the harness, never silent fine-tuning on site text~\cite{lewis2020,luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent}. In practical terms, session aliases remain short-lived, modality-tagged observations become episodic cards, asset passports provide semantic digests, and approved traces preserve procedural memory. The twin store, edition pins, and gates therefore evolve the operating package while keeping site facts outside the model weights.

These three layers are meant to compose, not to compete. Consider again: ``Is mid-storey hollow on the west elevation related to upstairs staining, and should we stage a gondola this quarter?'' L1 parses the mixed utterance and forces a template. L2 tools fetch seed passports and expand BIM neighbours; RAG packs those cards plus any pinned manual clauses; structured generation emits a draft whose IDs are subsets of the evidence set. L3 ensures that the long-term twin, not yesterday's chat, is the store being read, and that any ticket or revisit proposal waits behind a human gate. Skip L2 and the answer becomes parametric storytelling; skip structured checks and gates and fluent text leaks into CMMS. That composition is generative AI for decision support made operational for fa\c{c}ade O\&M~\cite{lewis2020,yao2023react,ji2023}.

What holds the three layers together in a real system is a \emph{model harness}. In software testing, a harness is scaffolding that exercises a component under controlled conditions. In contemporary LLM engineering the same word has come to mean the code-owned control layer around a replaceable language model: it builds prompts, chooses retrieval units, exposes typed tools, validates outputs against contracts, records traces, and decides when to abstain or escalate. Agent surveys already describe planning, memory, and tools as the ingredients of autonomy~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent}; tool-using reason-act loops and self-supervised tool learning show how models request actions without owning the world state~\cite{yao2023react,schick2023toolformer}. Holistic evaluation frameworks such as HELM further remind readers that what is measured, namely scenarios, metrics, and standardised run conditions, is itself a harness for comparison, not a single accuracy number~\cite{liang2023helm}. For fa\c{c}ade inspection we specialise that idea: the harness is where Asset-ID contracts, edition pins, modality tags, and write gates live so that swapping the underlying LLM does not silently rewrite O\&M policy.

The inspection harness has five cooperating parts; see Table~\ref{tab:ch8-harness}. The \emph{intake and routing} part classifies intent and resolves natural-language locations against GeoBIM aliases before any generation. The \emph{evidence assembly} part loads passports, neighbours, observation cards, and pinned manuals, the RAG and packing duties of Layer~L2~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024,krutli2025fmrags}. The \emph{tool surface} part exposes only a few typed actions, read a passport, expand neighbours, list observations, draft a report, propose a revisit, and refuses free-form SQL or flight commands; AEC tool agents for IFC, energy analysis, and FM robots illustrate the same separation between reasoning and world actions under approval~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,zhang2025llmagentschema,luo2025llmdtrobot,guo2025bimllmquery}. The \emph{contract validators} part checks machine-checkable obligations after drafting: every named Asset-ID must appear in the retrieved package; numeric defect claims must point to observation fields; modality conflicts must remain disclosed; write or dispatch requests must hit a human gate~\cite{ji2023,pan2024b,buga2025llmfmsql}. The \emph{trace and evaluation} part stores which tools ran, which edition was pinned, and which validator passed or failed, so answers can be replayed and graded, an inspection reading of multi-metric, scenario-based evaluation with multi-metric scenarios. Chat fluency alone is insufficient~\cite{liang2023helm,shuster2021}. Adaptive retrieval retries belong inside the harness as controlled loops, not as ad-hoc prompt improvisation~\cite{asai2024selfrag,jeong2024adaptive}.

Read Figure~\ref{fig:ch8-harness-fig} clockwise as one query. Intake resolves intent and Asset-ID seeds before any drafting. Evidence assembly packs passports, neighbours, and pinned manuals. The tool surface admits only typed twin reads and draft actions. The replaceable LLM composes language inside that package. Contract validators enforce cite-or-abstain and modality disclosure. Trace and evaluation keep the run replayable so the next query can reuse the same gates. The dashed LLM box is intentional: model weights may change; the surrounding contracts should not.

Two consequences follow for procurement and teaching. First, a prompt-only prototype is not yet a harness: instructions such as ``always cite Asset-IDs'' can be ignored by a fluent model, whereas a validator that blocks delivery when the ID subset check fails is load-bearing. Second, model substitution should be a harness test, not a project rewrite, if validators, tool schemas, and twin interfaces stay fixed, changing the hosted LLM should change phrasing, not which sources are eligible or whether a write gate exists~\cite{liang2023helm,saka2024gptconstruction}. Emerging enterprise discussions of harness engineering emphasise relocating behaviour from prompts into versioned contracts, manifests, and traces; this chapter keeps that intuition while citing only peer-published grounding, tool, agent, and evaluation results. In short: Layers~L1--L3 name the capabilities; the harness is the software that enforces them on every query.

Recent building-oriented LLM work makes the same shift from model selection alone to system design. Data readiness, trust, interoperability, and human oversight now sit ahead of parameter counts~\cite{ma2026tenquestions}: fa\c{c}ade O\&M succeeds when Asset-IDs, editioned manuals, and approval gates are ready. Published graph-twin, BIM-retrieval, IFC-tool, and FM-robot agents illustrate the same habit in adjacent settings: keep typed structure and gates outside the weights, and treat the LLM as a replaceable composer inside a harness~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,lu2026highwaymas,gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot,hellin2025bimmas,quan2026bimfacrag}. None of those systems is a warehouse fa\c{c}ade inspector out of the box; the transferable lesson is the control pattern.

Hallucination surveys explain what goes wrong when the discipline is missing: fluent systems invent entities, numbers, and citations~\cite{ji2023}. In inspection the costly failures are concrete, invented Asset-IDs, repair advice without attributable evidence, and summaries that erase disagreement between RGB and infrared observations. Grounding therefore means more than a clever prompt. Constrained schemas, retrieval and its adaptive variants, tool-using reason-act loops, and typed structured knowledge recur across NLP and AEC~\cite{lewis2020,gao2023hyde,asai2024selfrag,jeong2024adaptive,peng2024,yao2023react,schick2023toolformer,pan2024b}. Graph-oriented and hierarchical retrieval further remind readers that document similarity is not building adjacency~\cite{peng2024,sarthi2024raptor,sun2024tog}. Semantic embeddings cluster phrases such as ``damp'', ``stain'', and ``seepage''; building spaces cluster panels that share joints, storeys, and drainage paths. A hybrid retriever may use embeddings to rank symptom digests inside an already topology-constrained candidate set; it must not use embeddings to invent the candidate set for propagation questions~\cite{peng2024,jeon2025hybridrag,khattab2020}. When system rules, retrieved manuals, twin facts, and user requests conflict, precedence should be explicit: safety and write gates first; twin facts and observation fields second; pinned manual editions third; user style preferences last~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction}.

Civil and building applications already exercise these patterns. BIM assistants and NL model queries improve navigation; code, safety, defect, and facility RAG improve edition fidelity; schemas, typed tools, and approval make language interfaces operational~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,jeon2025hybridrag,krutli2025fmrags,gao2026ifcagent,buga2025llmfmsql}. High-resolution fa\c{c}ade datasets and physical quantification still set the granularity of claims that language may responsibly make~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}. For this monograph, Chapters~2--7 retain flight, geometry, observations, and GeoBIM identity; Chapter~8 turns those products into evidence packages~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg,han2025}. Readers should therefore retrieve over asset passports and BIM neighbours, pin manuals by edition, and treat success on public code questions as insufficient proof for a private fa\c{c}ade handbook~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,ma2026tenquestions}.

Memory failures make the ladder concrete. If a manager clarifies that ``west'' means the loading-dock face and tomorrow's chat forgets it, the repair is to persist the alias or require GeoBIM seed selection before generation, not to hope the model ``remembers.'' If RAG returns an outdated maintenance PDF, the repair is edition pins and corpus smoke tests~\cite{krutli2025fmrags,joffe2025buildingcodes}. If the model invents a crack length from generic web knowledge, the repair is to ban numeric defect claims unless an observation field supplies them~\cite{ji2023,zhao2026physical}. If the twin holds the correct Asset-ID but the orchestrator never fetched it, that is an infrastructure failure, not a wording problem~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024}. Who may write to twin memory after an LLM draft, how long dialogue clarifications persist, and which manual edition wins when two PDFs disagree are therefore foundations questions, not afterthought IT policy. For fa\c{c}ade O\&M we recommend a simple rule: the twin store is write-gated; the dialogue window is ephemeral; RAG indexes are editioned; parametric memory is never treated as a site database~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,rawat2025llmmas}.

Selecting an LLM stack for inspection is likewise a harness problem more than a model-selection problem. Teams should assess the five harness parts above, especially whether validators and tool gates are code-owned, plus native tool-calling reliability, long-context behaviour on passport sets, willingness to abstain under constrained decoding, on-prem versus cloud constraints for building data, and cost per grounded answer ahead of cost per chat token~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,wang2024surveyagents,liang2023helm}. A smaller model inside a strict cite-or-abstain harness often beats a larger model that improvises Asset-IDs in an unvalidated chat loop. Even before laboratory scoring, ten hand-checked answers already separate grounded homework from chat demos: listed Asset-IDs $\subseteq$ retrieved set; hypotheses tagged; modality tags preserved; abstention when neighbours are missing~\cite{ji2023,shuster2021,lewis2020}. The teaching takeaway is therefore three design choices: retrieve over maintainable assets and neighbours; force citation of IDs that appear in the retrieved set; and place irreversible actions behind approval gates. Sections~\ref{sec:ch8-dt-to-language}--\ref{sec:ch8-multimodal} next show how twin records and multimodal evidence make Layers~L2 and~L3 operational.



In summary of the foundations, Layers~L1--L3 name what a ready model can do under control, and the harness names the software that enforces those behaviours on every query. Readers who skip validators and tool gates will rediscover parametric storytelling regardless of model size~\cite{ji2023,liang2023helm,ma2026tenquestions}. The next section turns those layers into twin records: observation cards, asset passports, and intent-conditioned assembly.

\section{From Digital Twins to Language Reasoning}
\label{sec:ch8-dt-to-language}

Language reasoning over an inspection twin is not free-form storytelling. It is assembly of evidence that earlier chapters already created, the operational substrate for Layers~L2 and~L3 in Section~\ref{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}. We keep three layers conceptually separate even when implementations differ. An \emph{observation record} is one defect sighting with sensor provenance covering imagery, evidence, semantics, and host attachment; teaching content often packages that sighting as an integrated defect profile. An \emph{asset passport} aggregates observations and geometry or topology for a maintainable host as an integrated asset profile for retrieval. \emph{Query assembly} decides which passports and neighbours enter the context before generation; neighbour expansion along fa\c{c}ade adjacency supports vertical propagation reading. Perception writes observations; facility managers think in assets; language models must not invent either~\cite{pan2024b,zheng2023bimassistant}.

In UAV inspection practice, useful observation records store host Asset-ID, time, modality such as RGB, infrared, and related channels, detector signature, pose or surface coordinates, and paths to imagery. Registration should attach detections to BIM hosts before any LLM is involved~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg}. Natural-language location phrases help models, for example ``west elevation, third bay above the loading dock'', while metric coordinates remain for field navigation. Asset passports then add symptom summaries, geometry for severity normalisation, and neighbour links above, below, and lateral. Those links enable propagation-style reading, for example, mid-storey delamination considered with upstairs moisture, without confusing a document-similarity graph with a structural or BIM adjacency graph~\cite{peng2024}.

Geometry and BIM hosts from Chapter~4 provide attachment points, Chapters~5--6 provide labelled observations and confidence, and Chapter~7 provides city-scale Asset-IDs. This chapter consumes those products to make retrieval, memory, reports, and actions traceable.

A minimal observation schema for language use includes observation ID, host Asset-ID, campaign or capture time, modality tag, defect type and confidence from Chapter~6, optional severity features derived from geometry, image or crop URI, and a short human-readable location phrase. Optional fields include weather flags, inspector notes, and links to prior observations on the same host. What must not be left as free text alone is the host Asset-ID. A temporary image filename or folder label created at capture time records how a file was stored; it is not a facility register code. If the only link from image to host is such a filename convention, retrieval will eventually invent neighbours. Enterprise RAG often chunks PDFs, but inspection questions are usually about maintainable hosts and their neighbourhoods. Passports therefore package, for each Asset-ID, a compact symptom digest, recent observation IDs, geometry summaries used for severity normalisation, and explicit neighbour lists. Severity normalisation matters because a 20\,cm crack on a small panel is not the same operational object as a 20\,cm crack on a large wall bay; the language layer inherits that normalisation from twin geometry~\cite{zhao2026physical}. Neighbour lists should come from BIM or GeoBIM adjacency, not from embedding similarity among report sentences.

Query assembly is where inspection differs from generic enterprise RAG. Write the already-built records once, then let intent choose the package. An asset passport for host~$a$ packages identity, geometry summary~$G(a)$, one-hop BIM neighbours~$N_1(a)$, observation set~$O(a)$, and a compact symptom digest~$d(a)$:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ch8-passport}
P(a)=\bigl\langle a,\,G(a),\,N_1(a),\,O(a),\,d(a)\bigr\rangle.
\end{equation}
Given seed Asset-IDs~$A_q$ resolved from question~$q$ and hop depth~$k(\iota)$ set by intent~$\iota$: zero for local lookup, one for propagation, the BIM neighbourhood is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ch8-khop}
\mathcal{N}_{k}(A_q)=\bigl\{v:\exists\,a\in A_q,\;\mathrm{dist}_{\mathrm{BIM}}(a,v)\le k\bigr\},
\end{equation}
and the evidence package packed for generation is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ch8-package}
\mathcal{C}(q)=\mathrm{Pack}\!\Bigl(\bigl\{P(v):v\in\mathcal{N}_{k(\iota)}(A_q)\bigr\},\,R_{\mathrm{man}}(q),\,e\Bigr),
\end{equation}
where $R_{\mathrm{man}}(q)$ is the optional edition-pinned manual slice and $e$ the edition pin itself~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024,joffe2025buildingcodes}. A local lookup such as ``what defects were logged on asset~$A$ last month?'' uses $k=0$. A propagation question uses neighbours along fa\c{c}ade topology, not merely the top-$k$ text chunks that share vocabulary with the query. A floor- or elevation-level audit replaces hop expansion by a Chapter~7 spatial filter on passports. Intent therefore changes \emph{which} twin records enter~$\mathcal{C}(q)$, not only the prompt wording~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024}. Editioned manuals and safety clauses may join~$R_{\mathrm{man}}$ when the question mixes site evidence with code language~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,krutli2025fmrags}.

One recurring implementation pattern organises observations into asset passports and expands neighbours along BIM adjacency for multi-hop diagnosis. Published siblings share the grounding intuition on other corpora: hybrid defect RAG~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag}, code and safety RAG~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety}, facility-document RAG~\cite{krutli2025fmrags}, and BIM assistants~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant}. The monograph lesson is the pattern: inspection questions are asset-centric, so retrieval units should be asset-centric. When the assembly cannot support a claim, the correct behaviour is abstention or escalation to a human inspector, not a confident paragraph.

Worked micro-example. Suppose the manager asks: ``Is water ingress on the west stack likely propagating from above?'' Assembly should load the target passport, upstairs and lateral neighbours, observation cards tagged RGB and infrared, and any editioned moisture-related maintenance note linked to those Asset-IDs. Generation may then hypothesise a path \emph{only} if neighbour observations exist; otherwise it must say that propagation cannot be supported from the twin. That discipline is language reasoning over a twin, not storytelling beside a twin.


Implementation teams often ask how large a passport should be. Too small, and propagation reading starves. Too large, and the context window fills with irrelevant neighbours from an entire elevation. A practical teaching heuristic is intent-conditioned expansion: local lookup uses zero hops; propagation uses one hop or a small $k$ along BIM adjacency; elevation audits use spatial filters from Chapter~7 . Unbounded hops are avoided~\cite{peng2024,zhang2025}. Manual clauses are retrieved only when the question mentions compliance, safety, or repair standards~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety}.

Indexing choices should follow the same heuristic. Vector indexes over passport digests support fuzzy symptom search such as ``hollow sounding panels with staining above''. Graph or relational indexes over Asset-IDs support exact neighbour expansion. Keyword indexes over observation IDs support audit lookups. Hybrid retrieval is attractive precisely because inspection questions mix fuzzy language with exact identifiers~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag,krutli2025fmrags}. What should not be hybridised away is the authority of topology: embedding similarity must not silently replace BIM adjacency when the question is about propagation.

Abstention deserves equal teaching time. Engineers already know how to say ``insufficient evidence'' on site; language systems must learn the same habit. Let $\hat{y}$ be a draft answer and $\mathrm{IDs}(\cdot)$ the Asset-IDs and observation IDs named in a string or package. The machine-checkable cite-or-abstain gate is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ch8-cite}
\mathrm{IDs}(\hat{y})\subseteq\mathrm{IDs}\bigl(\mathcal{C}(q)\bigr)
\quad\text{else return }\texttt{ABSTAIN}.
\end{equation}
Severity claims must further point to members of~$O(\cdot)$ inside~$\mathcal{C}(q)$, and any propagation path must be a walk inside the expanded BIM neighbourhood of Eq.~\eqref{eq:ch8-khop}. If checks fail, the system returns a structured refusal plus the missing evidence type, such as no upstairs neighbour, conflicting modalities, or stale campaign. That behaviour is more valuable than a fluent paragraph that cannot be opened in the twin UI~\cite{ji2023,shuster2021}.

A second worked micro-example clarifies elevation audits. The manager asks: ``Summarise west-elevation issues above level three that need gondola access.'' Assembly should apply a Chapter~7 spatial filter, load the resulting passport set, keep modality tags, and rank by severity features inherited from geometry. It should not narrate the whole building, and it should not drop Asset-IDs in favour of storey prose alone. The output is a decision artefact for scheduling, not a travelogue.



Managers often ask whether a stain is ``new.'' Querying historical maintenance records is a first-class capability of the decision layer and a first-class assembly intent. Cross-campaign comparison requires the same Asset-ID across time, comparable modality coverage, prior observation IDs or work-order links when available, and explicit handling of missing priors. If the previous campaign lacks infrared, the system must not invent a thermal delta. If geometry was remeshed and Asset-IDs were remapped carelessly, language will compare the wrong hosts. A natural-language question such as ``what was repaired on the west elevation after the 2024 campaign?'' should retrieve editioned twin memory and any linked approved reports; it must abstain when those IDs are absent rather than invent a repair narrative~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg,chen2023facility}. Longitudinal discipline is therefore twin hygiene before it is model cleverness~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg,zhao2026physical}. Perception may emit confidences; geometry may support length or area features; O\&M may need ordinal severity for scheduling. The language layer should not convert ordinal triage into false millimetre certainty. Prefer phrases tied to fields, such as ``severity=medium from geometry-normalised crack length; confidence=0.71 from detector'', over cinematic adjectives. Physical quantification pipelines show why numbers need provenance~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}; the LLM's job is to carry that provenance into sentences, not to decorate it.

Algorithm~\ref{alg:ch8-assembly} summarises assembly as a numbered procedure. It is deliberately dull: every branch is a place students try to be clever, and where inspection systems usually break~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024,jeon2025hybridrag}.

\begin{algorithm}[htbp]
\caption{Intent-conditioned twin assembly for a grounded inspection answer.}
\label{alg:ch8-assembly}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\Require natural-language query $q$
\Ensure draft answer, or a structured abstention code
\State $\mathit{intent} \gets \textsc{Classify}(q)$
\State $\mathit{seeds} \gets \textsc{ResolveAssets}(q)$ \Comment{else \texttt{ABSTAIN:asset\_unresolved}}
\State $\mathit{packs} \gets \{\textsc{Passport}(s): s\in\mathit{seeds}\}$
\If{$\mathit{intent}=\texttt{propagation}$}
  \State $\mathit{packs} \gets \mathit{packs}\cup\textsc{Expand}(\mathit{seeds},k{=}1)$
\EndIf
\If{$\mathit{intent}=\texttt{elevation\_audit}$}
  \State $\mathit{packs} \gets \textsc{SpatialFilter}(\mathit{packs},q)$
\EndIf
\State $\mathit{obs} \gets \textsc{ListObservations}(\mathit{packs})$
\State $\mathit{manuals} \gets \textsc{RetrieveManuals}(q)$ if $\textsc{NeedsCode}(q)$, else $\emptyset$
\State $\mathcal{C}(q) \gets \{\mathit{intent},\mathit{packs},\mathit{obs},\mathit{manuals},\mathit{edition\_pin}\}$
\State $\hat{y} \gets \textsc{Generate}(\mathcal{C}(q))$ \Comment{cite-or-abstain}
\State $\textsc{ValidateIDs}(\hat{y},\mathcal{C}(q))$ \Comment{else \texttt{ABSTAIN:id\_not\_in\_package}}
\State \Return $\hat{y}$
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

Natural-language locations remain ambiguous: ``third bay'' may mean different hosts after remeshing. Resolution against GeoBIM aliases must precede passport fetch. Fuzzy string match can propose candidates; it must not silently pick one when two candidates score closely. Disambiguation questions are successful system behaviour, not UX failure~\cite{zhang2025,han2025,guo2025bimllmquery}. The same discipline appears in recent twin-query systems that refuse to answer from unconstrained text when the structured store lacks a path~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,buga2025llmfmsql}: for inspection, ``no upstairs neighbour in the twin'' is a better product behaviour than a fluent moisture story.

Published 2024--2025 retrieval practice also clarifies what not to reinvent. Hybrid defect-management RAG and safety-manual RAG show gains when corpora are editioned and noisy maintenance language is expected~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag,lee2024ragsafety}; code and regulation hybrids show that clause retrieval must still meet site evidence before advice becomes operational~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,he2025regulationhybrid,yang2024promptcode}. Facility-document RAG and structured FM querying show that building corpora scale only when schemas and Asset-ID links exist~\cite{krutli2025fmrags,buga2025llmfmsql,arslan2024ragenergy}. Translating those results into UAV fa\c{c}ade O\&M means treating observation cards and asset passports as the primary retrieval units, manuals as secondary pinned context, and topology expansion as a first-class assembly step~\cite{peng2024,pan2024b,ma2026tenquestions}.




Assembly discipline can be restated as teaching narrative that facilities and software teams share. Local lookups should load only the target passport and recent observations; stuffing an entire elevation into context makes cite-or-abstain hard to explain~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024}. Propagation questions require topology edges before symptom similarity; if edges are missing, abstain with \texttt{no\_neighbour} . Embedding a stain from the opposite elevation is refused~\cite{peng2024,jeon2025hybridrag,pan2026graphdtgpt}. Elevation audits should follow Chapter~7 spatial filters and return schedulable Asset-ID lists, not travelogues~\cite{zhang2025,buga2025llmfmsql}. Code-plus-site questions must pin manual editions and site passports together; clause-only or evidence-only packages should disclose the gap~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,he2025regulationhybrid,quan2026bimfacrag}. Cross-campaign comparisons put identity hygiene first: if Asset-IDs were remapped carelessly, abstain on ``worse than last time''~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg,zhao2026physical}. BIM NL multi-agent retrieval and condensed retrieval agents remind readers that clever navigation is not yet topology-aware diagnosis~\cite{hellin2025bimmas,koh2026bimllmretr,liu2025bimcoder}; facility RAG reminds them that document lakes without Asset-ID links still struggle to enter CMMS~\cite{krutli2025fmrags,quan2026bimfacrag}.

\section{Multi-Modal Fusion}
\label{sec:ch8-multimodal}

Fa\c{c}ade programmes are multimodal by construction: campaigns routinely produce RGB imagery, infrared channels, and textual metadata. In this chapter, \emph{multi-modal fusion} means disciplined assembly of those modality-tagged observation records, already written by Chapter~6 perception and attached by Chapters~4 and~7, inside grounded LLM retrieval and generation. Defect finding remains a Chapter~6 perception responsibility; language remains responsible for retrieval, explanation, abstention, and action proposals under citation rules.

Vision--language models (VLMs) belong in this phase of the monograph's decision-support story, but they do not replace the defect detectors of Chapters~5--6. A VLM may help an inspector narrate an openable crop already attached to an Asset-ID, align a free-text remark with a retrieved passport, or draft a caption that still cites the observation URI. It must not invent host codes, silently relabel detector classes, or treat a chat screenshot as a new twin fact. The operational split taught here is therefore simple: Chapter~6 writes typed observations; a VLM, when used, is an optional reader of those crops under the same cite-or-abstain gate as the LLM; the twin remains the authoritative store~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical,ji2023}. That split fuses visual defect data with generative AI without collapsing perception and language into one unsupervised model.

Fusing modalities without erasing conflict. Textbook query types for fa\c{c}ade O\&M include local lookup, asset-health summary, propagation or root-cause reading, and floor- or elevation-level audit. A minimal assembly is the passport of asset~$A$, its neighbours, and optional editioned manuals. Every Asset-ID or defect ID named in the answer must appear in that retrieved set; otherwise the system abstains or escalates. When RGB and infrared observations disagree, disclosure beats silent averaging: RGB-only evidence should not become a subsurface claim; infrared-only evidence should keep uncertainty flags. High-resolution fa\c{c}ade sensing and physical quantification work show why those flags matter: language that over-claims geometry inherits perception error~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}. A practical fusion rule for teaching is therefore record-level, not pixel-level: keep modality tags on observations; let the LLM narrate agreement or conflict; never collapse conflict into a single confident defect type without a human check flag.

The implementation principle is straightforward: a presentation layer may look like chat, but evidence assembly, persistence, and twin writes must pass typed services and harness gates.

Agents enter when inspection work requires multi-step procedures. Typical procedures include opening a ticket, proposing a UAV revisit polygon for Chapters~2--3, requesting a robot photo, or staging a twin update after approval~\cite{yao2023react,schick2023toolformer,wang2024surveyagents,luo2025llmdtrobot}. Memory is part of agency, not an afterthought. Short-term dialogue state tracks the current question; retrieval memory refreshes manuals and observation cards; twin memory supplies Asset-IDs and topology; procedural memory records which tools and approvals already ran~\cite{rawat2025llmmas,gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot}. Surveys of LLM agents and multi-agent systems emphasise the same ingredients of planning, memory, and tools while warning that autonomy without constraints is unsafe~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent}. The 2024--2026 AEC literature makes that warning concrete. Graph-DT multi-agent systems ground answers in twin graphs~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt}; human-DT MAS prototypes coordinate leader, reasoning, and member agents so infrastructure experts can complete tasks with lower cognitive load than steep GUIs~\cite{lu2026highwaymas}; multi-agent virtual modelling connects design-to-operation twins inside BIM~\cite{lee2025multiagentvbm}; IFC tool agents, FM robot brokers, and schema-guided energy agents illustrate typed tools under approval~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot,zhang2025llmagentschema}; router-style expert selection in foundation design shows specialised roles can help when humans keep safety oversight~\cite{youwai2026foundationmas}. Tool-augmented reasoning is therefore an expected ingredient of inspection language systems, not an optional plugin~\cite{yao2023react,schick2023toolformer}.

For fa\c{c}ade inspection we recommend \emph{bounded agency} realised inside the model harness of Section~\ref{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}. Multi-step retrieval and tool calls are welcome. Final claims stay citation-locked to the retrieved evidence set. Dispatch and twin writes stay behind geofence and human gates. A small typed tool surface with a handful of read and draft actions beats free-form SQL or unrestricted code execution. That stance specialises general agent literature to the inspection setting: the twin already encodes the world state that an agent must not rewrite casually; Chapters~2--3 already own flight planning; the language agent proposes, it does not commandeer the aircraft. Recent twin-interaction MAS studies reinforce the same division of labour: agents orchestrate functions and reduce interface friction, but they do not replace domain approval or chapter ownership~\cite{lu2026highwaymas,pan2026graphdtgpt,ma2026tenquestions}. The harness, not the chat transcript, is what makes those bounds enforceable on every run~\cite{yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents,liang2023helm}.

Worked micro-example. The manager asks for a revisit where infrared suggests moisture but RGB is inconclusive. A bounded agent may list the modality-tagged observations, notice the conflict, draft a report that marks human review, and emit a coarse revisit hint. It must not invent a flight trajectory in prose, and it must not write a new defect type into the twin without approval. That is multimodal fusion in the sense of this book: modality-aware records, grounded language, and tools that respect upstream chapter ownership.


Multi-modal fusion also interacts with campaign design. If infrared and RGB are captured on different days, time stamps must remain visible in observation cards; otherwise the LLM may treat them as simultaneous evidence. If one modality has systematically lower detector confidence, trust flags should inherit that asymmetry and must not average it away~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}. Fusion at language time is therefore inseparable from perception metadata quality. Garbage in still yields fluent garbage out, only now with citations that look official.

Agent design should stay boring on purpose. Spectacular autonomous stacks that rewrite BIM, dispatch aircraft, and email contractors in one loop are poor teaching targets for inspection safety. Bounded tools with explicit gates match how responsible O\&M already works: analysts propose; seniors approve; field teams execute~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,zhang2025llmagentschema}. Multi-agent role splits into retriever, topology expander, report drafter, and action proposer can be useful pedagogically, provided they share one twin memory and one approval policy~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,rawat2025llmmas}. Competing agents that invent different Asset-IDs for the same panel are not ``diversity''; they are a failure mode.

Procedural memory deserves a concrete inspection reading. When an agent has already expanded neighbours and listed observations, it should not re-expand endlessly in a loop. When a human rejected a twin write, that rejection should be remembered for the session so the agent does not resubmit the same mutation. When a revisit was proposed, the planning hint should carry the observation IDs that justified it, so Chapters~2--3 receive evidence with observation IDs~\cite{yao2023react,rawat2025llmmas}. Memory, in this sense, is operational discipline.



When demonstrating agents, show the tool trace beside the answer: which passports were fetched, which neighbours expanded, which manual edition pinned, which gate blocked a write. Traces make bounded agency inspectable and turn the harness into a teaching object. Surveys of LLM agents emphasise planning and tools; inspection teaching should emphasise the audit trail those tools leave~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,yao2023react,schick2023toolformer,liang2023helm}. A beautiful answer without a trace is a demo; a plain answer with a trace is an O\&M artefact. Splitting retriever, topology, drafter, and action roles can clarify software modules, as graph-twin and human-DT MAS designs also do~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,lu2026highwaymas}, but must not create competing Asset-ID realities. All roles read the same twin memory; only the gated writer may stage updates; only humans approve. Construction multi-agent frameworks that combine graph and vector memory are useful precedents for shared memory, not for unsupervised committee decisions about defects~\cite{rawat2025llmmas,gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot}.

A compact walkthrough makes the harness concrete. For a moisture-path question on host W-3-17, resolve intent as propagation, load the passport, expand one-hop neighbours, list modality-tagged observations, and abstain if upstairs evidence is missing. Draft under cite-or-abstain. If the manager requests action, draft a report that marks human review on RGB/IR conflict and propose a revisit only for conflicted assets; wait for approval in procedural memory; stage a twin write only after approve. Logging every step is the product; hiding steps to look clever is the anti-pattern~\cite{yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents,luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,lu2026highwaymas}. A short audit trace might record: intent=propagation; tools used=read passport, expand neighbours, list observations; package hosts=W-3-17, W-4-17, W-3-16; modality conflict on W-3-17; report draft awaiting human review; revisit pending; twin writes=none. Revisit tools must emit planning hints, not executable UAV commands. Coordinates should be coarse polygons or asset sets that Chapters~2--3 refine under their own safety stack. Robot photo requests, when available, should be tickets. Direct motor commands are refused~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot}. Once the user asks for tickets or revisits, the orchestrator can enter an explicit state machine DRAFT $\rightarrow$ REVIEW $\rightarrow$ APPROVED/REJECTED $\rightarrow$ SCHEDULED; the LLM fills fields inside a state and must not skip REVIEW because the user is in a hurry~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,zhang2025llmagentschema,rawat2025llmmas,lu2026highwaymas}. Agent tests can stay offline: stub tools return fixtures; assert that forbidden writes never occur; assert revisit hints always carry observation IDs; assert rejected drafts never reappear without new evidence. Those tests are cheaper than flight campaigns and catch the worst failures early~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,ma2026tenquestions}.



Operators can keep a one-page fusion decision table beside the console.

\hline
defect & defect $\rightarrow$ cite both; diagnose with caution \\
defect & normal/absent $\rightarrow$ disclose asymmetry; no subsurface leap \\

\end{center}
\vspace{0.5em}
Operators can tape this table beside the console. It turns Section~\ref{sec:ch8-multimodal} into muscle memory without a second perception stack~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}.

When not to call an agent. If the user only asks what is logged on an asset, reading the passport and listing observations suffice; no ticket machinery. Agents earn their complexity only for procedures. Premature agency increases gate surface area without increasing grounding~\cite{yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents}.




Conflict-asset week closes multimodal teaching without a subsection forest. Suppose three west-elevation hosts show RGB/IR conflict in one week: the system must not average them into one defect type; gondola lists should down-rank them by default; revisit hints must bind observation IDs for Chapters~2--3~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical,luo2025llmdtrobot}. If a manager asks to ``mark repaired first and gather evidence later,'' the harness must refuse direct writes and only stage pending updates behind approval~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,lu2026highwaymas}. When demonstrating agents, show TRACE beside the answer: passports fetched, neighbours expanded, manual edition pinned, which gate blocked a write. A beautiful answer without TRACE remains a demo; a plain answer with TRACE is an O\&M artefact~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,liang2023helm,hellin2025bimmas}. Role splits may clarify software modules, but they must not create multiple Asset-ID truths; graph-twin and human-DT MAS papers are precedents for shared memory and approval, not for unsupervised committee votes on defects~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,lu2026highwaymas,rawat2025llmmas,koh2026bimllmretr}.



In summary of multimodal fusion and bounded agency, modality tags travel with observation cards, TRACE makes tool use inspectable, and write gates keep the twin authoritative~\cite{zhao2026physical,yao2023react,luo2025llmdtrobot,lu2026highwaymas}. The next section turns grounded drafts into report schemas that facility teams can audit, with optional export into existing work systems.

\section{Inspection Report Generation and Management}
\label{sec:ch8-reports}

Inspection answers become useful when they land in artefacts facility-management systems already understand. Free-form paragraphs are hard to audit. A practical report schema groups identity fields for asset and defect IDs, diagnosis, propagation notes, recommended actions, and trust flags. Identity fields must be subsets of the retrieved evidence set, the report-level analogue of constrained decoding used in BIM and code assistants~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes}. Diagnosis fields should point back to observation IDs whose images open in the twin UI. Action fields should be readable by schedulers without requiring another LLM to re-parse prose.

Figure~\ref{fig:ch8-ablation} illustrates the kind of grounding outcome a reader should demand. Passport-centred packaging with one-hop expansion keeps answer IDs and cited IDs closer to the retrieved evidence than chunk-only packaging, so hop depth belongs to the evidence-assembly policy rather than a free prompt choice~\cite{lewis2020,jeon2025hybridrag,lee2024ragsafety}. Report drafts inherit that policy: every named Asset-ID must occur in the returned package, and every evidence URI must open~\cite{ji2023,peng2024}. For a question about water ingress on a vertical stack, the system should return neighbour passports and openable observation IDs, then draft a repair brief or abstain when evidence is thin. If a revisit is needed, an action proposer emits a planning hint for Chapters~2--3; it does not invent a flight path in prose.

Report management means more than one-shot generation. Drafts should be versioned; human sign-off should be recorded; approved facts may be written back into twin memory, the same long-term store introduced in Section~\ref{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}. Rejected drafts should not silently pollute passports. Where organisations already use CMMS or BIM issue trackers, the schema above may map to those fields as an optional export. Parallel unreproducible prose PDFs are avoided. Natural-language query interfaces can help non-specialists ask questions, but the authoritative artefact remains the constrained report plus openable evidence. A practical state machine keeps that discipline enforceable: DRAFT $\rightarrow$ VALIDATE $\rightarrow$ REVIEW $\rightarrow$ APPROVED/REJECTED $\rightarrow$ optional EXPORT $\rightarrow$ optional TWIN\_WRITE. The LLM may fill fields only inside DRAFT; Table~\ref{tab:ch8-report-accept} owns VALIDATE; humans own REVIEW; EXPORT is only for sites that already run a work system; only an approved path may call \texttt{stage\_twin\_write}. ReAct-style tool loops and schema-guided AEC agents already show that multi-step tool use is valuable when each step remains typed and gated~\cite{yao2023react,gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot}. Fluency must not skip VALIDATE because a meeting is starting in ten minutes~\cite{li2024surveymultiagent,rawat2025llmmas,hellin2025bimmas}. Software roles may split for clarity into retriever, drafter, optional mapper, and action proposer without creating multiple Asset-ID truths: all roles read one twin memory and one approval policy~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot,quan2026bimfacrag,buga2025llmfmsql,li2024surveymultiagent,wang2024surveyagents}.

Figure~\ref{fig:ch8-nlq-ui} is useful only when read together with Sections~\ref{sec:ch8-dt-to-language}--\ref{sec:ch8-multimodal}: structured twin memory, multimodal evidence assembly, and bounded tools. A fluent chat panel without those contracts is still a demo. A short worked reading of the interface pattern keeps that distinction sharp. Suppose the inspector asks which west-elevation hosts above storey three show moisture-related symptoms with openable evidence. A contract-respecting answer names Asset-IDs that already sit in the retrieved passport set, lists observation IDs whose crops open in the twin viewer, discloses RGB/IR conflict when present, and offers structured follow-ups such as expand neighbours or draft a schema-constrained brief. A failing interface may still look similar on screen: it returns a fluent paragraph, invents a panel nickname, and offers a ``mark repaired'' button that writes the twin without a gate. Readers should therefore score Figure~\ref{fig:ch8-nlq-ui} with the same four tests used in procurement reviews: stable Asset-IDs, openable citations, abstention when the package is thin, and gated writes~\cite{ji2023,zhang2025,buga2025llmfmsql}.

Report schemas should be negotiated with facility stakeholders early. Fields that nobody will open in a CMMS will not be maintained. Fields that lawyers require for liability may need stricter cite-or-abstain checks than fields used for internal triage. A teaching compromise is a core schema covering identity, diagnosis, evidence links, and trust, plus optional organisation-specific extensions covering cost codes, contractor IDs, and access windows. The LLM may draft extensions only from retrieved context or human-provided forms; it must not invent cost codes~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes}.

Human-in-the-loop is not a confession of failure; it is the product. Sign-off records should store who approved what, which evidence IDs were visible at approval time, and whether modality conflict flags were overridden. Those records become part of twin provenance for the next campaign. Without them, next season's LLM will treat last season's speculative draft as fact. Identity hygiene figures in this section are therefore not IT folklore: they are the precondition for longitudinal language reasoning~\cite{zhang2025}.

The same contracts expose weak demos quickly: PDF-only RAG without Asset-IDs, chat UIs that cannot open cited images, twin writes from unapproved drafts, severity language without geometry normalisation, or propagation stories built from embedding neighbours instead of BIM adjacency. Two or more of those marks leave the system a prototype. Delivery itself has a fixed half and an optional half. The fixed half is the schema-constrained draft with openable evidence: identity, diagnosis, trust flags, and URIs that open the correct crops. Asset-ID hygiene and cite-or-abstain are part of that half; they are not the whole product. Narrative must be rendered from filled fields; IDs must not be scraped from prose after the fact; nightly link checks belong beside detector quality control~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes,zhang2025,gao2026ifcagent}. The optional half is exporting those fields into whatever work system a site already runs. That sink may be a CMMS, a BIM issue tracker, a contractor portal, or a simple spreadsheet; this chapter does not invent a new CMMS product. When an export exists, keep the mapping short and mechanical: \texttt{asset\_ids} to register codes, evidence URIs to attachments, facility-scale priority rather than an LLM-invented scale, and \texttt{human\_check} as a hold that blocks auto-close~\cite{chen2023facility,krutli2025fmrags,buga2025llmfmsql}. Sites without a CMMS still succeed if they keep the constrained draft, openable evidence, and human sign-off.

Handover packages should therefore carry the Asset-ID dictionary, observation schema, manual edition pins, approval logs, and a frozen twin snapshot used for language evaluation. An export map is included only when the receiving system is known. Report-lifecycle thickness comes from those contracts: drafts pass ID subset validation before human review, rejections enter procedural memory, approved fields may export with minimal rewrite, and Asset-ID collisions stay visible beside the draft~\cite{buga2025llmfmsql,chen2023facility,quan2026bimfacrag,hellin2025bimmas}. Published FM robot and IFC-agent loops close a similar circle of language request, structured action, and twin or model update under approval; fa\c{c}ade O\&M specialises the circle so that every actionable artefact still cites openable observation crops before money or scaffolding moves~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,saka2024gptconstruction}. An ``official'' PDF without openable evidence is more dangerous than a chat hallucination for exactly that reason~\cite{ji2023}.

After each major flight week, freeze a twin snapshot, re-run Table~\ref{tab:ch8-report-accept} on a gold question set that includes abstentions, and score invented-ID rate and broken-evidence rate before swapping any hosted LLM~\cite{liang2023helm,zhang2025}. Cross-campaign claims such as ``worse than last season'' require prior observation IDs in the package; otherwise the harness must force abstention or a human-only narrative field. That seasonal hygiene protects longitudinal language the same way Asset-ID hygiene protects a single draft~\cite{chen2023facility,krutli2025fmrags}. The authoritative artefact remains a schema-constrained draft with openable evidence and human sign-off; Table~\ref{tab:ch8-report-accept} checks that core, while any work-system export remains an optional later recipe. The next section asks whether those artefacts actually change schedules under implementation-agnostic criteria.

\section{Decision Support and Predictive Maintenance}
\label{sec:ch8-dss}

Decision support closes the chapter by asking what a grounded language system is for. The operational answer is generative under control: automatically support comprehensive reports, natural-language queries over historical maintenance records, and predictive maintenance recommendations that accelerate scheduling while remaining under professional review. In teaching terms, that means named assets, openable evidence, ranked triage for the next gondola week, and honest abstention when prognosis evidence is thin. Evaluation criteria must therefore travel across implementations. For inspection language systems, ask whether cited IDs appear in the evidence set~\cite{ji2023,shuster2021}; whether propagation and historical-record questions improve when prior observations and neighbours are included; whether code and manual RAG beats parametric recall on editioned corpora~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,joffe2025buildingcodes,krutli2025fmrags}; whether outputs can drive operational schedules; and whether agent tool traces respect approval and geofence constraints~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,rawat2025llmmas}. Table~\ref{tab:ch8-eval} states those five criteria as a shared rubric for any implementation.

The rubric should be scored at the level of actions, not only at the level of sentences. A grounded paragraph that never changes a maintenance schedule has limited O\&M value; a terse ranked list of Asset-IDs with openable evidence may already plan a gondola week. Monthly boards can therefore track invented-ID rate, openable-evidence rate, modality-conflict disclosure rate, revisit-hint adoption, and, when a work-system export exists, export no-rewrite rate~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,luo2025llmdtrobot,rawat2025llmmas,liang2023helm}. If a hosted-LLM swap improves fluency but worsens invented IDs, reject the swap. If fluency is unchanged but TRACE completeness improves, accept the harness change. Model-size leaderboards remain secondary to those operability curves.

Figure~\ref{fig:ch8-ablation} gives one compact illustration of the rubric: retrieval packaging and controlled one-hop expansion can improve grounding. Assess that outcome by intent and by the safety of the final action, including whether cite-or-abstain validators catch unsupported IDs~\cite{ji2023,peng2024,lee2024ragsafety}.

Predictive maintenance in this chapter is therefore three deliverables, not one slogan. First, \emph{triage}: a ranked Asset-ID list with openable evidence for the next gondola week. Second, \emph{conditional advice}: statements such as ``risk rises if infrared is missing on the upstairs neighbour'' that name the missing evidence and stop short of inventing it. Third, \emph{external prognosis}: when a non-LLM temporal model exists, the harness may present its read-only output beside cited observations; the language system must not invent year-ahead spalling probabilities from a single campaign~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,zhang2025llmagentschema,zhao2026physical}.

Schema reuse is not forecasting skill, and a platform diagram is not a second detector~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,zhang2025llmagentschema,zhao2026physical}.

Whether a reader uses a phone, tablet, or desktop interface, the same test applies: named Asset-IDs must remain consistent and evidence must remain openable. An interface that invents a panel code or presents an unlinked floor count fails the rubric in Table~\ref{tab:ch8-eval}.

Adjacent 2024--2026 twin-interaction and BIM-language systems supply the same control lesson without a second literature tour: keep structure outside the weights, compose with the LLM, refuse when the structured path is missing, and treat governance as a design variable~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot,hellin2025bimmas,krutli2025fmrags,ma2026tenquestions}. User-experience gains count only while the five criteria in Table~\ref{tab:ch8-eval} remain green.

One end-to-end walkthrough is enough to bind those criteria to practice. Consider a two-day warehouse fa\c{c}ade campaign that Chapters~2--7 have already turned into a decision-ready twin. Day~1 yields RGB coverage; Day~2 adds infrared over suspected moisture zones; perception writes modality-tagged observations; registration and GeoBIM keep host Asset-IDs aligned with the facility register. On Day~3 the manager asks four meeting questions. Local lookup loads only the passport for \texttt{W-3-17} and returns openable observations. Cross-campaign comparison abstains if prior observation IDs are missing. Propagation expands upstairs and lateral neighbours, keeps RGB/IR tags, and either drafts a cited path hypothesis or refuses. Elevation audit applies a Chapter~7 spatial filter and emits a schema-constrained gondola list. Only when the manager requests action does the bounded agent draft tickets, attach evidence IDs, set a human-review flag on modality conflicts, and emit revisit hints for Chapters~2--3. It does not email contractors, rewrite BIM geometry, or clear conflict flags. Approved facts write back to twin memory; rejected drafts enter procedural memory and are not resubmitted in the same session. That single story is the chapter spine under load: twin contract, memory ladder, asset-centric assembly, multimodal disclosure, gated tools, report lifecycle, and operability metrics.

Deployment should follow the same spine. Begin by freezing Asset-IDs and neighbour edges, admitting only valid observation cards, and limiting use to local and health questions until topology is checked. Then enable one-hop expansion with a pinned manual edition, dry-run the five-tool surface with writes blocked, and introduce frozen-snapshot scoring and CMMS mapping before any hosted-LLM substitution~\cite{zhang2025,zhao2026physical,peng2024,lee2024ragsafety,gao2026ifcagent,liang2023helm,ma2026tenquestions,buga2025llmfmsql}. Graph-twin and multi-agent studies may inform role splits inside that programme, but they do not remove the need for identity hygiene~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,rawat2025llmmas,lu2026highwaymas}.

Triage language sits between diagnosis and schedule. An illustrative scheduler may score assets by $s = 0.4\,\tilde{\ell} + 0.3\,c + 0.2\,a + 0.1\,m$, where $\tilde{\ell}$ is geometry-normalised defect size, $c$ detector confidence, $a$ access urgency, and $m$ a modality-conflict penalty. Coefficients belong to the facility, not to the LLM; the language system exposes the cited terms behind each score and lets humans reorder; see Table~\ref{tab:ch8-triage}. Surface cracks require geometry-normalised lengths without uncited structural stories, moisture cues require multimodal disclosure, and prior repair patches require cross-campaign IDs before a system may claim deterioration~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical,jeon2025hybridrag}. A minimal constrained draft remains the machine-readable carrier of that triage:

\begin{verbatim}
{
  "asset_ids": ["W-3-17", "W-4-17"],
  "defect_ids": ["obs-2403-118", "obs-2403-204"],
  "diagnosis": {
    "type": "possible_moisture_path",
    "severity": "medium",
    "hypothesis": "upstairs staining with mid-storey hollow"
  },
  "propagation": ["W-4-17", "W-3-17"],
  "action": {"repair": "local_probe", "priority": 2},
  "trust": {"confidence": 0.62, "human_review": true,
            "notes": "RGB/IR disagreement on W-3-17"}
}
\end{verbatim}

Every identity in such a draft must be subset-checked against retrieval; the human paragraph is rendered from the fields, not scraped afterward~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes}.

Common failure modes collapse into the same contracts. Invented Asset-IDs appear when validators are missing; false moisture paths appear when embedding neighbourhood replaces BIM adjacency; confirmed seepage appears when infrared-only cards lose uncertainty flags; silent twin mutation appears when report drafting and twin write share one unguarded button; edition averaging appears when manuals are unpinned~\cite{ji2023,peng2024,lee2024ragsafety,luo2025llmdtrobot}. Adding more agent personas does not repair those failures. Shared twin memory, typed tools, and gates do~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,rawat2025llmmas,gao2026ifcagent}. Procurement and classroom assessment should therefore demand the same artefacts: Asset-ID dictionary, neighbour-edge provenance, observation schema, edition pins, intent--assembly policy, gated tool surface, CMMS field map, gold questions with abstentions, and paired TRACE files. Figures~\ref{fig:ch8-memory-ladder}--\ref{fig:ch8-agent-gate} and Tables~\ref{tab:ch8-harness} and~\ref{tab:ch8-triage} are load-bearing teaching objects for that demand~\cite{yao2023react,hellin2025bimmas,buga2025llmfmsql}.

Looking toward Chapter~9, the open problems are mostly socio-technical: incomplete topology on older buildings, contractor identifier collisions, scarce agent benchmarks for safe inspection tool use, and governance when a grounded-looking answer is wrong~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,ma2026tenquestions}. Larger models alone will not close those gaps. The constructive response taught here remains continuous with Sections~\ref{sec:ch8-dt}--\ref{sec:ch8-reports}: make the twin decision-ready, retrieve over assets, fuse modalities without erasing conflict, bound the agent, and evaluate what facility teams can schedule.

In summary, Chapter~8 is the monograph's fourth phase: next-generation decision support. It consumes Chapters~4--7, teaches ready-to-use LLM techniques and a model harness with an inspection-centred memory ladder, positions VLMs as optional crop readers under the same citation gates, grounds answers through asset-centric retrieval and bounded agents, and delivers historical-record queries, auditable reports, and predictive maintenance triage that facility teams can schedule. Drawing on published 2024--2026 AEC practice while specialising it to fa\c{c}ade O\&M, the measure of success is not rhetorical fluency, but named assets, cited evidence, and actions that remain under professional control.
