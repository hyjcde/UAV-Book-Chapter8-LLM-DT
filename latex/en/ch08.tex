\chapter{Digital Twin with LLMs in Inspection Analysis}
\label{ch:llm-dt}

\abstract*{After UAV planning, reconstruction, defect perception, and GIS anchoring, building inspection still needs a decision layer that can answer natural-language questions, cite twin evidence, and support maintenance actions. This chapter introduces ready-to-use LLM techniques for civil engineering, prompting, retrieval, structured outputs, tools, memory, and bounded agents, maps them to fa\c{c}ade inspection, and shows how they attach to a language-ready digital twin without assuming a site-specific training pipeline.}

\abstract{Chapters~2--7 of this monograph explain how to fly, reconstruct, detect, and map. Chapter~8 asks what an inspector or facility manager can do next with that twin: query it in ordinary language, fuse modality-tagged evidence already produced by perception, obtain answers that name real assets, and move toward reports and schedules. The chapter first clarifies what a language-ready twin must expose; then develops three layers of LLM technique for inspection (language interface, evidence grounding, and memory with bounded procedures), using off-the-shelf models. Site-specific training lies outside the scope of this chapter. Twin records, multimodal evidence, reports, and decision metrics then close the loop. Perception remains the source of typed defect findings; the LLM reads structured twin memory under citation rules. The primary readers are graduate students and researchers building inspection decision layers, facility engineers who must audit AI-assisted reports, and implementers who need harness contracts and auditable workflows.}


\section{Digital Twins for Building Inspection}
\label{sec:ch8-dt}

The monograph is one pipeline. Task and motion planning (Chapters~2--3) produce safe coverage. Reconstruction (Chapter~4) turns imagery into geometry. Datasets and perception models (Chapters~5--6) name defects. GIS and GeoBIM (Chapter~7) give assets addresses in the city. None of those products, alone, answers the questions that dominate fa\c{c}ade operations and maintenance (O\&M): which panels participate in a multi-storey moisture path, which assets should be staged for gondola access this quarter, or how to draft a repair brief that an engineer can audit. Those are decision-layer questions. They require connecting twin entities to language, retrieval, memory, and, when actions are proposed, tool-using agents.

Digital twins in construction and civil infrastructure are best understood as evolving information systems. Photorealistic viewers alone do not meet the same information requirements~\cite{boje2020,opoku2021,lu2020}. Survey work on construction twins emphasises lifecycle information flows, stakeholder coordination, and the gap between visualisation and actionable semantics. Building and heritage documentation literature likewise shows that geometry without provenance quickly becomes ornamental~\cite{bruno2018,tsilimantou2020}. For UAV fa\c{c}ade programmes the same lesson appears in sharper form: a mesh that cannot answer ``which maintainable host owns this detection?'' cannot support language-level O\&M.

A geometric twin answers location. A language-ready twin for inspection must also expose three further commitments. First, a stable \emph{asset identifier} for each maintainable host: the code that facility teams already use in work orders and registers. This chapter writes that code as Asset-ID after the first mention, so that language answers can name the same host that a CMMS ticket would name. Ad-hoc filenames invented at capture time are not Asset-IDs. Second, provenance for every observation: sensor modality (for example RGB or infrared), capture time, detector or annotator signature, and a pointer back to imagery that an engineer can open. Third, relations that support propagation reasoning: which maintainable hosts sit above, below, or beside one another on a fa\c{c}ade stack. Without those commitments, an LLM can only chat over loose files: fluent, but not auditable for facility management.

UAV--BIM inspection reviews sketched early automation agendas that already pointed toward this decision layer~\cite{zhang2022}. Published GeoBIM-coupled twin modelling~\cite{zhang2025} and detection--reconstruction--registration pipelines~\cite{yang2025detreconreg} supply the geometric and identifier premise used below; related twin deployments extend to site monitoring beyond fa\c{c}ades~\cite{han2025}. Facility-oriented digital-twin discussions further stress that O\&M value appears only when twins connect to work processes and document stores~\cite{chen2023facility,wu2022}. Readers need not re-derive reconstruction or GIS methods here. Chapters~4--7 should be treated as an \emph{interface contract}: if the twin cannot tell a language system which Asset-ID hosts a detection, when the image was captured, and which neighbours sit next to that host, then language interfaces will invent structure.

Consider a concrete O\&M scene. After a warehouse fa\c{c}ade campaign, a manager asks whether mid-storey delamination on the west elevation is linked to moisture staining above. A mesh viewer can show both regions. A Chapter~6 detector can label both patches. Only a language-ready twin can assemble neighbour passports, cite observation IDs with openable images, and either draft a repair brief or abstain when evidence is thin. That scene is the running example for the rest of the chapter: the twin is the long-term store; the LLM is a reader and proposer under rules, not a second source of geometry. Later worked examples use short host codes such as \texttt{W-3-17} and \texttt{W-4-17} only as illustrative Asset-IDs on a west elevation (elevation letter, storey, bay); live programmes should substitute their own facility register codes.

The same scene also clarifies what this chapter does \emph{not} do. It does not replace Chapters~5--6 perception models with a second detector stack inside the language system. Typed defect labels, confidences, and host attachments remain perception products. The decision layer consumes those products as structured records, retrieves related twin context, and generates language that can be audited. Readers who need detector design should return to Chapter~6; readers who need flight geometry should return to Chapters~2--4. What follows is how language systems should behave once those upstream products exist.


Beyond the warehouse vignette, language-ready twins must survive organisational reality. Inspection programmes rarely have a single owner: UAV contractors capture imagery, consultants interpret defects, facility teams issue work orders, and asset managers reconcile budgets. A twin that is only a visualisation shared by email cannot mediate those roles. Identifiers must be stable across organisations; observation provenance must survive file hand-offs; neighbour relations must be computable without calling the person who built the mesh. Those are information-system requirements, not rendering requirements~\cite{boje2020,opoku2021,lu2020,chen2023facility}.

It is also useful to distinguish three twin maturity levels that readers will meet in practice. A \emph{geometric twin} stores meshes, point clouds, or BIM shells. A \emph{semantic twin} attaches typed entities and Asset-IDs. A \emph{decision-ready twin} additionally exposes observation cards, topology for propagation, and interfaces that retrieval and tools can call. This chapter assumes the second level as a minimum and teaches the third. Many disappointing ``AI for inspection'' demos fail because they bolt an LLM onto the first level and then blame the model for inventing panels.

Stakeholder questions further clarify the interface contract. An inspector asks what changed since the last campaign. A facility manager asks which assets block a gondola day. A compliance officer asks which clause and which photo support a repair recommendation. A planner asks where to fly next. Only the last question returns partly to Chapters~2--3; the first three are decision-layer questions that need language over structured twin memory. If the twin cannot answer them with citations, no amount of prompt engineering will create auditability after the fact.



Historic and contemporary inspection practice already encodes many of the disciplines this chapter formalises for machines. Paper checklists force inspectors to name locations; photo logs force provenance; repair tickets force action fields. What digital twins and LLMs add is scale and queryability across campaigns, does not authorise teams to abandon those disciplines. On the contrary, automation without checklist discipline amplifies error. Readers from facilities backgrounds should therefore recognise familiar quality controls in the Asset-ID, provenance, and cite-or-abstain rules below; only the carrier changes from clipboard to twin memory~\cite{zhang2022,chen2023facility,wu2022}.

Geographic scale introduces further constraints. A single warehouse elevation is already multimodal and multi-storey; a campus or housing estate multiplies identifier collisions and manual-edition conflicts. Chapter~7's GIS integration is the right place to keep city-scale identity coherent. Chapter~8 assumes that coherence and refuses to ``fix'' identity drift with clever language. If two contractors use different panel codes for the same host, the language system should surface the collision, not pick a winner in prose~\cite{zhang2025,han2025}. The same identity lesson appears when recent multi-agent twin systems scale from room graphs to city graphs: language access helps only if the underlying identifiers and edges remain trustworthy~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,lu2026highwaymas,ma2026tenquestions}.

Seasoned inspectors already practise a form of grounding: they photograph before writing, they name bays before diagnosing, and they refuse to sign a report that cannot be walked on site. The decision-ready twin simply makes those habits machine-checkable across campaigns. Table~\ref{tab:ch8-twin-contract} is therefore not an IT wishlist; it is the digital continuation of field craft~\cite{zhang2022,chen2023facility}. When organisations skip the contract and buy a chat interface first, they usually rediscover the same craft six months later, after the first invented Asset-ID reaches a work order. Procurement language often asks for ``AI dashboards.'' A more precise ask is whether the system can return openable evidence for every named asset, abstain when evidence is thin, expand neighbours from BIM adjacency, not from text similarity, and gate writes. Vendors that answer only with model-size slides are selling parametric memory; inspection buyers need twin interfaces~\cite{boje2020,opoku2021,wu2022}. Chapter~8 gives readers vocabulary to insist on those interfaces before licence fees are paid.

Three gaps in neighbouring practice motivate the rest of the chapter. BIM assistants and NL model queries improve navigation, but navigating an object is not topology-aware defect diagnosis across fa\c{c}ade hosts~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,hellin2025bimmas,liu2025bimcoder}. Document-similarity RAG improves clause lookup, but embedding neighbourhood is not BIM adjacency for moisture-path questions~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,jeon2025hybridrag,peng2024}. Multi-agent demos can look autonomous, yet fluent tickets without cite-or-abstain and write gates are not auditable O\&M artefacts~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,ma2026tenquestions}. Sections~\ref{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}--\ref{sec:ch8-dss} therefore specialise ready-to-use LLM layers and a model harness to close exactly those gaps.

A monograph-style reading of structural monitoring books reinforces the same spine without changing the chapter's ready-to-use stance. Those books typically open with background and gaps, state objectives, then present a technical roadmap before methods and cases. Chapter~8 follows that order inside one decision layer: twin contract and gaps first, L1--L3 and harness second, assembly and multimodal agents third, reports and operability metrics last. Site-specific fine-tuning is outside the agenda of this chapter. Campaign facts belong in editioned twin memory and RAG indexes, not in weights that will be stale after the next flight~\cite{lewis2020,krutli2025fmrags,ma2026tenquestions,saka2024gptconstruction}.

Teaching objectives for this chapter can be stated as four checkable outcomes. After reading, a student or implementer should be able to (i)~list the minimum twin commitments in Table~\ref{tab:ch8-twin-contract} and explain one failure mode for each missing commitment; (ii)~map a fa\c{c}ade question to Layers~L1--L3 and to one harness part in Table~\ref{tab:ch8-harness}; (iii)~assemble a cite-or-abstain package for a propagation question using passports and BIM neighbours; and (iv)~show a TRACE in which a write or revisit request stops at a human gate~\cite{peng2024,yao2023react,liang2023helm,hellin2025bimmas}. Those outcomes are the chapter's research-framework analogue: not a new detector, but a decision-layer contract that earlier chapters can satisfy and later chapters can harden.

\section{Foundations of LLMs for Civil Engineering Applications}
\label{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}

Contemporary LLMs rest on the Transformer architecture~\cite{vaswani2017attention}. Self-attention models long-range dependencies in text, which matters when an inspection question mixes symptoms, locations, and code clauses in one utterance. Ready-to-use models already follow natural-language instructions and can draft fluent building prose~\cite{brown2020,ouyang2022instructgpt}. Chain-of-thought prompting can elicit intermediate steps that help multi-hop questions~\cite{wei2022cot}, but it does not guarantee that a named fa\c{c}ade panel actually exists in the twin. Reviews of GPT-style tools in construction make the same caution for AEC practice: capability is real, governance and factual grounding are not automatic~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction}.

This chapter therefore takes a deliberate stance. We treat LLMs as \emph{deployable language components}: systems that are prompted, constrained, and wired to twin stores and tools. We do not develop a site-specific training or fine-tuning agenda. Campaign facts change every flight; the honest path for living buildings is to keep those facts outside the model weights, in editioned manuals and Asset-ID twin memory, and to let the model read them at query time~\cite{lewis2020,krutli2025fmrags}. For inspection teaching it also helps to separate three behaviours that are often conflated. \emph{Linguistic competence} is the ability to parse and produce fluent text about buildings. \emph{Parametric recall} is whatever generic knowledge the weights already contain, useful for vocabulary, unreliable for site-specific Asset-IDs. \emph{Grounded reasoning} is the ability to answer using evidence that the system retrieved or was given in the current session. Fa\c{c}ade O\&M lives or dies on the third behaviour. An LLM that can write elegant moisture narratives without citing twin observations is not an inspection assistant; it is a drafting toy.

This section introduces generative language technology for civil engineering and attaches it to inspection analysis. It organises ready-to-use techniques into three layers that compose into one decision stack (Table~\ref{tab:ch8-tech-families}). Perception remains Chapter~6's job; here language technologies consume typed, modality-tagged observations. Layer~L1 is the \emph{language interface}: how an off-the-shelf model is steered with prompts and answer templates. Layer~L2 is \emph{evidence grounding}: how retrieval, structured outputs, and tools keep answers attached to twin records. Layer~L3 is \emph{memory and bounded procedures}: how long-term twin memory and gated agents support multi-step O\&M work without unsupervised action. The rest of this section develops the three layers as continuous narrative and then shows how published AEC systems specialise, or fail to specialise, to fa\c{c}ade inspection.

Layer~L1 begins with what a ready model can already do when an orchestrator supplies a careful prompt. The model can parse mixed spatial-symptom questions such as ``mid-storey hollow with upstairs staining,'' draft readable briefs for different stakeholders, and follow a short answer template that forces named Asset-IDs, separates observations from hypotheses, and emits an abstention code when checks fail~\cite{ouyang2022instructgpt,ji2023}. Stepwise prompting can then verbalise a propagation hypothesis, but only after neighbours have already entered the evidence package; otherwise the model may invent a structural story that merely sounds plausible~\cite{wei2022cot,ji2023}. A practical system prompt for fa\c{c}ade O\&M can therefore be short: classify intent; speak only about Asset-IDs present in the retrieved package; preserve modality tags from Chapter~6; and emit \texttt{ABSTAIN:<reason>} when evidence is thin. Few-shot examples in the prompt should demonstrate abstention, not only success. What L1 must \emph{not} be asked to do is remember last campaign's Asset-IDs from parametric recall, invent crack lengths, or override twin facts when a hurried manager says ``just mark it repaired.'' Those duties belong to grounding and gates, not to fluent wording.

Layer~L2 is where inspection systems leave chat demos. Retrieval-augmented generation loads editioned manuals or observation cards at query time~\cite{lewis2020,shuster2021}; civil deployments already cover codes, safety manuals, facility-management documents, and defect dialogue~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,krutli2025fmrags,jeon2025hybridrag,he2025regulationhybrid}. Adaptive and self-reflective variants retry when retrieval looks weak~\cite{gao2023hyde,asai2024selfrag,jeong2024adaptive}, but the inspection design choice is sharper than ``add RAG'': for asset-centric questions, prefer entity or passport units over arbitrary PDF chunks~\cite{peng2024}. Structured and constrained generation then keeps outputs machine-checkable, report fields, CMMS maps, and validators that require cited Asset-IDs to be subsets of the evidence set~\cite{pan2024b,chen2024bimllmontology,iversen2026,buga2025llmfmsql}. Tools and function calling separate reasoning from world actions: typed calls such as \texttt{get\_passport}, \texttt{expand\_neighbours}, \texttt{list\_observations}, \texttt{draft\_report}, and \texttt{propose\_revisit} let the model request twin facts without inventing them~\cite{schick2023toolformer,yao2023react,guo2025bimllmquery,liu2025bimcoder,li2025b,li2025c}. Twin writes and dispatch remain behind server-side approval~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent}. Long context is does not authorise teams to dump an entire elevation into the prompt. Packing should prefer structured digests, Asset-ID, top symptoms, recent observation IDs, neighbour IDs, short location phrases, with image paths so humans can open evidence. Raw pixel dumps are avoided. If a digest still overflows, drop low-confidence observations first, not the topology links needed for a propagation question~\cite{lewis2020,asai2024selfrag}.

Layer~L3 makes the stack durable across sessions and actionable under control. Before agents, readers need a memory ladder (Table~\ref{tab:ch8-memory}). Parametric memory is whatever generic knowledge the weights already hold, useful for language, almost useless for last week's crack growth on a specific warehouse elevation~\cite{brown2020}. Contextual memory is the prompt window and short dialogue state: powerful, length-limited, and session-fragile; tomorrow's new chat may forget that ``west'' meant the loading-dock face unless an external store kept the alias. Retrieval memory loads editioned manuals and observation cards on demand~\cite{lewis2020,shuster2021,krutli2025fmrags}. Structured long-term twin memory is the Asset-ID store itself, geometry, topology, and time-stamped observations in BIM/GIS, optionally linked to knowledge graphs~\cite{pan2024b,zhang2025}. For fa\c{c}ade O\&M the twin is authoritative; the model should read it through retrieval and tools, not pretend the weights remember the site. Bounded agents then compose planning, memory, and tools into procedures such as tickets or revisits under geofence and human gates~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,rawat2025llmmas,pan2026graphdtgpt,zhang2025llmagentschema}. They are not a licence for unsupervised aircraft command or silent twin mutation. ReAct-style loops without retrieval merely re-enact parametric storytelling with extra steps; tool calls without twin memory can hit the wrong host; memory streams without write gates can accumulate speculative notes as if they were facts~\cite{yao2023react,park2023generativeagents}.

These three layers are meant to compose, not to compete. Consider again: ``Is mid-storey hollow on the west elevation related to upstairs staining, and should we stage a gondola this quarter?'' L1 parses the mixed utterance and forces a template. L2 tools fetch seed passports and expand BIM neighbours; RAG packs those cards plus any pinned manual clauses; structured generation emits a draft whose IDs are subsets of the evidence set. L3 ensures that the long-term twin, not yesterday's chat, is the store being read, and that any ticket or revisit proposal waits behind a human gate. Skip L2 and the answer becomes parametric storytelling; skip structured checks and gates and fluent text leaks into CMMS. That composition is the proposal's ``generative AI for decision support'' made operational for fa\c{c}ade O\&M~\cite{lewis2020,yao2023react,ji2023}.

What holds the three layers together in a real system is not another model brand, but a \emph{model harness}. In software testing, a harness is scaffolding that exercises a component under controlled conditions. In contemporary LLM engineering the same word has come to mean the code-owned control layer around a replaceable language model: it builds prompts, chooses retrieval units, exposes typed tools, validates outputs against contracts, records traces, and decides when to abstain or escalate. Agent surveys already describe planning, memory, and tools as the ingredients of autonomy~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent}; tool-using reason-act loops and self-supervised tool learning show how models request actions without owning the world state~\cite{yao2023react,schick2023toolformer}. Holistic evaluation frameworks such as HELM further remind readers that what is measured (scenarios, metrics, and standardised run conditions) is itself a harness for comparison, not a single accuracy number~\cite{liang2023helm}. For fa\c{c}ade inspection we specialise that idea: the harness is where Asset-ID contracts, edition pins, modality tags, and write gates live so that swapping the underlying LLM does not silently rewrite O\&M policy.

A teaching decomposition of the inspection harness has five cooperating parts (Table~\ref{tab:ch8-harness}). The \emph{intake and routing} part classifies intent and resolves natural-language locations against GeoBIM aliases before any generation. The \emph{evidence assembly} part loads passports, neighbours, observation cards, and pinned manuals, the RAG and packing duties of Layer~L2~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024,krutli2025fmrags}. The \emph{tool surface} part offers a small typed API (\texttt{get\_passport}, \texttt{expand\_neighbours}, \texttt{list\_observations}, \texttt{draft\_report}, \texttt{propose\_revisit}) and refuses free-form SQL or flight commands; AEC tool agents for IFC, energy analysis, and FM robots illustrate the same separation between reasoning and world actions under approval~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,zhang2025llmagentschema,luo2025llmdtrobot,guo2025bimllmquery}. The \emph{contract validators} part checks machine-checkable obligations after drafting: every named Asset-ID must appear in the retrieved package; numeric defect claims must point to observation fields; modality conflicts must remain disclosed; write or dispatch requests must hit a human gate~\cite{ji2023,pan2024b,buga2025llmfmsql}. The \emph{trace and evaluation} part stores which tools ran, which edition was pinned, and which validator passed or failed, so answers can be replayed and graded, an inspection reading of multi-metric, scenario-based evaluation with multi-metric scenarios. Chat fluency alone is insufficient~\cite{liang2023helm,shuster2021}. Adaptive retrieval retries belong inside the harness as controlled loops, not as ad-hoc prompt improvisation~\cite{asai2024selfrag,jeong2024adaptive}.

Two consequences follow for procurement and teaching. First, a prompt-only prototype is not yet a harness: instructions such as ``always cite Asset-IDs'' can be ignored by a fluent model, whereas a validator that blocks delivery when the ID subset check fails is load-bearing. Second, model substitution should be a harness test, not a project rewrite, if validators, tool schemas, and twin interfaces stay fixed, changing the hosted LLM should change phrasing, not which sources are eligible or whether a write gate exists~\cite{liang2023helm,saka2024gptconstruction}. Emerging enterprise discussions of harness engineering emphasise relocating behaviour from prompts into versioned contracts, manifests, and traces; this chapter keeps that intuition while citing only peer-published grounding, tool, agent, and evaluation results. In short: Layers~L1--L3 name the capabilities; the harness is the software that enforces them on every query.

Recent building-oriented LLM syntheses make the same shift from model brand to system design. A 2026 ``ten questions'' agenda for building applications organises open problems around data readiness, trust, interoperability, and human oversight with data readiness and oversight placed ahead of parameter counts~\cite{ma2026tenquestions}. That agenda aligns with this chapter: fa\c{c}ade O\&M succeeds when Asset-IDs, editioned manuals, and approval gates are ready, not when a larger chat model is procured. Multi-agent systems published in 2025--2026 further show how harness ideas appear in AEC practice. Graph-grounded multi-agent twins answer natural-language questions by decomposing work into decision, query, and extraction agents whose outputs remain tied to structured twin graphs~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt}. Human--digital-twin interaction studies replace steep GUI workflows with leader/reasoning/member agent teams that orchestrate twin functions under evaluation with domain experts~\cite{lu2026highwaymas}. Multi-agent virtual in-situ modelling pipelines connect design-to-operation digital twins inside BIM~\cite{lee2025multiagentvbm}; construction-management frameworks combine graph and vector memory for coordinated agents~\cite{rawat2025llmmas}; router-driven expert-selection agents for foundation design show that specialised roles can improve task routing when humans remain responsible for safety-critical checks~\cite{youwai2026foundationmas}. IFC tool agents and FM robot brokers likewise emphasise typed tools and approval~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot,zhang2025llmagentschema}. Multi-agent BIM information retrieval and condensed multi-LLM retrieval agents published in 2025--2026 further show that natural-language access to model objects can be decomposed into specialised roles without abandoning typed interfaces~\cite{hellin2025bimmas,koh2026bimllmretr}; facility-data RAG on BIM O\&M stores shows the same structure-first habit for document-model joining~\cite{quan2026bimfacrag}. None of these systems is a warehouse fa\c{c}ade inspector out of the box. Their transferable lesson for 2024--2026 practice is consistent: put structure and gates outside the weights; keep the LLM as a replaceable composer inside a harness.

Hallucination surveys explain what goes wrong when the discipline is missing: fluent systems invent entities, numbers, and citations~\cite{ji2023}. In inspection the costly failures are concrete, invented Asset-IDs, repair advice without attributable evidence, and summaries that erase disagreement between RGB and infrared observations. Grounding therefore means more than a clever prompt. Constrained schemas, retrieval and its adaptive variants, tool-using reason-act loops, and typed structured knowledge recur across NLP and AEC~\cite{lewis2020,gao2023hyde,asai2024selfrag,jeong2024adaptive,peng2024,yao2023react,schick2023toolformer,pan2024b}. Graph-oriented and hierarchical retrieval further remind readers that document similarity is not building adjacency~\cite{peng2024,sarthi2024raptor,sun2024tog}. Semantic embeddings cluster phrases such as ``damp'', ``stain'', and ``seepage''; building spaces cluster panels that share joints, storeys, and drainage paths. A hybrid retriever may use embeddings to rank symptom digests inside an already topology-constrained candidate set; it must not use embeddings to invent the candidate set for propagation questions~\cite{peng2024,jeon2025hybridrag,khattab2020}. When system rules, retrieved manuals, twin facts, and user requests conflict, precedence should be explicit: safety and write gates first; twin facts and observation fields second; pinned manual editions third; user style preferences last~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction}.

Civil and building applications already exercise these patterns, and each maps onto inspection with a twist. BIM virtual assistants, query--DSL systems, and NL-to-BIM stacks help users navigate models~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,guo2025bimllmquery,liu2025bimcoder}, but navigation is not yet topology-aware defect diagnosis across fa\c{c}ade assets. Building semantic query systems combine RAG and structured stores for building data access~\cite{li2025c,li2025b}. RAG over codes and safety manuals improves factual answers relative to parametric recall alone~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,yang2024promptcode}; hybrid retrieval has been studied for defect-management dialogue~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag}; LLM--ontology and knowledge-graph checkers push automated compliance beyond pure chat~\cite{chen2024bimllmontology,iversen2026,li2024kgllmcheck,nuyts2024comparativeacc,kruiper2024platformnlp}. Energy-analysis agents, IFC tool agents, and graph-twin multi-agent systems show how domain tools can be versioned and how LLMs can broker among users, twins, and robots under human approval~\cite{zhang2025llmagentschema,luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,pan2026graphdtgpt}; structured FM querying further rewards exposing schemas before free-form SQL~\cite{buga2025llmfmsql,arslan2024ragenergy}. Knowledge-enhanced industrial systems for causal diagnosis and cognitive-twin proposals likewise stress machine-readable domain structure that language systems can query. They should not invent that structure~\cite{zhou2024causalkgpt,shi2025cognitivedt}. High-resolution fa\c{c}ade datasets and physical quantification pipelines matter because language inherits the granularity of perception~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2025icra,zhao2026physical}: if perception writes coarse labels, the LLM cannot honestly emit millimetre claims.

Memory failures make the ladder concrete. If a manager clarifies that ``west'' means the loading-dock face and tomorrow's chat forgets it, the repair is to persist the alias or require GeoBIM seed selection before generation, not to hope the model ``remembers.'' If RAG returns an outdated maintenance PDF, the repair is edition pins and corpus smoke tests~\cite{krutli2025fmrags,joffe2025buildingcodes}. If the model invents a crack length from generic web knowledge, the repair is to ban numeric defect claims unless an observation field supplies them~\cite{ji2023,zhao2026physical}. If the twin holds the correct Asset-ID but the orchestrator never fetched it, that is an infrastructure failure, not a wording problem~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024}. Who may write to twin memory after an LLM draft, how long dialogue clarifications persist, and which manual edition wins when two PDFs disagree are therefore foundations questions, not afterthought IT policy. For fa\c{c}ade O\&M we recommend a simple rule: the twin store is write-gated; the dialogue window is ephemeral; RAG indexes are editioned; parametric memory is never treated as a site database~\cite{park2023generativeagents,rawat2025llmmas}.

Selecting an LLM stack for inspection is likewise a harness problem more than a brand problem. Teams should assess the five harness parts above, especially whether validators and tool gates are code-owned, plus native tool-calling reliability, long-context behaviour on passport sets, willingness to abstain under constrained decoding, on-prem versus cloud constraints for building data, and cost per grounded answer ahead of cost per chat token~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,wang2024surveyagents,liang2023helm}. A smaller model inside a strict cite-or-abstain harness often beats a larger model that improvises Asset-IDs in an unvalidated chat loop. Even before laboratory scoring, ten hand-checked answers already separate grounded homework from chat demos: listed Asset-IDs $\subseteq$ retrieved set; hypotheses tagged; modality tags preserved; abstention when neighbours are missing~\cite{ji2023,shuster2021,lewis2020}. The teaching takeaway is therefore three design choices, not a model name: retrieve over maintainable assets and neighbours; force citation of IDs that appear in the retrieved set; and place irreversible actions behind approval gates. Sections~\ref{sec:ch8-dt-to-language}--\ref{sec:ch8-multimodal} next show how twin records and multimodal evidence make Layers~L2 and~L3 operational.



In summary of the foundations, Layers~L1--L3 name what a ready model can do under control, and the harness names the software that enforces those behaviours on every query. Readers who skip validators and tool gates will rediscover parametric storytelling regardless of model size~\cite{ji2023,liang2023helm,ma2026tenquestions}. The next section turns those layers into twin records: observation cards, asset passports, and intent-conditioned assembly.

\section{From Digital Twins to Language Reasoning}
\label{sec:ch8-dt-to-language}

Language reasoning over an inspection twin is not free-form storytelling. It is assembly of evidence that earlier chapters already created, the operational substrate for Layers~L2 and~L3 in Section~\ref{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}. We keep three layers conceptually separate even when implementations differ. An \emph{observation record} is one defect sighting with sensor provenance (imagery, evidence, semantics, and host attachment, the teaching content often packaged as an integrated defect profile). An \emph{asset passport} aggregates observations and geometry or topology for a maintainable host (an integrated asset profile for retrieval). \emph{Query assembly} decides which passports and neighbours enter the context before generation; neighbour expansion along fa\c{c}ade adjacency supports vertical propagation reading. Perception writes observations; facility managers think in assets; language models must not invent either~\cite{pan2024b,zheng2023bimassistant}.

In UAV inspection practice, useful observation records store host Asset-ID, time, modality (RGB, infrared, and related channels), detector signature, pose or surface coordinates, and paths to imagery. Registration should attach detections to BIM hosts before any LLM is involved~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg}. Natural-language location phrases help models (``west elevation, third bay above the loading dock''), while metric coordinates remain for field navigation. Asset passports then add symptom summaries, geometry for severity normalisation, and neighbour links (above, below, lateral). Those links enable propagation-style reading, for example, mid-storey delamination considered with upstairs moisture, without confusing a document-similarity graph with a structural or BIM adjacency graph~\cite{peng2024}.

A minimal observation schema for language use includes observation ID, host Asset-ID, campaign or capture time, modality tag, defect type and confidence from Chapter~6, optional severity features derived from geometry, image or crop URI, and a short human-readable location phrase. Optional fields include weather flags, inspector notes, and links to prior observations on the same host. What must not be left as free text alone is the host ID: if the only link from image to asset is a filename convention, retrieval will eventually invent neighbours. Enterprise RAG often chunks PDFs, but inspection questions are usually about maintainable hosts and their neighbourhoods. Passports therefore package, for each Asset-ID, a compact symptom digest, recent observation IDs, geometry summaries used for severity normalisation, and explicit neighbour lists. Severity normalisation matters because a 20\,cm crack on a small panel is not the same operational object as a 20\,cm crack on a large wall bay; language systems should inherit that normalisation from twin geometry . The language layer inherits that normalisation from twin geometry~\cite{zhao2026physical}. Neighbour lists should come from BIM or GeoBIM adjacency, not from embedding similarity among report sentences.

Query assembly is where inspection differs from generic enterprise RAG. A local lookup (``what defects were logged on asset~$A$ last month?'') needs the passport of~$A$ and little else. A propagation question needs neighbours along fa\c{c}ade topology, not merely the top-$k$ text chunks that happen to share vocabulary with the query. A floor- or elevation-level audit needs a controlled set of passports under a spatial filter from Chapter~7. Intent should therefore change \emph{which} twin records enter the context, not only the prompt wording~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024}. Editioned manuals and safety clauses may be retrieved in parallel when the question mixes site evidence with code language~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,krutli2025fmrags}.

One recurring implementation pattern, illustrated in laboratory prototypes and therefore not cited here as a peer-reviewed source, organises observations into asset passports and expands neighbours along BIM adjacency for multi-hop diagnosis. Published siblings share the grounding intuition on other corpora: hybrid defect RAG~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag}, code and safety RAG~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety}, facility-document RAG~\cite{krutli2025fmrags}, and BIM assistants~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant}. The monograph lesson is the pattern, not a brand name: inspection questions are asset-centric, so retrieval units should be asset-centric. When the assembly cannot support a claim, the correct behaviour is abstention or escalation to a human inspector, not a confident paragraph.

Worked micro-example. Suppose the manager asks: ``Is water ingress on the west stack likely propagating from above?'' Assembly should load the target passport, upstairs and lateral neighbours, observation cards tagged RGB and infrared, and any editioned moisture-related maintenance note linked to those Asset-IDs. Generation may then hypothesise a path \emph{only} if neighbour observations exist; otherwise it must say that propagation cannot be supported from the twin. That discipline is language reasoning over a twin, not storytelling beside a twin.


Implementation teams often ask how large a passport should be. Too small, and propagation reading starves. Too large, and the context window fills with irrelevant neighbours from an entire elevation. A practical teaching heuristic is intent-conditioned expansion: local lookup uses zero hops; propagation uses one hop (or a small $k$) along BIM adjacency; elevation audits use spatial filters from Chapter~7 . Unbounded hops are avoided~\cite{peng2024,zhang2025}. Manual clauses are retrieved only when the question mentions compliance, safety, or repair standards~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety}.

Indexing choices should follow the same heuristic. Vector indexes over passport digests support fuzzy symptom search (``hollow sounding panels with staining above''). Graph or relational indexes over Asset-IDs support exact neighbour expansion. Keyword indexes over observation IDs support audit lookups. Hybrid retrieval is attractive precisely because inspection questions mix fuzzy language with exact identifiers~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag,krutli2025fmrags}. What should not be hybridised away is the authority of topology: embedding similarity must not silently replace BIM adjacency when the question is about propagation.

Abstention deserves equal teaching time. Engineers already know how to say ``insufficient evidence'' on site; language systems must learn the same habit. Cite-or-abstain rules can be checked automatically: every Asset-ID in the answer string must appear in the retrieved set; every severity claim must point to an observation ID; every propagation path must be a sequence of neighbour links that were expanded. If checks fail, the system returns a structured refusal plus the missing evidence type (no upstairs neighbour, conflicting modalities, stale campaign, and so on). That behaviour is more valuable than a fluent paragraph that cannot be opened in the twin UI~\cite{ji2023,shuster2021}.

A second worked micro-example clarifies elevation audits. The manager asks: ``Summarise west-elevation issues above level three that need gondola access.'' Assembly should apply a Chapter~7 spatial filter, load the resulting passport set, keep modality tags, and rank by severity features inherited from geometry. It should not narrate the whole building, and it should not drop Asset-IDs in favour of storey prose alone. The output is a decision artefact for scheduling, not a travelogue.



Managers often ask whether a stain is ``new.'' Cross-campaign comparison is a first-class intent: it requires the same Asset-ID across time, comparable modality coverage, and explicit handling of missing prior campaigns. If the previous campaign lacks infrared, the system must not invent a thermal delta. If geometry was remeshed and Asset-IDs were remapped carelessly, language will compare the wrong hosts. Longitudinal discipline is therefore twin hygiene before it is model cleverness~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg,zhao2026physical}. Perception may emit confidences; geometry may support length or area features; O\&M may need ordinal severity for scheduling. The language layer should not convert ordinal triage into false millimetre certainty. Prefer phrases tied to fields (``severity=medium from geometry-normalised crack length; confidence=0.71 from detector'') over cinematic adjectives. Physical quantification pipelines show why numbers need provenance~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}; the LLM's job is to carry that provenance into sentences, not to decorate it.

The following teaching pseudocode summarises assembly. It is deliberately dull: every branch is a place students try to be clever, and where inspection systems usually break~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024,jeon2025hybridrag}.
\begin{verbatim}
function answer(q):
  intent <- classify(q)
  seeds  <- resolve_assets(q)          # else ABSTAIN:asset_unresolved
  packs  <- [passport(s) for s in seeds]
  if intent in {propagation}:
      packs <- packs + expand(seeds,k=1)
  if intent in {elevation_audit}:
      packs <- spatial_filter(packs,q)
  obs <- list_obs(packs)
  manuals <- retrieve_manuals(q) if needs_code(q) else []
  package <- {intent,packs,obs,manuals,edition_pin}
  draft <- generate(package)           # cite-or-abstain
  validate_ids(draft, package)         # else ABSTAIN:id_not_in_package
  return draft
\end{verbatim}
Natural-language locations remain ambiguous: ``third bay'' may mean different hosts after remeshing. Resolution against GeoBIM aliases must precede passport fetch. Fuzzy string match can propose candidates; it must not silently pick one when two candidates score closely. Disambiguation questions are successful system behaviour, not UX failure~\cite{zhang2025,han2025,guo2025bimllmquery}. The same discipline appears in recent twin-query systems that refuse to answer from unconstrained text when the structured store lacks a path~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,buga2025llmfmsql}: for inspection, ``no upstairs neighbour in the twin'' is a better product behaviour than a fluent moisture story.

Published 2024--2025 retrieval practice also clarifies what not to reinvent. Hybrid defect-management RAG and safety-manual RAG show gains when corpora are editioned and noisy maintenance language is expected~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag,lee2024ragsafety}; code and regulation hybrids show that clause retrieval must still meet site evidence before advice becomes operational~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,he2025regulationhybrid,yang2024promptcode}. Facility-document RAG and structured FM querying show that building corpora scale only when schemas and Asset-ID links exist~\cite{krutli2025fmrags,buga2025llmfmsql,arslan2024ragenergy}. Translating those results into UAV fa\c{c}ade O\&M means treating observation cards and asset passports as the primary retrieval units, manuals as secondary pinned context, and topology expansion as a first-class assembly step as a first-class assembly step~\cite{peng2024,pan2024b,ma2026tenquestions}.




Assembly discipline can be restated as teaching narrative that facilities and software teams share. Local lookups should load only the target passport and recent observations; stuffing an entire elevation into context makes cite-or-abstain hard to explain~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024}. Propagation questions require topology edges before symptom similarity; if edges are missing, abstain with \texttt{no\_neighbour} . Embedding a stain from the opposite elevation is refused~\cite{peng2024,jeon2025hybridrag,pan2026graphdtgpt}. Elevation audits should follow Chapter~7 spatial filters and return schedulable Asset-ID lists, not travelogues~\cite{zhang2025,buga2025llmfmsql}. Code-plus-site questions must pin manual editions and site passports together; clause-only or evidence-only packages should disclose the gap~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,he2025regulationhybrid,quan2026bimfacrag}. Cross-campaign comparisons put identity hygiene first: if Asset-IDs were remapped carelessly, abstain on ``worse than last time''~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg,zhao2026physical}. BIM NL multi-agent retrieval and condensed retrieval agents remind readers that clever navigation is not yet topology-aware diagnosis~\cite{hellin2025bimmas,koh2026bimllmretr,liu2025bimcoder}; facility RAG reminds them that document lakes without Asset-ID links still struggle to enter CMMS~\cite{krutli2025fmrags,quan2026bimfacrag}.



In summary of twin-to-language assembly, intent decides which passports and neighbours enter the package; cite-or-abstain decides whether a draft may leave the harness; GeoBIM resolution decides which host is even eligible to be named~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024,zhang2025}. The next section adds modality tags and bounded agents so that RGB/IR conflict and ticket proposals remain under the same contracts.

\section{Multi-Modal Fusion}
\label{sec:ch8-multimodal}

Fa\c{c}ade programmes are multimodal by construction: campaigns routinely produce RGB imagery, infrared channels, and textual metadata. In this chapter, \emph{multi-modal fusion} means disciplined assembly of those modality-tagged observation records, already written by Chapter~6 perception and attached by Chapters~4 and~7, inside grounded LLM retrieval and generation. Defect finding remains a Chapter~6 perception responsibility; language remains responsible for retrieval, explanation, abstention, and action proposals under citation rules.

Fusing modalities without erasing conflict. Textbook query types for fa\c{c}ade O\&M include local lookup, asset-health summary, propagation or root-cause reading, and floor- or elevation-level audit. A minimal assembly is the passport of asset~$A$, its neighbours, and optional editioned manuals. Every Asset-ID or defect ID named in the answer must appear in that retrieved set; otherwise the system abstains or escalates. When RGB and infrared observations disagree, disclosure beats silent averaging: RGB-only evidence should not become a subsurface claim; infrared-only evidence should keep uncertainty flags. High-resolution fa\c{c}ade sensing and physical quantification work show why those flags matter: language that over-claims geometry inherits perception error~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}. A practical fusion rule for teaching is therefore record-level, not pixel-level: keep modality tags on observations; let the LLM narrate agreement or conflict; never collapse conflict into a single confident defect type without a human check flag.

Agents enter when inspection work requires multi-step procedures. Typical procedures include opening a ticket, proposing a UAV revisit polygon for Chapters~2--3, requesting a robot photo, or staging a twin update after approval~\cite{yao2023react,schick2023toolformer,wang2024surveyagents,park2023generativeagents}. Memory is part of agency, not an afterthought. Short-term dialogue state tracks the current question; retrieval memory refreshes manuals and observation cards; twin memory supplies Asset-IDs and topology; procedural memory records which tools and approvals already ran~\cite{rawat2025llmmas,gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot}. Surveys of LLM agents and multi-agent systems emphasise the same ingredients (planning, memory, and tools) while warning that autonomy without constraints is unsafe~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent}. The 2024--2026 AEC literature makes that warning concrete. Graph-DT multi-agent systems ground answers in twin graphs~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt}; human-DT MAS prototypes coordinate leader, reasoning, and member agents so infrastructure experts can complete tasks with lower cognitive load than steep GUIs~\cite{lu2026highwaymas}; multi-agent virtual modelling connects design-to-operation twins inside BIM~\cite{lee2025multiagentvbm}; IFC tool agents, FM robot brokers, and schema-guided energy agents illustrate typed tools under approval~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot,zhang2025llmagentschema}; router-style expert selection in foundation design shows specialised roles can help when humans keep safety oversight~\cite{youwai2026foundationmas}. Tool-augmented reasoning is therefore an expected ingredient of inspection language systems, not an optional plugin~\cite{yao2023react,schick2023toolformer}.

For fa\c{c}ade inspection we recommend \emph{bounded agency} realised inside the model harness of Section~\ref{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}. Multi-step retrieval and tool calls are welcome. Final claims stay citation-locked to the retrieved evidence set. Dispatch and twin writes stay behind geofence and human gates. A small typed tool surface, get passport, expand neighbours, list observations, draft report, propose revisit, beats free-form SQL or unrestricted code execution. That stance specialises general agent literature to the inspection setting: the twin already encodes the world state that an agent must not rewrite casually; Chapters~2--3 already own flight planning; the language agent proposes, it does not commandeer the aircraft. Recent twin-interaction MAS studies reinforce the same division of labour: agents orchestrate functions and reduce interface friction, but they do not replace domain approval or chapter ownership~\cite{lu2026highwaymas,pan2026graphdtgpt,ma2026tenquestions}. The harness, not the chat transcript, is what makes those bounds enforceable on every run~\cite{yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents,liang2023helm}.

Worked micro-example. The manager asks for a revisit where infrared suggests moisture but RGB is inconclusive. A bounded agent may call \texttt{list\_observations}, notice the modality conflict, draft a report with \texttt{human\_check=true}, and emit a revisit polygon hint. It must not invent a flight trajectory in prose, and it must not write a new defect type into the twin without approval. That is multimodal fusion in the sense of this book: modality-aware records, grounded language, and tools that respect upstream chapter ownership.


Multi-modal fusion also interacts with campaign design. If infrared and RGB are captured on different days, time stamps must remain visible in observation cards; otherwise the LLM may treat them as simultaneous evidence. If one modality has systematically lower detector confidence, trust flags should inherit that asymmetry and must not average it away~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}. Fusion at language time is therefore inseparable from perception metadata quality. Garbage in still yields fluent garbage out, only now with citations that look official.

Agent design should stay boring on purpose. Spectacular autonomous stacks that rewrite BIM, dispatch aircraft, and email contractors in one loop are poor teaching targets for inspection safety. Bounded tools with explicit gates match how responsible O\&M already works: analysts propose; seniors approve; field teams execute~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,zhang2025llmagentschema}. Multi-agent role splits (retriever, topology expander, report drafter, action proposer) can be useful pedagogically, provided they share one twin memory and one approval policy~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,rawat2025llmmas}. Competing agents that invent different Asset-IDs for the same panel are not ``diversity''; they are a failure mode.

Procedural memory deserves a concrete inspection reading. When an agent has already expanded neighbours and listed observations, it should not re-expand endlessly in a loop. When a human rejected a twin write, that rejection should be remembered for the session so the agent does not resubmit the same mutation. When a revisit was proposed, the planning hint should carry the observation IDs that justified it, so Chapters~2--3 receive evidence with observation IDs~\cite{yao2023react,park2023generativeagents}. Memory, in this sense, is operational discipline.



When demonstrating agents, show the tool trace beside the answer: which passports were fetched, which neighbours expanded, which manual edition pinned, which gate blocked a write. Traces make bounded agency inspectable and turn the harness into a teaching object. Surveys of LLM agents emphasise planning and tools; inspection teaching should emphasise the audit trail those tools leave~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,yao2023react,schick2023toolformer,liang2023helm}. A beautiful answer without a trace is a demo; a plain answer with a trace is an O\&M artefact. Splitting retriever, topology, drafter, and action roles can clarify software modules, as graph-twin and human-DT MAS designs also do~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,lu2026highwaymas}, but must not create competing Asset-ID realities. All roles read the same twin memory; only the gated writer may stage updates; only humans approve. Construction multi-agent frameworks that combine graph and vector memory are useful precedents for shared memory, not for unsupervised committee decisions about defects~\cite{rawat2025llmmas,gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot}.

A compact walkthrough makes the harness concrete. Parse intent=propagation for a moisture-path question on W-3-17; call \texttt{get\_passport} and \texttt{expand\_neighbours}; list modality-tagged observations; abstain if upstairs evidence is missing; draft under cite-or-abstain; if the manager requests action, draft a report with \texttt{human\_check} on RGB/IR conflict and \texttt{propose\_revisit} only for conflicted assets; wait for approval in procedural memory; stage a twin write only on approve. Logging every step is the product; hiding steps to look ``smart'' is the anti-pattern~\cite{yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents,luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,lu2026highwaymas}.
\begin{verbatim}

tools: get_passport -> expand_neighbours -> list_observations
package_assets: [W-3-17, W-4-17, W-3-16]

\end{verbatim}
\texttt{propose\_revisit} must emit planning hints, not executable UAV commands. Coordinates should be coarse polygons or asset sets that Chapters~2--3 refine under their own safety stack.Robot photo requests, when available, should be tickets. Direct motor commands are refused~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot}. Once the user asks for tickets or revisits, the orchestrator can enter an explicit state machine DRAFT $\rightarrow$ REVIEW $\rightarrow$ APPROVED/REJECTED $\rightarrow$ SCHEDULED; the LLM fills fields inside a state and must not skip REVIEW because the user is in a hurry~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,zhang2025llmagentschema,rawat2025llmmas,lu2026highwaymas}. Agent tests can stay offline: stub tools return fixtures; assert that forbidden writes never occur; assert revisit hints always carry observation IDs; assert rejected drafts never reappear without new evidence. Those tests are cheaper than flight campaigns and catch the worst failures early~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,ma2026tenquestions}.

Operators can tape this table beside the console. It turns Section~\ref{sec:ch8-multimodal} into muscle memory without a second perception stack~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}.

When not to call an agent. If the user only asks what is logged on an asset, tools \texttt{get\_passport} and \texttt{list\_observations} suffice; no ticket machinery. Agents earn their complexity only for procedures. Premature agency increases gate surface area without increasing grounding~\cite{yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents}.




Conflict-asset week closes multimodal teaching without a subsection forest. Suppose three west-elevation hosts show RGB/IR conflict in one week: the system must not average them into one defect type; gondola lists should down-rank them by default; revisit hints must bind observation IDs for Chapters~2--3~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical,luo2025llmdtrobot}. If a manager asks to ``mark repaired first and gather evidence later,'' the harness must refuse direct writes and only stage pending updates behind approval~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,lu2026highwaymas}. When demonstrating agents, show TRACE beside the answer: passports fetched, neighbours expanded, manual edition pinned, which gate blocked a write. A beautiful answer without TRACE remains a demo; a plain answer with TRACE is an O\&M artefact~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,liang2023helm,hellin2025bimmas}. Role splits may clarify software modules, but they must not create multiple Asset-ID truths; graph-twin and human-DT MAS papers are precedents for shared memory and approval, not for unsupervised committee votes on defects~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,lu2026highwaymas,rawat2025llmmas,koh2026bimllmretr}.



In summary of multimodal fusion and bounded agency, modality tags travel with observation cards, TRACE makes tool use inspectable, and write gates keep the twin authoritative~\cite{zhao2026physical,yao2023react,luo2025llmdtrobot,lu2026highwaymas}. The next section turns grounded drafts into report schemas and CMMS maps that facility teams can schedule.

\section{Inspection Report Generation and Management}
\label{sec:ch8-reports}

Inspection answers become useful when they land in artefacts facility-management systems already understand. Free-form paragraphs are hard to audit. A practical report schema groups identity fields (asset and defect IDs), diagnosis, propagation notes, recommended actions, and trust flags. Identity fields must be subsets of the retrieved evidence set, the report-level analogue of constrained decoding used in BIM and code assistants~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes}. Diagnosis fields should point back to observation IDs whose images open in the twin UI. Action fields should be readable by schedulers without requiring another LLM to re-parse prose.

Language quality collapses when site tags and twin IDs diverge, so field identity hygiene belongs in this chapter, not only in IT operations~\cite{zhang2025}. Consider again the manager asking about water ingress on a vertical stack. The system returns neighbour passports, cites observation IDs whose images open in the twin UI, and either drafts a repair brief under the schema above or abstains if evidence is thin. If a revisit is needed, an action proposer emits a planning hint for Chapters~2--3 . Flight paths are not invented in prose.

Report lifecycle. Report management means more than one-shot generation. Drafts should be versioned; human sign-off should be recorded; approved facts may be written back into twin memory, the same long-term store introduced in Section~\ref{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}. Rejected drafts should not silently pollute passports. Where organisations already use CMMS or BIM issue trackers, the schema above should map to those fields . Parallel unreproducible prose PDFs are avoided. Natural-language query interfaces can help non-specialists ask questions, but the authoritative artefact remains the constrained report plus openable evidence.

That walkthrough is the product of Sections~\ref{sec:ch8-dt-to-language}--\ref{sec:ch8-multimodal} working together: structured twin memory, multimodal evidence assembly, and bounded tools. Readers should evaluate any vendor interface by the same tests: Are Asset-IDs stable? Are citations openable? Can the system abstain? Are writes gated?


Report schemas should be negotiated with facility stakeholders early. Fields that nobody will open in a CMMS will not be maintained. Fields that lawyers require for liability may need stricter cite-or-abstain checks than fields used for internal triage. A teaching compromise is a core schema (identity, diagnosis, evidence links, trust) plus optional organisation-specific extensions (cost codes, contractor IDs, access windows). The LLM may draft extensions only from retrieved context or human-provided forms; it must not invent cost codes~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes}.

Human-in-the-loop is not a confession of failure; it is the product. Sign-off records should store who approved what, which evidence IDs were visible at approval time, and whether modality conflict flags were overridden. Those records become part of twin provenance for the next campaign. Without them, next season's LLM will treat last season's speculative draft as fact. Identity hygiene figures in this section are therefore not IT folklore: they are the precondition for longitudinal language reasoning~\cite{zhang2025}.

A short anti-pattern catalogue helps teams review vendor demos. Anti-pattern~1: PDF-only RAG with no Asset-IDs. Anti-pattern~2: chat UI that cannot open the cited image. Anti-pattern~3: automatic twin writes from unapproved drafts. Anti-pattern~4: severity language without geometry normalisation.Anti-pattern~5: propagation stories built from embedding neighbours in place of BIM adjacency. Any demo that exhibits two or more remains a prototype. It is not yet an O\&M system.



A schema-constrained draft is only half the delivery. The other half is a field map: \texttt{asset\_ids} to equipment codes, \texttt{priority} to work-order priority, \texttt{human\_check} to inspection hold flags, evidence URIs to attachment slots. If the map does not exist, fluent reports accumulate as PDFs that nobody schedules. Facility digital-twin discussions already stress process coupling; language systems inherit that requirement~\cite{chen2023facility,wu2022,krutli2025fmrags}. Structured FM querying and building-data RAG published since 2024 likewise reward exposing schemas before free-form generation~\cite{buga2025llmfmsql,li2025b,li2025c}. Teaching projects should submit both a sample JSON draft and a one-page CMMS map. Campaigns change contractors, so handover packages should include an Asset-ID dictionary, observation schema, manual edition pins, approval logs, and a frozen twin snapshot used for language evaluation. Redact personal data in inspector notes before RAG indexing. Without handover artefacts, the next team will chat over loose PDFs and lose the twin contract, returning to parametric storytelling~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,zhang2025,ma2026tenquestions}.

Report narrative should be generated from schema fields, not the reverse. Bad pattern: write a paragraph, then scrape IDs with a regex. Good pattern: fill schema fields first; render a paragraph from fields; if rendering would require an ID not in the schema, stop. BIM assistants, code checkers, and IFC agents already lean on constrained structures; inspection reports should too~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes,gao2026ifcagent,chen2024bimllmontology}. A one-line renderer template is enough for teaching: ``Assets \{ids\} show \{type\} (severity \{sev\}) based on observations \{obs\}. Propagation path \{path\}. Action \{action\}. Trust: \{trust\}.'' Every evidence URI must open to the correct crop or frame; broken links are grounding failures even when IDs are correct. Nightly link checkers belong in O\&M pipelines alongside detector quality control~\cite{zhang2025,chen2023facility}. In harness terms, the report schema and CMMS map are contracts at the composition boundary: the LLM may phrase sentences, but it may not invent fields that validators did not admit~\cite{liang2023helm,buga2025llmfmsql,lu2026highwaymas}.




Report-lifecycle thickness comes from contracts. Ornate paragraphs are secondary. Schema-constrained drafts should pass ID subset validation before human review; rejections enter procedural memory; approved fields should map into CMMS with minimal rewrite, if facility staff always retype tickets, the language layer has not yet met the O\&M contract~\cite{buga2025llmfmsql,chen2023facility,krutli2025fmrags}. Field Asset-ID hygiene is part of report quality: collisions, missing aliases, and contractor code drift should be visible beside the draft, not smoothed away in prose~\cite{zhang2025,han2025}. Cite-or-abstain is especially important for reports: an ``official'' PDF without openable evidence is more dangerous than a chat hallucination because it moves money and scaffolding~\cite{ji2023,saka2024gptconstruction}. Reading 2024--2026 facility RAG, structured FM querying, and BIM retrieval agents as report contracts is more useful than another brand bake-off~\cite{quan2026bimfacrag,buga2025llmfmsql,hellin2025bimmas,koh2026bimllmretr}.

A second teaching pass over evaluation boards keeps decision support honest. Monthly invented-ID rate should fall as validators harden; openable-evidence rate should rise as packing improves; conflict-disclosure rate should rise without collapsing scheduler adoption; revisit-hint adoption should rise only when hints carry observation IDs; CMMS no-rewrite rate should rise when schema maps stabilise~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,luo2025llmdtrobot,rawat2025llmmas,liang2023helm}. If a hosted-LLM swap improves fluency but worsens invented IDs, reject the swap. If fluency is unchanged but TRACE completeness improves, accept the harness change even when demo videos look duller~\cite{ma2026tenquestions,pan2026graphdtgpt,lu2026highwaymas}.



In summary of report management, the authoritative artefact is a schema-constrained draft with openable evidence, human sign-off, and a field map into existing CMMS or issue trackers, not a parallel universe of unreproducible PDFs~\cite{buga2025llmfmsql,chen2023facility,quan2026bimfacrag}. The next section scores whether those artefacts actually change schedules under implementation-agnostic criteria.

\section{Decision Support and Predictive Maintenance}
\label{sec:ch8-dss}

Monograph readers need evaluation criteria that travel across implementations, not a bake-off among brand names. For inspection language systems, ask whether cited IDs appear in the evidence set~\cite{ji2023,shuster2021}; whether propagation questions improve when neighbours are included; whether code and manual RAG beats parametric recall on editioned corpora~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,joffe2025buildingcodes,krutli2025fmrags}; whether outputs can drive operational schedules; and whether agent tool traces respect approval and geofence constraints~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,rawat2025llmmas}. Published community evaluations on defect, safety, and facility corpora already show that structured retrieval helps. Laboratory prototypes on warehouse fa\c{c}ades may illustrate the same structural lesson, but unpublished numbers are not treated as bibliography claims in this chapter.

Open issues remain: topology completeness, Asset-ID hygiene, missing agent benchmarks for safe inspection tool traces, tighter coupling between detector confidence and language abstention thresholds, and governance questions for building LLMs~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,ma2026tenquestions}. Those frontiers belong with Chapter~9 and with the planning chapters that already address multi-UAV operations.

A 2024--2026 reading of decision support should also absorb what twin-interaction experiments already measured outside fa\c{c}ades. Human--DT multi-agent studies report lower cognitive load and higher task completion than GUI baselines for infrastructure experts~\cite{lu2026highwaymas}; graph-twin agents show that structured grounding can raise answer correctness relative to loosely retrieved pipelines~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt}; design-to-operation multi-agent modelling shows that twins can be updated as workflows, not only queried as static databases~\cite{lee2025multiagentvbm}. For fa\c{c}ade O\&M the transferable metrics are still the ones in Table~\ref{tab:ch8-eval}: grounding, topology use, corpus fidelity, operability, and agent safety. User-experience gains matter only when those five remain green. Likewise, router-style expert selection in neighbouring civil domains is a useful analogy for intent routing inside the harness, does not authorise teams to automate structural conclusions without human sign-off~\cite{youwai2026foundationmas,ma2026tenquestions}. Predictive language remains triage language until repeated campaigns and non-LLM temporal models enter the loop.


Decision support should be scored at the level of actions, not only at the level of sentences. A grounded paragraph that never changes a maintenance schedule has limited O\&M value. Conversely, a terse ranked list of Asset-IDs with openable evidence may be enough to plan a gondola week. Metrics should therefore include operability: fraction of outputs that map to work-order fields; fraction of agent traces that respect gates; fraction of propagation answers that use true neighbours~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,luo2025llmdtrobot,rawat2025llmmas}. Exact-match bake-offs among commercial chat models are secondary.

Predictive maintenance claims need temporal humility. Priority scores from one campaign are triage, not prognosis. Prognosis requires repeated observations on the same Asset-IDs, comparable modalities, and models that live outside the LLM prompt, survival models, deterioration curves, or physics-informed predictors, depending on the defect family. The language system can present those model outputs and keep them cited; it should not improvise a forecast to satisfy a manager's wording. Transferability figures in this section mean reusable schemas and tools, not reusable prophecies~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,zhang2025llmagentschema}.

Looking toward Chapter~9, the open problems are mostly socio-technical. Topology will remain incomplete on older buildings. Asset-ID regimes will collide across contractors. Agent benchmarks for safe tool use in inspection barely exist. Governance questions, who is responsible when a grounded-looking answer is wrong?, will not be solved by larger models alone~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,ma2026tenquestions}. The constructive response taught here is modest and actionable: make the twin decision-ready, retrieve over assets, fuse modalities without erasing conflict, bound the agent, and evaluate what facility teams can actually use.

Readers who must plan a year of O\&M can treat the chapter as a seasonal programme. A one-shot demo is insufficient for year-scale O\&M planning. In quarter~Q1, freeze Asset-IDs and neighbour edges for one elevation; ingest modality-tagged observations with openable URIs; run only local-lookup questions through a cite-or-abstain harness. In Q2, open one-hop expansion for propagation questions and pin one manual edition for code-plus-site queries, following the edition discipline emphasised in recent safety and regulation RAG~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,he2025regulationhybrid,joffe2025buildingcodes}. In Q3, enable the five-tool agent surface with write gates, adopting the approval habits of IFC, FM-robot, and human-DT multi-agent systems~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot,lu2026highwaymas}. In Q4, score the five evaluation criteria on a frozen twin snapshot, map approved drafts into CMMS fields, and only then consider swapping the hosted LLM, a model-substitution test of the harness, not a restart of the twin contract~\cite{liang2023helm,ma2026tenquestions,buga2025llmfmsql}. Graph-twin and design-to-operation multi-agent work published in 2025--2026 can inform how roles are split inside that programme, but they should not tempt teams to skip Q1 identity hygiene~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,lee2025multiagentvbm,rawat2025llmmas}. The seasonal view also clarifies predictive maintenance: Q1--Q3 produce triage and schedules; prognosis waits until the same Asset-IDs accumulate comparable multimodal history and a non-LLM temporal model is wired into the harness as a read-only tool.

Consider a two-day warehouse fa\c{c}ade campaign that Chapters~2--7 have already turned into a decision-ready twin. Day~1 yields RGB coverage of the west and south elevations; Day~2 adds infrared passes over suspected moisture zones. Perception writes modality-tagged observations; registration attaches them to BIM hosts; GeoBIM keeps city-scale Asset-IDs aligned with the facility register. On Day~3 a facility manager opens the language interface with four questions that mirror real O\&M meetings.

Question~1 is local: ``What is logged on asset W-3-17?'' Assembly loads only that passport and returns typed observations with openable image links. Question~2 is comparative: ``What changed on W-3-17 since the previous campaign?'' Assembly needs historical observation IDs on the same Asset-ID; if history is missing, the system abstains on change and asks for the prior campaign import. Question~3 is propagative: ``Could staining above W-3-17 explain hollow readings on W-3-17?'' Assembly expands upstairs and lateral neighbours, keeps RGB/IR tags, and either drafts a path hypothesis with citations or refuses. Question~4 is operational: ``Which west-elevation assets above level three should we stage for gondola access next week?'' Assembly applies a spatial filter, ranks by geometry-normalised severity and access constraints, and emits a schema-constrained list that a CMMS can ingest.

The walkthrough continues into agency only when the manager asks for action. ``Open a ticket for the top three assets and propose a revisit where IR and RGB disagree.'' The bounded agent drafts tickets from the ranked list, attaches evidence IDs, sets \texttt{human\_check=true} on modality conflicts, and emits revisit hints for Chapters~2--3. It does not email contractors, does not rewrite BIM geometry, and does not clear the conflict flags. A senior inspector approves two tickets, rejects one for insufficient evidence, and the rejection is stored in procedural memory so the agent does not resubmit it in the same session. Approved facts are written back to twin memory; rejected drafts are not. That end-to-end story is the chapter's pedagogical spine: twin contract, memory ladder, asset-centric assembly, multimodal disclosure, bounded tools, report lifecycle, and operability metrics.

Dialogue discipline. In teaching demos it is tempting to show long chat transcripts. Useful transcripts are short and checkable. Each assistant turn should (i)~restate the intent class, (ii)~list Asset-IDs entered into context, (iii)~separate observations from hypotheses, and (iv)~end with either citations or an abstention code. Turns that jump from a vague question to a repair cost estimate without evidence violate the decision-layer contract even if they sound helpful. Facility users can be trained to ask better questions, but the system must remain safe when they do not~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,ji2023}.

Relation to earlier monograph chapters. Chapter~8 should be readable after Chapters~4--7 without requiring readers to re-learn detectors or planners. Conversely, authors of those chapters can treat this chapter as a consumer specification: if your reconstruction export lacks host IDs, language fails; if your detector omits modality tags, fusion fails; if your GIS identifiers drift from field tags, reports fail. The cleanest cross-chapter collaboration is a shared Asset-ID and observation schema, not a shared buzzword list.



A concrete retrieval recipe for inspection teaching. The following recipe is intentionally boring; that is its virtue. (1)~Parse the user question into an intent class among local lookup, asset health, propagation, elevation audit, and code-plus-site. (2)~Resolve mentioned locations to Asset-IDs using GeoBIM aliases and field tags; if resolution fails, ask a disambiguation question . The system asks a disambiguation question when resolution fails. (3)~Load passports for the resolved seeds. (4)~Expand neighbours only if the intent is propagation or if the user explicitly asks about adjacency. (5)~Attach observation cards with modality tags and confidences. (6)~Optionally retrieve editioned manual clauses when compliance language appears. (7)~Build a context package whose every ID is typed. (8)~Generate under cite-or-abstain constraints. (9)~Validate the output string against the package. (10)~If validation fails, return a structured refusal naming the missing evidence. Steps~(2), (6) are twin and corpus engineering; steps~(7), (10) are language-system engineering. Confusing the two is how projects become endless prompt tuning~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024,jeon2025hybridrag,joffe2025buildingcodes}.

Every identity in such a draft must be subset-checked against retrieval. The narrative paragraph shown to humans should be generated from this structure, not the other way around~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes}.

Evaluation protocol one can run without a bake-off. A teaching laboratory can evaluate an inspection language system with a few dozen gold questions and a frozen twin snapshot. Score grounding by ID overlap; score topology by whether neighbour-required questions improve when expansion is enabled; score corpus fidelity by swapping manual editions on purpose and checking whether answers follow the active edition; score operability by asking facility staff whether outputs can become work orders without rewriting; score agent safety by attempting forbidden writes and verifying gates~\cite{ji2023,lee2024ragsafety,luo2025llmdtrobot,rawat2025llmmas}. Publish the protocol and the twin schema; do not publish unpublished site numbers as if they were general laws.

Why fifty-page depth matters for a monograph chapter. Springer monograph readers are not conference-paper skimmers. They need enough worked structure to implement or to supervise implementers. Depth here means more interface contracts, memory rules, intent tables, refusal behaviours, and evaluation checklists, not denser equations for their own sake. The chapter therefore prefers repeated specialisation of the same spine (twin $\rightarrow$ memory $\rightarrow$ assembly $\rightarrow$ agents $\rightarrow$ reports $\rightarrow$ metrics) over a forest of weakly connected subsections.



Extended worked questions (manager $\leftrightarrow$ system). The following dialogues are compressed teaching scripts. They are not logs from a deployed product; they show the behaviours readers should demand.

\textit{Q:} ``Is the west elevation safe?''\\
\textit{Intent:} too vague for a twin answer.\\
\textit{System:} ask for Asset-ID scope or storey band; refuse binary ``safe/unsafe'' without evidence package.\\
\textit{Why:} safety is a professional determination; the language layer can only summarise cited observations~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction}.

\textit{Q:} ``List hollow-sounding panels on the west elevation that also have IR anomalies within one storey above.''\\
\textit{Intent:} propagation-style filter.\\
\textit{System:} resolve west elevation hosts; load passports; keep observations with hollow and IR tags; expand one storey up; return Asset-IDs with both evidence types; abstain where only one modality exists.\\
\textit{Why:} this is multimodal fusion without collapsing conflict~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}.

\textit{Q:} ``Draft a repair brief for W-3-17 citing HKHA maintenance language if applicable.''\\
\textit{Intent:} code-plus-site.\\
\textit{System:} load passport(W-3-17); retrieve editioned manual clauses; draft schema fields; if manual edition is unset, stop and ask which edition is active.\\
\textit{Why:} corpus fidelity before prose~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,krutli2025fmrags}.

\textit{Q:} ``Update the twin: mark W-3-17 as repaired.''\\
\textit{Intent:} write.\\
\textit{System:} refuse direct write; stage a pending update; require human approval and evidence of repair inspection.\\
\textit{Why:} bounded agency~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent}.

\textit{Q:} ``Plan tomorrow's flight to re-check conflicting assets.''\\
\textit{Intent:} action toward Chapters~2--3.\\
\textit{System:} emit revisit polygons/hints tied to observation IDs; do not invent waypoints in chat.\\
\textit{Why:} chapter ownership~\cite{yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents}.

These scripts also show how memory layers interact. Contextual memory holds the disambiguation that ``west elevation'' means the warehouse west face in this session. Retrieval memory loads manuals. Twin memory supplies Asset-IDs. Procedural memory records that a write was staged and not yet approved. If any layer is missing, the dialogue collapses into either endless clarification or unsafe improvisation~\cite{park2023generativeagents,rawat2025llmmas}.

Design notes for course projects. Student or internship projects can implement a thin vertical slice: ten assets, thirty observations, twenty questions, one manual edition, and a five-tool agent. Grading should privilege refusal quality and citation correctness over conversational charm. Extending to full elevations is then an engineering scaling problem, not a new scientific story. That is how monograph chapters should steer practice: by making the thin slice honest enough that scaling does not invent a different ethics.

Common failure post-mortems. Failure~A: the system cites an Asset-ID that never entered retrieval because a prompt example mentioned it, fix by validating IDs. Failure~B: neighbour expansion used embedding similarity and linked two panels that share the word ``stain'' but sit on opposite elevations, fix by authority of BIM adjacency. Failure~C: infrared-only evidence was verbalised as confirmed seepage, fix by modality tags and human\_check. Failure~D: an agent cleared a ticket after drafting a report without approval, fix by separating draft\_report from stage\_twin\_write. Failure~E: two manual editions disagreed and the model averaged them, fix by edition pins~\cite{ji2023,peng2024,lee2024ragsafety}.



Illustrative priority scoring (not a field law). Suppose a scheduler scores assets by $s = 0.4\,\tilde{\ell} + 0.3\,c + 0.2\,a + 0.1\,m$, where $\tilde{\ell}$ is geometry-normalised defect size in $[0,1]$, $c$ is detector confidence, $a$ is access urgency (gondola day soon), and $m$ is a modality-conflict penalty (lower when RGB/IR disagree). The formula is a teaching toy: coefficients belong to the facility, not to the LLM. The language system should expose the terms and citations behind each score, then let humans reorder. Predictive maintenance begins only when repeated campaigns feed a temporal model; until then, $s$ is triage~\cite{zhao2026physical,gao2026ifcagent}.

Defect-family reading notes for language systems. Different fa\c{c}ade symptoms stress the decision layer differently. \emph{Surface cracking} is often RGB-rich; language should keep crack-length features tied to geometry normalisation and avoid structural-cause stories without neighbour or manual support. \emph{Spalling or delamination} may be visible in RGB yet linked to moisture hypotheses that need infrared or upstairs evidence; disclosure of missing modalities is mandatory. \emph{Staining and moisture cues} are multimodal by nature; infrared-only claims must stay uncertain until a human or a second modality confirms. \emph{Sealant and joint failures} are local but operationally urgent for water paths; elevation audits should not bury them under wall-bay averages. \emph{Previous repair patches} create temporal traps: a ``new'' stain beside an old patch is not automatically progression without cross-campaign IDs. These notes do not replace Chapter~6 taxonomies; they tell the language layer how to talk once taxonomy labels exist~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2025icra,zhao2026physical,jeon2025hybridrag}.

Laboratory protocol (thin vertical slice). A course-scale laboratory can freeze a toy twin with 12 Asset-IDs on one elevation, 40 observations (mixed RGB/IR), a 1-hop neighbour graph, and one pinned manual edition of five clauses. Prepare 25 gold questions: 8 local, 5 health, 5 propagation, 4 audit, 3 code-plus-site. For each question store the gold Asset-ID set and whether abstention is required. Students implement the ten-step recipe, then score: exact ID precision/recall; abstention accuracy; neighbour necessity (propagation questions with expansion off versus on); edition fidelity (swap the manual pin and expect answer change); forbidden-write tests (agent must not call \texttt{stage\_twin\_write} without approval). Publish student scores as protocol compliance, not as claims about commercial models~\cite{ji2023,lee2024ragsafety,peng2024}.

Operator playbook (one page in prose). Before a live query session, operators pin the campaign snapshot and manual edition; verify Asset-ID collision reports are empty; confirm image URIs open. During the session, they reject any answer that names an ID absent from the side panel evidence list; they treat \texttt{human\_check=true} as a hold, not as decoration. After the session, they export approved schema drafts to CMMS, store abstention logs, and open revisit hints for planning only after senior sign-off. This playbook is deliberately non-heroic. Inspection language systems fail when operators are asked to be heroes and succeed when operators enforce boring gates~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,luo2025llmdtrobot}.

Data-contract excerpt (teaching). An observation record accepted by the language layer should validate against a JSON Schema conceptually equivalent to: required \texttt{obs\_id}, \texttt{asset\_id}, \texttt{time}, \texttt{modality}, \texttt{defect\_type}, \texttt{confidence}, \texttt{image\_uri}; optional \texttt{geometry\_features}, \texttt{location\_phrase}, \texttt{detector\_id}. Passports require \texttt{asset\_id}, \texttt{neighbours[]}, \texttt{obs\_ids[]}, and a digest string. Retrieval packages require \texttt{intent}, \texttt{seed\_assets[]}, \texttt{passports[]}, \texttt{manual\_hits[]}, \texttt{edition\_pin}. Generation may not invent keys outside the package. Such contracts sound software-heavy; without them, monograph advice collapses into prompt folklore~\cite{pan2024b,zheng2023bimassistant,gao2026ifcagent}.

Comparing retrieval units on the same question. Take ``Is moisture likely propagating to W-3-17?'' PDF-chunk RAG may retrieve generic moisture paragraphs and invent a path. Passport-only RAG without neighbours may correctly list local stains yet miss upstairs evidence. Topology-aware passport RAG loads W-3-17 plus upstairs hosts and can either support or abstain honestly. Manual-only RAG may cite a clause without site evidence. The teaching comparison is qualitative: students should see all four behaviours on one frozen twin. Published hybrid and code RAG studies motivate pieces of this comparison; the topology axis is the inspection-specific addition~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag,joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,peng2024,krutli2025fmrags}.

Human factors: trust calibration. If the interface always answers fluently, inspectors stop opening evidence. If it abstains too often without naming missing evidence types, inspectors abandon it. Calibrated systems abstain with reasons (``no IR on upstairs neighbour'', ``Asset-ID collision'', ``manual edition unset'') and succeed with openable citations. Trust literature in hallucinating systems warns that fluency is a trap~\cite{ji2023}; facility settings amplify the trap because work orders move money and scaffolding. Design the UI to show evidence first and prose second.

Closing bridge toward metrics. Once contracts, symptom notes, laboratory scores, and operator playbooks exist, decision-support metrics stop being abstract. Grounding scores map to ID checks; topology scores map to expansion ablation in the lab; corpus scores map to edition swaps; operability scores map to CMMS field maps; safety scores map to forbidden-write tests. Section~\ref{sec:ch8-dss} then reads as the grading rubric for the same spine, not as a separate competition track.



Week-by-week implementation roadmap (teaching). Week~1 freezes Asset-IDs and neighbour edges for one elevation; no LLM yet. Week~2 lands observation ingest with modality tags and image URI checks. Week~3 builds passport digests and a read-only retrieval API. Week~4 adds intent parsing and cite-or-abstain generation on local/health questions only. Week~5 enables one-hop expansion for propagation questions and records topology ablation scores. Week~6 pins a manual edition and adds code-plus-site questions. Week~7 introduces the five-tool agent with all writes gated. Week~8 connects schema drafts to a stub CMMS map and runs the operator playbook on a live dry-run with three facility users. Teams that skip Week~1 almost always spend Week~8 debugging invented panels. The roadmap is intentionally retrieval-first; model swaps are allowed only after ID metrics plateau~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024,zhang2025,saka2024gptconstruction}.

On bilingual operations. Many fa\c{c}ade programmes mix English manuals, local administrative Chinese, and site slang. The twin IDs must stay language-agnostic. Location phrases may be bilingual, but Asset-IDs should not. RAG indexes may be language-specific per corpus, yet cite-or-abstain still keys on IDs. When translating drafts for stakeholders, translate prose after schema validation, never before. Otherwise translation can smuggle in uncited entities. Construction LLM reviews that discuss workplace language change are relevant here as governance context, not as a translation API endorsement~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,krutli2025fmrags,joffe2025buildingcodes}.

Security and misuse briefly. A language interface that can draft work orders is a process surface, not a toy chatbot. Access control should follow facility roles; tool gates should be server-side, not prompt-only; audit logs should be tamper-evident enough for internal review. Prompt injection via malicious PDF manuals is a real RAG risk: pin editions and prefer structured clauses. These points stay brief because the chapter's centre is grounding for inspection, but ignoring them turns a grounded demo into an operational hazard~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,rawat2025llmmas,saka2024gptconstruction}.

What success looks like after one quarter. Success is not ``the model sounds like an engineer.'' Success is measurable: fewer invented IDs in drafts; higher fraction of answers with openable evidence; fewer silent modality collapses; revisit hints that planning can consume; CMMS tickets that do not need full rewrite; abstentions that name missing evidence. If those curves are flat, change retrieval and contracts before changing base models. That is the monograph's recurring specialisation of general LLM advice to building inspection~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024,zhao2026physical,gao2026ifcagent}.



Deepening case: moisture-path assembly. Let upstairs asset W-4-17 carry RGB stain obs-A (confidence 0.78) and IR anomaly obs-B (0.66); mid-storey W-3-17 carry hollow obs-C (RGB, 0.81) with no IR. The manager asks about propagation. The orchestrator marks intent=propagation, seeds W-3-17, expands one hop to W-4-17 and lateral hosts, and builds a package with three passports and three observations (no manual). Generation may hypothesise coexistence of upstairs moisture cues and mid-storey hollow, but must cite the IDs, declare missing IR on W-3-17, set \texttt{human\_check=true}, and refuse prices or certain seepage paths. Student systems that emit costs or hide missing IR fail the lab. The case compresses Sections~\ref{sec:ch8-dt-to-language}--\ref{sec:ch8-reports} into one gradable trace~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical,peng2024,ji2023}.

Deepening case: manual edition pin. The same asset-health question under a 2018 pin versus a 2024 pin should yield distinguishable citation sets. Soft-averaging two editions is a corpus-fidelity failure. Code-RAG papers motivate editioned corpora; inspection classes must implement the pin as a switch, not a footnote~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,krutli2025fmrags}.

Deepening case: identifier collision. Field tag ``West-3-17'' may alias to Asset-ID ``W-3-17''; contractor export ``Panel-West-317'' may not. Resolution must return unresolved/collision, never ``nearest embedding ID.'' Wrong hosts are more expensive than abstention~\cite{zhang2025,han2025}.

Turning the evaluation table into a quarterly board. Track monthly: invented-ID rate; openable-evidence rate; modality-conflict disclosure rate; revisit-hint adoption rate; CMMS no-rewrite rate. If invented IDs fall but openable evidence does not rise, the system learned silence without retrieval. If evidence rises but CMMS no-rewrite does not, the schema map is broken. Table~\ref{tab:ch8-eval} thereby becomes management language~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,luo2025llmdtrobot,rawat2025llmmas}.

Pushing back on ``just add another agent.''. Failure often triggers role inflation. Without shared memory and gates, more roles invent more Asset-ID stories. Stabilise a five-tool single agent before splitting personalities. Multi-agent surveys and AEC agent papers should be read as arguments for shared memory and tool contracts, not for more personas~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,rawat2025llmmas,gao2026ifcagent}.

Re-stating the predictive-maintenance boundary. This chapter may explain triage terms and may cite external temporal-model outputs; it must not invent ``73\% spalling probability in eighteen months'' from a single campaign context. Product claims that equate Chapter~8 with full predictive maintenance over-promise; the monograph stays modest so Chapter~9 and real temporal methods still have work to do~\cite{zhao2026physical,saka2024gptconstruction,gao2026ifcagent}.



Classroom and laboratory practice can stay continuous with the same spine. Discussion prompts for foundations should force students to explain, with one fa\c{c}ade example each, why a larger model cannot replace an Asset-ID contract; what fails when chain-of-thought runs without neighbours in the package; which memory rung stays empty if only the dialogue window is used across campaigns; why embedding similarity between non-adjacent panels is the wrong error class for propagation; and why spoken user commands cannot outrank write gates~\cite{brown2020,lewis2020,ji2023,peng2024,saka2024gptconstruction,ma2026tenquestions}. Multimodal and agent prompts should ask students to draw the tool sequence from a propagation question to a ticket, to explain resubmission risk without procedural memory, to bind revisit hints to observation IDs before Chapters~2--3, to prevent multi-agent role splits from creating multiple Asset-ID truths, and to list the ``never'' assertions that offline fixture tests must catch~\cite{yao2023react,luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,rawat2025llmmas,lu2026highwaymas}.

Executable multimodal fusion for operators can be stated as short rules without becoming a subsection forest. RGB-only defect observations must not become ``confirmed internal seepage'' sentences; IR-only anomalies require uncertainty markers and \texttt{human\_check=true}; modality timestamps that differ beyond a facility threshold (for example 48\,h) must disclose non-simultaneity; conflicted assets default below same-severity consistent-modality assets on gondola lists unless an override enters the sign-off log~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical,ji2023}. Passport digests that feed the harness can follow a stable template, Asset-ID, storey or bay, neighbours, symptoms, observation IDs with modality, confidence, and time, plus flags, short enough to batch and long enough to support cite-or-abstain~\cite{peng2024,jeon2025hybridrag,zhang2025}. From demo to handover, teams need at least ten artefacts: Asset-ID dictionary, neighbour edges, observation schema, passport build notes, manual edition pins, intent--assembly policy, tool surface and gates, CMMS field map, gold questions with abstentions, and paired success/abstention TRACE files~\cite{zhang2025,krutli2025fmrags,saka2024gptconstruction,buga2025llmfmsql}. Missing any item, demos can still dazzle and handovers still fail.

A forty-five-minute memory-ladder mini-lab makes Section~\ref{sec:ch8-llm-foundations} physical: four cards (parametric, contextual, RAG, twin) and the question ``Is W-3-17 worse than last month?'' force abstention until prior observation IDs appear, then force abstention again under an ID collision~\cite{lewis2020,ji2023,zhang2025}. Reading 2024--2026 civil papers as transferable sentences keeps literature useful without brand worship: BIM assistants solved object navigation, not topology-aware defect diagnosis; code and regulation RAG pin clause editions that inspection must join to Asset-IDs; defect dialogue RAG hybridises noisy text but must change the unit to passports; FM RAG retrieves document stores that must link back to hosts; IFC, FM-robot, graph-twin, and human-DT agents supply tools and approvals that inspection must specialise with geofence and chapter ownership~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes,he2025regulationhybrid,jeon2025hybridrag,krutli2025fmrags,gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot,pan2026graphdtgpt,lu2026highwaymas,lee2025multiagentvbm,ma2026tenquestions}. TRACE grading can use five 0--2 items, intent, tool order, ID coverage, gates before writes, rejections in procedural memory, with score $\ge 8$ to pass and no style points for ornate prose that hides missing gates~\cite{yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents,rawat2025llmmas}. A modality-conflict notice template (``RGB obs $A$ disagrees with IR obs $B$; human\_check set; do not write repaired'') beats improvised disclaimers; a ten-minute scheduler script asks only whether evidence opens, conflicts are flagged, and revisit hints carry observation IDs~\cite{zhao2026physical,chen2023facility,luo2025llmdtrobot}. Standard abstention codes, \texttt{asset\_unresolved}, \texttt{no\_history}, \texttt{no\_neighbour}, \texttt{edition\_unset}, \texttt{empty\_package}, \texttt{id\_not\_in\_package}, belong in monthly boards, not only in apologetic chat~\cite{ji2023,peng2024,lee2024ragsafety}.

Students can implement five operability metrics without a commercial leaderboard: invented-ID rate, openable-evidence rate, conflict-disclosure rate, revisit adoption rate, and CMMS no-rewrite rate~\cite{ji2023,lee2024ragsafety,luo2025llmdtrobot}. Gaming one curve should move another: speaking less can lower invented IDs while collapsing openable evidence; stacking links without CMMS maps can raise evidence while leaving no-rewrite flat; marking every answer \texttt{human\_check} can raise disclosure while killing scheduler adoption~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,chen2023facility,ma2026tenquestions}. Holistic evaluation thinking from HELM and recent twin-interaction studies converges here: scenarios and multi-metric boards matter more than a single fluency score~\cite{liang2023helm,lu2026highwaymas,pan2026graphdtgpt}. Three IOUs remain for Chapter~9 and beyond, city-scale identity governance, safe inspection-agent benchmarks, and true temporal predictors wired as read-only tools, and none should be erased by a larger hosted model~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,zhao2026physical,rawat2025llmmas,youwai2026foundationmas}.

A comparative reading of 2024--2026 systems helps readers decide what to borrow without cloning any one product. Graph-DT-GPT-style multi-agent twins show that natural-language access canstay faithful when every answer step is routed through structured graph queries. Unconstrained retrieval is refused~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt}. For fa\c{c}ade inspection the analogue is clear: treat Asset-ID passports and BIM neighbours as the graph, and refuse to answer propagation questions when the required edges are missing. HighwayMAS-style human-DT interaction shows that leader/reasoning/member teams can lower cognitive load for infrastructure experts relative to steep GUIs, yet still coordinate specialised functions under human evaluation~\cite{lu2026highwaymas}. Inspection should borrow the interaction gain and the role clarity, while keeping write gates and chapter ownership that highway GUIs never owned. Design-to-operation multi-agent modelling inside BIM shows that twins can be updated as workflows and can be updated as workflows~\cite{lee2025multiagentvbm}; inspection should allow approved write-back of verified observations, not unsupervised mutation of geometry. Construction-management MAS frameworks that combine graph and vector memory~\cite{rawat2025llmmas} and IFC tool agents with dual memory~\cite{gao2026ifcagent} supply patterns for shared memory and typed tools; FM robot brokers supply the approval habit when language proposes field action~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot}. Router-driven expert selection in foundation design~\cite{youwai2026foundationmas} is a useful analogy for intent routing inside the harness, local lookup, propagation, elevation audit, code-plus-site, but does not authorise teams to automate structural conclusions. Building-application ``ten questions'' agendas~\cite{ma2026tenquestions} and construction GPT reviews~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction} close the loop by insisting that data readiness, trust, and governance precede model-size shopping.

On the retrieval side, 2024--2025 practice is equally specific. Hybrid defect-management RAG and safety-manual RAG demonstrate gains when corpora are noisy or editioned~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag,lee2024ragsafety}; regulation hybrids and prompt-based code transformation show that clause retrieval must still meet site evidence~\cite{he2025regulationhybrid,yang2024promptcode,joffe2025buildingcodes}; ontology and knowledge-graph checkers push compliance beyond chat~\cite{chen2024bimllmontology,iversen2026,li2024kgllmcheck}; facility-document RAG and structured FM querying show that schemas and Asset-ID links are what make building corpora operable~\cite{krutli2025fmrags,buga2025llmfmsql,arslan2024ragenergy}; BIM NL-to-query stacks lower navigation barriers without yet diagnosing multi-storey defect paths~\cite{liu2025bimcoder,guo2025bimllmquery,zheng2023bimassistant}. Adaptive and self-reflective RAG variants~\cite{asai2024selfrag,jeong2024adaptive,gao2023hyde} belong inside the harness as controlled retries, not as excuses to abandon passport units~\cite{peng2024}. HELM-style multi-scenario evaluation~\cite{liang2023helm} and the five operability metrics of this chapter are the right procurement language: ask vendors for traces, abstention codes, and CMMS maps, not only for demo videos.

Putting those borrowings together yields a compact inspection playbook. Freeze identity and topology first. Ingest modality-tagged observations second. Assemble by intent third. Generate under cite-or-abstain fourth. Enable gated tools fifth. Score operability monthly sixth. Only then swap the hosted LLM as a harness substitution test. That order is the practical meaning of ready-to-use LLMs for civil engineering inspection: the technology families and the harness are introduced so that they can be applied to inspection analysis without pretending that training a site-specific model will keep a living fa\c{c}ade up to date.

Procurement language for 2024--2026 stacks should stay boring and checkable. Ask whether Asset-IDs are stable across UAV, BIM, and CMMS namespaces; whether observation cards carry modality, time, confidence, and openable URIs; whether neighbour expansion uses BIM or GeoBIM adjacency (embedding similarity must not define neighbours); whether manual editions are pinned; whether every delivered answer either cites IDs from the package or returns a typed abstention; whether tool gates for writes and revisits are server-side; and whether monthly boards track invented-ID rate, openable-evidence rate, conflict-disclosure rate, revisit adoption, and CMMS no-rewrite rate~\cite{ma2026tenquestions,saka2024gptconstruction,liang2023helm,buga2025llmfmsql}. Vendors that answer only with parameter counts or demo videos are selling parametric fluency. Inspection buyers need harness artefacts: schemas, TRACE samples, abstention codes, and a CMMS field map~\cite{liang2023helm,lu2026highwaymas,pan2026graphdtgpt}.

Three further 2025--2026 BIM-language systems sharpen what to borrow for fa\c{c}ade O\&M. Multi-agent natural-language retrieval over BIM models shows that specialised agents can decompose information-seeking without forcing users through steep GUI paths~\cite{hellin2025bimmas}. Condensed multi-LLM agent code generation for BIM retrieval shows a cost-aware path that keeps intervention minimal while still exposing typed retrieval behaviours~\cite{koh2026bimllmretr}. Facility-data LLM--RAG frameworks that sit on BIM O\&M stores show how document and model corpora can be joined for AECO questions~\cite{quan2026bimfacrag}. None of these systems is a warehouse fa\c{c}ade inspector. Their transferable lesson is identical to the graph-twin and human-DT literature already cited: put structure outside the weights; keep the LLM as a composer; refuse to answer when the structured path is missing~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,lu2026highwaymas,lee2025multiagentvbm,gao2026ifcagent}. For inspection, replace default BIM object navigation with asset passports and topology-aware assembly; keep Chapter~6 detectors as writers of modality-tagged observations; and treat CMMS as a contract sink, not as a free-text dump~\cite{krutli2025fmrags,buga2025llmfmsql,jeon2025hybridrag}.

A second full campaign narrative makes the seasonal programme concrete. In a housing-estate pilot, Q1 freezes Asset-IDs for one block face and imports two prior campaigns so change questions have history. Q2 opens one-hop expansion only for moisture-path questions and pins the 2024 maintenance manual edition used by the estate office~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,he2025regulationhybrid,joffe2025buildingcodes}. Q3 enables five tools with write gates; senior inspectors approve tickets twice a week; rejected drafts stay in procedural memory~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot,lu2026highwaymas}. Q4 scores the five evaluation criteria on a frozen snapshot, maps approved drafts into CMMS, and only then runs a model-substitution test: swap the hosted LLM, keep validators and tool schemas fixed, and verify that invented-ID rate and abstention codes do not regress~\cite{liang2023helm,ma2026tenquestions}. If invented IDs rise after the swap, the harness, not the twin, needs hardening. If invented IDs stay flat but openable evidence falls, retrieval packing regressed. That is how HELM-style multi-metric thinking becomes facility language~\cite{liang2023helm,pan2026graphdtgpt}.

Readers implementing course projects can treat Figures~\ref{fig:ch8-memory-ladder}--\ref{fig:ch8-agent-gate} as the only wall posters they need. The memory ladder poster stops students from calling the chat window ``long-term memory.'' The harness poster stops them from equating a system prompt with a validator. The agent-gate poster stops them from wiring \texttt{stage\_twin\_write} to the same button as \texttt{draft\_report}. Classroom TRACE grading then becomes a five-item checklist against those posters, not a taste test for fluent English~\cite{yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents,rawat2025llmmas,hellin2025bimmas}. The same posters also travel to procurement meetings: if a vendor cannot point to the four memory rungs, the five harness parts, and the write gate, the product is still a chatbot with a building wallpaper~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,ma2026tenquestions,quan2026bimfacrag}.

On corpus construction for 2024--2025 practice, prefer editioned clause stores and Asset-ID-linked facility documents over undifferentiated PDF lakes~\cite{krutli2025fmrags,quan2026bimfacrag,arslan2024ragenergy}. Hybrid defect dialogue corpora motivate noisy-language retrieval, but the retrieval unit for inspection should still be the passport~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag,peng2024}. Safety and regulation RAG motivate edition pins that inspection must join to site evidence before advice becomes operational~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,he2025regulationhybrid,yang2024promptcode}. Ontology and knowledge-graph checkers push compliance beyond chat and remind readers that machine-checkable structure is the product~\cite{chen2024bimllmontology,iversen2026,li2024kgllmcheck,nuyts2024comparativeacc}. BIM NL multi-agent retrieval lowers navigation cost; inspection still needs topology-aware diagnosis after navigation succeeds~\cite{hellin2025bimmas,koh2026bimllmretr,liu2025bimcoder,zheng2023bimassistant}. Adaptive RAG retries belong inside evidence assembly as controlled loops when the first package is thin, not as a licence to abandon Asset-ID units~\cite{asai2024selfrag,jeong2024adaptive,gao2023hyde,peng2024}.

Finally, keep the predictive-maintenance boundary visible in every stakeholder slide. Language systems triage and schedule; temporal models prognose; humans decide. Repeated campaigns on the same Asset-IDs, comparable modalities, and read-only temporal tools are the prerequisites for prognosis language. Until those exist, ``priority this quarter'' is honest and ``failure probability next year'' is not~\cite{zhao2026physical,gao2026ifcagent,saka2024gptconstruction,youwai2026foundationmas}. Chapter~9 and the planning chapters inherit that humility when multi-UAV operations and city-scale identity enter the story.





Extended comparative reading of 2025--2026 BIM retrieval and twin-interaction systems helps readers decide what not to copy. Hellin et al.\ show multi-agent decomposition for natural-language information retrieval from BIM models; the transferable piece is role clarity under typed retrieval, not a claim that GUI-free chat is always safer~\cite{hellin2025bimmas}. Koh et al.\ emphasise cost-effective, minimal-intervention multi-LLM agent code generation for BIM retrieval; the transferable piece is keeping intervention small when the structured store already exists, not generating unconstrained scripts that mutate geometry~\cite{koh2026bimllmretr}. Quan et al.\ join BIM facility data with LLM--RAG; the transferable piece is linking document corpora to model objects for O\&M questions, which inspection should specialise by preferring Asset-ID passports as primary units and manuals as pinned secondary context~\cite{quan2026bimfacrag,krutli2025fmrags,peng2024}. Graph-DT-GPT, HighwayMAS, and design-to-operation multi-agent modelling remain the twin-interaction anchors: structured graphs, human evaluation, and workflow updates under approval~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,lu2026highwaymas,lee2025multiagentvbm}. IFC dual-memory agents and FM robot brokers remain the tool-and-gate anchors~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot}. Ten-questions building agendas and construction GPT reviews remain the governance anchors~\cite{ma2026tenquestions,saka2024gptconstruction}. Together they justify the chapter's refusal to centre fine-tuning: living fa\c{c}ades change faster than site-specific weights can honestly track~\cite{lewis2020,krutli2025fmrags}.

A worked TRACE for a code-plus-site question makes those borrowings operational. Intent classification returns code-plus-site when the manager asks for a repair brief that cites the estate maintenance manual. Intake resolves ``W-3-17'' against GeoBIM aliases; evidence assembly loads passport(W-3-17), recent RGB/IR observations, and the pinned 2024 manual edition; tools may call \texttt{list\_observations} but must not call free-form SQL; generation drafts schema fields with clause IDs and observation IDs; validators check that every Asset-ID and clause ID appear in the package; TRACE stores the edition pin and tool order; delivery proceeds only if human review accepts \texttt{human\_check} flags when modalities conflict~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,he2025regulationhybrid,joffe2025buildingcodes,liang2023helm}.If the edition pin is unset, abstain with \texttt{edition\_unset}. Averaging 2018 and 2024 wording is refused. If upstairs neighbours are required for a moisture hypothesis but were never expanded, abstain with \texttt{no\_neighbour}. Those codes are the product surface that monthly boards can count~\cite{ji2023,peng2024}.

Classroom assessment can then stay aligned with procurement. Students who implement the thin vertical slice should be graded on refusal quality and citation correctness first, conversational charm last. Buyers who score vendors should request the same artefacts: Asset-ID dictionary samples, neighbour-edge provenance, observation schema, passport digests, edition pins, intent--assembly policy, tool surface with gates, CMMS field map, gold questions with abstentions, and paired success/abstention TRACE files~\cite{zhang2025,krutli2025fmrags,buga2025llmfmsql,hellin2025bimmas}. Missing any item, demos can still dazzle and handovers still fail. Onboarding week for a facility pilot makes the same spine concrete without new subsections. Monday exports the Asset-ID dictionary and neighbour edges; collisions stop the meeting before any model demo~\cite{zhang2025,han2025}. Tuesday admits only observation cards that pass modality, time, URI, and host checks~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}. Wednesday allows local and health questions only; propagation abstains until edges are frozen~\cite{peng2024,ji2023}. Thursday opens one-hop expansion and one pinned manual edition~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,he2025regulationhybrid,quan2026bimfacrag}. Friday dry-runs the five-tool surface: drafts allowed, writes blocked; a senior rejection is stored in procedural memory~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot,lu2026highwaymas,hellin2025bimmas}. No base-model swap occurs that week. The week succeeds if harness artefacts exist, not if chat sounds expert~\cite{ma2026tenquestions,saka2024gptconstruction,koh2026bimllmretr}.

Report lifecycle thickness comes from contracts, not from prettier paragraphs. Schema drafts should pass ID subset checks before human review; rejections enter procedural memory; approved fields should map into CMMS with minimal rewrite~\cite{buga2025llmfmsql,chen2023facility,krutli2025fmrags}. Field Asset-ID hygiene is part of report quality: collisions and alias gaps should be visible in the side panel, not smoothed away in prose~\cite{zhang2025,han2025}. Cite-or-abstain matters especially in reports because an ``official'' PDF without openable evidence is more dangerous than a chat hallucination~\cite{ji2023,saka2024gptconstruction}. Reading 2024--2026 facility RAG, structured FM querying, and BIM retrieval agents as report contracts is more useful than another brand bake-off~\cite{quan2026bimfacrag,buga2025llmfmsql,hellin2025bimmas,koh2026bimllmretr}.
