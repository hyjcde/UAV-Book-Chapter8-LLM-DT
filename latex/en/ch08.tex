\chapter{Digital Twin with LLMs in Inspection Analysis}
\label{ch:llm-dt}

\abstract*{This chapter develops the decision layer of the UAV inspection pipeline: after planning, reconstruction, detection, and GIS anchoring, how can digital twins support language-level diagnosis and maintenance decisions? We introduce foundations of large language models (LLMs) and vision--language models (VLMs), survey grounding strategies (prompting, retrieval, tools, and structured outputs), and organise a book-level framework that separates observation records, asset-centric knowledge, retrieval assembly, agentic tooling, and report delivery. Community work across BIM assistants, construction RAG, and twin--robot agents is synthesised alongside group contributions; an asset-centric RAG study appears only as one worked example.}

\abstract{Chapters~2--7 explain how to fly, reconstruct, detect, and map. Chapter~8 asks what happens next: how inspectors and facility managers query a twin in natural language, fuse visual and textual evidence, obtain traceable answers, and trigger follow-up actions. The chapter follows a deliberate teaching sequence---\emph{why language}, \emph{what LLMs/VLMs can and cannot do}, \emph{how to ground them on twin evidence}, \emph{how to organise twin knowledge for retrieval}, \emph{how multimodal queries and agents operate}, and \emph{how reports and decision metrics close the loop}. Both LLMs and VLMs are in scope for this decision layer. The narrative is a monograph synthesis, not a restatement of any single manuscript.}

\section{Digital Twins for Building Inspection}
\label{sec:ch8-dt}

\subsection{Where this chapter sits in the book}
The monograph is organised as one pipeline. Task and motion planning (Chapters~2--3) produce safe coverage. Reconstruction (Chapter~4) turns imagery into geometry. Datasets and perception models (Chapters~5--6) name defects. GIS and GeoBIM (Chapter~7) give assets addresses in the city. None of those chapters, alone, answers questions such as ``which panels participate in a multi-storey moisture path?'' or ``draft a repair brief that cites evidence an engineer can audit.'' That is the job of the \emph{decision layer}: connecting twin entities to language, retrieval, and---increasingly---tool-using agents.

\subsection{What a twin must provide for language use}
A geometric twin answers location. A decision-ready twin must also expose (i)~stable asset identifiers, (ii)~provenance for each observation (sensor, time, detector), and (iii)~relations that support propagation reasoning. Without those, language models can only chat over loose files---fluent, but not auditable for facility management (FM).

Digital-twin surveys in construction emphasise that twins are not merely 3D viewers: they are evolving information systems linking as-built geometry, semantics, and operations~\cite{boje2020,opoku2021,lu2020}. UAV--BIM inspection reviews sketched early automation agendas~\cite{zhang2022}. Within this research group, Zhang et al.\ demonstrated AI-empowered twin modelling that couples UAV sensing with GeoBIM for high-precision defect management~\cite{zhang2025}; Det-Recon-Reg packages detection, reconstruction, and registration for large-scale inspection~\cite{yang2025detreconreg}. Related twin uses extend beyond façades, including worker monitoring on large sites~\cite{han2025}. These works supply the geometric and identifier premise for the language layer developed below.

\section{Foundations of LLMs for Civil Engineering Applications}
\label{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}

\subsection{From language models to large language models}
Contemporary LLMs rest on the Transformer architecture~\cite{vaswani2017attention}: self-attention lets a model weigh long-range dependencies in text, which is essential for reading specifications, maintenance logs, and multi-sentence inspection questions. Scaling pretraining on large corpora produced few-shot learners that can perform new tasks from natural-language instructions with little or no task-specific fine-tuning~\cite{brown2020}. Instruction tuning and preference optimisation further align models to follow user intent rather than merely continue text~\cite{ouyang2022instructgpt}. Chain-of-thought prompting can elicit intermediate reasoning steps that improve multi-hop answers on many benchmarks~\cite{wei2022cot}---useful for inspection dialogues that combine symptoms, locations, and regulations---but does \emph{not} by itself guarantee factual correctness about a particular façade.

For textbook purposes, treat an LLM as a powerful conditional text generator whose knowledge is (i)~frozen at training cut-off for parametric memory and (ii)~easily overwritten by the prompt context. That dual nature is why grounding strategies matter more than brand names when the answer must cite a real Asset-ID.

\subsection{Vision--language models as visual interfaces}
Inspection data are not text-only. Contrastive vision--language pretraining (e.g., CLIP) aligns images and captions in a shared embedding space~\cite{radford2021clip}. Instruction-tuned multimodal models such as LLaVA---and subsequent GPT-4-class multimodal systems---accept images together with text prompts~\cite{liu2023llava}. In this book, VLMs are a genuine part of the decision layer: they can describe visual evidence, compare RGB/IR cues in language, and support report drafting. They are complementary to---not a replacement for---Chapter~6 detectors when audit trails require typed defect labels, confidences, and stable host IDs.

A practical division of labour used throughout this chapter is:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textbf{Perception stack} (Chapters~5--6, optionally with a VLM head) writes modality-tagged observations;
\item \textbf{Twin / GIS stack} (Chapters~4 and~7) attaches observations to Asset-IDs and topology;
\item \textbf{LLM/VLM stack} (this chapter) retrieves, explains, and proposes actions under citation rules.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Failure modes: hallucination, wrong IDs, and unauditable advice}
Surveys of hallucination show that fluent NLG systems readily invent entities, numbers, and citations when evidence is missing or weakly retrieved~\cite{ji2023}. In façade O\&M the high-cost failures are concrete: invented Asset-IDs; repair advice without attributable clauses; and summaries that silently drop contradictory RGB/IR cues. Explainability surveys likewise stress that post-hoc verbal justifications are not the same as evidence grounding~\cite{zhao2024}. Construction-oriented reviews of GPT-class tools echo the same warning: opportunity is high, but domain reliability requires external knowledge and process controls~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction}.

\subsection{Memory ladder: parametric, contextual, retrieval, and twin stores}
Professional readers should separate four memory regimes before jumping to products:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textbf{Parametric memory} --- facts compressed into model weights at training time; hard to update after every inspection flight~\cite{brown2020}.
\item \textbf{Working / contextual memory} --- the prompt window and short dialogue state; powerful but length-limited and session-fragile.
\item \textbf{Non-parametric retrieval memory} --- RAG and dense indexes that load editioned manuals or observation cards on demand~\cite{lewis2020,shuster2021}; facility-management deployments show this pattern on building document corpora~\cite{krutli2025fmrags}.
\item \textbf{Structured long-term twin memory} --- Asset-IDs, topology, and time-stamped observations in BIM/GIS/twin databases, optionally linked to knowledge graphs~\cite{pan2024b,zhang2025}. Generative-agent literature shows why explicit memory streams matter for multi-step behaviour~\cite{park2023generativeagents}; construction multi-agent frameworks likewise combine graph memory with vector retrieval~\cite{rawat2025llmmas}.
\end{enumerate}

For façade programmes, the twin is the authoritative long-term store; the LLM should \emph{read} it through retrieval and tools, not pretend the weights remember last week's crack growth.

\subsection{Grounding strategies: prompting, retrieval, tools, and structure}
Four complementary grounding strategies appear across NLP and AEC deployments:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textbf{Prompting and schemas.} Constrained output formats (JSON/report templates) reduce free-form drift; chain-of-thought helps multi-step questions~\cite{wei2022cot} but must still be checked against evidence.
\item \textbf{Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG).} Classic RAG attaches generation to external memory so answers can cite retrieved passages~\cite{lewis2020,shuster2021}. Published conference variants rewrite queries (HyDE), reflect on usefulness (Self-RAG), or adapt retrieval depth (Adaptive-RAG)~\cite{gao2023hyde,asai2024selfrag,jeong2024adaptive}; graph-oriented retrieval surveys connect LLMs to structured corpora~\cite{peng2024}.
\item \textbf{Tools and agents.} Models can call APIs, databases, or robots in reason--act loops~\cite{yao2023react,schick2023toolformer}; surveys catalogue single- and multi-agent stacks~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent}.
\item \textbf{Structured knowledge.} Unifying LLMs with knowledge graphs and typed entities improves navigability and constraint checking~\cite{pan2024b}. For buildings, this means Asset-IDs, IFC/BIM objects, and observation records---not anonymous text chunks alone.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{How AEC is already using LLMs and RAG}
A growing AEC literature shows where these strategies help---and where inspection still differs:

\paragraph{BIM navigation and virtual assistants.}
Prompted assistants can search BIM information and return object-linked answers~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant}. Query--DSL alignment systems connect natural language to executable BIM retrieval libraries~\cite{guo2025bimllmquery}. These systems excel at \emph{navigating} design/as-built models; they do not by themselves diagnose defect propagation across façade assets.

\paragraph{Codes, safety, and compliance corpora.}
RAG improves answers about building codes and safety manuals relative to parametric GPT alone~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety}. Hybrid retrieval designs have been explored for sensitive defect-management dialogue~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag}. Facility-document RAG systems likewise show secure retrieval over operations manuals and drawings~\cite{krutli2025fmrags}. Construction planning has used graph RAG to reduce information silos~\cite{he2025graphrag}. The lesson for Chapter~8: edition-versioned expert corpora are valuable, but they must be kept separate from web scrape and from twin observation stores.

\paragraph{Energy, FM, and robot-facing agents.}
Agent schemas for energy modelling~\cite{zhang2025llmagentschema}, LLM brokers among users, twins, and robots~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot}, schema-guided multi-agent tool use on IFC~\cite{gao2026ifcagent}, and construction-oriented LLM multi-agent frameworks with hybrid memory~\cite{rawat2025llmmas} show the trajectory from answering to acting. Cognitive-twin discussions emphasise retrieval over interoperable knowledge~\cite{shi2025cognitivedt}.

\paragraph{Perception context for language.}
High-resolution façade datasets and detectors~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2025icra} and physical quantification from instance segmentation~\cite{zhao2026physical} matter here because language systems inherit the granularity and bias of the perception stack.

\subsection{Teaching takeaway for this chapter}
For façade inspection dialogue, three design choices dominate brand choice: (i)~retrieve over \emph{maintainable assets} and their neighbours, not only document chunks; (ii)~force citation of Asset-IDs / observation IDs that appear in the retrieved set; (iii)~place irreversible actions (twin writes, UAV/robot dispatch) behind approval gates. The next sections turn those choices into a teaching framework.

\paragraph{Bridge.}
We now specify how twin products from Chapters~4--7 should be packaged so that grounding strategies have something reliable to retrieve.

\section{From Digital Twins to Language Reasoning}
\label{sec:ch8-dt-to-language}

\subsection{A teaching framework (book-level)}
Rather than importing any one paper's module list, we keep three layers separate:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textbf{Observation records} --- one defect sighting with sensor provenance;
\item \textbf{Asset passports} --- all observations and geometry/topology for a maintainable host;
\item \textbf{Query assembly} --- how a natural-language question selects passports and neighbours before generation.
\end{enumerate}

Different research groups implement these layers with different schemas. The pedagogical point is the \emph{separation}: perception writes observations; FM thinks in assets; language models must not be asked to invent either. This stance is consistent with structured-knowledge + LLM roadmaps~\cite{pan2024b} and with BIM-IR systems that refuse to treat the model as a database~\cite{guo2025bimllmquery,zheng2023bimassistant}.

\subsection{Observation records in practice}
Across UAV inspection programmes, useful observation records typically store: host asset ID, time, modality (RGB/IR/\ldots), detector signature, pose or surface coordinates, and paths to imagery. GeoBIM-DT pipelines show how registration attaches detections to BIM hosts before any LLM is involved~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg}. Chapter~6 supplies the detector; Chapters~4--7 supply the host ID.

For teaching, one may write a generic observation as a tuple of identity, evidence, localisation, and analysis fields---without committing the chapter prose to any one project's notation. Natural-language location phrases (``top centre of the southeast panel'') help LLMs, while metric coordinates remain for field navigation.

\subsection{Asset passports and topology}
Facility questions are usually asset-centric. Passports therefore aggregate observations per maintainable host and attach (i)~symptom summaries, (ii)~host geometry for severity normalisation, and (iii)~neighbour links (above/below/lateral). Neighbour links enable propagation-style reading---e.g., mid-storey delamination considered together with upstairs moisture---without pretending a document-similarity graph is a structural graph. Graph-oriented RAG~\cite{peng2024,he2025graphrag} is related in spirit; the inspection-specific requirement is that graph edges come from BIM/GIS adjacency and registration, not only from text co-occurrence.

\subsection{Worked example: asset-centric retrieval}
One concrete implementation pattern---used in ongoing group experiments and therefore \emph{not} entered here as a bibliography item until peer-published---organises observation records into asset passports and expands neighbours along BIM adjacency for multi-hop diagnosis. In monograph voice we treat it as a teaching pattern among many published siblings: hybrid RAG for defect dialogue~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag}, code/safety RAG~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety}, facility-document RAG~\cite{krutli2025fmrags}, and BIM assistants~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant}. Readers who need implementation minutiae should consult those primary sources; this chapter keeps the ideas needed to connect the book pipeline to decision support.

\section{Multi-Modal Fusion and Topology-Aware RAG}
\label{sec:ch8-multimodal}

\subsection{Pipeline fusion versus end-to-end VLMs}
Façade programmes are multi-modal, and VLMs are a real part of the decision layer~\cite{radford2021clip,liu2023llava}. ``Fusion'', however, need not collapse into one opaque end-to-end VLM for every query. A book-consistent pattern that remains auditable today is: detectors (or a VLM perception head) write modality-tagged observations; retrieval selects asset passports; an LLM or VLM then composes answers under citation rules. End-to-end VLM-native twins that jointly read imagery and twin topology remain an active research direction (Chapter~9); the pedagogical point is to use VLMs where they add visual grounding, while keeping Asset-IDs and provenance outside the model's free text.

\subsection{Intent, neighbourhood expansion, and citation rules}
Textbook query types include local lookup, asset-health summary, propagation/root-cause reading, and floor- or elevation-level audit. Intent should change \emph{which neighbours enter the context}, not only the prompt wording. RAG and graph-RAG surveys emphasise that retrieval quality dominates generation quality when facts are corpus-bound~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024}.

A minimal assembly pattern is:
\[
\text{context}
=
\text{passport}(A)
\cup
\text{neighbours}(A)
\cup
\text{optional manuals}.
\]
Hard teaching rule: every cited asset/defect ID in the answer must appear in the retrieved set; otherwise the system must abstain or ask for human review. Optional expert corpora (codes, repair manuals) should be edition-versioned and separate from web scrape---a lesson shared with compliance-oriented BIM and code assistants~\cite{guo2025bimllmquery,joffe2025buildingcodes}.

\subsection{RGB/IR disagreements}
When thermal and visual evidence disagree, good practice is disclosure rather than silent averaging: RGB-only evidence should not be escalated to subsurface claims; IR-only evidence should keep uncertainty flags. Perception chapters define what each modality can see; this chapter defines how language systems must talk about conflict. VLMs may help \emph{describe} the disagreement; they must not erase the disagreement in fluent prose.

\subsection{From static RAG to bounded agency}
Frontier systems increasingly interleave retrieval with tool calls (agentic RAG). For inspection we recommend \emph{bounded agency}: multi-step retrieval is welcome; final claims remain citation-locked to twin entities~\cite{yao2023react,asai2024selfrag}. Unbounded tool loops without Asset-ID checks recreate hallucination with extra latency~\cite{ji2023,shuster2021}.

\paragraph{Bridge.}
Agents become essential when the system must \emph{act}, not only answer.

\section{Agentic Workflows for Twin-Grounded Inspection}
\label{sec:ch8-agents}

\subsection{Why the book needs an agent section}
RAG answers evidence-seeking questions. FM operations also require procedures: open a ticket, propose a UAV revisit, request a robot photo, or stage a twin update after human approval. That is the scope of agents~\cite{yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents,park2023generativeagents}.

\subsection{What others have built}
Luo et al.\ place an LLM between the user, the twin, and robot middleware, emphasising human-approved twin commits~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot}. Gao et al.\ show schema-guided multi-agent tool composition on IFC~\cite{gao2026ifcagent}. Rawat et al.\ propose construction LLM multi-agent systems with hybrid graph/vector memory~\cite{rawat2025llmmas}. Energy-oriented agent libraries show how domain tools can be versioned~\cite{zhang2025llmagentschema}. None of these replace Chapters~2--7; they consume twin state those chapters create.

\subsection{A pedagogical agent stack for façade programmes}
We describe four \emph{roles} (logical, not necessarily four model instances):
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textbf{Retriever} --- asset-passport search with citation packing;
\item \textbf{Topology aide} --- neighbour expansion and conflict checks;
\item \textbf{Report clerk} --- fills a constrained schema (Section~\ref{sec:ch8-reports});
\item \textbf{Action proposer} --- tickets / revisit polygons / IFC patches, always behind approval.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Micro-scenario (teaching).}
``If seepage propagates two storeys above panel A-07, which assets need gondola access?'' Retriever loads A-07; topology aide expands vertical/lateral neighbours; report clerk drafts a priority list with evidence IDs; action proposer suggests a revisit polygon for Chapters~2--3 planning---but does not dispatch until approval.

\subsection{Tools worth exposing}
A small typed tool surface beats free-form SQL generation: get/search passports; expand neighbours; list observations; open evidence URIs; draft report; propose revisit; propose twin patch. This follows the spirit of schema-guided tool agents~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,schick2023toolformer}.

\subsection{Non-negotiables}
No invented Asset-IDs; no silent twin overwrites; no robot/UAV dispatch without geofence and safety checks~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,rawat2025llmmas}. Metrics that only score answer strings are insufficient for agent claims (Section~\ref{sec:ch8-dss}).

\paragraph{Bridge.}
Answers and proposals must land in artefacts FM systems already understand: reports.

\section{Inspection Report Generation and Management}
\label{sec:ch8-reports}

\subsection{Schema-first generation}
Free-form paragraphs are hard to audit. A practical report schema groups identity (asset/defect IDs), diagnosis, propagation notes, recommended actions, and trust flags (confidence, human verification). Identity fields must be subsets of retrieved evidence. Constrained generation is the report-level analogue of structured decoding used in BIM assistants and code QA systems~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes}.

\subsection{Walkthrough box}
A manager asks about water ingress on a vertical stack. The system returns neighbour passports, cites observation IDs whose images open in the twin UI, and either drafts a repair brief or abstains if evidence is thin. If a revisit is needed, the action proposer emits a planning hint for Chapters~2--3 rather than inventing a flight path in prose.

\section{Decision Support and Predictive Maintenance}
\label{sec:ch8-dss}

\subsection{What to evaluate in a book context}
Monograph readers need criteria that travel across implementations:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textbf{Grounding:} are cited IDs in the evidence set?~\cite{ji2023,shuster2021}
\item \textbf{Topology sensitivity:} do propagation questions improve when neighbours are included?
\item \textbf{Corpus fidelity:} for codes/manuals, does RAG beat parametric recall?~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,joffe2025buildingcodes}
\item \textbf{Operational usefulness:} can outputs drive schedules (gondola staging, revisit planning)?
\item \textbf{Agent safety (emerging):} tool success under approval and geofence constraints~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,rawat2025llmmas}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Case-study snapshot (illustrative)}
Published community evaluations already show that structured retrieval helps: hybrid defect-management RAG~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag}, safety/code RAG~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,joffe2025buildingcodes}, and facility-document RAG~\cite{krutli2025fmrags}. Separately, ongoing group experiments on logistics-warehouse façades (not yet peer-published; excluded from the reference list) report illustrative multi-hop gains when asset passports and neighbour expansion are used---useful for teaching \emph{why structure matters}, not as universal constants. Figures in this section visualise that teaching pattern.

\subsection{Towards predictive and cross-domain use}
Priority scoring and operational planning convert diagnosis into schedules. Transfer ideas (renovation sequencing, construction monitoring) appear in DT/agent programmes~\cite{rawat2025llmmas,luo2025llmdtrobot} and should be taught as \emph{schema reuse}, not as guaranteed forecasting. True deterioration prediction still needs temporal models beyond a single inspection epoch.

\subsection{Open issues}
Topology completeness and Asset-ID hygiene dominate error modes. Standard agent benchmarks for inspection (safe tool traces) are still missing. Stronger VLM grounding on twin entities, fully VLM-native twins, richer long-term agent memory, and swarm-continuous updates belong to Chapter~9's frontier outlook, building on planning chapters for multi-UAV operations. Privacy and governance questions for building LLMs~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction} remain open as portfolios scale.

\paragraph{Chapter takeaways.}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
\item Chapter~8 is the book's decision layer: it consumes Ch.~4--7 products and answers with grounded language, not with geometry alone.
\item LLM/VLM foundations (Transformers, instruction following, multimodal interfaces) explain capability; hallucination and ID errors explain why grounding is mandatory~\cite{vaswani2017attention,brown2020,ji2023}.
\item Memory must be layered: weights $\neq$ context window $\neq$ RAG index $\neq$ twin store~\cite{lewis2020,krutli2025fmrags,pan2024b}.
\item RAG, tools/agents, and structured asset knowledge are complementary; AEC already uses them for BIM, codes, FM, and safety---façade twins add topology and Asset-ID discipline~\cite{lewis2020,zheng2023bimassistant,lee2024ragsafety,luo2025llmdtrobot,rawat2025llmmas}.
\end{enumerate}
