\chapter{Digital Twin with LLMs in Inspection Analysis}
\label{ch:llm-dt}

\abstract*{This chapter is the monograph's AI decision-support phase: after autonomous flight, digital representation, and AI defect analysis, a language-ready digital twin is fused with large language models (LLMs) and, where useful, vision--language models (VLMs). Readers learn how to query historical maintenance records in ordinary language, generate auditable inspection reports, and obtain predictive maintenance recommendations under citation and approval rules, without assuming a site-specific training pipeline.}

\abstract{The monograph follows a four-phase inspection lifecycle: intelligent data acquisition (Chapters~2--3), digital representation and spatial context (Chapters~4 and~7), AI-driven defect analysis (Chapters~5--6), and next-generation decision support (this chapter). Chapter~8 therefore asks what an inspector or facility manager can do once a decision-ready twin exists: fuse visual defect evidence with generative AI, query historical maintenance records naturally, draft comprehensive inspection reports, and receive predictive maintenance recommendations that remain under professional control. The chapter first clarifies what a language-ready twin must expose; then develops three layers of ready-to-use LLM technique (language interface, evidence grounding, and memory with bounded procedures), positions VLMs relative to Chapter~6 detectors, and closes with reports and decision metrics. Site-specific fine-tuning lies outside this chapter; campaign facts stay in editioned twin memory and retrieval indexes. Perception remains the source of typed defect findings; language systems read structured twin memory under citation rules. Primary readers include graduate students and researchers building inspection decision layers, facility engineers who must audit AI-assisted reports, and implementers who need runtime control-layer contracts (the model harness below) and auditable workflows.}


\section{Digital Twins for Building Inspection}
\label{sec:ch8-dt}

The monograph is one end-to-end pipeline, matching the proposal's four didactic phases. Intelligent data acquisition (Chapters~2--3) produces safe coverage. Digital representation (Chapter~4) turns imagery into geometry, and GIS/GeoBIM (Chapter~7) give assets addresses in the city. AI-driven defect analysis (Chapters~5--6) names defects with typed, modality-tagged observations. This chapter is phase four: next-generation decision support. None of the earlier products, alone, answers the questions that dominate fa\c{c}ade operations and maintenance (O\&M): which panels participate in a multi-storey moisture path, what changed since the last campaign in the historical record, which assets should be staged for gondola access this quarter, or how to draft a repair brief that an engineer can audit. Those are decision-layer questions. They require connecting twin entities to language, retrieval, memory, multimodal evidence (including VLM-assisted reading when appropriate), and, when actions are proposed, tool-using agents.

Digital twins in construction and civil infrastructure are best understood as evolving information systems. Photorealistic viewers alone do not meet the same information requirements~\cite{boje2020,opoku2021,lu2020}. Survey work on construction twins emphasises lifecycle information flows, stakeholder coordination, and the gap between visualisation and actionable semantics. Building and heritage documentation literature likewise stresses diagnosis-linked semantics and monitoring provenance beyond geometry alone~\cite{bruno2018,tsilimantou2020}. For UAV fa\c{c}ade programmes the same lesson appears in sharper form: a mesh that cannot answer ``which maintainable host owns this detection?'' cannot support language-level O\&M.

A geometric twin answers location. A language-ready twin for inspection must also expose three further commitments. First, each maintainable host needs a stable \emph{asset identifier}: the host code already used in facility registers and work-order systems. Language answers must use that same code so that a drafted ticket can name the same host as the computerised maintenance management system (CMMS). After this first explanation, the chapter abbreviates the host code as Asset-ID. Second, provenance for every observation: sensor modality (for example RGB or infrared), capture time, detector or annotator signature, and a pointer back to imagery that an engineer can open. Third, relations that support propagation reasoning: which maintainable hosts sit above, below, or beside one another on a fa\c{c}ade stack. Without those commitments, an LLM can only chat over loose files: fluent, but not auditable for facility management.

UAV--BIM inspection reviews sketched early automation agendas that already pointed toward this decision layer~\cite{zhang2022}. Published GeoBIM-coupled twin modelling~\cite{zhang2025} and detection--reconstruction--registration pipelines~\cite{yang2025detreconreg} supply the geometric and identifier premise used below; related twin deployments extend to site monitoring beyond fa\c{c}ades~\cite{han2025}. Facility-oriented digital-twin discussions further stress that O\&M value appears only when twins connect to work processes~\cite{chen2023facility,lu2020}; construction NLP surveys likewise show why document stores must remain queryable living archives, not unstructured file dumps~\cite{wu2022}. Readers need not re-derive reconstruction or GIS methods here. Chapters~4--7 should be treated as an \emph{interface contract}: if the twin cannot tell a language system which Asset-ID hosts a detection, when the image was captured, and which neighbours sit next to that host, then language interfaces will invent structure.

Consider a concrete O\&M scene. After a warehouse fa\c{c}ade campaign, a manager asks whether mid-storey delamination on the west elevation is linked to moisture staining above. A mesh viewer can show both regions. A Chapter~6 detector can label both patches. Only a language-ready twin can assemble neighbour asset records (later called asset passports), cite observation IDs with openable images, and either draft a repair brief or abstain when evidence is thin. That scene is the running example for the rest of the chapter: the twin is the long-term store; the LLM is a reader and proposer under rules, not a second source of geometry. Later worked examples use short placeholder Asset-IDs such as \texttt{W-3-17} and \texttt{W-4-17} (west elevation, storey, bay) only to keep traces readable; live programmes should substitute their own facility register codes.

The same scene also clarifies what this chapter does \emph{not} do. It does not replace Chapters~5--6 perception models with a second detector stack inside the language system. Typed defect labels, confidences, and host attachments remain perception products. The decision layer consumes those products as structured records, retrieves related twin context, and generates language that can be audited. Readers who need detector design should return to Chapter~6; readers who need flight geometry should return to Chapters~2--4. What follows is how language systems should behave once those upstream products exist.


Beyond the warehouse vignette, language-ready twins must survive organisational reality. Inspection programmes rarely have a single owner: UAV contractors capture imagery, consultants interpret defects, facility teams issue work orders, and asset managers reconcile budgets. A twin that is only a visualisation shared by email cannot mediate those roles. Identifiers must be stable across organisations; observation provenance must survive file hand-offs; neighbour relations must be computable without calling the person who built the mesh. Those are information-system requirements, not rendering requirements~\cite{boje2020,opoku2021,lu2020,chen2023facility}.

It is also useful to distinguish three twin maturity levels that readers will meet in practice. A \emph{geometric twin} stores meshes, point clouds, or BIM shells. A \emph{semantic twin} attaches typed entities and Asset-IDs. A \emph{decision-ready twin} additionally exposes observation cards, topology for propagation, and interfaces that retrieval and tools can call. This chapter assumes the second level as a minimum and teaches the third. Many disappointing ``AI for inspection'' demos fail because they bolt an LLM onto the first level and then blame the model for inventing panels.

Stakeholder questions further clarify the interface contract. An inspector asks what changed since the last campaign. A facility manager asks which assets block a gondola day. A compliance officer asks which clause and which photo support a repair recommendation. A planner asks where to fly next. Only the last question returns partly to Chapters~2--3; the first three are decision-layer questions that need language over structured twin memory. If the twin cannot answer them with citations, no amount of prompt engineering will create auditability after the fact.



Historic and contemporary inspection practice already encodes many of the disciplines this chapter formalises for machines. Paper checklists force inspectors to name locations; photo logs force provenance; repair tickets force action fields. What digital twins and LLMs add is scale and queryability across campaigns; that addition does not licence teams to abandon those disciplines. Automation without checklist discipline amplifies error. Readers from facilities backgrounds should therefore recognise familiar quality controls in Asset-ID naming and observation provenance already practised in UAV--BIM and facility inspection workflows~\cite{zhang2022,chen2023facility}. The later cite-or-abstain rules (every named ID must come from retrieved evidence) extend those disciplines into machine-checkable form; only the carrier changes from clipboard to twin memory~\cite{lewis2020,shuster2021,ji2023}.

Geographic scale introduces further constraints. A single warehouse elevation is already multimodal and multi-storey; a campus or housing estate multiplies identifier collisions and manual-edition conflicts. Chapter~7's GIS integration is the right place to keep city-scale identity coherent. Chapter~8 assumes that coherence and refuses to ``fix'' identity drift with clever language. If two contractors use different panel codes for the same host, the language system should surface the collision, not pick a winner in prose~\cite{zhang2025,han2025}. The same identity lesson appears when recent multi-agent twin systems scale from room graphs to city graphs: language access helps only if the underlying identifiers and edges remain trustworthy~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,lu2026highwaymas,ma2026tenquestions}.

Seasoned inspectors already practise a form of grounding: they photograph before writing, they name bays before diagnosing, and they refuse to sign a report that cannot be walked on site. The decision-ready twin simply makes those habits machine-checkable across campaigns. Table~\ref{tab:ch8-twin-contract} is therefore not an IT wishlist; it is the digital continuation of field craft~\cite{zhang2022,chen2023facility}. When organisations skip the contract and buy a chat interface first, they usually rediscover the same craft six months later, after the first invented Asset-ID reaches a work order. Procurement language often asks for ``AI dashboards.'' A more precise ask is whether the system can return openable evidence for every named asset, abstain when evidence is thin, expand neighbours from BIM adjacency, not from text similarity, and gate writes. Vendors that answer only with model-size slides are selling parametric memory; inspection buyers need twin interfaces~\cite{boje2020,opoku2021,lu2020}. Chapter~8 gives readers vocabulary to insist on those interfaces in procurement and design reviews.

Three gaps in neighbouring practice motivate the rest of the chapter. BIM assistants and NL model queries improve navigation, but navigating an object is not topology-aware defect diagnosis across fa\c{c}ade hosts~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,hellin2025bimmas,liu2025bimcoder}. Document-similarity RAG improves clause lookup, but embedding neighbourhood is not BIM adjacency for moisture-path questions~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,jeon2025hybridrag,peng2024}. Multi-agent demos can look autonomous, yet fluent tickets without cite-or-abstain and write gates are not auditable O\&M artefacts~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,ma2026tenquestions}. Sections~\ref{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}--\ref{sec:ch8-dss} therefore specialise ready-to-use LLM layers and a runtime control layer (model harness) to close exactly those gaps.

The remainder of the chapter follows a single decision-layer order: twin contract and gaps first, L1--L3 and harness second, assembly and multimodal agents third, reports and operability metrics last. Site-specific fine-tuning is outside the agenda of this chapter. Campaign facts belong in editioned twin memory and RAG indexes, not in weights that will be stale after the next flight~\cite{lewis2020,krutli2025fmrags,ma2026tenquestions,saka2024gptconstruction}.

After reading this chapter, a student or implementer should be able to (i)~list the minimum twin commitments in Table~\ref{tab:ch8-twin-contract} and explain one failure mode for each missing commitment; (ii)~map a fa\c{c}ade question to Layers~L1--L3 and to one harness part in Table~\ref{tab:ch8-harness}; (iii)~assemble a cite-or-abstain package for a propagation or historical-record question using passports and BIM neighbours; (iv)~state the VLM-versus-detector split in Section~\ref{sec:ch8-multimodal}; and (v)~show a tool TRACE in which a write or revisit request stops at a human gate~\cite{peng2024,yao2023react,liang2023helm,hellin2025bimmas}. Those outcomes define a decision-layer contract that earlier chapters can satisfy and later chapters can harden. They do not introduce a new detector.

\section{Foundations of LLMs for Civil Engineering Applications}
\label{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}

Contemporary LLMs rest on the Transformer architecture~\cite{vaswani2017attention}. Self-attention models long-range dependencies in text, which matters when an inspection question mixes symptoms, locations, and code clauses in one utterance. Ready-to-use models already follow natural-language instructions and can draft fluent building prose~\cite{brown2020,ouyang2022instructgpt}. Chain-of-thought prompting can elicit intermediate steps that help multi-hop questions~\cite{wei2022cot}, but it does not guarantee that a named fa\c{c}ade panel actually exists in the twin. Reviews of GPT-style tools in construction make the same caution for AEC practice: capability is real, governance and factual grounding are not automatic~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction}.

Beyond architecture and prompting, civil deployments should also separate three LLM behaviours that vendors often conflate. Instruction-following models can answer under templates supplied by an orchestrator, so short replies can separate observation from hypothesis~\cite{ouyang2022instructgpt,brown2020}. Tool-calling models can request typed functions without writing world state into weights, which keeps passport reads and neighbour expansion outside the model~\cite{schick2023toolformer,yao2023react}. Long-context models can hold packed evidence digests, but they still need validators: a longer window does not invent Asset-IDs that were never retrieved~\cite{lewis2020,ji2023,liang2023helm}. Construction GPT reviews and building-application agendas therefore treat governance, data readiness, and human oversight as design variables alongside model choice~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,ma2026tenquestions}. For fa\c{c}ade inspection those variables become edition pins, modality tags, cite-or-abstain checks, and write gates. Contemporary foundation-model interfaces also differ in shape: chat-tuned APIs emphasise instruction following; tool-calling APIs expose typed function schemas; retrieval-augmented deployments keep site facts outside the weights. Inspection systems should choose and compose those interfaces by contract~\cite{ouyang2022instructgpt,schick2023toolformer,lewis2020,saka2024gptconstruction}.

This chapter therefore takes a deliberate stance. We treat LLMs as \emph{deployable language components}: systems that are prompted, constrained, and wired to twin stores and tools. We do not develop a site-specific training or fine-tuning agenda. Campaign facts change every flight; the honest path for living buildings is to keep those facts outside the model weights, in editioned manuals and Asset-ID twin memory, and to let the model read them at query time~\cite{lewis2020,krutli2025fmrags}. It also helps to separate three behaviours that are often conflated. \emph{Linguistic competence} is the ability to parse and produce fluent text about buildings. \emph{Parametric recall} is whatever generic knowledge the weights already contain, useful for vocabulary, unreliable for site-specific Asset-IDs. \emph{Grounded reasoning} is the ability to answer using evidence that the system retrieved or was given in the current session. Fa\c{c}ade O\&M lives or dies on the third behaviour. An LLM that can write elegant moisture narratives without citing twin observations is not an inspection assistant; it is a drafting toy.

This section introduces generative language technology for civil engineering and attaches it to inspection analysis. It organises ready-to-use techniques into three layers that compose into one decision stack (Table~\ref{tab:ch8-tech-families}). Perception remains Chapter~6's job; here language technologies consume typed, modality-tagged observations. Layer~L1 is the \emph{language interface}: how an off-the-shelf model is steered with prompts and answer templates. Layer~L2 is \emph{evidence grounding}: how retrieval, structured outputs, and tools keep answers attached to twin records. Layer~L3 is \emph{memory and bounded procedures}: how long-term twin memory and gated agents support multi-step O\&M work without unsupervised action. The rest of this section develops the three layers as continuous narrative and then shows how published AEC systems specialise, or fail to specialise, to fa\c{c}ade inspection.

Layer~L1 begins with what a ready model can already do when an orchestrator supplies a careful prompt. The model can parse mixed spatial-symptom questions such as ``mid-storey hollow with upstairs staining,'' draft readable briefs for different stakeholders, and follow a short answer template that forces named Asset-IDs, separates observations from hypotheses, and emits an abstention code when checks fail~\cite{ouyang2022instructgpt,ji2023}. Stepwise prompting can then verbalise a propagation hypothesis, but only after neighbours have already entered the evidence package; otherwise the model may invent a structural story that merely sounds plausible~\cite{wei2022cot,ji2023}. A practical system prompt for fa\c{c}ade O\&M can therefore be short: classify intent; speak only about Asset-IDs present in the retrieved package; preserve modality tags from Chapter~6; and emit \texttt{ABSTAIN:<reason>} when evidence is thin. Few-shot examples in the prompt should demonstrate abstention, not only success. What L1 must \emph{not} be asked to do is remember last campaign's Asset-IDs from parametric recall, invent crack lengths, or override twin facts when a hurried manager says ``just mark it repaired.'' Those duties belong to grounding and gates, not to fluent wording.

Layer~L2 is where inspection systems leave chat demos. Retrieval-augmented generation loads editioned manuals or observation cards at query time~\cite{lewis2020,shuster2021}; civil deployments already cover codes, safety manuals, facility-management documents, and defect dialogue~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,krutli2025fmrags,jeon2025hybridrag,he2025regulationhybrid}. Adaptive and self-reflective variants retry when retrieval looks weak~\cite{gao2023hyde,asai2024selfrag,jeong2024adaptive}, but the inspection design choice is sharper than ``add RAG'': for asset-centric questions, prefer entity or passport units over arbitrary PDF chunks~\cite{peng2024}. Structured and constrained generation then keeps outputs machine-checkable, report fields, CMMS maps, and validators that require cited Asset-IDs to be subsets of the evidence set~\cite{pan2024b,chen2024bimllmontology,iversen2026,buga2025llmfmsql}. Tools and function calling separate reasoning from world actions: typed calls such as \texttt{get\_passport}, \texttt{expand\_neighbours}, \texttt{list\_observations}, \texttt{draft\_report}, and \texttt{propose\_revisit} let the model request twin facts without inventing them~\cite{schick2023toolformer,yao2023react,guo2025bimllmquery,liu2025bimcoder,li2025b,li2025c}. Twin writes and dispatch remain behind server-side approval~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent}. Long context does not authorise teams to dump an entire elevation into the prompt. Packing should prefer structured digests, Asset-ID, top symptoms, recent observation IDs, neighbour IDs, short location phrases, with image paths so humans can open evidence. Raw pixel dumps are avoided. If a digest still overflows, drop low-confidence observations first, not the topology links needed for a propagation question~\cite{lewis2020,asai2024selfrag}.

Layer~L3 makes the stack durable across sessions and actionable under control. Before agents, readers need a memory ladder (Table~\ref{tab:ch8-memory}). Cognitive accounts of agent memory often distinguish working, episodic, semantic, and procedural stores; the inspection ladder is a field specialisation of that idea rather than a second taxonomy. Parametric memory is whatever generic knowledge the weights already hold, useful for language, almost useless for last week's crack growth on a specific warehouse elevation~\cite{brown2020}. Contextual memory is the prompt window and short dialogue state, the working store: powerful, length-limited, and session-fragile; tomorrow's new chat may forget that ``west'' meant the loading-dock face unless an external store kept the alias. Retrieval memory loads editioned manuals and observation cards on demand, the episodic and semantic digests that must stay edition-pinned~\cite{lewis2020,shuster2021,krutli2025fmrags}. Structured long-term twin memory is the Asset-ID store itself, geometry, topology, and time-stamped observations in BIM/GIS, optionally linked to knowledge graphs~\cite{pan2024b,zhang2025}. For fa\c{c}ade O\&M the twin is authoritative; the model should read it through retrieval and tools, not pretend the weights remember the site. Bounded agents then compose planning, memory, and tools into procedures such as tickets or revisits under geofence and human gates: procedural memory that lives in the harness, not as silent weight updates~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,rawat2025llmmas,pan2026graphdtgpt,zhang2025llmagentschema}. They are not a licence for unsupervised aircraft command or silent twin mutation. ReAct-style loops without retrieval merely re-enact parametric storytelling with extra steps~\cite{yao2023react}; tool calls without twin memory can hit the wrong host; unchecked memory streams can accumulate speculative notes as if they were facts~\cite{park2023generativeagents}; write gates therefore belong in the harness~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent}.

Agent-memory research often writes a generate-update-evolve loop over experiential entries~\cite{park2023generativeagents,yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents}. For fa\c{c}ade inspection we keep the same symbols but force \emph{externalised} evolution: the twin and harness change, model weights do not. Let $S_t$ be the reasoning state of query~$t$ (intent, tool TRACE, validator outcomes) and $M_t$ the external memory (twin store plus edition pins and procedural traces). Then
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ch8-mem-loop}
M_{t+1}=\mathrm{Update}\!\bigl(\mathrm{Generate}(S_t,M_t),\,M_t\bigr),\qquad
\pi_{t+1}=\mathrm{Evolve}_{\mathrm{ext}}(\pi_t,M_{t+1}),
\end{equation}
where $\mathrm{Generate}$ may emit a passport digest, a rejected-draft note, or a revisit hint; $\mathrm{Update}$ merges only through gated writeback or procedural logs; and $\mathrm{Evolve}_{\mathrm{ext}}$ changes tool policy, edition pins, or retrieved packages inside the harness, never silent fine-tuning on site text~\cite{lewis2020,luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent}. In practical terms, session aliases remain short-lived, modality-tagged observations become episodic cards, asset passports provide semantic digests, and approved traces preserve procedural memory. The twin store, edition pins, and gates therefore evolve the operating package while keeping site facts outside the model weights.

These three layers are meant to compose, not to compete. Consider again: ``Is mid-storey hollow on the west elevation related to upstairs staining, and should we stage a gondola this quarter?'' L1 parses the mixed utterance and forces a template. L2 tools fetch seed passports and expand BIM neighbours; RAG packs those cards plus any pinned manual clauses; structured generation emits a draft whose IDs are subsets of the evidence set. L3 ensures that the long-term twin, not yesterday's chat, is the store being read, and that any ticket or revisit proposal waits behind a human gate. Skip L2 and the answer becomes parametric storytelling; skip structured checks and gates and fluent text leaks into CMMS. That composition is generative AI for decision support made operational for fa\c{c}ade O\&M~\cite{lewis2020,yao2023react,ji2023}.

What holds the three layers together in a real system is a \emph{model harness}. In software testing, a harness is scaffolding that exercises a component under controlled conditions. In contemporary LLM engineering the same word has come to mean the code-owned control layer around a replaceable language model: it builds prompts, chooses retrieval units, exposes typed tools, validates outputs against contracts, records traces, and decides when to abstain or escalate. Agent surveys already describe planning, memory, and tools as the ingredients of autonomy~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent}; tool-using reason-act loops and self-supervised tool learning show how models request actions without owning the world state~\cite{yao2023react,schick2023toolformer}. Holistic evaluation frameworks such as HELM further remind readers that what is measured (scenarios, metrics, and standardised run conditions) is itself a harness for comparison, not a single accuracy number~\cite{liang2023helm}. For fa\c{c}ade inspection we specialise that idea: the harness is where Asset-ID contracts, edition pins, modality tags, and write gates live so that swapping the underlying LLM does not silently rewrite O\&M policy.

The inspection harness has five cooperating parts (Table~\ref{tab:ch8-harness}). The \emph{intake and routing} part classifies intent and resolves natural-language locations against GeoBIM aliases before any generation. The \emph{evidence assembly} part loads passports, neighbours, observation cards, and pinned manuals, the RAG and packing duties of Layer~L2~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024,krutli2025fmrags}. The \emph{tool surface} part offers a small typed API (\texttt{get\_passport}, \texttt{expand\_neighbours}, \texttt{list\_observations}, \texttt{draft\_report}, \texttt{propose\_revisit}) and refuses free-form SQL or flight commands; AEC tool agents for IFC, energy analysis, and FM robots illustrate the same separation between reasoning and world actions under approval~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,zhang2025llmagentschema,luo2025llmdtrobot,guo2025bimllmquery}. The \emph{contract validators} part checks machine-checkable obligations after drafting: every named Asset-ID must appear in the retrieved package; numeric defect claims must point to observation fields; modality conflicts must remain disclosed; write or dispatch requests must hit a human gate~\cite{ji2023,pan2024b,buga2025llmfmsql}. The \emph{trace and evaluation} part stores which tools ran, which edition was pinned, and which validator passed or failed, so answers can be replayed and graded, an inspection reading of multi-metric, scenario-based evaluation with multi-metric scenarios. Chat fluency alone is insufficient~\cite{liang2023helm,shuster2021}. Adaptive retrieval retries belong inside the harness as controlled loops, not as ad-hoc prompt improvisation~\cite{asai2024selfrag,jeong2024adaptive}.

Two consequences follow for procurement and teaching. First, a prompt-only prototype is not yet a harness: instructions such as ``always cite Asset-IDs'' can be ignored by a fluent model, whereas a validator that blocks delivery when the ID subset check fails is load-bearing. Second, model substitution should be a harness test, not a project rewrite, if validators, tool schemas, and twin interfaces stay fixed, changing the hosted LLM should change phrasing, not which sources are eligible or whether a write gate exists~\cite{liang2023helm,saka2024gptconstruction}. Emerging enterprise discussions of harness engineering emphasise relocating behaviour from prompts into versioned contracts, manifests, and traces; this chapter keeps that intuition while citing only peer-published grounding, tool, agent, and evaluation results. In short: Layers~L1--L3 name the capabilities; the harness is the software that enforces them on every query.

Recent building-oriented LLM syntheses make the same shift from model selection alone to system design. A 2026 ``ten questions'' agenda for building applications organises open problems around data readiness, trust, interoperability, and human oversight with data readiness and oversight placed ahead of parameter counts~\cite{ma2026tenquestions}. That agenda aligns with this chapter: fa\c{c}ade O\&M succeeds when Asset-IDs, editioned manuals, and approval gates are ready, not when a larger chat model is procured. Multi-agent systems published in 2025--2026 further show how harness ideas appear in AEC practice. Graph-grounded multi-agent twins answer natural-language questions by decomposing work into decision, query, and extraction agents whose outputs remain tied to structured twin graphs~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt}. Human--digital-twin interaction studies replace steep GUI workflows with leader/reasoning/member agent teams that orchestrate twin functions under evaluation with domain experts~\cite{lu2026highwaymas}. Multi-agent virtual in-situ modelling pipelines connect design-to-operation digital twins inside BIM~\cite{lee2025multiagentvbm}; construction-management frameworks combine graph and vector memory for coordinated agents~\cite{rawat2025llmmas}; router-driven expert-selection agents for foundation design show that specialised roles can improve task routing when humans remain responsible for safety-critical checks~\cite{youwai2026foundationmas}. IFC tool agents and FM robot brokers likewise emphasise typed tools and approval~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot,zhang2025llmagentschema}. Multi-agent BIM information retrieval and condensed multi-LLM retrieval agents published in 2025--2026 further show that natural-language access to model objects can be decomposed into specialised roles without abandoning typed interfaces~\cite{hellin2025bimmas,koh2026bimllmretr}; facility-data RAG on BIM O\&M stores shows the same structure-first habit for document-model joining~\cite{quan2026bimfacrag}. None of these systems is a warehouse fa\c{c}ade inspector out of the box. Their transferable lesson for 2024--2026 practice is consistent: put structure and gates outside the weights; keep the LLM as a replaceable composer inside a harness.

Hallucination surveys explain what goes wrong when the discipline is missing: fluent systems invent entities, numbers, and citations~\cite{ji2023}. In inspection the costly failures are concrete, invented Asset-IDs, repair advice without attributable evidence, and summaries that erase disagreement between RGB and infrared observations. Grounding therefore means more than a clever prompt. Constrained schemas, retrieval and its adaptive variants, tool-using reason-act loops, and typed structured knowledge recur across NLP and AEC~\cite{lewis2020,gao2023hyde,asai2024selfrag,jeong2024adaptive,peng2024,yao2023react,schick2023toolformer,pan2024b}. Graph-oriented and hierarchical retrieval further remind readers that document similarity is not building adjacency~\cite{peng2024,sarthi2024raptor,sun2024tog}. Semantic embeddings cluster phrases such as ``damp'', ``stain'', and ``seepage''; building spaces cluster panels that share joints, storeys, and drainage paths. A hybrid retriever may use embeddings to rank symptom digests inside an already topology-constrained candidate set; it must not use embeddings to invent the candidate set for propagation questions~\cite{peng2024,jeon2025hybridrag,khattab2020}. When system rules, retrieved manuals, twin facts, and user requests conflict, precedence should be explicit: safety and write gates first; twin facts and observation fields second; pinned manual editions third; user style preferences last~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction}.

Civil and building applications already exercise these patterns, and each maps onto inspection with a particular boundary. BIM virtual assistants, query--DSL systems, and NL-to-BIM stacks help users navigate models~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,guo2025bimllmquery,liu2025bimcoder}; building semantic query systems combine RAG and structured stores~\cite{li2025c,li2025b}; and code, safety, defect, and facility-management RAG show the value of editioned corpora and structured evidence~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,yang2024promptcode,jeon2025hybridrag,krutli2025fmrags}. LLM--ontology checkers, structured FM querying, energy-analysis agents, IFC tool agents, and graph-twin systems further show that schemas, typed tools, and approval can make language interfaces operational~\cite{chen2024bimllmontology,iversen2026,li2024kgllmcheck,buga2025llmfmsql,zhang2025llmagentschema,luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,pan2026graphdtgpt}. These works supply useful control patterns, while high-resolution fa\c{c}ade datasets and physical quantification determine the granularity of claims that the language layer can responsibly make~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2025icra,zhao2026physical}.

For this monograph, Chapters~2--3 retain flight and coverage decisions; Chapter~4 supplies registered geometry; Chapters~5--6 write modality-tagged observations; and Chapter~7 maintains GeoBIM Asset-IDs and spatial identity~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg,zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical,han2025}. Chapter~8 turns those existing products into evidence packages for queries, reports, and gated action proposals. Readers should therefore adopt asset passports and BIM neighbours as retrieval units, pin manuals by edition, and treat corpus mismatch as an operational risk: success on public code questions does not establish reliability on a private fa\c{c}ade handbook~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,jeon2025hybridrag,ma2026tenquestions}. Community BIM assistants, code RAG, IFC agents, and graph-twin multi-agent systems contribute transferable control ideas, not a ready-made fa\c{c}ade inspector~\cite{guo2025bimllmquery,lee2024ragsafety,gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot,pan2026graphdtgpt}.

Memory failures make the ladder concrete. If a manager clarifies that ``west'' means the loading-dock face and tomorrow's chat forgets it, the repair is to persist the alias or require GeoBIM seed selection before generation, not to hope the model ``remembers.'' If RAG returns an outdated maintenance PDF, the repair is edition pins and corpus smoke tests~\cite{krutli2025fmrags,joffe2025buildingcodes}. If the model invents a crack length from generic web knowledge, the repair is to ban numeric defect claims unless an observation field supplies them~\cite{ji2023,zhao2026physical}. If the twin holds the correct Asset-ID but the orchestrator never fetched it, that is an infrastructure failure, not a wording problem~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024}. Who may write to twin memory after an LLM draft, how long dialogue clarifications persist, and which manual edition wins when two PDFs disagree are therefore foundations questions, not afterthought IT policy. For fa\c{c}ade O\&M we recommend a simple rule: the twin store is write-gated; the dialogue window is ephemeral; RAG indexes are editioned; parametric memory is never treated as a site database~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,rawat2025llmmas}.

Selecting an LLM stack for inspection is likewise a harness problem more than a model-selection problem. Teams should assess the five harness parts above, especially whether validators and tool gates are code-owned, plus native tool-calling reliability, long-context behaviour on passport sets, willingness to abstain under constrained decoding, on-prem versus cloud constraints for building data, and cost per grounded answer ahead of cost per chat token~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,wang2024surveyagents,liang2023helm}. A smaller model inside a strict cite-or-abstain harness often beats a larger model that improvises Asset-IDs in an unvalidated chat loop. Even before laboratory scoring, ten hand-checked answers already separate grounded homework from chat demos: listed Asset-IDs $\subseteq$ retrieved set; hypotheses tagged; modality tags preserved; abstention when neighbours are missing~\cite{ji2023,shuster2021,lewis2020}. The teaching takeaway is therefore three design choices: retrieve over maintainable assets and neighbours; force citation of IDs that appear in the retrieved set; and place irreversible actions behind approval gates. Sections~\ref{sec:ch8-dt-to-language}--\ref{sec:ch8-multimodal} next show how twin records and multimodal evidence make Layers~L2 and~L3 operational.



In summary of the foundations, Layers~L1--L3 name what a ready model can do under control, and the harness names the software that enforces those behaviours on every query. Readers who skip validators and tool gates will rediscover parametric storytelling regardless of model size~\cite{ji2023,liang2023helm,ma2026tenquestions}. The next section turns those layers into twin records: observation cards, asset passports, and intent-conditioned assembly.

\section{From Digital Twins to Language Reasoning}
\label{sec:ch8-dt-to-language}

Language reasoning over an inspection twin is not free-form storytelling. It is assembly of evidence that earlier chapters already created, the operational substrate for Layers~L2 and~L3 in Section~\ref{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}. We keep three layers conceptually separate even when implementations differ. An \emph{observation record} is one defect sighting with sensor provenance (imagery, evidence, semantics, and host attachment, the teaching content often packaged as an integrated defect profile). An \emph{asset passport} aggregates observations and geometry or topology for a maintainable host (an integrated asset profile for retrieval). \emph{Query assembly} decides which passports and neighbours enter the context before generation; neighbour expansion along fa\c{c}ade adjacency supports vertical propagation reading. Perception writes observations; facility managers think in assets; language models must not invent either~\cite{pan2024b,zheng2023bimassistant}.

In UAV inspection practice, useful observation records store host Asset-ID, time, modality (RGB, infrared, and related channels), detector signature, pose or surface coordinates, and paths to imagery. Registration should attach detections to BIM hosts before any LLM is involved~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg}. Natural-language location phrases help models (``west elevation, third bay above the loading dock''), while metric coordinates remain for field navigation. Asset passports then add symptom summaries, geometry for severity normalisation, and neighbour links (above, below, lateral). Those links enable propagation-style reading, for example, mid-storey delamination considered with upstairs moisture, without confusing a document-similarity graph with a structural or BIM adjacency graph~\cite{peng2024}.

Figure~\ref{fig:ch8-obs-passport} separates what perception writes (one sighting with provenance) from what facility language needs (a host-level passport with neighbours and recent observations). The LLM must consume the second form; it must not invent either form from generic web text.
Geometry and BIM hosts from Chapter~4 provide attachment points, Chapters~5--6 provide labelled observations and confidence, and Chapter~7 provides city-scale Asset-IDs. This chapter consumes those products to make retrieval, memory, reports, and actions traceable.

A minimal observation schema for language use includes observation ID, host Asset-ID, campaign or capture time, modality tag, defect type and confidence from Chapter~6, optional severity features derived from geometry, image or crop URI, and a short human-readable location phrase. Optional fields include weather flags, inspector notes, and links to prior observations on the same host. What must not be left as free text alone is the host Asset-ID. A temporary image filename or folder label created at capture time records how a file was stored; it is not a facility register code. If the only link from image to host is such a filename convention, retrieval will eventually invent neighbours. Enterprise RAG often chunks PDFs, but inspection questions are usually about maintainable hosts and their neighbourhoods. Passports therefore package, for each Asset-ID, a compact symptom digest, recent observation IDs, geometry summaries used for severity normalisation, and explicit neighbour lists. Severity normalisation matters because a 20\,cm crack on a small panel is not the same operational object as a 20\,cm crack on a large wall bay; the language layer inherits that normalisation from twin geometry~\cite{zhao2026physical}. Neighbour lists should come from BIM or GeoBIM adjacency, not from embedding similarity among report sentences.

Query assembly is where inspection differs from generic enterprise RAG. Write the already-built records once, then let intent choose the package. An asset passport for host~$a$ packages identity, geometry summary~$G(a)$, one-hop BIM neighbours~$N_1(a)$, observation set~$O(a)$, and a compact symptom digest~$d(a)$:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ch8-passport}
P(a)=\bigl\langle a,\,G(a),\,N_1(a),\,O(a),\,d(a)\bigr\rangle.
\end{equation}
Given seed Asset-IDs~$A_q$ resolved from question~$q$ and hop depth~$k(\iota)$ set by intent~$\iota$ (zero for local lookup, one for propagation), the BIM neighbourhood is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ch8-khop}
\mathcal{N}_{k}(A_q)=\bigl\{v:\exists\,a\in A_q,\;\mathrm{dist}_{\mathrm{BIM}}(a,v)\le k\bigr\},
\end{equation}
and the evidence package packed for generation is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ch8-package}
\mathcal{C}(q)=\mathrm{Pack}\!\Bigl(\bigl\{P(v):v\in\mathcal{N}_{k(\iota)}(A_q)\bigr\},\,R_{\mathrm{man}}(q),\,e\Bigr),
\end{equation}
where $R_{\mathrm{man}}(q)$ is the optional edition-pinned manual slice and $e$ the edition pin itself~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024,joffe2025buildingcodes}. A local lookup (``what defects were logged on asset~$A$ last month?'') uses $k=0$. A propagation question uses neighbours along fa\c{c}ade topology, not merely the top-$k$ text chunks that share vocabulary with the query. A floor- or elevation-level audit replaces hop expansion by a Chapter~7 spatial filter on passports. Intent therefore changes \emph{which} twin records enter~$\mathcal{C}(q)$, not only the prompt wording~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024}. Editioned manuals and safety clauses may join~$R_{\mathrm{man}}$ when the question mixes site evidence with code language~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,krutli2025fmrags}.

One recurring implementation pattern organises observations into asset passports and expands neighbours along BIM adjacency for multi-hop diagnosis. Published siblings share the grounding intuition on other corpora: hybrid defect RAG~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag}, code and safety RAG~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety}, facility-document RAG~\cite{krutli2025fmrags}, and BIM assistants~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant}. The monograph lesson is the pattern: inspection questions are asset-centric, so retrieval units should be asset-centric. When the assembly cannot support a claim, the correct behaviour is abstention or escalation to a human inspector, not a confident paragraph.

Worked micro-example. Suppose the manager asks: ``Is water ingress on the west stack likely propagating from above?'' Assembly should load the target passport, upstairs and lateral neighbours, observation cards tagged RGB and infrared, and any editioned moisture-related maintenance note linked to those Asset-IDs. Generation may then hypothesise a path \emph{only} if neighbour observations exist; otherwise it must say that propagation cannot be supported from the twin. That discipline is language reasoning over a twin, not storytelling beside a twin.


Implementation teams often ask how large a passport should be. Too small, and propagation reading starves. Too large, and the context window fills with irrelevant neighbours from an entire elevation. A practical teaching heuristic is intent-conditioned expansion: local lookup uses zero hops; propagation uses one hop (or a small $k$) along BIM adjacency; elevation audits use spatial filters from Chapter~7 . Unbounded hops are avoided~\cite{peng2024,zhang2025}. Manual clauses are retrieved only when the question mentions compliance, safety, or repair standards~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety}.

Indexing choices should follow the same heuristic. Vector indexes over passport digests support fuzzy symptom search (``hollow sounding panels with staining above''). Graph or relational indexes over Asset-IDs support exact neighbour expansion. Keyword indexes over observation IDs support audit lookups. Hybrid retrieval is attractive precisely because inspection questions mix fuzzy language with exact identifiers~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag,krutli2025fmrags}. What should not be hybridised away is the authority of topology: embedding similarity must not silently replace BIM adjacency when the question is about propagation.

Abstention deserves equal teaching time. Engineers already know how to say ``insufficient evidence'' on site; language systems must learn the same habit. Let $\hat{y}$ be a draft answer and $\mathrm{IDs}(\cdot)$ the Asset-IDs (and observation IDs) named in a string or package. The machine-checkable cite-or-abstain gate is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ch8-cite}
\mathrm{IDs}(\hat{y})\subseteq\mathrm{IDs}\bigl(\mathcal{C}(q)\bigr)
\quad\text{else return }\texttt{ABSTAIN}.
\end{equation}
Severity claims must further point to members of~$O(\cdot)$ inside~$\mathcal{C}(q)$, and any propagation path must be a walk inside the expanded BIM neighbourhood of Eq.~\eqref{eq:ch8-khop}. If checks fail, the system returns a structured refusal plus the missing evidence type (no upstairs neighbour, conflicting modalities, stale campaign, and so on). That behaviour is more valuable than a fluent paragraph that cannot be opened in the twin UI~\cite{ji2023,shuster2021}.

A second worked micro-example clarifies elevation audits. The manager asks: ``Summarise west-elevation issues above level three that need gondola access.'' Assembly should apply a Chapter~7 spatial filter, load the resulting passport set, keep modality tags, and rank by severity features inherited from geometry. It should not narrate the whole building, and it should not drop Asset-IDs in favour of storey prose alone. The output is a decision artefact for scheduling, not a travelogue.



Managers often ask whether a stain is ``new.'' Querying historical maintenance records is a first-class proposal capability and a first-class assembly intent. Cross-campaign comparison requires the same Asset-ID across time, comparable modality coverage, prior observation IDs or work-order links when available, and explicit handling of missing priors. If the previous campaign lacks infrared, the system must not invent a thermal delta. If geometry was remeshed and Asset-IDs were remapped carelessly, language will compare the wrong hosts. A natural-language question such as ``what was repaired on the west elevation after the 2024 campaign?'' should retrieve editioned twin memory and any linked approved reports; it must abstain when those IDs are absent rather than invent a repair narrative~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg,chen2023facility}. Longitudinal discipline is therefore twin hygiene before it is model cleverness~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg,zhao2026physical}. Perception may emit confidences; geometry may support length or area features; O\&M may need ordinal severity for scheduling. The language layer should not convert ordinal triage into false millimetre certainty. Prefer phrases tied to fields (``severity=medium from geometry-normalised crack length; confidence=0.71 from detector'') over cinematic adjectives. Physical quantification pipelines show why numbers need provenance~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}; the LLM's job is to carry that provenance into sentences, not to decorate it.

Algorithm~\ref{alg:ch8-assembly} summarises assembly as a numbered procedure. It is deliberately dull: every branch is a place students try to be clever, and where inspection systems usually break~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024,jeon2025hybridrag}.

\begin{algorithm}[htbp]
\caption{Intent-conditioned twin assembly for a grounded inspection answer.}
\label{alg:ch8-assembly}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\Require natural-language query $q$
\Ensure draft answer, or a structured abstention code
\State $\mathit{intent} \gets \textsc{Classify}(q)$
\State $\mathit{seeds} \gets \textsc{ResolveAssets}(q)$ \Comment{else \texttt{ABSTAIN:asset\_unresolved}}
\State $\mathit{packs} \gets \{\textsc{Passport}(s): s\in\mathit{seeds}\}$
\If{$\mathit{intent}=\texttt{propagation}$}
  \State $\mathit{packs} \gets \mathit{packs}\cup\textsc{Expand}(\mathit{seeds},k{=}1)$
\EndIf
\If{$\mathit{intent}=\texttt{elevation\_audit}$}
  \State $\mathit{packs} \gets \textsc{SpatialFilter}(\mathit{packs},q)$
\EndIf
\State $\mathit{obs} \gets \textsc{ListObservations}(\mathit{packs})$
\State $\mathit{manuals} \gets \textsc{RetrieveManuals}(q)$ if $\textsc{NeedsCode}(q)$, else $\emptyset$
\State $\mathcal{C}(q) \gets \{\mathit{intent},\mathit{packs},\mathit{obs},\mathit{manuals},\mathit{edition\_pin}\}$
\State $\hat{y} \gets \textsc{Generate}(\mathcal{C}(q))$ \Comment{cite-or-abstain}
\State $\textsc{ValidateIDs}(\hat{y},\mathcal{C}(q))$ \Comment{else \texttt{ABSTAIN:id\_not\_in\_package}}
\State \Return $\hat{y}$
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

Natural-language locations remain ambiguous: ``third bay'' may mean different hosts after remeshing. Resolution against GeoBIM aliases must precede passport fetch. Fuzzy string match can propose candidates; it must not silently pick one when two candidates score closely. Disambiguation questions are successful system behaviour, not UX failure~\cite{zhang2025,han2025,guo2025bimllmquery}. The same discipline appears in recent twin-query systems that refuse to answer from unconstrained text when the structured store lacks a path~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,buga2025llmfmsql}: for inspection, ``no upstairs neighbour in the twin'' is a better product behaviour than a fluent moisture story.

Published 2024--2025 retrieval practice also clarifies what not to reinvent. Hybrid defect-management RAG and safety-manual RAG show gains when corpora are editioned and noisy maintenance language is expected~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag,lee2024ragsafety}; code and regulation hybrids show that clause retrieval must still meet site evidence before advice becomes operational~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,he2025regulationhybrid,yang2024promptcode}. Facility-document RAG and structured FM querying show that building corpora scale only when schemas and Asset-ID links exist~\cite{krutli2025fmrags,buga2025llmfmsql,arslan2024ragenergy}. Translating those results into UAV fa\c{c}ade O\&M means treating observation cards and asset passports as the primary retrieval units, manuals as secondary pinned context, and topology expansion as a first-class assembly step~\cite{peng2024,pan2024b,ma2026tenquestions}.




Assembly discipline can be restated as teaching narrative that facilities and software teams share. Local lookups should load only the target passport and recent observations; stuffing an entire elevation into context makes cite-or-abstain hard to explain~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024}. Propagation questions require topology edges before symptom similarity; if edges are missing, abstain with \texttt{no\_neighbour} . Embedding a stain from the opposite elevation is refused~\cite{peng2024,jeon2025hybridrag,pan2026graphdtgpt}. Elevation audits should follow Chapter~7 spatial filters and return schedulable Asset-ID lists, not travelogues~\cite{zhang2025,buga2025llmfmsql}. Code-plus-site questions must pin manual editions and site passports together; clause-only or evidence-only packages should disclose the gap~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,he2025regulationhybrid,quan2026bimfacrag}. Cross-campaign comparisons put identity hygiene first: if Asset-IDs were remapped carelessly, abstain on ``worse than last time''~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg,zhao2026physical}. BIM NL multi-agent retrieval and condensed retrieval agents remind readers that clever navigation is not yet topology-aware diagnosis~\cite{hellin2025bimmas,koh2026bimllmretr,liu2025bimcoder}; facility RAG reminds them that document lakes without Asset-ID links still struggle to enter CMMS~\cite{krutli2025fmrags,quan2026bimfacrag}.

In summary of twin-to-language assembly, intent decides which passports and neighbours enter the package; cite-or-abstain decides whether a draft may leave the harness; GeoBIM resolution decides which host is even eligible to be named~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024,zhang2025}. Table~\ref{tab:ch8-assembly-fail} makes those decisions checkable before Section~\ref{sec:ch8-multimodal} adds modality tags and bounded agents under the same contracts.

\section{Multi-Modal Fusion}
\label{sec:ch8-multimodal}

Fa\c{c}ade programmes are multimodal by construction: campaigns routinely produce RGB imagery, infrared channels, and textual metadata. In this chapter, \emph{multi-modal fusion} means disciplined assembly of those modality-tagged observation records, already written by Chapter~6 perception and attached by Chapters~4 and~7, inside grounded LLM retrieval and generation. Defect finding remains a Chapter~6 perception responsibility; language remains responsible for retrieval, explanation, abstention, and action proposals under citation rules.

Vision--language models (VLMs) belong in this phase of the monograph's decision-support story, but they do not replace the defect detectors of Chapters~5--6. A VLM may help an inspector narrate an openable crop already attached to an Asset-ID, align a free-text remark with a retrieved passport, or draft a caption that still cites the observation URI. It must not invent host codes, silently relabel detector classes, or treat a chat screenshot as a new twin fact. The operational split taught here is therefore simple: Chapter~6 writes typed observations; a VLM, when used, is an optional reader of those crops under the same cite-or-abstain gate as the LLM; the twin remains the authoritative store~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical,ji2023}. That split realises the proposal's fusion of visual defect data with generative AI without collapsing perception and language into one unsupervised model.

Fusing modalities without erasing conflict. Textbook query types for fa\c{c}ade O\&M include local lookup, asset-health summary, propagation or root-cause reading, and floor- or elevation-level audit. A minimal assembly is the passport of asset~$A$, its neighbours, and optional editioned manuals. Every Asset-ID or defect ID named in the answer must appear in that retrieved set; otherwise the system abstains or escalates. When RGB and infrared observations disagree, disclosure beats silent averaging: RGB-only evidence should not become a subsurface claim; infrared-only evidence should keep uncertainty flags. High-resolution fa\c{c}ade sensing and physical quantification work show why those flags matter: language that over-claims geometry inherits perception error~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}. A practical fusion rule for teaching is therefore record-level, not pixel-level: keep modality tags on observations; let the LLM narrate agreement or conflict; never collapse conflict into a single confident defect type without a human check flag.

The implementation principle is straightforward: a presentation layer may look like chat, but evidence assembly, persistence, and twin writes must pass typed services and harness gates.

Agents enter when inspection work requires multi-step procedures. Typical procedures include opening a ticket, proposing a UAV revisit polygon for Chapters~2--3, requesting a robot photo, or staging a twin update after approval~\cite{yao2023react,schick2023toolformer,wang2024surveyagents,luo2025llmdtrobot}. Memory is part of agency, not an afterthought. Short-term dialogue state tracks the current question; retrieval memory refreshes manuals and observation cards; twin memory supplies Asset-IDs and topology; procedural memory records which tools and approvals already ran~\cite{rawat2025llmmas,gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot}. Surveys of LLM agents and multi-agent systems emphasise the same ingredients (planning, memory, and tools) while warning that autonomy without constraints is unsafe~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent}. The 2024--2026 AEC literature makes that warning concrete. Graph-DT multi-agent systems ground answers in twin graphs~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt}; human-DT MAS prototypes coordinate leader, reasoning, and member agents so infrastructure experts can complete tasks with lower cognitive load than steep GUIs~\cite{lu2026highwaymas}; multi-agent virtual modelling connects design-to-operation twins inside BIM~\cite{lee2025multiagentvbm}; IFC tool agents, FM robot brokers, and schema-guided energy agents illustrate typed tools under approval~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot,zhang2025llmagentschema}; router-style expert selection in foundation design shows specialised roles can help when humans keep safety oversight~\cite{youwai2026foundationmas}. Tool-augmented reasoning is therefore an expected ingredient of inspection language systems, not an optional plugin~\cite{yao2023react,schick2023toolformer}.

For fa\c{c}ade inspection we recommend \emph{bounded agency} realised inside the model harness of Section~\ref{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}. Multi-step retrieval and tool calls are welcome. Final claims stay citation-locked to the retrieved evidence set. Dispatch and twin writes stay behind geofence and human gates. A small typed tool surface, get passport, expand neighbours, list observations, draft report, propose revisit, beats free-form SQL or unrestricted code execution. That stance specialises general agent literature to the inspection setting: the twin already encodes the world state that an agent must not rewrite casually; Chapters~2--3 already own flight planning; the language agent proposes, it does not commandeer the aircraft. Recent twin-interaction MAS studies reinforce the same division of labour: agents orchestrate functions and reduce interface friction, but they do not replace domain approval or chapter ownership~\cite{lu2026highwaymas,pan2026graphdtgpt,ma2026tenquestions}. The harness, not the chat transcript, is what makes those bounds enforceable on every run~\cite{yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents,liang2023helm}.

Worked micro-example. The manager asks for a revisit where infrared suggests moisture but RGB is inconclusive. A bounded agent may call \texttt{list\_observations}, notice the modality conflict, draft a report with \texttt{human\_check=true}, and emit a revisit polygon hint. It must not invent a flight trajectory in prose, and it must not write a new defect type into the twin without approval. That is multimodal fusion in the sense of this book: modality-aware records, grounded language, and tools that respect upstream chapter ownership.


Multi-modal fusion also interacts with campaign design. If infrared and RGB are captured on different days, time stamps must remain visible in observation cards; otherwise the LLM may treat them as simultaneous evidence. If one modality has systematically lower detector confidence, trust flags should inherit that asymmetry and must not average it away~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}. Fusion at language time is therefore inseparable from perception metadata quality. Garbage in still yields fluent garbage out, only now with citations that look official.

Agent design should stay boring on purpose. Spectacular autonomous stacks that rewrite BIM, dispatch aircraft, and email contractors in one loop are poor teaching targets for inspection safety. Bounded tools with explicit gates match how responsible O\&M already works: analysts propose; seniors approve; field teams execute~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,zhang2025llmagentschema}. Multi-agent role splits (retriever, topology expander, report drafter, action proposer) can be useful pedagogically, provided they share one twin memory and one approval policy~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,rawat2025llmmas}. Competing agents that invent different Asset-IDs for the same panel are not ``diversity''; they are a failure mode.

Procedural memory deserves a concrete inspection reading. When an agent has already expanded neighbours and listed observations, it should not re-expand endlessly in a loop. When a human rejected a twin write, that rejection should be remembered for the session so the agent does not resubmit the same mutation. When a revisit was proposed, the planning hint should carry the observation IDs that justified it, so Chapters~2--3 receive evidence with observation IDs~\cite{yao2023react,rawat2025llmmas}. Memory, in this sense, is operational discipline.



When demonstrating agents, show the tool trace beside the answer: which passports were fetched, which neighbours expanded, which manual edition pinned, which gate blocked a write. Traces make bounded agency inspectable and turn the harness into a teaching object. Surveys of LLM agents emphasise planning and tools; inspection teaching should emphasise the audit trail those tools leave~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,yao2023react,schick2023toolformer,liang2023helm}. A beautiful answer without a trace is a demo; a plain answer with a trace is an O\&M artefact. Splitting retriever, topology, drafter, and action roles can clarify software modules, as graph-twin and human-DT MAS designs also do~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,lu2026highwaymas}, but must not create competing Asset-ID realities. All roles read the same twin memory; only the gated writer may stage updates; only humans approve. Construction multi-agent frameworks that combine graph and vector memory are useful precedents for shared memory, not for unsupervised committee decisions about defects~\cite{rawat2025llmmas,gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot}.

A compact walkthrough makes the harness concrete. Parse intent=propagation for a moisture-path question on W-3-17; call \texttt{get\_passport} and \texttt{expand\_neighbours}; list modality-tagged observations; abstain if upstairs evidence is missing; draft under cite-or-abstain; if the manager requests action, draft a report with \texttt{human\_check} on RGB/IR conflict and \texttt{propose\_revisit} only for conflicted assets; wait for approval in procedural memory; stage a twin write only on approve. Logging every step is the product; hiding steps to look ``smart'' is the anti-pattern~\cite{yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents,luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,lu2026highwaymas}.
\begin{verbatim}
TRACE example (illustrative)
intent: propagation
tools: get_passport -> expand_neighbours -> list_observations
package_assets: [W-3-17, W-4-17, W-3-16]
modality_conflict: W-3-17
gates: draft_report(human_check=true); propose_revisit(pending)
writes: none
\end{verbatim}
\texttt{propose\_revisit} must emit planning hints, not executable UAV commands. Coordinates should be coarse polygons or asset sets that Chapters~2--3 refine under their own safety stack. Robot photo requests, when available, should be tickets. Direct motor commands are refused~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot}. Once the user asks for tickets or revisits, the orchestrator can enter an explicit state machine DRAFT $\rightarrow$ REVIEW $\rightarrow$ APPROVED/REJECTED $\rightarrow$ SCHEDULED; the LLM fills fields inside a state and must not skip REVIEW because the user is in a hurry~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,zhang2025llmagentschema,rawat2025llmmas,lu2026highwaymas}. Agent tests can stay offline: stub tools return fixtures; assert that forbidden writes never occur; assert revisit hints always carry observation IDs; assert rejected drafts never reappear without new evidence. Those tests are cheaper than flight campaigns and catch the worst failures early~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,ma2026tenquestions}.



Operators can keep a one-page fusion decision table beside the console.

Operators can tape this table beside the console. It turns Section~\ref{sec:ch8-multimodal} into muscle memory without a second perception stack~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}.

When not to call an agent. If the user only asks what is logged on an asset, tools \texttt{get\_passport} and \texttt{list\_observations} suffice; no ticket machinery. Agents earn their complexity only for procedures. Premature agency increases gate surface area without increasing grounding~\cite{yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents}.




Conflict-asset week closes multimodal teaching without a subsection forest. Suppose three west-elevation hosts show RGB/IR conflict in one week: the system must not average them into one defect type; gondola lists should down-rank them by default; revisit hints must bind observation IDs for Chapters~2--3~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical,luo2025llmdtrobot}. If a manager asks to ``mark repaired first and gather evidence later,'' the harness must refuse direct writes and only stage pending updates behind approval~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,lu2026highwaymas}. When demonstrating agents, show TRACE beside the answer: passports fetched, neighbours expanded, manual edition pinned, which gate blocked a write. A beautiful answer without TRACE remains a demo; a plain answer with TRACE is an O\&M artefact~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,liang2023helm,hellin2025bimmas}. Role splits may clarify software modules, but they must not create multiple Asset-ID truths; graph-twin and human-DT MAS papers are precedents for shared memory and approval, not for unsupervised committee votes on defects~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,lu2026highwaymas,rawat2025llmmas,koh2026bimllmretr}.



In summary of multimodal fusion and bounded agency, modality tags travel with observation cards, TRACE makes tool use inspectable, and write gates keep the twin authoritative~\cite{zhao2026physical,yao2023react,luo2025llmdtrobot,lu2026highwaymas}. The next section turns grounded drafts into report schemas that facility teams can audit, with optional export into existing work systems.

\section{Inspection Report Generation and Management}
\label{sec:ch8-reports}

Inspection answers become useful when they land in artefacts facility-management systems already understand. Free-form paragraphs are hard to audit. A practical report schema groups identity fields (asset and defect IDs), diagnosis, propagation notes, recommended actions, and trust flags. Identity fields must be subsets of the retrieved evidence set, the report-level analogue of constrained decoding used in BIM and code assistants~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes}. Diagnosis fields should point back to observation IDs whose images open in the twin UI. Action fields should be readable by schedulers without requiring another LLM to re-parse prose.

Figure~\ref{fig:ch8-ablation} illustrates the kind of grounding outcome a reader should demand. Passport-centred packaging with one-hop expansion keeps answer IDs and cited IDs closer to the retrieved evidence than chunk-only packaging, so hop depth belongs to the evidence-assembly policy rather than a free prompt choice~\cite{lewis2020,jeon2025hybridrag,lee2024ragsafety}. Report drafts inherit that policy: every named Asset-ID must occur in the returned package, and every evidence URI must open~\cite{ji2023,peng2024}. For a question about water ingress on a vertical stack, the system should return neighbour passports and openable observation IDs, then draft a repair brief or abstain when evidence is thin. If a revisit is needed, an action proposer emits a planning hint for Chapters~2--3; it does not invent a flight path in prose.

Report management means more than one-shot generation. Drafts should be versioned; human sign-off should be recorded; approved facts may be written back into twin memory, the same long-term store introduced in Section~\ref{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}. Rejected drafts should not silently pollute passports. Where organisations already use CMMS or BIM issue trackers, the schema above may map to those fields as an optional export. Parallel unreproducible prose PDFs are avoided. Natural-language query interfaces can help non-specialists ask questions, but the authoritative artefact remains the constrained report plus openable evidence. A practical state machine keeps that discipline enforceable: DRAFT $\rightarrow$ VALIDATE $\rightarrow$ REVIEW $\rightarrow$ APPROVED/REJECTED $\rightarrow$ (optional) EXPORT $\rightarrow$ (optional) TWIN\_WRITE. The LLM may fill fields only inside DRAFT; Table~\ref{tab:ch8-report-accept} owns VALIDATE; humans own REVIEW; EXPORT is only for sites that already run a work system; only an approved path may call \texttt{stage\_twin\_write}. ReAct-style tool loops and schema-guided AEC agents already show that multi-step tool use is valuable when each step remains typed and gated~\cite{yao2023react,gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot}. Fluency must not skip VALIDATE because a meeting is starting in ten minutes~\cite{li2024surveymultiagent,rawat2025llmmas,hellin2025bimmas}. Software roles may split for clarity (retriever, drafter, optional mapper, action proposer) without creating multiple Asset-ID truths: all roles read one twin memory and one approval policy~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot,quan2026bimfacrag,buga2025llmfmsql,li2024surveymultiagent,wang2024surveyagents}.

A fluent chat panel without those contracts is still a demo. A short worked reading of the interface pattern keeps that distinction sharp. Suppose the inspector asks which west-elevation hosts above storey three show moisture-related symptoms with openable evidence. A contract-respecting answer names Asset-IDs that already sit in the retrieved passport set, lists observation IDs whose crops open in the twin viewer, discloses RGB/IR conflict when present, and offers structured follow-ups such as expand neighbours or draft a schema-constrained brief. A failing interface may still look similar on screen: it returns a fluent paragraph, invents a panel nickname, and offers a ``mark repaired'' button that writes the twin without a gate. Readers should therefore score Figure~\ref{fig:ch8-nlq-ui} with the same four tests used for vendors: stable Asset-IDs, openable citations, abstention when the package is thin, and gated writes~\cite{ji2023,zhang2025,buga2025llmfmsql}.

Report schemas should be negotiated with facility stakeholders early. Fields that nobody will open in a CMMS will not be maintained. Fields that lawyers require for liability may need stricter cite-or-abstain checks than fields used for internal triage. A teaching compromise is a core schema (identity, diagnosis, evidence links, trust) plus optional organisation-specific extensions (cost codes, contractor IDs, access windows). The LLM may draft extensions only from retrieved context or human-provided forms; it must not invent cost codes~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes}.

Human-in-the-loop is not a confession of failure; it is the product. Sign-off records should store who approved what, which evidence IDs were visible at approval time, and whether modality conflict flags were overridden. Those records become part of twin provenance for the next campaign. Without them, next season's LLM will treat last season's speculative draft as fact. Identity hygiene figures in this section are therefore not IT folklore: they are the precondition for longitudinal language reasoning~\cite{zhang2025}.

The same contracts expose weak demos quickly: PDF-only RAG without Asset-IDs, chat UIs that cannot open cited images, twin writes from unapproved drafts, severity language without geometry normalisation, or propagation stories built from embedding neighbours instead of BIM adjacency. Two or more of those marks leave the system a prototype. Delivery itself has a fixed half and an optional half. The fixed half is the schema-constrained draft with openable evidence: identity, diagnosis, trust flags, and URIs that open the correct crops. Asset-ID hygiene and cite-or-abstain are part of that half; they are not the whole product. Narrative must be rendered from filled fields; IDs must not be scraped from prose after the fact; nightly link checks belong beside detector quality control~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes,zhang2025,gao2026ifcagent}. The optional half is exporting those fields into whatever work system a site already runs. That sink may be a CMMS, a BIM issue tracker, a contractor portal, or a simple spreadsheet; this chapter does not invent a new CMMS product. When an export exists, keep the mapping short and mechanical: \texttt{asset\_ids} to register codes, evidence URIs to attachments, facility-scale priority rather than an LLM-invented scale, and \texttt{human\_check} as a hold that blocks auto-close~\cite{chen2023facility,krutli2025fmrags,buga2025llmfmsql}. Sites without a CMMS still succeed if they keep the constrained draft, openable evidence, and human sign-off.

Handover packages should therefore carry the Asset-ID dictionary, observation schema, manual edition pins, approval logs, and a frozen twin snapshot used for language evaluation. An export map is included only when the receiving system is known. Report-lifecycle thickness comes from those contracts: drafts pass ID subset validation before human review, rejections enter procedural memory, approved fields may export with minimal rewrite, and Asset-ID collisions stay visible beside the draft~\cite{buga2025llmfmsql,chen2023facility,quan2026bimfacrag,hellin2025bimmas}. Published FM robot and IFC-agent loops close a similar circle (language request, structured action, twin or model update under approval); fa\c{c}ade O\&M specialises the circle so that every actionable artefact still cites openable observation crops before money or scaffolding moves~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,saka2024gptconstruction}. An ``official'' PDF without openable evidence is more dangerous than a chat hallucination for exactly that reason~\cite{ji2023}.

After each major flight week, freeze a twin snapshot, re-run Table~\ref{tab:ch8-report-accept} on a gold question set that includes abstentions, and score invented-ID rate and broken-evidence rate before swapping any hosted LLM~\cite{liang2023helm,zhang2025}. Cross-campaign claims (``worse than last season'') require prior observation IDs in the package; otherwise the harness must force abstention or a human-only narrative field. That seasonal hygiene protects longitudinal language the same way Asset-ID hygiene protects a single draft~\cite{chen2023facility,krutli2025fmrags}. The authoritative artefact remains a schema-constrained draft with openable evidence and human sign-off; Table~\ref{tab:ch8-report-accept} checks that core, while any work-system export remains an optional later recipe. The next section asks whether those artefacts actually change schedules under implementation-agnostic criteria.

\section{Decision Support and Predictive Maintenance}
\label{sec:ch8-dss}

Decision support closes the chapter by asking what a grounded language system is for. The proposal answer is operational and generative: automatically support comprehensive reports, natural-language queries over historical maintenance records, and predictive maintenance recommendations that accelerate scheduling while remaining under professional control. In teaching terms, that means named assets, openable evidence, ranked triage for the next gondola week, and honest abstention when prognosis evidence is thin. Evaluation criteria must therefore travel across implementations. For inspection language systems, ask whether cited IDs appear in the evidence set~\cite{ji2023,shuster2021}; whether propagation and historical-record questions improve when prior observations and neighbours are included; whether code and manual RAG beats parametric recall on editioned corpora~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,joffe2025buildingcodes,krutli2025fmrags}; whether outputs can drive operational schedules; and whether agent tool traces respect approval and geofence constraints~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,rawat2025llmmas}. Table~\ref{tab:ch8-eval} states those five criteria as a shared rubric for any implementation.

The rubric should be scored at the level of actions, not only at the level of sentences. A grounded paragraph that never changes a maintenance schedule has limited O\&M value; a terse ranked list of Asset-IDs with openable evidence may already plan a gondola week. Monthly boards can therefore track invented-ID rate, openable-evidence rate, modality-conflict disclosure rate, revisit-hint adoption, and, when a work-system export exists, export no-rewrite rate~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,luo2025llmdtrobot,rawat2025llmmas,liang2023helm}. If a hosted-LLM swap improves fluency but worsens invented IDs, reject the swap. If fluency is unchanged but TRACE completeness improves, accept the harness change. Exact-match contests among foundation models remain secondary to those operability curves.

Figure~\ref{fig:ch8-ablation} gives one compact illustration of the rubric: retrieval packaging and controlled one-hop expansion can improve grounding. In implementation, assess this outcome by intent and by the safety of the final action, including whether cite-or-abstain validators catch unsupported IDs~\cite{ji2023,peng2024,lee2024ragsafety}. This keeps evaluation focused on what readers can inspect and facility teams can use.

Transferability and predictive maintenance must be kept on the same argument line. Figure~\ref{fig:ch8-transfer} shows that an observation--geometry--dependency spine can be instantiated for building O\&M, renovation sequencing, or construction-progress monitoring by changing entity semantics and edge types. Schema reuse is real; forecasting skill is not automatic. A language system may rank assets by evidence-backed severity and access constraints, and it may present outputs from an external temporal model that lives outside the prompt. It should not invent year-ahead spalling probabilities from a single campaign~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,zhang2025llmagentschema,zhao2026physical}. Priority scores from one epoch are triage. Prognosis waits for repeated observations on the same Asset-IDs, comparable modalities, and a non-LLM temporal model wired as a read-only tool.

Whether a reader uses a phone, tablet, or desktop interface, the same test applies: named Asset-IDs must remain consistent and evidence must remain openable. An interface that invents a panel code or presents an unlinked floor count fails the rubric in Table~\ref{tab:ch8-eval}.

Published 2024--2026 twin-interaction and BIM-language systems supply the surrounding practice without needing a second literature tour. Graph-twin agents keep answers faithful by routing steps through structured queries~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt}; human--DT multi-agent designs lower interface burden while retaining human evaluation~\cite{lu2026highwaymas}; design-to-operation modelling updates twins as workflows under approval~\cite{lee2025multiagentvbm}; IFC tool agents and FM robot brokers show typed tools with write gates~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot}; BIM NL retrieval and facility RAG join documents to model objects, but still need Asset-ID passports and topology for fa\c{c}ade diagnosis~\cite{hellin2025bimmas,koh2026bimllmretr,quan2026bimfacrag,krutli2025fmrags}. Hybrid defect and safety RAG motivate editioned corpora~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag,lee2024ragsafety,he2025regulationhybrid,joffe2025buildingcodes}. The transferable lesson is uniform: keep structure outside the weights, compose with the LLM, refuse when the structured path is missing, and treat governance and data readiness as first-class design variables~\cite{ma2026tenquestions,saka2024gptconstruction,liang2023helm}. User-experience gains count only while the five criteria in Table~\ref{tab:ch8-eval} remain green.

One end-to-end walkthrough is enough to bind those criteria to practice. Consider a two-day warehouse fa\c{c}ade campaign that Chapters~2--7 have already turned into a decision-ready twin. Day~1 yields RGB coverage; Day~2 adds infrared over suspected moisture zones; perception writes modality-tagged observations; registration and GeoBIM keep host Asset-IDs aligned with the facility register. On Day~3 the manager asks four meeting questions. Local lookup loads only passport(\texttt{W-3-17}) and returns openable observations. Cross-campaign comparison abstains if prior observation IDs are missing. Propagation expands upstairs and lateral neighbours, keeps RGB/IR tags, and either drafts a cited path hypothesis or refuses. Elevation audit applies a Chapter~7 spatial filter and emits a schema-constrained gondola list. Only when the manager requests action does the bounded agent draft tickets, attach evidence IDs, set \texttt{human\_check=true} on modality conflicts, and emit revisit hints for Chapters~2--3. It does not email contractors, rewrite BIM geometry, or clear conflict flags. Approved facts write back to twin memory; rejected drafts enter procedural memory and are not resubmitted in the same session. That single story is the chapter spine under load: twin contract, memory ladder, asset-centric assembly, multimodal disclosure, gated tools, report lifecycle, and operability metrics.

Deployment should follow the same spine. Begin by freezing Asset-IDs and neighbour edges, admitting only valid observation cards, and limiting use to local and health questions until topology is checked. Then enable one-hop expansion with a pinned manual edition, dry-run the five-tool surface with writes blocked, and introduce frozen-snapshot scoring and CMMS mapping before any hosted-LLM substitution~\cite{zhang2025,zhao2026physical,peng2024,lee2024ragsafety,gao2026ifcagent,liang2023helm,ma2026tenquestions,buga2025llmfmsql}. Graph-twin and multi-agent studies may inform role splits inside that programme, but they do not remove the need for identity hygiene~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,rawat2025llmmas,lu2026highwaymas}.

Triage language sits between diagnosis and schedule. An illustrative scheduler may score assets by $s = 0.4\,\tilde{\ell} + 0.3\,c + 0.2\,a + 0.1\,m$, where $\tilde{\ell}$ is geometry-normalised defect size, $c$ detector confidence, $a$ access urgency, and $m$ a modality-conflict penalty. Coefficients belong to the facility, not to the LLM; the language system exposes the cited terms behind each score and lets humans reorder (Table~\ref{tab:ch8-triage}). Surface cracks require geometry-normalised lengths without uncited structural stories, moisture cues require multimodal disclosure, and prior repair patches require cross-campaign IDs before a system may claim deterioration~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical,jeon2025hybridrag}. A minimal constrained draft remains the machine-readable carrier of that triage:

Common failure modes collapse into the same contracts. Invented Asset-IDs appear when validators are missing; false moisture paths appear when embedding neighbourhood replaces BIM adjacency; confirmed seepage appears when infrared-only cards lose uncertainty flags; silent twin mutation appears when \texttt{draft\_report} and \texttt{stage\_twin\_write} share a button; edition averaging appears when manuals are unpinned~\cite{ji2023,peng2024,lee2024ragsafety,luo2025llmdtrobot}. Adding more agent personas does not repair those failures. Shared twin memory, typed tools, and gates do~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,rawat2025llmmas,gao2026ifcagent}. Procurement and classroom assessment should therefore demand the same artefacts: Asset-ID dictionary, neighbour-edge provenance, observation schema, edition pins, intent--assembly policy, gated tool surface, CMMS field map, gold questions with abstentions, and paired TRACE files. Figures~\ref{fig:ch8-memory-ladder}--\ref{fig:ch8-agent-gate} and Tables~\ref{tab:ch8-harness} and~\ref{tab:ch8-triage} are load-bearing teaching objects for that demand~\cite{yao2023react,hellin2025bimmas,buga2025llmfmsql}.

Looking toward Chapter~9, the open problems are mostly socio-technical: incomplete topology on older buildings, contractor identifier collisions, scarce agent benchmarks for safe inspection tool use, and governance when a grounded-looking answer is wrong~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,ma2026tenquestions}. Larger models alone will not close those gaps. The constructive response taught here remains continuous with Sections~\ref{sec:ch8-dt}--\ref{sec:ch8-reports}: make the twin decision-ready, retrieve over assets, fuse modalities without erasing conflict, bound the agent, and evaluate what facility teams can schedule.

In summary, Chapter~8 is the monograph's fourth phase: next-generation decision support. It consumes Chapters~4--7, teaches ready-to-use LLM techniques and a model harness with an inspection-centred memory ladder, positions VLMs as optional crop readers under the same citation gates, grounds answers through asset-centric retrieval and bounded agents, and delivers historical-record queries, auditable reports, and predictive maintenance triage that facility teams can schedule. Drawing on published 2024--2026 AEC practice while specialising it to fa\c{c}ade O\&M, the measure of success is not rhetorical fluency, but named assets, cited evidence, and actions that remain under professional control.
