\chapter{Digital Twin with LLMs in Inspection Analysis}
\label{ch:llm-dt}

\abstract*{This chapter closes the monograph's pipeline: after planning, reconstruction, detection, and GIS anchoring, how can digital twins support language-level diagnosis and maintenance decisions? We survey UAV--DT--BIM lines of work (including contributions from this research group and the wider community), introduce a teaching framework that separates structured twin knowledge, retrieval grounding, and agentic tooling, and use asset-centric RAG only as one illustrative case---rewritten for textbook use rather than as a paper reprint.}

\abstract{Chapters~2--7 explain how to fly, reconstruct, detect, and map. Chapter~8 asks what happens next: how inspectors and facility managers query a twin in natural language, obtain traceable answers, and trigger follow-up actions. The chapter is written as a \emph{synthesis for the book}, not as a restatement of any single journal manuscript. We first map prior work on digital twins, defect perception, LLM/BIM assistants, RAG, and agents; then we organise a decision-layer framework that previous chapters can feed; finally we discuss reports, decision support, and open issues. An asset-centric, topology-aware RAG study associated with this group~\cite{huang2026iaprag} appears as a \emph{case study}, with wording and emphasis adapted to monograph teaching.}

\section{Digital Twins for Building Inspection}
\label{sec:ch8-dt}

\subsection{Where this chapter sits in the book}
The monograph is organised as one pipeline. Task and motion planning (Chapters~2--3) produce safe coverage. Reconstruction (Chapter~4) turns imagery into geometry. Datasets and perception models (Chapters~5--6) name defects. GIS and GeoBIM (Chapter~7) give assets addresses in the city. None of those chapters, alone, answers questions such as ``which panels participate in a multi-storey moisture path?'' or ``draft a repair brief that cites evidence an engineer can audit.'' That is the job of the \emph{decision layer}: connecting twin entities to language, retrieval, and---increasingly---tool-using agents.

\subsection{What a twin must provide for language use}
A geometric twin answers location. A decision-ready twin must also expose (i)~stable asset identifiers, (ii)~provenance for each observation (sensor, time, detector), and (iii)~relations that support propagation reasoning. Without those, LLMs can only chat over loose files.

\section{Foundations of LLMs for Civil Engineering Applications}
\label{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}

\subsection{Related work map (group + community)}
We organise prior art along the same pipeline the book uses.

\paragraph{Twins, GeoBIM, and registration.}
UAV--BIM inspection reviews sketched early automation agendas~\cite{zhang2022}. Within this research group, Zhang et al.\ demonstrated AI-empowered digital-twin modelling that couples UAV sensing with GeoBIM for high-precision defect management~\cite{zhang2025}---work that directly supports Chapters~4 and~7 and supplies the geometric premise for language grounding. Det-Recon-Reg further packages detection, reconstruction, and registration for large-scale UAV inspection~\cite{yang2025detreconreg}. Related twin uses extend beyond façades: Han et al.\ combine UAVs and digital twins for worker monitoring on large sites~\cite{han2025}.

\paragraph{Perception and datasets.}
High-resolution defect datasets and detectors from unmanned platforms~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2025icra} and physical quantification from instance segmentation~\cite{zhao2026physical} make Chapters~5--6 concrete. For Chapter~8 they matter because language systems inherit whatever bias and granularity the perception stack encodes.

\paragraph{LLMs for buildings and BIM interaction.}
Surveys of LLMs in building applications discuss opportunities and risks (privacy, unverifiable advice)~\cite{xiao2025tenquestions}. Practical BIM interaction includes query--DSL alignment for information retrieval~\cite{guo2025bimllmquery} and agent schemas for energy modelling~\cite{zhang2025llmagentschema}. These systems show that language helps \emph{navigate} building data, but navigation is not yet topology-aware defect diagnosis.

\paragraph{RAG families.}
Classic RAG attaches generation to external memory~\cite{lewis2020}. HyDE, Self-RAG, Adaptive-RAG, and GraphRAG explore query rewriting, reflection, and graph corpora~\cite{gao2023hyde,asai2024selfrag,jeong2024adaptive,edge2024graphrag}. In construction planning, graph RAG has been used to reduce information silos~\cite{he2025graphrag}. Cognitive-twin discussions in industry likewise emphasise retrieval over interoperable knowledge~\cite{shi2025cognitivedt}. The textbook takeaway: RAG improves evidence access; it does not by itself define asset units or safe actions.

\paragraph{Agents and closed loops.}
ReAct and Toolformer established reason--act and tool-learning patterns~\cite{yao2023react,schick2023toolformer}; surveys catalogue single- and multi-agent stacks~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent}. In AEC, Luo et al.\ broker natural-language FM requests among users, twins, and robots~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot}; IFC-Agent performs schema-guided multi-agent tool use on native IFC~\cite{gao2026ifcagent}; BIM2RDT pursues agentic, safety-first robot-ready twins~\cite{akhavian2025bim2rdt}. These works motivate Section~\ref{sec:ch8-agents}.

\subsection{Failure modes that matter for façade O\&M}
For inspection dialogue, three failures dominate regardless of model brand: invented asset IDs; repair advice without attributable clauses; fluent summaries that drop contradictory RGB/IR cues. The rest of the chapter is organised to mitigate those failures through structure, retrieval constraints, and approval gates---not through longer prompts alone.

\section{From Digital Twins to Language Reasoning}
\label{sec:ch8-dt-to-language}

\subsection{A teaching framework (book-level)}
Rather than importing a paper's module list verbatim, we describe three layers students should keep separate:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textbf{Observation records} --- one defect sighting with sensor provenance;
\item \textbf{Asset passports} --- all observations and geometry/topology for a maintainable host;
\item \textbf{Query assembly} --- how a natural-language question selects passports and neighbours before generation.
\end{enumerate}

Different research groups implement these layers with different schemas. The important pedagogical point is the \emph{separation}: perception writes observations; FM thinks in assets; language models must not be asked to invent either.

\subsection{Observation records in practice}
Across UAV inspection programmes, useful observation records typically store: host asset ID, time, modality (RGB/IR/\ldots), detector signature, pose or surface coordinates, and paths to imagery. Group GeoBIM-DT pipelines show how registration attaches detections to BIM hosts before any LLM is involved~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg}. Chapter~6 supplies the detector; Chapter~4--7 supply the host ID.

For teaching, one may write a generic observation as a tuple of identity, evidence, localisation, and analysis fields---without committing the chapter prose to any one paper's notation. Natural-language location phrases (``top centre of the southeast panel'') help LLMs, while metric coordinates remain for field navigation.

\subsection{Asset passports and topology}
Facility questions are usually asset-centric. Passports therefore aggregate observations per maintainable host and attach (i)~symptom summaries, (ii)~host geometry for severity normalisation, and (iii)~neighbour links (above/below/lateral). Neighbour links are what enable propagation-style reading---e.g., mid-storey delamination considered together with upstairs moisture---without pretending a document-similarity graph is a structural graph.

\subsection{Case study pointer: asset-centric RAG}
One concrete implementation of observation records + asset passports + neighbour expansion is the asset-centric, propagation-aware RAG line associated with this group~\cite{huang2026iaprag}. In monograph voice we treat it as a \emph{worked example}: it shows that organising knowledge by maintainable assets and expanding along BIM adjacency can improve multi-hop diagnosis relative to chunk baselines. Readers who need implementation minutiae should consult that manuscript; this chapter keeps only the ideas needed to connect the book pipeline to decision support.

\section{Multi-Modal Fusion and Topology-Aware RAG}
\label{sec:ch8-multimodal}

\subsection{Pipeline fusion versus end-to-end VLMs}
Façade programmes are multi-modal, but ``fusion'' need not mean one giant vision--language model. A book-consistent pattern is: detectors write modality-tagged observations; retrieval selects asset passports; the LLM composes answers under citation rules. End-to-end VLMs remain promising (Chapter~9 outlook) but currently struggle with auditable Asset-IDs on portfolio-scale twins.

\subsection{Intent, neighbourhood expansion, and citation rules}
Textbook query types include local lookup, asset-health summary, propagation/root-cause reading, and floor- or elevation-level audit. Intent should change \emph{which neighbours enter the context}, not only the prompt wording.

A minimal assembly pattern is:
\[
\text{context}
=
\text{passport}(A)
\cup
\text{neighbours}(A)
\cup
\text{optional manuals}.
\]
Hard teaching rule: every cited asset/defect ID in the answer must appear in the retrieved set; otherwise the system must abstain or ask for human review. Optional expert corpora (codes, repair manuals) should be edition-versioned and separate from web scrape---a lesson shared with compliance-oriented BIM assistants~\cite{guo2025bimllmquery,xiao2025tenquestions}.

\subsection{RGB/IR disagreements}
When thermal and visual evidence disagree, good practice is disclosure rather than silent averaging: RGB-only evidence should not be escalated to subsurface claims; IR-only evidence should keep uncertainty flags. Perception chapters define what each modality can see; this chapter defines how language systems must talk about conflict.

\subsection{From static RAG to bounded agency}
Agentic RAG can rewrite queries and call tools iteratively~\cite{singh2024agenticragsurvey}. For inspection we recommend \emph{bounded agency}: multi-step retrieval is welcome; final claims remain citation-locked to twin entities. Unbounded tool loops without Asset-ID checks recreate hallucination with extra latency.

\paragraph{Bridge.}
Agents become essential when the system must \emph{act}, not only answer.

\section{Agentic Workflows for Twin-Grounded Inspection}
\label{sec:ch8-agents}

\subsection{Why the book needs an agent section}
RAG answers evidence-seeking questions. FM operations also require procedures: open a ticket, propose a UAV revisit, request a robot photo, or stage a twin update after human approval. That is the scope of agents~\cite{yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents}.

\subsection{What others have built}
Luo et al.\ place an LLM between the user, the twin, and robot middleware, emphasising human-approved twin commits~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot}. Gao et al.\ show schema-guided multi-agent tool composition on IFC~\cite{gao2026ifcagent}. BIM2RDT stresses safety-first robot-ready twins~\cite{akhavian2025bim2rdt}. Energy-oriented agent libraries show how domain tools can be versioned~\cite{zhang2025llmagentschema}. None of these replace Chapters~2--7; they consume twin state those chapters create.

\subsection{A pedagogical agent stack for façade programmes}
We describe four \emph{roles} (logical, not necessarily four model instances):
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textbf{Retriever} --- asset-passport search with citation packing;
\item \textbf{Topology aide} --- neighbour expansion and conflict checks;
\item \textbf{Report clerk} --- fills a constrained schema (Section~\ref{sec:ch8-reports});
\item \textbf{Action proposer} --- tickets / revisit polygons / IFC patches, always behind approval.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Micro-scenario (teaching).}
``If seepage propagates two storeys above panel A-07, which assets need gondola access?'' Retriever loads A-07; topology aide expands vertical/lateral neighbours; report clerk drafts a priority list with evidence IDs; action proposer suggests a revisit polygon for Chapters~2--3 planning---but does not dispatch until approval.

\subsection{Tools worth exposing}
A small typed tool surface beats free-form SQL generation: get/search passports; expand neighbours; list observations; open evidence URIs; draft report; propose revisit; propose twin patch. This follows the spirit of schema-guided tool agents~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,schick2023toolformer}.

\subsection{Non-negotiables}
No invented Asset-IDs; no silent twin overwrites; no robot/UAV dispatch without geofence and safety checks~\cite{akhavian2025bim2rdt,luo2025llmdtrobot}. Metrics that only score answer strings are insufficient for agent claims (Section~\ref{sec:ch8-dss}).

\paragraph{Bridge.}
Answers and proposals must land in artefacts FM systems already understand: reports.

\section{Inspection Report Generation and Management}
\label{sec:ch8-reports}

\subsection{Schema-first generation}
Free-form paragraphs are hard to audit. A practical report schema groups identity (asset/defect IDs), diagnosis, propagation notes, recommended actions, and trust flags (confidence, human verification). Identity fields must be subsets of retrieved evidence.

\subsection{Field identity hygiene}
Language quality collapses when site tags and twin IDs diverge. Registration audits (Fig.~\ref{fig:ch8-asset-id}) are therefore part of the decision layer, not an IT afterthought---consistent with GeoBIM-era twin practice~\cite{zhang2025}.

\subsection{Walkthrough box (non-paper narrative)}
A manager asks about water ingress on a vertical stack. The system returns neighbour passports, cites observation IDs whose images open in the twin UI, and either drafts a repair brief or abstains if evidence is thin. If a revisit is needed, the action proposer emits a planning hint for Chapters~2--3 rather than inventing a flight path in prose.

\section{Decision Support and Predictive Maintenance}
\label{sec:ch8-dss}

\subsection{What to evaluate in a book context}
Monograph readers need criteria, not only one paper's tables:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textbf{Grounding:} are cited IDs in the evidence set?
\item \textbf{Topology sensitivity:} do propagation questions improve when neighbours are included?
\item \textbf{Operational usefulness:} can outputs drive schedules (gondola staging, revisit planning)?
\item \textbf{Agent safety (emerging):} tool success under approval and geofence constraints.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Case-study snapshot (illustrative)}
As one data point---not the chapter's sole content---the asset-centric RAG case study on logistics-warehouse façades reports strong multi-hop localisation when asset passports and neighbour expansion are used, relative to chunk-style baselines~\cite{huang2026iaprag}. Headline figures from that study (retrieval Exact Hit $0.609$; multi-hop Exact Hit $0.926$; citation validity $0.878$) may be quoted when teaching why structure matters; they should not be mistaken for universal constants across cities or defect taxonomies.

\subsection{Towards predictive and cross-domain use}
Priority scoring and operational planning convert diagnosis into schedules. Transfer ideas (renovation sequencing, construction monitoring) appear in several DT/agent programmes~\cite{singh2025multiagentvbm,akhavian2025bim2rdt} and should be taught as \emph{schema reuse}, not as guaranteed forecasting. True deterioration prediction still needs temporal models beyond a single inspection epoch.

\subsection{Open issues}
Topology completeness and Asset-ID hygiene dominate error modes. Standard agent benchmarks for inspection (safe tool traces) are still missing. Fully VLM-native twins and swarm-continuous updates belong to Chapter~9's forward look, building on planning chapters for multi-UAV operations.

\paragraph{Chapter takeaways.}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
\item Chapter~8 is the book's decision layer: it consumes Ch.~4--7 products and must not be read as an isolated paper.
\item Prior work already covers twinning, perception, BIM-LLMs, RAG, and agents; the synthesis is to keep observation/asset/query layers distinct and auditable.
\item Asset-centric RAG~\cite{huang2026iaprag} is one case study among broader community and group contributions~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg,zhao2026physical,luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent}.
\end{enumerate}
