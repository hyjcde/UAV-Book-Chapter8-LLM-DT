\section{Digital Twins for Building Inspection}
\label{sec:ch8-dt}

Cities maintain large stocks of ageing buildings under rising safety and cost pressure. Manual fa\c{c}ade inspection remains labour-intensive and hazardous, so unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs), sensing, and artificial intelligence have entered structural health monitoring (SHM) and building operations and maintenance (O\&M). Chapters~2--7 of this monograph already follow that pipeline in sequence: task and motion planning, three-dimensional reconstruction, datasets and defect models, and geographic information that hosts typed findings on maintainable assets. What those chapters deliver is a surveyed, registered, and spatially organised building. What they do not yet finish is ordinary-language decision support. Day-to-day O\&M is rarely complete when a detection box appears on a mesh. Managers still ask which panels share a moisture path, what changed since the last campaign, which assets should enter next week's gondola schedule, and how to draft a repair brief that an engineer can audit. This chapter is therefore continuous with the upstream stack, yet distinct in job: it consumes those products and develops the language and decision layer above them.

For this chapter, a building-inspection \emph{digital twin} is an editioned, queryable, and controllably writable representation of facility state that binds geometry, typed observations, maintainable hosts, topology, and provenance so that language and scheduling systems can retrieve openable evidence rather than invent it~\cite{boje2020,opoku2021,lu2020,chen2023facility}. It is not synonymous with a photorealistic mesh viewer, a static BIM file store, or a one-way digital shadow that only mirrors the last flight. Construction and heritage literatures already stress lifecycle information, diagnosis-linked semantics, and work-process coupling beyond visualisation~\cite{bruno2018,tsilimantou2020,lu2020}. Table~\ref{tab:ch8-twin-maturity} states the maturity ladder used here; the chapter target is a \emph{decision-ready} twin, with a \emph{closed-loop} twin allowed only when approved maintenance outcomes write back under gates.

Building-inspection practice supplies that twin through a repeatable pipeline: UAV campaigns produce imagery and pose; reconstruction supplies geometry; detectors supply typed findings; BIM or GIS registration attaches findings to maintainable hosts~\cite{zhang2022,zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg,zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}. In book terms, the earlier chapters already hand over what this chapter needs to remember. Chapter~4 supplies geometry and BIM hosts---host codes, geometry digests, and adjacency seeds. Chapters~5--6 supply typed detections with defect labels, confidence, modality tags, and openable evidence URIs. Chapter~7 supplies GeoBIM identity: facility register codes, spatial filters, and city-scale hosts. Campaign metadata (pose, time, weather flags) pin timestamps and twin editions. Only when those products sit in twin memory is language reasoning honest. Large language models (LLMs) then act as language interfaces, not as detectors or site databases: retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), structured outputs, and tool calling keep living facts outside the weights~\cite{lewis2020,shuster2021,yao2023react,ji2023}. Vision--language models (VLMs), when used, may narrate an already attached crop; they must not invent host codes or rewrite detector labels. Neighbouring AEC systems already show fragments of these patterns---BIM assistants, code RAG, FM Q\&A, IFC agents---and also show that inspection needs stricter evidence discipline~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes,krutli2025fmrags,gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot}.

Four governance questions decide whether the store is a twin rather than a structured photo archive. \emph{Update cadence:} how often UAV campaigns refresh the twin (per flight week, per season, or on demand). \emph{Authority:} when BIM, GIS, and computerised maintenance management system (CMMS) codes disagree, which source wins for host identity and work orders. \emph{Snapshots:} each language evaluation and report must pin a twin snapshot identifier. \emph{Write-back:} only approved repair or status changes may update long-term records; rejected LLM drafts must not enter the official store~\cite{zhang2022,chen2023facility,zhang2025}.

The running example is a warehouse fa\c{c}ade campaign in which a manager asks whether mid-storey delamination on the west elevation is linked to moisture staining above. A mesh viewer can show both regions; only a decision-ready twin can assemble neighbouring host records, cite openable observation IDs, and draft or abstain. Later placeholders such as W-3-17 and W-4-17 are teaching codes; live programmes substitute facility register codes.

Only after that conceptual path is clear does this book need a \emph{process} picture of how a digital twin and a large language model sit inside one inspection workflow. Figure~\ref{fig:ch8-framework} is best read by duty, with the workflow showing how data move. \emph{Module~I (bottom)} is physical acquisition: UAV imagery, pose, BIM/GIS context, and campaign metadata enter the pipeline. \emph{Module~II (left)} is twin construction: a geometry branch builds point clouds, meshes, and BIM alignment; a perception branch adds defect detection, segmentation, and modality or confidence tags. Those branches do not feed the language model directly. They first enter the central \emph{twin memory}: observation records, host attachment, topology links, and openable evidence. \emph{Module~III (right)} is language reasoning: it retrieves twin memory and editioned manuals under citation checks, then drafts answers; it neither rebuilds geometry nor rewrites detector labels. \emph{Module~IV (top)} is human O\&M delivery: a 3D viewer and dashboard consume twin visualisation, while grounded natural-language answers and gated tickets or revisits consume language-layer outputs. Read bottom-to-top and left-to-right as one decision chain, not as a software stack from a single paper. The finer technical path from UAV campaign to language-ready records is developed with Figure~\ref{fig:ch8-dt-pipeline} in Section~\ref{sec:ch8-dt-to-language}. Chapter-specific terms such as Asset-ID, asset passport, and cite-or-abstain are defined there as well, after Chapter~7 facility identity is connected.

\section{Foundations of LLMs for Civil Engineering Applications}
\label{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}

Ready LLMs can follow instructions, emit structured fields, and hold long evidence digests~\cite{brown2020,ouyang2022instructgpt,vaswani2017attention}. For inspection they help parse mixed spatial-symptom questions and draft briefs. Alone they remain unreliable: fluent text can invent host codes, erase RGB/IR disagreement, or recall the wrong manual edition---hallucination and parametric memory are not a site database~\cite{ji2023,lewis2020,saka2024gptconstruction}. Civil applications already wire models to BIM queries, editioned code RAG, FM tickets, and gated twin tools; value comes from data, tools, and workflows more than from base-model size alone~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes,krutli2025fmrags,gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot,ma2026tenquestions}. This chapter therefore treats LLMs as \emph{deployable language components}: prompted, constrained, and attached to twin stores---not fine-tuned as field databases. \emph{Grounded reasoning} on retrieved evidence is the behaviour that matters; linguistic fluency without citations is only drafting~\cite{lewis2020,krutli2025fmrags}.

This section introduces generative language technology for civil engineering and attaches it to inspection analysis. It organises ready-to-use techniques into three layers that compose into one decision stack. Perception remains Chapter~6's job; here language technologies consume typed, modality-tagged observations. Layer~L1 is the \emph{language interface}: prompts, templates, and stepwise explanation that parse questions, draft briefs, and keep observation separate from hypothesis. Layer~L2 is \emph{evidence grounding}: RAG, schemas, and tools that fetch twin records or manuals, constrain report fields, and enforce cite-or-abstain. Layer~L3 is \emph{memory and bounded procedures}: twin or RAG memory plus gated agents that keep site memory authoritative and put tickets or revisits behind gates. The rest of this section develops the three layers as continuous narrative and then shows how published AEC systems specialise, or fail to specialise, to fa\c{c}ade inspection.

Layer~L1 begins with what a ready model can already do when an orchestrator supplies a careful prompt. The model can parse mixed spatial-symptom questions such as ``mid-storey hollow with upstairs staining,'' draft readable briefs for different stakeholders, and follow a short answer template that forces citation of building objects already present in the retrieved package, separates observations from hypotheses, and emits an abstention code when checks fail~\cite{ouyang2022instructgpt,ji2023}. Stepwise prompting can then verbalise a propagation hypothesis, but only after neighbours have already entered the evidence package; otherwise the model may invent a structural story that merely sounds plausible~\cite{wei2022cot,ji2023}. A practical system prompt for fa\c{c}ade O\&M can therefore be short: classify intent; speak only about objects present in the retrieved package; preserve modality tags from Chapter~6; and abstain with an explicit reason when evidence is thin. Few-shot examples in the prompt should demonstrate abstention, not only success. What L1 must \emph{not} be asked to do is remember last campaign's register codes from parametric recall, invent crack lengths, or override twin facts when a hurried manager says ``just mark it repaired.'' Those duties belong to grounding and gates, not to fluent wording.

Layer~L2 is where inspection systems leave chat demos. RAG loads editioned manuals or observation cards at query time~\cite{lewis2020,shuster2021}; civil deployments already cover codes, safety manuals, facility-management documents, and defect dialogue~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,krutli2025fmrags,jeon2025hybridrag,he2025regulationhybrid}. Adaptive and self-reflective variants retry when retrieval looks weak~\cite{gao2023hyde,asai2024selfrag,jeong2024adaptive}, but the inspection design choice is sharper than ``add RAG'': for host-centred questions, prefer \emph{host-level twin records} over arbitrary PDF chunks~\cite{peng2024}. Structured and constrained generation then keeps outputs machine-checkable: report fields, computerised maintenance management system (CMMS) maps, and validators that require cited object codes to be subsets of the evidence set~\cite{pan2024b,chen2024bimllmontology,iversen2026,buga2025llmfmsql}. Tools and function calling separate reasoning from world actions: the model may request a host record bundle, expand neighbours, list observations, draft a report, or propose a revisit, but it must obtain those facts from the twin rather than invent them~\cite{schick2023toolformer,yao2023react,guo2025bimllmquery,liu2025bimcoder,li2025b,li2025c}. Twin writes and dispatch remain behind server-side approval~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent}. Long context does not authorise teams to dump an entire elevation into the prompt. Packing should prefer structured digests---host code, top symptoms, recent observation IDs, neighbour IDs, short location phrases---with image paths so humans can open evidence. Raw pixel dumps are avoided. If a digest still overflows, drop low-confidence observations first, not the topology links needed for a propagation question~\cite{lewis2020,asai2024selfrag}.

Layer~L3 makes the stack durable across sessions and actionable under control. Fa\c{c}ade O\&M questions often span campaigns, manual editions, and multiple maintainable hosts, so readers need a clear answer to \emph{where memory lives}. Figure~\ref{fig:ch8-memory-ladder} and Table~\ref{tab:ch8-memory} give a four-rung inspection memory ladder: from left to right, information becomes more site-specific and more authoritative for language use. Cognitive accounts of agent memory often distinguish working, episodic, semantic, and procedural stores; the inspection ladder is a field specialisation of that idea rather than a second taxonomy. \emph{Parametric memory} is whatever generic knowledge the weights already hold, useful for language, almost useless for last week's crack growth on a specific warehouse elevation~\cite{brown2020}. \emph{Contextual memory} is the prompt window and short dialogue state, the working store: powerful, length-limited, and session-fragile; tomorrow's new chat may forget that ``west'' meant the loading-dock face unless an external store kept the alias. \emph{Retrieval memory (RAG)} loads editioned manuals and observation cards on demand, the episodic and semantic digests that must stay edition-pinned~\cite{lewis2020,shuster2021,krutli2025fmrags}. The \emph{twin store} is structured long-term site memory: maintainable host codes, BIM/GIS geometry and topology, and time-stamped observations. For fa\c{c}ade O\&M the twin is authoritative; the model should read it through retrieval and tools, not pretend the weights remember the site~\cite{pan2024b,zhang2025}. Bounded agents then compose planning, memory, and tools into procedures such as tickets or revisits under geofence and human gates: procedural memory that lives in the harness described below, not as silent weight updates~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,rawat2025llmmas,pan2026graphdtgpt,zhang2025llmagentschema}. They are not a licence for unsupervised aircraft command or silent twin mutation. ReAct-style loops without retrieval merely re-enact parametric storytelling with extra steps~\cite{yao2023react}; tool calls without twin memory can hit the wrong host; unchecked memory streams can accumulate speculative notes as if they were facts~\cite{park2023generativeagents}; write gates therefore belong in the harness~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent}.

Agent-memory research often writes a generate-update-evolve loop over experiential entries~\cite{park2023generativeagents,yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents}. For fa\c{c}ade inspection we keep the same symbols but force \emph{externalised} evolution: the twin and harness change, model weights do not. Let $S_t$ be the reasoning state of query~$t$, covering intent, tool TRACE, and validator outcomes, and $M_t$ the external memory of twin store, edition pins, and procedural traces. Then
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ch8-mem-loop}
M_{t+1}=\mathrm{Update}\!\bigl(\mathrm{Generate}(S_t,M_t),\,M_t\bigr),\qquad
\pi_{t+1}=\mathrm{Evolve}_{\mathrm{ext}}(\pi_t,M_{t+1}),
\end{equation}
where $\mathrm{Generate}$ may emit a host-record digest, a rejected-draft note, or a revisit hint; $\mathrm{Update}$ merges only through gated writeback or procedural logs; and $\mathrm{Evolve}_{\mathrm{ext}}$ changes tool policy, edition pins, or retrieved packages inside the harness, never silent fine-tuning on site text~\cite{lewis2020,luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent}. In practical terms, session aliases remain short-lived, modality-tagged observations become episodic cards, host-level record bundles provide semantic digests, and approved traces preserve procedural memory. The twin store, edition pins, and gates therefore evolve the operating package while keeping site facts outside the model weights.

These three layers are meant to compose, not to compete. Consider again: ``Is mid-storey hollow on the west elevation related to upstairs staining, and should we stage a gondola this quarter?'' L1 parses the mixed utterance and forces a template. L2 tools fetch seed host records and expand BIM neighbours; RAG packs those cards plus any pinned manual clauses; structured generation emits a draft whose object codes are subsets of the evidence set. L3 ensures that the long-term twin, not yesterday's chat, is the store being read, and that any ticket or revisit proposal waits behind a human gate. Skip L2 and the answer becomes parametric storytelling; skip structured checks and gates and fluent text leaks into CMMS. That composition is generative AI for decision support made operational for fa\c{c}ade O\&M~\cite{lewis2020,yao2023react,ji2023}.

What holds the three layers together in a real system is a \emph{model harness}, the runtime control layer. In software testing, a harness is scaffolding that exercises a component under controlled conditions. In contemporary LLM engineering the same word has come to mean the code-owned control layer around a replaceable language model: it builds prompts, chooses retrieval units, exposes typed tools, validates outputs against contracts, records traces, and decides when to abstain or escalate. Agent surveys already describe planning, memory, and tools as the ingredients of autonomy~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent}; tool-using reason-act loops and self-supervised tool learning show how models request actions without owning the world state~\cite{yao2023react,schick2023toolformer}. Holistic evaluation frameworks such as HELM further remind readers that what is measured---scenarios, metrics, and standardised run conditions---is itself a harness for comparison, not a single accuracy number~\cite{liang2023helm}. For fa\c{c}ade inspection, object-identity contracts, edition pins, modality tags, and write gates should live in the harness so that swapping the underlying LLM does not silently rewrite O\&M policy.

The inspection harness has six cooperating parts; see Table~\ref{tab:ch8-harness}. \emph{Intake} classifies intent; \emph{asset resolution} maps place phrases to Asset-IDs preferably by deterministic GeoBIM lookup. \emph{Evidence assembly} loads passports, typed neighbours, observations, and pinned manuals without free generation~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024}. The \emph{tool surface} admits typed twin reads and draft actions only---no free SQL or flight commands~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot}. \emph{Validators} own V1--V4 checks in code; \emph{trace} records tools, editions, and pass/fail so runs are replayable~\cite{ji2023,liang2023helm}. Adaptive retrieval retries stay inside the harness as controlled loops~\cite{asai2024selfrag,jeong2024adaptive}.

Read Figure~\ref{fig:ch8-harness-fig} clockwise as one query around the central harness. Intake resolves intent and seed host codes before any drafting. Evidence assembly packs host records, neighbours, and pinned manuals. The tool surface admits only typed twin reads and draft actions. The replaceable LLM composes language inside that package. The dashed LLM box is intentional: model weights may change; the surrounding contracts should not.

Two consequences follow for procurement and teaching. First, a prompt-only prototype is not yet a harness: instructions such as ``always cite object codes'' can be ignored by a fluent model, whereas a validator that blocks delivery when the code-subset check fails is load-bearing. Second, model substitution should be a harness test, not a project rewrite: if validators, tool schemas, and twin interfaces stay fixed, changing the hosted LLM should change phrasing, not which sources are eligible or whether a write gate exists~\cite{liang2023helm,saka2024gptconstruction}. In short: Layers~L1--L3 name the capabilities; the harness is the software that enforces them on every query.

Building LLM work likewise puts data readiness and gates ahead of parameter counts~\cite{ma2026tenquestions,pan2026graphdtgpt,gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot}. Costly inspection failures are concrete: invented codes, advice without evidence, and RGB/IR disagreement averaged away~\cite{ji2023}. Embeddings may rank digests inside a topology-constrained set; they must not invent neighbours for moisture paths~\cite{peng2024,jeon2025hybridrag}. Precedence when sources conflict: safety and write gates; twin fields; pinned manuals; user style last~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction}. Simple memory rule: twin store write-gated; dialogue ephemeral; RAG editioned; parametric memory never a site database~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,rawat2025llmmas}.

Selecting an inspection stack is primarily a harness problem: validators and tool gates must be code-owned; tool schemas and twin interfaces must stay fixed when a hosted LLM is swapped~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,liang2023helm}. Hand-checks that matter before leaderboards: object codes $\subseteq$ retrieved set; hypotheses tagged; modality tags preserved; abstention when neighbours are missing~\cite{ji2023,shuster2021,lewis2020}. Sections~\ref{sec:ch8-dt-to-language}--\ref{sec:ch8-multimodal} next make Layers~L2 and~L3 operational.



In summary of the foundations, Layers~L1--L3 name what a ready model can do under control, and the harness names the software that enforces those behaviours on every query. Readers who skip validators and tool gates will rediscover parametric storytelling regardless of model size~\cite{ji2023,liang2023helm,ma2026tenquestions}. This chapter treats the language layer as a checkable, handover-ready decision interface rather than a fluent but un-auditable chat demo. After reading it through, a reader should be able to explain where an answer fails when the twin lacks one minimum prerequisite in Section~\ref{sec:ch8-dt-to-language}, Table~\ref{tab:ch8-twin-contract}; which L1--L3 layer a fa\c{c}ade question belongs to and which harness part in Table~\ref{tab:ch8-harness} carries it; how host records and BIM neighbours assemble a cite-or-abstain package for propagation or historical-record questions; where Section~\ref{sec:ch8-multimodal} draws the line between a VLM and the Chapter~6 detector; and how a twin write or revisit proposal leaves a replayable tool TRACE at a human gate~\cite{peng2024,yao2023react,liang2023helm,hellin2025bimmas}. \section{From Digital Twins to Language Reasoning}
\label{sec:ch8-dt-to-language}

Language reasoning over an inspection twin is not free-form storytelling. It is assembly of evidence that earlier chapters already created, the operational substrate for Layers~L2 and~L3 in Section~\ref{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}. Chapter~7 already supplies a maintainable GeoBIM host and facility-register identity; this section abbreviates such a stable building-object code as an \emph{Asset-ID}, meaning a long-lived twin identifier that can align with work orders and CMMS records, not a temporary filename or detector-box label. Language can be grounded only when object identity, provenance, neighbours, time, and modality are machine-checkable.

Table~\ref{tab:ch8-twin-contract} is not an IT wishlist; it is the digital continuation of field craft. Seasoned inspectors photograph before writing, name bays before diagnosing, and refuse to sign reports that cannot be walked on site. A decision-ready twin makes those habits machine-checkable across campaigns~\cite{zhang2022,chen2023facility}. Geographic scale sharpens the same point: a campus or housing estate multiplies identifier collisions and manual-edition conflicts, so city-scale identity must stay coherent in the GIS layer rather than being ``fixed'' later by clever prose~\cite{zhang2025,han2025}.

Chapters~2--7 already cover flight planning, reconstruction, defect detection, and GeoBIM attachment separately. Figure~\ref{fig:ch8-dt-pipeline} collects those chapters into the \emph{modelling path} that the language layer consumes, so readers can see what the language system is actually reading. Read it in three stages. \emph{Stage~I (physical data collection)}: the UAV collects imagery, pose (POS/RTK), and optical parameters together with the BIM database and task metadata; panel~(b) shows the flight path, image matrix, and BIM shell. \emph{Stage~II (twin modelling and processing)}: structure-from-motion and multi-view stereo (SfM/MVS) build a mesh, a deep-learning detector supplies defect class and confidence, and registration maps detections to BIM surface coordinates; panel~(c) shows the mesh, defect metadata, and localised defects. \emph{Stage~III (GIS+BIM virtual integration)}: the platform organises defects hierarchically, for example project, storey, component (such as Wall N 204), then defect entry (such as Defect 01), and opens the target image in a 3D viewer; panel~(d) shows the structured defect panel and online visualisation. The language layer does not repeat these algorithms, but it requires their outputs to satisfy Table~\ref{tab:ch8-twin-contract} once ingested, especially \emph{stable host codes}, \emph{openable imagery}, and \emph{BIM adjacency}. The component and defect codes visible in the Phase~III tree are the platform-side form of Chapter~7 facility-register identity; this section abbreviates them as \emph{Asset-IDs}, distinct from temporary filenames or detector-box labels.

Four persistent entities, plus one assembly view, organise the twin for language use. An \emph{Asset} is a long-lived maintainable building object---fa\c{c}ade panel, wall bay, window unit, structural component, or drainage element---identified by a canonical Asset-ID. An \emph{Observation} is one timed, modality-specific sighting with at least the fields below.
\begin{programcode}{Minimum observation fields}
\begin{plainlisting}
observation id; asset id; campaign timestamp; modality;\\
detector version; defect label; confidence;\\
surface coordinate; evidence URI; registration confidence.
\end{plainlisting}
\end{programcode}
A \emph{Defect instance} is the engineering entity that may persist across one or more observations; one observation is never assumed to be a long-lived defect. Suggested states are candidate, confirmed, under review, repair planned, repaired, closed, and recurrent. Matching new observations to old instances, splitting or merging after remeshing, and encoding recurrence after repair are twin hygiene problems before they are language problems~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg}. A \emph{Repair event} records approved maintenance outcomes. An \emph{asset passport} is an editioned \emph{materialised retrieval unit}, not a second truth store: Assets, Observations, Defect instances, and Repair events persist; the passport aggregates identity, geometry digest~$G(a)$, typed neighbours, active observations~$O(a)$, defect instances~$D(a)$, symptom digest~$d(a)$, freshness, and provenance for query loading. The \emph{evidence package}~$\mathcal{C}(q)$ is a query-time read-only context assembled from passports and pinned manuals~\cite{pan2024b,zheng2023bimassistant}.

Topology is a typed, directed graph $G=(V,E,\tau)$ with vertices~$V$ as assets, edges~$E$ as relations, and $\tau(e)$ the relation type. Minimal types include above, below, adjacent to, shares joint with, drains to, same fa\c{c}ade system, structurally connected to, and accessed with. Propagation must not treat all edges alike: a moisture intent may admit
$R_{\mathrm{moisture}}=\{\mathrm{above},\mathrm{drains\_to},\mathrm{shares\_joint\_with}\}$,
while gondola scheduling may use
$R_{\mathrm{access}}=\{\mathrm{same\_facade\_system},\mathrm{accessed\_with}\}$~\cite{peng2024}.
Cross-version identity needs an explicit mapping so historical queries survive IFC re-export, host split/merge, and GIS--BIM--CMMS disagreement~\cite{zhang2025,han2025}. At minimum the map keeps a canonical Asset-ID as the stable twin key; the source system and source object ID; a validity window (model version, valid from/to); predecessor and successor IDs for split, merge, or remodel; and audit fields for mapping method, confidence, and approver.

Query assembly writes records once, then lets intent choose the package. A passport for host~$a$ is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ch8-passport}
P(a)=\bigl\langle a,\,G(a),\,N_{\tau}(a),\,O(a),\,D(a),\,d(a)\bigr\rangle,
\end{equation}
where $N_{\tau}(a)$ are typed neighbours. Given seeds~$A_q$, hop depth~$k(\iota)$, and allowed edge types~$R(\iota)$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ch8-khop}
\mathcal{N}_{k,R}(A_q)=\bigl\{v:\exists\,a\in A_q,\;\mathrm{dist}_{R}(a,v)\le k\bigr\},
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ch8-package}
\mathcal{C}(q)=\mathrm{Pack}\!\Bigl(\bigl\{P(v):v\in\mathcal{N}_{k(\iota),R(\iota)}(A_q)\bigr\},\,R_{\mathrm{man}}(q),\,e\Bigr),
\end{equation}
with $R_{\mathrm{man}}(q)$ an optional edition-pinned manual slice and $e$ the edition pin~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024,joffe2025buildingcodes}. Local lookup uses $k=0$; moisture propagation uses $R_{\mathrm{moisture}}$; elevation audits use Chapter~7 spatial filters. Embedding similarity may rank digests inside an already topology-constrained set; it must not invent the candidate set~\cite{peng2024,jeon2025hybridrag}.

One recurring implementation pattern organises observations into asset passports and expands neighbours along BIM adjacency for multi-hop diagnosis. Published siblings share the grounding intuition on other corpora: hybrid defect RAG~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag}, code and safety RAG~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety}, facility-document RAG~\cite{krutli2025fmrags}, and BIM assistants~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant}. The monograph lesson is the pattern: inspection questions are asset-centric, so retrieval units should be asset-centric. When the assembly cannot support a claim, the correct behaviour is abstention or escalation to a human inspector, not a confident paragraph.

Worked micro-example. Suppose the manager asks: ``Is water ingress on the west stack likely propagating from above?'' Assembly should load the target passport, upstairs and lateral neighbours, observation cards tagged RGB and infrared, and any editioned moisture-related maintenance note linked to those Asset-IDs. Generation may then hypothesise a path \emph{only} if neighbour observations exist; otherwise it must say that propagation cannot be supported from the twin. That discipline is language reasoning over a twin, not storytelling beside a twin.


Implementation teams often ask how large a passport should be. Too small, and propagation reading starves. Too large, and the context window fills with irrelevant neighbours from an entire elevation. A practical teaching heuristic is intent-conditioned expansion: local lookup uses zero hops; propagation uses one hop or a small $k$ along BIM adjacency; elevation audits use spatial filters from Chapter~7 . Unbounded hops are avoided~\cite{peng2024,zhang2025}. Manual clauses are retrieved only when the question mentions compliance, safety, or repair standards~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety}.

Indexing choices should follow the same heuristic. Vector indexes over passport digests support fuzzy symptom search such as ``hollow sounding panels with staining above''. Graph or relational indexes over Asset-IDs support exact neighbour expansion. Keyword indexes over observation IDs support audit lookups. Hybrid retrieval is attractive precisely because inspection questions mix fuzzy language with exact identifiers~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag,krutli2025fmrags}. What should not be hybridised away is the authority of topology: embedding similarity must not silently replace BIM adjacency when the question is about propagation.

Cite-or-abstain remains the teaching shorthand for the minimum contract, but it is not a full truth guarantee. Validation has four layers. \emph{V1 Identity:} every Asset-ID, Observation-ID, and Defect-ID in the draft must appear in~$\mathcal{C}(q)$:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ch8-cite}
\mathrm{IDs}(\hat{y})\subseteq\mathrm{IDs}\bigl(\mathcal{C}(q)\bigr)
\quad\text{else return }ABSTAIN.
\end{equation}
\emph{V2 Field:} numeric and structured fields (crack length, confidence, capture date, relation path) must bind to allowed twin fields, not parametric invention. \emph{V3 Claim--evidence:} each diagnostic statement declares claim\_type (observation vs hypothesis), evidence\_ids, optional relation\_path, and limitations. \emph{V4 Action:} repair or revisit proposals state approval need, manual edition (if any), and whether the act is irreversible. The accurate rule is therefore \emph{validate, cite, qualify, or abstain}~\cite{ji2023,shuster2021,peng2024}.

A second worked micro-example clarifies elevation audits. The manager asks: ``Summarise west-elevation issues above level three that need gondola access.'' Assembly should apply a Chapter~7 spatial filter, load the resulting passport set, keep modality tags, and rank by severity features inherited from geometry. It should not narrate the whole building, and it should not drop Asset-IDs in favour of storey prose alone. The output is a decision artefact for scheduling, not a travelogue.



Managers often ask whether a stain is ``new.'' Querying historical maintenance records is a first-class capability of the decision layer and a first-class assembly intent. Cross-campaign comparison requires the same Asset-ID across time, comparable modality coverage, prior observation IDs or work-order links when available, and explicit handling of missing priors. If the previous campaign lacks infrared, the system must not invent a thermal delta. If geometry was remeshed and Asset-IDs were remapped carelessly, language will compare the wrong hosts. A natural-language question such as ``what was repaired on the west elevation after the 2024 campaign?'' should retrieve editioned twin memory and any linked approved reports; it must abstain when those IDs are absent rather than invent a repair narrative~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg,chen2023facility}. Longitudinal discipline is therefore twin hygiene before it is model cleverness~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg,zhao2026physical}. Perception may emit confidences; geometry may support length or area features; O\&M may need ordinal severity for scheduling. The language layer should not convert ordinal triage into false millimetre certainty. Prefer phrases tied to fields, such as ``severity=medium from geometry-normalised crack length; confidence=0.71 from detector'', over cinematic adjectives. Physical quantification pipelines show why numbers need provenance~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}; the LLM's job is to carry that provenance into sentences, not to decorate it.

Algorithm~\ref{alg:ch8-assembly} summarises assembly as a numbered procedure. It is deliberately dull: every branch is a place students try to be clever, and where inspection systems usually break~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024,jeon2025hybridrag}.

\begin{algorithm}[htbp]
\caption{Intent-conditioned twin assembly for a grounded inspection answer.}
\label{alg:ch8-assembly}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\Require natural-language query $q$
\Ensure draft answer, or a structured abstention code
\State $\mathit{intent} \gets \textsc{Classify}(q)$
\State $\mathit{seeds} \gets \textsc{ResolveAssets}(q)$ \Comment{else ABSTAIN:asset\_unresolved}
\State $\mathit{packs} \gets \{\textsc{Passport}(s): s\in\mathit{seeds}\}$
\If{$\mathit{intent}=propagation$}
 \State $\mathit{packs} \gets \mathit{packs}\cup\textsc{Expand}(\mathit{seeds},k{=}1,R{=}R_{\mathrm{moisture}})$
\EndIf
\If{$\mathit{intent}=elevation\_audit$}
 \State $\mathit{packs} \gets \textsc{SpatialFilter}(\mathit{packs},q)$
\EndIf
\State $\mathit{obs} \gets \textsc{ListObservations}(\mathit{packs})$
\State $\mathit{manuals} \gets \textsc{RetrieveManuals}(q)$ if $\textsc{NeedsCode}(q)$, else $\emptyset$
\State $\mathcal{C}(q) \gets \{\mathit{intent},\mathit{packs},\mathit{obs},\mathit{manuals},\mathit{edition\_pin}\}$
\State $\hat{y} \gets \textsc{Generate}(\mathcal{C}(q))$
\State $\textsc{ValidateV1toV4}(\hat{y},\mathcal{C}(q))$ \Comment{else qualify or ABSTAIN}
\State \Return $\hat{y}$
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

Natural-language locations remain ambiguous: ``third bay'' may mean different hosts after remeshing. Resolution against GeoBIM aliases must precede passport fetch. Fuzzy string match can propose candidates; it must not silently pick one when two candidates score closely. Disambiguation questions are successful system behaviour, not UX failure~\cite{zhang2025,han2025,guo2025bimllmquery}. The same discipline appears in recent twin-query systems that refuse to answer from unconstrained text when the structured store lacks a path~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,buga2025llmfmsql}: for inspection, ``no upstairs neighbour in the twin'' is a better product behaviour than a fluent moisture story.

Published 2024--2025 retrieval practice also clarifies what not to reinvent. Hybrid defect-management RAG and safety-manual RAG show gains when corpora are editioned and noisy maintenance language is expected~\cite{jeon2025hybridrag,lee2024ragsafety}; code and regulation hybrids show that clause retrieval must still meet site evidence before advice becomes operational~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,he2025regulationhybrid,yang2024promptcode}. Facility-document RAG and structured FM querying show that building corpora scale only when schemas and Asset-ID links exist~\cite{krutli2025fmrags,buga2025llmfmsql,arslan2024ragenergy}. Translating those results into UAV fa\c{c}ade O\&M means treating observation cards and asset passports as the primary retrieval units, manuals as secondary pinned context, and topology expansion as a first-class assembly step~\cite{peng2024,pan2024b,ma2026tenquestions}.




Assembly discipline can be restated as teaching narrative that facilities and software teams share. Local lookups should load only the target passport and recent observations; stuffing an entire elevation into context makes cite-or-abstain hard to explain~\cite{lewis2020,peng2024}. Propagation questions require topology edges before symptom similarity; if edges are missing, abstain with no\_neighbour . Embedding a stain from the opposite elevation is refused~\cite{peng2024,jeon2025hybridrag,pan2026graphdtgpt}. Elevation audits should follow Chapter~7 spatial filters and return schedulable Asset-ID lists, not travelogues~\cite{zhang2025,buga2025llmfmsql}. Code-plus-site questions must pin manual editions and site passports together; clause-only or evidence-only packages should disclose the gap~\cite{joffe2025buildingcodes,lee2024ragsafety,he2025regulationhybrid,quan2026bimfacrag}. Cross-campaign comparisons put identity hygiene first: if Asset-IDs were remapped carelessly, abstain on ``worse than last time''~\cite{zhang2025,yang2025detreconreg,zhao2026physical}. BIM NL multi-agent retrieval and condensed retrieval agents remind readers that clever navigation is not yet topology-aware diagnosis~\cite{hellin2025bimmas,koh2026bimllmretr,liu2025bimcoder}; facility RAG reminds them that document lakes without Asset-ID links still struggle to enter CMMS~\cite{krutli2025fmrags,quan2026bimfacrag}.

\section{Multi-Modal Fusion}
\label{sec:ch8-multimodal}

In this chapter, multi-modal fusion refers primarily to \emph{decision-level} and \emph{language-level} orchestration of already registered modality-specific observations, rather than pixel-level or feature-level sensor fusion. Table~\ref{tab:ch8-fusion-levels} states the boundary. Fa\c{c}ade campaigns routinely produce RGB, infrared, and textual metadata; Chapter~6 writes typed observations and Chapters~4 and~7 attach them. Language retrieves, explains, abstains, and proposes actions under V1--V4~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}.

Vision--language models (VLMs) produce \emph{non-authoritative derived interpretation} by default. A VLM may describe an attached crop, draft a caption, help match inspector notes, or propose review questions. Without approval it must not change detector labels, invent Asset-IDs, rewrite host attachment, promote a hypothesis to confirmed, update repair status, or override human inspection. Detector--VLM conflict enters a modality or model conflict state and must escalate to human review, not LLM arbitration~\cite{ji2023,zhao2026physical}.

Before comparing RGB and infrared cards, assembly must check spatial alignment, capture time, environment, calibration, host-attachment confidence, and detector confidence. Observations such as RGB on a dry morning and IR two evenings later after rain are not temporally comparable; the system should say so rather than emit a bare ``modalities disagree''~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}. When cards are comparable and still conflict, disclosure beats silent averaging; never collapse conflict into one confident defect type without a human-check flag.

Bounded agents propose multi-step procedures---tickets, revisit hints for Chapters~2--3, staged twin writes---inside the harness of Section~\ref{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}. Final claims stay citation-locked; dispatch and writes stay behind geofence and human gates; the language agent proposes and does not commandeer aircraft~\cite{yao2023react,luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,wang2024surveyagents}.

Worked micro-example. The manager asks for a revisit where infrared suggests moisture but RGB is inconclusive. A bounded agent may list the modality-tagged observations, notice the conflict, draft a report that marks human review, and emit a coarse revisit hint. It must not invent a flight trajectory in prose, and it must not write a new defect type into the twin without approval. That is multimodal fusion in the sense of this book: modality-aware records, grounded language, and tools that respect upstream chapter ownership.


Multi-modal fusion also interacts with campaign design. If infrared and RGB are captured on different days, time stamps must remain visible in observation cards; otherwise the LLM may treat them as simultaneous evidence. If one modality has systematically lower detector confidence, trust flags should inherit that asymmetry and must not average it away~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical}. Fusion at language time is therefore inseparable from perception metadata quality. Garbage in still yields fluent garbage out, only now with citations that look official.

Agent design should stay boring on purpose. Spectacular autonomous stacks that rewrite BIM, dispatch aircraft, and email contractors in one loop are poor teaching targets for inspection safety. Bounded tools with explicit gates match how responsible O\&M already works: analysts propose; seniors approve; field teams execute~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,zhang2025llmagentschema}. Multi-agent role splits into retriever, topology expander, report drafter, and action proposer can be useful pedagogically, provided they share one twin memory and one approval policy~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,rawat2025llmmas}. Competing agents that invent different Asset-IDs for the same panel are not ``diversity''; they are a failure mode.

Procedural memory deserves a concrete inspection reading. When an agent has already expanded neighbours and listed observations, it should not re-expand endlessly in a loop. When a human rejected a twin write, that rejection should be remembered for the session so the agent does not resubmit the same mutation. When a revisit was proposed, the planning hint should carry the observation IDs that justified it, so Chapters~2--3 receive evidence with observation IDs~\cite{yao2023react,rawat2025llmmas}. Memory, in this sense, is operational discipline.



When demonstrating agents, show the tool trace beside the answer: which passports were fetched, which neighbours expanded, which manual edition pinned, which gate blocked a write. Traces make bounded agency inspectable and turn the harness into a teaching object. Surveys of LLM agents emphasise planning and tools; inspection teaching should emphasise the audit trail those tools leave~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,yao2023react,schick2023toolformer,liang2023helm}. A beautiful answer without a trace is a demo; a plain answer with a trace is an O\&M artefact. Splitting retriever, topology, drafter, and action roles can clarify software modules, as graph-twin and human-DT MAS designs also do~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,lu2026highwaymas}, but must not create competing Asset-ID realities. All roles read the same twin memory; only the gated writer may stage updates; only humans approve. Construction multi-agent frameworks that combine graph and vector memory are useful precedents for shared memory, not for unsupervised committee decisions about defects~\cite{rawat2025llmmas,gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot}.

A compact walkthrough makes the harness concrete. For a moisture-path question on host W-3-17, resolve intent as propagation, load the passport, expand one-hop neighbours, list modality-tagged observations, and abstain if upstairs evidence is missing. Draft under cite-or-abstain. If the manager requests action, draft a report that marks human review on RGB/IR conflict and propose a revisit only for conflicted assets; wait for approval in procedural memory; stage a twin write only after approve. Logging every step is the product; hiding steps to look clever is the anti-pattern~\cite{yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents,luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,lu2026highwaymas}. A short audit trace might record: intent=propagation; tools used=read passport, expand neighbours, list observations; package hosts=W-3-17, W-4-17, W-3-16; modality conflict on W-3-17; report draft awaiting human review; revisit pending; twin writes=none. Revisit tools must emit planning hints, not executable UAV commands. Coordinates should be coarse polygons or asset sets that Chapters~2--3 refine under their own safety stack. Robot photo requests, when available, should be tickets. Direct motor commands are refused~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot}. Once the user asks for tickets or revisits, the orchestrator can enter an explicit state machine with stages draft, review, approved or rejected, then scheduled; the LLM fills fields inside a stage and must not skip review because the user is in a hurry~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,zhang2025llmagentschema,rawat2025llmmas,lu2026highwaymas}. Agent tests can stay offline: stub tools return fixtures; assert that forbidden writes never occur; assert revisit hints always carry observation IDs; assert rejected drafts never reappear without new evidence. Those tests are cheaper than flight campaigns and catch the worst failures early~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent,ma2026tenquestions}.

Agents earn their complexity only for procedures. Premature agency increases gate surface area without increasing grounding~\cite{yao2023react,wang2024surveyagents}.




Conflict-asset week closes multimodal teaching without a subsection forest. Suppose three west-elevation hosts show RGB/IR conflict in one week: the system must not average them into one defect type; gondola lists should down-rank them by default; revisit hints must bind observation IDs for Chapters~2--3~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical,luo2025llmdtrobot}. If a manager asks to ``mark repaired first and gather evidence later,'' the harness must refuse direct writes and only stage pending updates behind approval~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,lu2026highwaymas}. When demonstrating agents, show TRACE beside the answer: passports fetched, neighbours expanded, manual edition pinned, which gate blocked a write. A beautiful answer without TRACE remains a demo; a plain answer with TRACE is an O\&M artefact~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,liang2023helm,hellin2025bimmas}. Role splits may clarify software modules, but they must not create multiple Asset-ID truths; graph-twin and human-DT MAS papers are precedents for shared memory and approval, not for unsupervised committee votes on defects~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,lu2026highwaymas,rawat2025llmmas,koh2026bimllmretr}.



In summary of multimodal fusion and bounded agency, modality tags travel with observation cards, TRACE makes tool use inspectable, and write gates keep the twin authoritative~\cite{zhao2026physical,yao2023react,luo2025llmdtrobot,lu2026highwaymas}. The next section turns grounded drafts into report schemas that facility teams can audit, with optional export into existing work systems.

Report lifecycle is a state machine with ordered stages: draft, validated, under review, approved or rejected, exported, write staged, then write committed.
Forbidden transitions include writing the twin from a draft, exporting after failed validation, automatically re-proposing rejected drafts, promoting possible findings to confirmed without approval, and approving when an evidence URI is broken. A replayable TRACE must record twin snapshot, graph version, manual edition, embedding model, LLM name/version, system-prompt version, tool-schema version, retrieval parameters, validator version, generation parameters, operator, reviewer, and timestamp~\cite{liang2023helm}.

A fluent chat that invents panel nicknames or marks hosts repaired without approval fails the same tests~\cite{ji2023,zhang2025,buga2025llmfmsql}. Section~\ref{sec:ch8-dss} returns to Sid with the platform stack and other device screens.

Report schemas should be negotiated with facility stakeholders early. Fields that nobody will open in a CMMS will not be maintained. Fields that lawyers require for liability may need stricter cite-or-abstain checks than fields used for internal triage. A teaching compromise is a core schema covering identity, diagnosis, evidence links, and trust, plus optional organisation-specific extensions covering cost codes, contractor IDs, and access windows. The LLM may draft extensions only from retrieved context or human-provided forms; it must not invent cost codes~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes}.

Human-in-the-loop is not a confession of failure; it is the product. Sign-off records should store who approved what, which evidence IDs were visible at approval time, and whether modality conflict flags were overridden. Those records become part of twin provenance for the next campaign. Without them, next season's LLM will treat last season's speculative draft as fact. Identity hygiene figures in this section are therefore not IT folklore: they are the precondition for longitudinal language reasoning~\cite{zhang2025}.

The same contracts expose weak demos quickly: PDF-only RAG without Asset-IDs, chat UIs that cannot open cited images, twin writes from unapproved drafts, severity language without geometry normalisation, or propagation stories built from embedding neighbours instead of BIM adjacency. Two or more of those marks leave the system a prototype. Delivery itself has a fixed half and an optional half. The fixed half is the schema-constrained draft with openable evidence: identity, diagnosis, trust flags, and URIs that open the correct crops. Asset-ID hygiene and cite-or-abstain are part of that half; they are not the whole product. Narrative must be rendered from filled fields; IDs must not be scraped from prose after the fact; nightly link checks belong beside detector quality control~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes,zhang2025,gao2026ifcagent}. The optional half is exporting those fields into whatever work system a site already runs. That sink may be a CMMS, a BIM issue tracker, a contractor portal, or a simple spreadsheet; this chapter does not invent a new CMMS product. When an export exists, keep the mapping short and mechanical: asset ids map to register codes, evidence URIs map to attachments, facility-scale priority replaces an LLM-invented scale, and a human-check flag holds auto-close~\cite{chen2023facility,krutli2025fmrags,buga2025llmfmsql}. Sites without a CMMS still succeed if they keep the constrained draft, openable evidence, and human sign-off.

Handover packages should therefore carry the Asset-ID dictionary, observation schema, manual edition pins, approval logs, and a frozen twin snapshot used for language evaluation. An export map is included only when the receiving system is known. Report-lifecycle thickness comes from those contracts: drafts pass ID subset validation before human review, rejections enter procedural memory, approved fields may export with minimal rewrite, and Asset-ID collisions stay visible beside the draft~\cite{buga2025llmfmsql,chen2023facility,quan2026bimfacrag,hellin2025bimmas}. Published FM robot and IFC-agent loops close a similar circle of language request, structured action, and twin or model update under approval; fa\c{c}ade O\&M specialises the circle so that every actionable artefact still cites openable observation crops before money or scaffolding moves~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot,gao2026ifcagent,saka2024gptconstruction}. An ``official'' PDF without openable evidence is more dangerous than a chat hallucination for exactly that reason~\cite{ji2023}.

After each major flight week, freeze a twin snapshot, re-run Table~\ref{tab:ch8-report-accept} on a gold question set that includes abstentions, and score invented-ID rate and broken-evidence rate before swapping any hosted LLM~\cite{liang2023helm,zhang2025}. Cross-campaign claims such as ``worse than last season'' require prior observation IDs in the package; otherwise the harness must force abstention or a human-only narrative field. That seasonal hygiene protects longitudinal language the same way Asset-ID hygiene protects a single draft~\cite{chen2023facility,krutli2025fmrags}. The authoritative artefact remains a schema-constrained draft with openable evidence and human sign-off; Table~\ref{tab:ch8-report-accept} checks that core, while any work-system export remains an optional later recipe. The next section asks whether those artefacts actually change schedules under implementation-agnostic criteria.

\section{Decision Support and Predictive Maintenance}
\label{sec:ch8-dss}

Decision support in this section has three layers that must not be conflated. \emph{Maintenance prioritisation} ranks current defects by severity, access, and evidence completeness for the next gondola week. \emph{Conditional decision support} states what follows if evidence is missing---for example, abstain on moisture propagation when upstairs IR is absent and recommend a revisit. \emph{Predictive maintenance} requires an external time or deterioration model (multi-campaign series, survival or state-transition estimates, remaining-life); the LLM may explain those model outputs under citation, but must not invent future failure probabilities from a single campaign~\cite{chen2023facility,zhao2026physical}. Evaluation criteria still travel across implementations: cited IDs in the evidence set; neighbours and history when needed; editioned manuals over parametric recall; schedule-usable outputs; gated tool TRACE~\cite{ji2023,lee2024ragsafety,luo2025llmdtrobot}. Table~\ref{tab:ch8-eval} states the shared rubric; Table~\ref{tab:ch8-cap-maturity} separates proposed, implemented, evaluated, and future capabilities so proposal language is not mistaken for field proof.

The rubric should be scored at the level of actions, not only at the level of sentences. A grounded paragraph that never changes a maintenance schedule has limited O\&M value; a terse ranked list of Asset-IDs with openable evidence may already plan a gondola week. Monthly boards can therefore track invented-ID rate, openable-evidence rate, modality-conflict disclosure rate, revisit-hint adoption, and, when a work-system export exists, export no-rewrite rate~\cite{lee2024ragsafety,luo2025llmdtrobot,rawat2025llmmas,liang2023helm}. If a hosted-LLM swap improves fluency but worsens invented IDs, reject the swap. If fluency is unchanged but TRACE completeness improves, accept the harness change. Model-size leaderboards remain secondary to those operability curves.

Figure~\ref{fig:ch8-ablation} supplies one compact quantitative check for that rubric: under a frozen twin, packaging policy and controlled one-hop expansion can raise ID containment before any fluency ranking. Readers should still judge systems by intent coverage and by the safety of the final action, including whether cite-or-abstain validators catch unsupported IDs~\cite{ji2023,peng2024,lee2024ragsafety}.

Predictive maintenance in this chapter is therefore three deliverables, not one slogan. First, \emph{triage}: a ranked Asset-ID list with openable evidence for the next gondola week. Second, \emph{conditional advice}: statements such as ``risk rises if infrared is missing on the upstairs neighbour'' that name the missing evidence and stop short of inventing it. Third, \emph{external prognosis}: when a non-LLM temporal model exists, the harness may present its read-only output beside cited observations; the language system must not invent year-ahead spalling probabilities from a single campaign~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,zhang2025llmagentschema,zhao2026physical}.

Software has to make those decision layers usable. In this chapter we call the working front end Sid---a product-style name for the screens, not a technical acronym. Sid is not a second perception stack. It is built from Chapters~2--7 twin products plus the harness contracts already taught: passports, typed neighbours, cite-or-abstain, and gated writes.

Middle: an API gateway and application services that read the twin, assemble evidence, and draft reports under the harness. Bottom: Asset-IDs, observations, and editioned manuals outside model weights. In plain terms, Sid asks through the gateway, the twin answers with checkable records, and only approved actions leave the gate~\cite{gao2026ifcagent,luo2025llmdtrobot}.

One end-to-end walkthrough is enough to bind those criteria to practice. Consider a two-day warehouse fa\c{c}ade campaign that Chapters~2--7 have already turned into a decision-ready twin. Day~1 yields RGB coverage; Day~2 adds infrared over suspected moisture zones; perception writes modality-tagged observations; registration and GeoBIM keep host Asset-IDs aligned with the facility register. On Day~3 the manager asks four meeting questions. Local lookup loads only the passport for W-3-17 and returns openable observations. Cross-campaign comparison abstains if prior observation IDs are missing. Propagation expands upstairs and lateral neighbours, keeps RGB/IR tags, and either drafts a cited path hypothesis or refuses. Elevation audit applies a Chapter~7 spatial filter and emits a schema-constrained gondola list. Only when the manager requests action does the bounded agent draft tickets, attach evidence IDs, set a human-review flag on modality conflicts, and emit revisit hints for Chapters~2--3. It does not email contractors, rewrite BIM geometry, or clear conflict flags. Approved facts write back to twin memory; rejected drafts enter procedural memory and are not resubmitted in the same session. That single story is the chapter spine under load: twin contract, memory ladder, asset-centric assembly, multimodal disclosure, gated tools, report lifecycle, and operability metrics.

Deployment should follow the same spine. Begin by freezing Asset-IDs and neighbour edges, admitting only valid observation cards, and limiting use to local and health questions until topology is checked. Then enable one-hop expansion with a pinned manual edition, dry-run the five-tool surface with writes blocked, and introduce frozen-snapshot scoring and CMMS mapping before any hosted-LLM substitution~\cite{zhang2025,zhao2026physical,peng2024,lee2024ragsafety,gao2026ifcagent,liang2023helm,ma2026tenquestions,buga2025llmfmsql}. Graph-twin and multi-agent studies may inform role splits inside that programme, but they do not remove the need for identity hygiene~\cite{pan2026graphdtgpt,rawat2025llmmas,lu2026highwaymas}.

Triage language sits between diagnosis and schedule. An \emph{illustrative, non-operational} score is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ch8-score}
s = w_{\ell}\,\tilde{\ell} + w_{c}\,c + w_{a}\,a + w_{m}\,m,
\end{equation}
where $\tilde{\ell}$ is geometry-normalised defect size, $c$ detector confidence, $a$ access urgency, and $m$ a modality-conflict term. The weights $w_{\cdot}$ are facility placeholders set by managers or MCDA; they must not be used for field scheduling without calibration, sensitivity analysis, and professional approval. Detector confidence is not structural risk; modality conflict may raise revisit priority rather than simply lower~$s$. The language system exposes cited terms behind each score and lets humans reorder: $\tilde{\ell}$ from twin geometry and observations (no invented millimetre claims), $c$ from the Chapter~6 detector (keep confidence visible), $a$ from the FM calendar or access plan (do not invent dates), and $m$ from RGB/IR conflict flags (disclose disagreement). Surface cracks require geometry-normalised lengths without uncited structural stories, moisture cues require multimodal disclosure, and prior repair patches require cross-campaign IDs before a system may claim deterioration~\cite{zhao2024facade,zhao2026physical,jeon2025hybridrag}. A minimal claim-level draft remains the machine-readable carrier of that triage:

\begin{programcode}{Illustrative claim-level triage draft}
\begin{plainlisting}
schema version: ch8-claim-1\\
twin snapshot id: warehouse-v12\\
asset ids: W-3-17, W-4-17\\
claim 1 (observation): RGB staining on W-3-17; evidence obs-2403-118\\
claim 2 (hypothesis): possible moisture path; evidence obs-2403-118 and obs-2403-204;\\
relation path W-4-17, W-3-17; requires human review\\
action: local probe; status proposed; approval required\\
trust: workflow status draft; human check true;\\
notes RGB/IR disagreement on W-3-17
\end{plainlisting}
\end{programcode}

Every identity in such a draft must be subset-checked against retrieval; the human paragraph is rendered from the fields, not scraped afterward~\cite{zheng2023bimassistant,joffe2025buildingcodes}.

\paragraph{Illustrative end-to-end case (warehouse façade).}
\label{par:ch8-e2e}
The following walkthrough is teaching-only: Asset-IDs and counts are placeholders, not a field census. Query family covers local diagnosis, history, moisture propagation, audit, report draft, revisit proposal, abstention, and a rejected write-back.

\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Local:} ``What is on W-4-17?'' loads passport $\mathcal{P}(a)$ with RGB/IR observations; V1--V3 pass; the TRACE pins twin snapshot and detector versions.
\item \emph{History:} ``Was W-4-17 repaired last year?'' uses the Asset-ID map to resolve a predecessor code; if no approved Repair Event exists, answer unknown rather than invent a CMMS row.
\item \emph{Propagation:} ``Could moisture reach W-3-17?'' expands on $R_{\mathrm{moisture}}$; when upstairs IR is missing, V3 fails for a confirmed-path claim and the system escalates to human review with a revisit hint.
\item \emph{Audit:} ``Show evidence for claim C2.'' returns observation IDs and openable crops from the claim schema, with no new LLM narrative.
\item \emph{Report:} the draft is validated and then approved; export is allowed only after human sign-off.
\item \emph{Revisit / abstain:} the agent proposes a revisit under geofence; the TRACE records tool arguments and the operator.
\item \emph{Rejected write:} the operator rejects a draft Repair Event; the report may stay approved, but twin write remains forbidden (no write committed).
\end{enumerate}

Common failure modes collapse into the same contracts. Invented Asset-IDs appear when validators are missing; false moisture paths appear when embedding neighbourhood replaces BIM adjacency; confirmed seepage appears when infrared-only cards lose uncertainty flags; silent twin mutation appears when report drafting and twin write share one unguarded button; edition averaging appears when manuals are unpinned~\cite{ji2023,peng2024,lee2024ragsafety,luo2025llmdtrobot}. Adding more agent personas does not repair those failures. Shared twin memory, typed tools, and gates do~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,rawat2025llmmas,gao2026ifcagent}. Procurement and classroom assessment should therefore demand the same artefacts: Asset-ID dictionary, neighbour-edge provenance, observation schema, edition pins, intent--assembly policy, gated tool surface, CMMS field map, gold questions with abstentions, and paired TRACE files. Figures~\ref{fig:ch8-memory-ladder}, \ref{fig:ch8-harness-fig}, and~\ref{fig:ch8-sid-console} and Tables~\ref{tab:ch8-harness} and~\ref{tab:ch8-cap-maturity} are load-bearing teaching objects for that demand~\cite{yao2023react,hellin2025bimmas,buga2025llmfmsql}.

Looking toward Chapter~9, the open problems are mostly socio-technical: incomplete topology on older buildings, contractor identifier collisions, scarce agent benchmarks for safe inspection tool use, and governance when a grounded-looking answer is wrong~\cite{saka2024gptconstruction,ma2026tenquestions}. Larger models alone will not close those gaps. The constructive response taught here remains continuous with Sections~\ref{sec:ch8-dt}--\ref{sec:ch8-reports}: make the twin decision-ready, retrieve over assets, fuse modalities without erasing conflict, bound the agent, and evaluate what facility teams can schedule.

In summary, this chapter is the monograph's decision-support phase. It consumes Chapters~4--7, teaches a model harness with an inspection-centred memory ladder, treats VLMs as optional crop readers under citation gates, and delivers claim-level reports with triage and conditional advice under professional control. Predictive maintenance remains an external-model interface unless multi-campaign evidence exists. The measure of success is not rhetorical fluency, but named assets, cited evidence, and actions that remain gated.
