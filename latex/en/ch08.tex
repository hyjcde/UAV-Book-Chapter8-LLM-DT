\chapter{Digital Twin with LLMs in Inspection Analysis}
\label{ch:llm-dt}

\abstract*{This chapter connects building digital twins with large language models for façade inspection analysis. We structure defect evidence as Integrated Defect and Asset Profiles, retrieve it with topology-aware RAG, extend the stack with agentic tool-use and multi-agent workflows, and show how grounded generation supports report drafting and maintenance decision support.}

\abstract{This chapter connects building digital twins with large language models for façade inspection analysis. Beyond visualisation, twins become decision-ready when defect observations are provenance-aware, asset-addressable, and retrievable under citation constraints. We present Integrated Defect Profiles (IDPs) and Integrated Asset Profiles (IAPs), Vertical Defect Propagation Paths (VDPPs) for topology-aware retrieval, multi-modal fusion, and---extending recent work that stops at RAG---agentic workflows that plan tools, query IFC/BIM services, and close loops with field robots and human approval. Report schemas, Asset-ID audits, and benchmarked decision support complete the pipeline. Target length for this chapter is approximately fifty pages in monograph format.}

\section{Digital Twins for Building Inspection}
\label{sec:ch8-dt}

Building inspection increasingly depends on digital twins (DTs): living digital counterparts that organise imagery, geometry, and semantics around physical assets~\cite{shi2025cognitivedt}. In facility management (FM), twins are often used to browse 3D models, locate equipment, and store inspection photos. That is necessary but not sufficient. Inspectors and asset managers ask questions that require \emph{reasoning over evidence}: which cladding panels participate in a multi-storey moisture path; which assets should be prioritised for gondola access next quarter; whether a repair recommendation is consistent with the governing code clause.

\label{fig:ch8-dt-pipeline}
\end{figure}

\subsection{From geometric twins to decision-ready twins}
A geometric twin answers ``where is it?''. A decision-ready twin must also answer ``what is wrong, how might it propagate, what should we do, and which evidence supports that claim?''. Closing this gap requires three ingredients that later sections develop:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textbf{Structured defect knowledge} with provenance (who measured what, when, and with which detector);
\item \textbf{Asset-centric indices} aligned with maintainable units rather than arbitrary text chunks;
\item \textbf{Grounded language interfaces}---RAG and agents---that cite twin entities instead of inventing them.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Interfaces to upstream chapters}
This monograph's earlier chapters supply the twin's physical and perceptual layers. Reconstruction and registration (Chapter~4) provide geometry and BIM/GeoBIM anchors. Detection and segmentation models (Chapter~6) produce candidate defect observations. GIS integration (Chapter~7) supplies city-scale asset identifiers and spatiotemporal dashboards. Chapter~8 consumes those products and adds a \emph{cognitive} layer~\cite{shi2025cognitivedt}: language-accessible knowledge and decision support.

Recent agentic frameworks further argue that twins should not remain static databases. Luo et al.\ connect LLMs, twins, and robots so that natural-language FM requests can trigger inspection missions and human-approved twin updates~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot}. BIM2RDT transforms design BIM into robot-ready site twins with safety-first agentic control~\cite{akhavian2025bim2rdt}. Multi-agent systems have also been proposed to construct and calibrate virtual building models across design-to-operation~\cite{singh2025multiagentvbm}. These works motivate treating the twin as both a knowledge base \emph{and} an action substrate.

\section{Foundations of LLMs for Civil Engineering Applications}
\label{sec:ch8-llm-foundations}

Large language models (LLMs) provide flexible interfaces to heterogeneous project knowledge: specifications, inspection notes, BIM property sets, and maintenance logs. Surveys of building applications emphasise opportunities in compliance checking, energy analysis, and operations support, while warning about bias, privacy, and unverifiable advice~\cite{xiao2025tenquestions}. For façade inspection, three failure modes dominate: (i)~hallucinated asset identifiers; (ii)~repair advice without code grounding; (iii)~fluent summaries that omit contradictory evidence.

\subsection{Grounding mechanisms}
Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG)~\cite{lewis2020} attaches parametric generation to external memory. Variants such as HyDE~\cite{gao2023hyde}, Self-RAG~\cite{asai2024selfrag}, Adaptive-RAG~\cite{jeong2024adaptive}, and GraphRAG~\cite{edge2024graphrag} improve query formulation, reflection, or graph-structured corpora. In construction planning, graph RAG has been used to reduce information silos~\cite{he2025graphrag}. These methods address \emph{evidence access}. They do not, by themselves, decide which tool to call next, when to request a new UAV flight, or how to update an IFC attribute after human approval.

\subsection{From RAG to agents}
Agent paradigms interleave reasoning and acting~\cite{yao2023react}, teach models to use tools~\cite{schick2023toolformer}, and organise multi-agent workflows~\cite{wang2024surveyagents,li2024surveymultiagent}. In AEC, agents have been used for IFC querying and modification~\cite{gao2026ifcagent}, building-energy modelling schemas~\cite{zhang2025llmagentschema}, and BIM information retrieval via DSL alignment~\cite{guo2025bimllmquery}. The practical lesson for inspection is a division of labour:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textbf{RAG} maximises evidence precision and citation fidelity for diagnosis questions;
\item \textbf{Agents} orchestrate multi-step procedures (retrieve $\rightarrow$ check topology $\rightarrow$ draft report $\rightarrow$ open work order $\rightarrow$ dispatch robot);
\item \textbf{Schemas and Asset-IDs} remain the audit backbone for both.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Bridge.}
Agents and RAG both presuppose clean knowledge units. The next section defines those units inside the twin.

\section{From Digital Twins to Language Reasoning}
\label{sec:ch8-dt-to-language}

\subsection{Integrated Defect Profiles (IDPs)}
\label{sec:ch8-idp-detail}
We represent each maintainable defect observation as an Integrated Defect Profile (IDP), a provenance-aware tuple linking imagery evidence, semantic attributes, spatial pose, and asset association. Following the IAP-RAG formulation used in our industrial study, each atomic IDP instance is
\begin{equation}
P_i = \langle \mathcal{I},\ \mathcal{E},\ \mathcal{S},\ \mathcal{A} \rangle,
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{I}$ is an identity header, $\mathcal{E}$ multi-modal evidence, $\mathcal{S}$ spatio-semantic localisation, and $\mathcal{A}$ analysis attributes (type, severity, material context).

\paragraph{Identity and naming.}
The header uses a GUID-based convention $\mathcal{I}=\texttt{IDP\_\{g\}\_\{n\}}$, where $g$ is the host BIM component GUID and $n$ a per-component sequence number. This makes every observation portfolio-unique and immediately traceable to a maintainable host---a prerequisite for citation checks later.

\paragraph{Modality-aware evidence.}
Evidence $\mathcal{E}$ keeps RGB and infrared (IR) references separate rather than prematurely fusing them. IR often signals subsurface anomalies (e.g., delamination, moisture), while RGB documents surface symptoms (cracks, spalling, discoloration). Preserving modality provenance lets the LLM---and the conflict policy in Section~\ref{sec:ch8-multimodal}---weight complementary physics instead of averaging incompatible claims.

\paragraph{Spatio-semantic localisation.}
Localisation $\mathcal{S}$ combines global coordinates $\mathbf{P}_g$ (e.g., WGS84) with normalised surface coordinates on the host component. If $\mathbf{V}_o$ is the component origin and $\hat{\mathbf{u}},\hat{\mathbf{v}}$ unit axes of length $L$ and height $H$,
\begin{equation}
 r_x = \frac{(\mathbf{P}_g - \mathbf{V}_o) \cdot \hat{\mathbf{u}}}{L}, \quad
 r_y = \frac{(\mathbf{P}_g - \mathbf{V}_o) \cdot \hat{\mathbf{v}}}{H}.
\end{equation}
Because LLMs handle ``top-centre of the southeast panel'' better than raw metres, the twin also stores a natural-language location descriptor. Field crews still receive metric local coordinates for navigation.

\paragraph{Why not store raw files only?}
Raw image folders and detector JSON dumps are brittle for language systems: filenames drift, detector versions change, and cross-view duplicates explode. The IDP discipline forces every claim-relevant observation to carry a stable identifier, a timestamp, a detector signature, and an asset link. That discipline is what later allows citation checks such as Cited$\in$Retr.

\begin{algorithm}[t]
\caption{Sketch of IDP construction from registered detections}
\label{alg:ch8-idp}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\Require Registered detection set $\mathcal{D}$, BIM/asset map $\mathcal{M}$, imagery store $\mathcal{I}$
\Ensure IDP corpus $\mathcal{P}$
\State $\mathcal{P}\leftarrow\emptyset$
\For{each detection $d\in\mathcal{D}$}
  \State Resolve asset $a\leftarrow\mathrm{Associate}(d,\mathcal{M})$; \textbf{continue} if unresolved
  \State Pack imagery refs $I\leftarrow\mathrm{CollectViews}(d,\mathcal{I})$
  \State Pack evidence $E\leftarrow\{\mathrm{detector}, \mathrm{score}, \mathrm{time}, \mathrm{pose}\}$
  \State Pack semantics $S\leftarrow\{\mathrm{type}, \mathrm{severity}, \mathrm{attributes}\}$
  \State $\mathcal{P}\leftarrow\mathcal{P}\cup\{\langle I,E,S,a\rangle\}$
\EndFor
\State \Return $\mathcal{P}$
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\subsection{Integrated Asset Profiles (IAPs)}
\label{sec:ch8-iap-detail}
Maintenance questions are usually asset-centric (``what is the status of panel P-12?'') rather than chunk-centric. Chunk RAG often severs the link between a symptom and its host, inviting hallucinations that mix locations. IAPs reverse that failure mode: each IAP aggregates all Atomic IDPs on one BIM host and becomes the \emph{primary retrieval unit}.

An IAP identifier is derived from the BIM GUID (direct $O(1)$ lookup). Internally it has three layers:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textbf{Symptoms registry:} modality-tagged inventories (e.g., IR delamination $\times 2$, RGB crack $\times 3$) plus a composite risk/severity summary;
\item \textbf{Geometry:} length, height, area---needed to normalise severity (a 200\,mm crack is not the same on a parapet and a shear wall);
\item \textbf{Topology:} above/below/lateral neighbours, floor level, and façade orientation---the substrate for VDPP expansion.
\end{enumerate}
In the locked corpus, 604 IDPs collapse into 44 maintainable assets---compression that matches FM cognition and improves multi-hop retrieval over chunk baselines (Section~\ref{sec:ch8-dss}).

\subsection{Five-level hierarchical index}
\label{sec:ch8-index}
To serve queries from portfolio dashboards down to a single image, knowledge is organised as
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{H}:\ \mathrm{L1(Site)} \supset \mathrm{L2(Topology\ cluster)} \supset \mathrm{L3(IAP)} \supset \mathrm{L4(Atomic\ IDP)} \supset \mathrm{L5(Raw\ metadata)}.
\end{equation}
L1 supports site-wide risk lists; L2 groups structurally related neighbourhoods (e.g., a north elevation); L3 returns a full asset passport; L4 drills into one observation; L5 preserves raw imagery paths and timestamps for audit. Provenance never stops at the summary layer.

\subsection{Vertical Defect Propagation Paths (VDPPs)}
\label{sec:ch8-vdpp}
VDPPs encode the diagnostic premise that related defects should be read together. Lexical similarity alone may omit upstairs moisture when the query mentions a mid-storey delamination. For a seed asset $A_j$,
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{N}_k(A_j)=\{n:d_{\mathrm{topo}}(A_j,n)\le k\}.
\end{equation}
In reported runs, retrieval takes $k_{\mathrm{seed}}{=}3$ dense-cosine IAP seeds (BGE-M3), then expands related neighbours with a small fixed policy (above-storey and horizontal adjacencies, de-duplicated). Neither hop integer is fitted on the 200-question benchmark. This is complementary to generic GraphRAG: the graph is BIM adjacency tied to Asset-IDs, not a document co-occurrence graph.

\paragraph{Teaching note.}
Contrast three graphs: (i)~document similarity; (ii)~text entity co-occurrence; (iii)~BIM adjacency. Only (iii) encodes physical propagation hypotheses inspectors already use. LLMs may narrate (iii); they must not invent missing edges.

\section{Multi-Modal Fusion and Topology-Aware RAG}
\label{sec:ch8-multimodal}

Façade inspection is multi-modal by nature: RGB and infrared imagery, geometric registration residuals, textual manuals, and historical work orders. Fusion in this chapter is \emph{pipeline fusion} rather than a single end-to-end vision--language call. Detectors and registration modules write IDPs; dense retrieval operates over textualised IAP views; the LLM composes answers under citation constraints.

\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.92\textwidth]{rag_workflow_topology.pdf}
\caption{Topology-aware RAG workflow: intent parsing, IAP retrieval, VDPP expansion, and grounded generation.}
\label{fig:ch8-rag}
\end{figure}

\subsection{Three-phase retrieval execution}
\label{sec:ch8-rag-exec}
Topology-aware RAG runs in three phases.

\paragraph{Phase 1 --- Intent parsing.}
Natural-language queries become structured directives. A deterministic keyword/trigger extractor assigns intents such as localised lookup, asset-health summary, root-cause/propagation analysis, or floor-level audit. Intent controls which IAP fields and neighbourhood scope are emphasised.

\paragraph{Phase 2 --- VDPP expansion.}
Seed IAPs are retrieved by dense cosine search; neighbours enter via $\mathcal{N}_k$. A severe host finding is more useful when upstairs moisture and lateral cracking on the same elevation share one context package.

\paragraph{Phase 3 --- Context assembly.}
The package $\mathcal{K}$ for the LLM is
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{K}
=
\mathrm{IAP}(A_j)
\cup
\bigcup_{n\in\widetilde{\mathcal{N}}(A_j)}\omega_n\cdot\mathrm{IAP}(n)
\cup
\mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{relevant}},
\end{equation}
where $\widetilde{\mathcal{N}}$ is the related-neighbour set and $\mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{relevant}}$ is an optional expert-knowledge block (disabled in the locked primary runs). Hard constraints require cited Asset-/Defect-IDs to be subsets of retrieved IDs; otherwise the system must lower confidence and request human verification.

An optional ranking mode mixes semantic, topological, physical-slot, and evidence-coverage signals,
\begin{equation}
R(a_i,q)=\lambda_s S_{\mathrm{sem}}+\lambda_t S_{\mathrm{topo}}+\lambda_p S_{\mathrm{phys}}+\lambda_e S_{\mathrm{evid}},
\end{equation}
with pilot weights $(\lambda_s,\lambda_t,\lambda_p,\lambda_e)=(0.45,0.25,0.20,0.10)$. Primary tables use dense cosine + VDPP expansion; $R(\lambda)$ is a same-contract sensitivity check.

\subsection{Conflict handling and expert grounding}
RGB/IR fusion follows a host-level policy: concordant modalities raise confidence; RGB-only evidence must not escalate to subsurface claims; IR-only evidence retains uncertainty; conflicting GUID/location/timestamp metadata blocks definitive diagnosis. Outputs expose \texttt{confidence\_level}, \texttt{evidence\_ids}, and \texttt{recommended\_human\_verification}.

Expert manuals and codes (repair guides, safety notes, practice notes) are designed as a curated, edition-versioned corpus separate from web scrape. In reported primary runs the expert index is off; the chapter still presents it as the normative grounding path for compliance-oriented answers under engineer review.

\subsection{Static RAG versus agentic RAG}
Agentic RAG systems rewrite queries, call multiple tools, and iterate~\cite{singh2024agenticragsurvey}. They can improve coverage on complex tasks, but they also expand the attack surface for untraceable intermediate steps. For inspection, we recommend \emph{bounded agency}: allow multi-step retrieval and tool calls, but require every final claim to cite twin entities (Asset-ID / Defect-ID) present in the retrieved set.

\section{Agentic Workflows for Twin-Grounded Inspection}
\label{sec:ch8-agents}

This section is the main extension beyond the DefectGPT/IAP-RAG paper. RAG answers evidence-seeking questions; agents execute procedures that change system state---opening tickets, commanding robots, patching IFC properties---under human oversight.

\subsection{Reference architectures in AEC}
Luo et al.\ place an LLM as a broker among user, twin, and robot middleware: if twin data are stale, the system emits structured robot tasks and logs human-approved updates~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot}. IFC-Agent uses schema-guided multi-agent tool composition for native IFC reasoning and edits~\cite{gao2026ifcagent}. BIM2RDT emphasises safety-first agentic updates of site twins from robot sensing~\cite{akhavian2025bim2rdt}. Energy-oriented agent schemas show how domain libraries can be modularised~\cite{zhang2025llmagentschema}. Together they suggest a reusable pattern: \textbf{perceive $\rightarrow$ ground in twin schema $\rightarrow$ act with tools $\rightarrow$ verify with humans $\rightarrow$ commit to twin}.

\subsection{A façade-inspection agent stack}
For UAV façade programmes we propose four cooperating roles (logical agents, not necessarily four model instances):
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textbf{Retriever agent:} IAP/VDPP RAG with citation packing;
\item \textbf{Topology agent:} neighbourhood expansion and conflict checks on BIM adjacency;
\item \textbf{Report agent:} fills a constrained output schema (Section~\ref{sec:ch8-reports});
\item \textbf{Action agent:} optional tools---GIS ticket API, robot/UAV tasking, IFC property update---always behind approval gates.
\end{enumerate}
ReAct-style traces~\cite{yao2023react} make intermediate tool calls inspectable. Dual-memory ideas (procedural context + entity cache) from IFC-Agent~\cite{gao2026ifcagent} map naturally onto our Asset-ID caches.

\paragraph{Worked micro-scenario.}
A manager asks: ``Which assets above panel A-07 need gondola access if seepage propagates two storeys?'' The retriever returns A-07's IAP; the topology agent expands a VDPP of hop $k{=}2$; the report agent drafts a priority list with Defect-IDs; the action agent \emph{proposes} a revisit polygon for UAV tasking but does not dispatch until approval. This is deliberately narrower than fully autonomous site robots, yet already beyond single-shot RAG.

\subsection{Related agent systems and what we reuse}
IFC-Agent's schema-guided tool composition~\cite{gao2026ifcagent} informs how we expose ``expand VDPP'', ``get IAP'', and ``render defect image'' as tools rather than hoping the LLM invents SQL. Luo et al.'s human-in-the-loop twin commit~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot} informs our approval gate. BIM2RDT's safety-first posture~\cite{akhavian2025bim2rdt} informs geofencing requirements when action tools include robots. Energy agent libraries~\cite{zhang2025llmagentschema} suggest packaging façade tools as versioned schemas so that prompts do not hard-code brittle APIs.

\subsection{Tool catalogue for façade programmes}
A minimal tool surface that already exceeds single-shot RAG includes:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item \texttt{get\_iap(asset\_id)} / \texttt{search\_iaps(query,$k$)};
\item \texttt{expand\_vdpp(asset\_id,$k$)};
\item \texttt{list\_idps(asset\_id, modality?)};
\item \texttt{open\_evidence(defect\_id)} (returns image URIs for UI);
\item \texttt{draft\_report(schema\_id, context\_pkg)};
\item \texttt{propose\_uav\_revisit(polygon, priority)} (proposal only);
\item \texttt{propose\_twin\_patch(ifc\_ref, field, value)} (requires approval).
\end{itemize}
Exposing these as typed tools---in the spirit of IFC-Agent's schema-guided composition~\cite{gao2026ifcagent} and Toolformer-style APIs~\cite{schick2023toolformer}---keeps the LLM from inventing SQL against the twin database.

\subsection{Evaluation gap for agents}
Static Exact Hit metrics (Section~\ref{sec:ch8-dss}) measure evidence grounding. They do not measure whether an agent chose a safe tool sequence. Future chapter revisions should add task success under constraints (no geofence violation; no unapproved twin write; citation validity after multi-step traces). Until then, agents in this monograph remain advisory orchestrators.

\subsection{What agents must not do}
Agents must not invent Asset-IDs, silently overwrite twin fields, or dispatch robots without geofence and safety checks~\cite{akhavian2025bim2rdt}. In regulated buildings, the twin commit step remains human-confirmed, matching Luo et al.'s approval loop~\cite{luo2025llmdtrobot}.

\paragraph{Bridge.}
Whether RAG-only or agentic, deliverables must be report artefacts that FM systems can store.

\subsection{Towards predictive maintenance}
Priority scoring and operational planning (e.g., gondola staging) convert diagnosis into schedules. Predictive claims must remain calibrated: agents may propose revisit plans, but deterioration forecasting still requires temporal models beyond a single inspection epoch.

\subsection{Limitations and outlook}
Quality depends on topology completeness and Asset-ID hygiene. Fully VLM-native twins and swarm-continuous updates remain future work, as do standardised benchmarks for \emph{agentic} inspection tasks (tool success under safety constraints), which static Exact Hit metrics do not fully capture.

\paragraph{Chapter takeaways.}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
\item Decision-ready twins need provenance-aware, asset-addressable defect knowledge---not only 3D meshes.
\item RAG grounds answers; agents orchestrate tools and twin updates; both require citation and approval constraints.
\item Report schemas and field Asset-ID audits are as critical as model choice for deployable inspection assistants.
\end{enumerate}
